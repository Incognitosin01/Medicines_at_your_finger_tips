Uses for Nedocromil (EENT)

Allergic Conjunctivitis

Nedocromil (EENT) Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Administer at regular intervals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If more than 1 topical ophthalmic drug is used, administer the drugs at least 10–15 minutes apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of the solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Continue therapy throughout period of exposure (i.e., until pollen season is over or until exposure to offending allergen is terminated), even in absence of symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as nedocromil sodium; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children ≥3 years of age: 1 or 2 drops of a 2% solution in each eye twice daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Nedocromil (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Minimally absorbed (<4%) following topical application to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Rapid onset (within minutes).<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Not metabolized.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Excreted principally unchanged in urine (70%) and feces (30%).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Tightly closed bottle at 2–25°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Suppresses release of mediators (e.g., histamine) from cells involved in hypersensitivity reactions (including mast cells).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Decreases chemotaxis and activation of eosinophils.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Inhibits release of secondary inflammatory mediators from activated inflammatory cells (e.g., eosinophils, macrophages, neutrophils, monocytes).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contamination of the solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not wearing contact lenses in the presence of signs and symptoms of allergic conjunctivitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If contact lenses must be worn, delay insertion of soft contact lenses after instillation, since benzalkonium chloride preservative may be absorbed by some soft contact lenses.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of administering different topical ophthalmic preparations at least 10–15 minutes apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information. (See Cautions.)
