Uses for Antithrombin alfa

Congenital Antithrombin III Deficiency

Prevention of perioperative and peripartum thromboembolism in patients with congenital antithrombin III deficiency; designated an orphan drug by FDA for use in these conditions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Short-term thromboprophylaxis with an appropriate anticoagulant recommended in patients with congenital antithrombin III deficiency during high-risk situations (e.g., surgery, pregnancy, postpartum period);<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> antithrombin III concentrates may be used when anticoagulant therapy is neither feasible (e.g., due to a bleeding risk) nor sufficient.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Not indicated for treatment of thromboembolic events.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Heparin Resistance

Has been used for management of heparin resistance during cardiopulmonary bypass (CPB)†.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

May reduce requirements for fresh frozen plasma and additional heparin during CPB.<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Antithrombin alfa Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer by IV infusion using 0.22-µm inline filter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Allow vials of antithrombin alfa (antithrombin [recombinant]) powder for injection to reach room temperature (20–25°C) ≤3 hours prior to reconstitution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute vial containing approximately 1750 units of drug with 10 mL sterile water for injection immediately before use; do not shake.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May be further diluted (e.g., to obtain a concentration of 100 units/mL) for IV administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Withdraw reconstituted contents of 1 or more vials (depending on calculated dose) into a sterile disposable syringe or add to an infusion bag containing 0.9% sodium chloride injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer as soon as possible or within 8–12 hours of preparation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May be stored at room temperature until administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discard any unused portions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer initial IV loading dose over 15 minutes followed by a continuous IV maintenance infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Potency expressed in international units (IU, units) as tested against activity of the WHO reference standard.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Each vial contains approximately 1750 units.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use different dosing formulas for treatment of pregnant versus nonpregnant patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> (See Distribution: Special Populations, under Pharmacokinetics and see Elimination: Special Populations, under Pharmacokinetics.)

Pregnant women undergoing a surgical procedure should be treated according to the dosing formula for pregnant patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> Initiate treatment prior to delivery or approximately 24 hours before surgery; continue until adequate follow-on anticoagulation is established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Maintenance dose (units/hr) = [ (100 − baseline antithrombin III activity level) / 5.4] × body weight in kg

Adjust dosage based on serial monitoring of serum antithrombin III activity levels (expressed as a percentage of normal).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> Monitor serum antithrombin III activity levels prior to initiation of therapy and at regular intervals (i.e., once or twice daily) during therapy to achieve therapeutic antithrombin III activity levels in the range of 80–120% of normal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Antithrombin alfa

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to goat and goat milk proteins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to goat and goat milk proteins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Potential for enhanced anticoagulant effects if used concomitantly with unfractionated heparin, low molecular weight heparin, or other drugs that use antithrombin III to exert their anticoagulant effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Interactions.)

Perform coagulation tests (e.g., aPTT, anti-factor Xa activity) at regular and frequent intervals in patients receiving concomitant anticoagulant therapy, especially in the first few hours following initiation or withdrawal of antithrombin alfa (antithrombin [recombinant]) therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Closely monitor for bleeding or thrombosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potential for allergic-type hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylaxis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Closely monitor for manifestations of hypersensitivity (e.g., hives, generalized urticaria, chest tightness, wheezing, hypotension, anaphylaxis).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Immediately discontinue drug and administer appropriate emergency treatment if hypersensitivity occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potential for antibody development to antithrombin alfa, goat antithrombin, or goat-milk proteins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> Confirmed immunologic reactions not reported to date.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

A patient registry has been established to further evaluate immunogenic potential of antithrombin alfa.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Clinicians encouraged to participate by calling Lundbeck Inc. at 800-455-1141.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Does not appear to be associated with an increased risk of fetal abnormalities when administered during the third trimester.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> However, use during pregnancy only if clearly indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distributed into milk in low concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use with caution and only if clearly indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients <18 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether they respond differently than younger patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Select dosage with caution, initiating at low end of dosage range, because of age-related decreases in hepatic, renal, and/or cardiac function and of concomitant disease and drug therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Risk of allergic-type hypersensitivity reactions; importance of recognizing early manifestations of hypersensitivity (e.g., hives, generalized urticaria, chest tightness, wheezing, hypotension, anaphylaxis) and immediately informing a clinician if any of these signs or symptoms occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of reporting any previous or current known hypersensitivity to goat or goat milk proteins prior to receiving antithrombin alfa.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of bleeding with concurrent use of antithrombin alfa and other anticoagulants; importance of informing a clinician if any bleeding occurs during treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
