Introduction

Uses for Aprepitant/Fosaprepitant

Cancer Chemotherapy-induced Nausea and Vomiting

Prevention of acute and delayed nausea and vomiting associated with initial and repeat courses of highly emetogenic cancer chemotherapy, including high-dose cisplatin therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Used in combination with other antiemetic agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Prevention of nausea and vomiting associated with initial and repeat courses of moderately emetogenic cancer chemotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Used in combination with other antiemetic agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

For prevention of nausea and vomiting associated with highly emetogenic chemotherapy regimens (including an anthracycline plus cyclophosphamide), ASCO recommends a 3-drug antiemetic regimen consisting of an NK1 receptor antagonist (e.g., either oral aprepitant or IV fosaprepitant), a 5-HT3 receptor antagonist (e.g., dolasetron, granisetron, ondansetron, palonosetron), and dexamethasone.<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> ASCO states that fixed-combination netupitant and palonosetron plus dexamethasone is an additional treatment option.<html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html>

For moderately emetogenic chemotherapy regimens, ASCO recommends a 2-drug antiemetic regimen preferably consisting of palonosetron and dexamethasone.<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> If palonosetron is not available, a first-generation 5-HT3 receptor antagonist (preferably granisetron or ondansetron) may be substituted.<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> Limited evidence suggests that aprepitant may be added to this regimen; in such cases, use of any 5-HT3 receptor antagonist is appropriate.<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

For chemotherapy regimens with a low emetogenic risk, ASCO recommends administration of a single dose of dexamethasone prior to chemotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

For chemotherapy regimens with minimal emetogenic risk, ASCO states that routine antiemetic administration is not necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy for chronic use or for treatment of established nausea and vomiting not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>
