Uses for Adenovirus Vaccine

Prevention of Adenovirus Respiratory Disease

Prevention of febrile acute respiratory disease caused by adenovirus type 4 and type 7.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Labeled by FDA for use in military populations 17 through 50 years of age;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> not commercially available for use in other individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> (See Restricted Distribution under Dosage and Administration.)

Adenoviruses, especially adenovirus type 4 and type 7, commonly cause acute respiratory disease (e.g., runny nose, fever, sore throat, breathing problems, cough, headache, croup, bronchitis).<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Certain adenovirus serotypes cause other illnesses (e.g., conjunctivitis, keratoconjunctivitis, otitis media, gastroenteritis, cystitis).<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Disseminated or life-threatening infections can occur (e.g., severe pneumonia, hepatitis, meningitis, encephalitis).<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Adenoviruses spread person to person via direct contact, respiratory droplet transmission, or food and/or water contaminated with feces.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Fomites also may be involved in transmission since adenoviruses survive for long periods outside of the body, including on environmental surfaces, and are unusually stable when exposed to chemical and physical agents or adverse pH conditions.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Military recruits are at increased risk of acute respiratory illnesses during basic training because of several factors, including close sleeping and training environments where transmission of respiratory pathogens is facilitated, congregation of young adults arriving from wide geographic distributions who may enter basic training carrying pathogens capable of being spread to others who are immunologically susceptible, and stressful nature of basic training and military operations.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> Adenoviruses, especially adenovirus type 4 and type 7, are a well documented cause of acute respiratory illness in military recruits;<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> adenoviruses reportedly cause 50â€“80% of cases of acute respiratory disease in this population.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> Outbreaks of adenovirus-associated disease also occur in other populations (e.g., healthcare-associated outbreaks),<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> but the combination of sustained transmission and relatively high and predictable attack rates of adenovirus-associated respiratory disease appears to be unique to military basic trainees.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

US Department of Defense (DOD) requires that all enlisted US military recruits 17 through 50 years of age receive a single dose of adenovirus type 4 and type 7 vaccine live oral at the earliest opportunity upon arrival at initial entry training (basic military training), unless contraindicated (see Contraindications under Cautions).<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> May also be recommended for other military personnel at high risk for adenovirus infection,<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> but not required for cadre working at enlisted basic training sites.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Adenovirus Vaccine Dosage and Administration

General

Developed specifically for use in military populations.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Labeled by FDA for use in military populations 17 through 50 years of age;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> not available for use in other populations.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer orally as 2 separate tablets:<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> one tablet containing adenovirus type 4 vaccine component and one tablet containing adenovirus type 7 vaccine component.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tablets contain live adenovirus inside an enteric coating;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> designed to pass through stomach intact and release live vaccine virus in the intestine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Swallow tablets whole; do not chew or crush.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> If tablets are chewed, adenovirus will be released too soon and could expose the upper respiratory tract to live vaccine virus and result in adenovirus disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Defer administration of vaccine tablets in individuals with vomiting and/or diarrhea.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> (See Concomitant Illness under Cautions.)

May be given simultaneously with or at any interval before or after other vaccines.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>134</sup></body></html> (See Vaccines under Interactions.)

Warnings/Precautions

Natural adenovirus infection during pregnancy has been associated with fetal harm.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not known whether adenovirus vaccine can cause fetal harm.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Pregnancy under Cautions.)

Contraindicated in pregnant women;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> avoid pregnancy for at least 6 weeks after vaccination.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Because vaccinees shed live vaccine virus<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> for up to 28 days after vaccination<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> and because of possibility of fetal harm if a pregnant woman is exposed to adenovirus, advise vaccine recipients to use caution for 28 days after vaccination if in close contact with a pregnant woman.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

US Public Health Service Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP) states that individuals with altered immunocompetence generally should not receive live vaccines since these individuals may be at increased risk for adverse reactions to such vaccines and may have diminished or suboptimal immune responses to vaccines.<html><head></head><body><sup>134</sup></body></html> These experts state that use of live virus vaccines can be considered in patients with leukemia, lymphoma, or other malignancies if the disease is in remission and chemotherapy was terminated at least 3 months prior to vaccination.<html><head></head><body><sup>134</sup></body></html> (See Immunosuppressive Agents under Interactions.)

Adenovirus vaccine contains live adenovirus;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> vaccine virus is shed in the stool of vaccine recipients and can be transmitted to and cause disease in close contacts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
