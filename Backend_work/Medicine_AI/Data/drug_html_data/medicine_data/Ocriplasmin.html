Uses for Ocriplasmin

Vitreomacular Adhesion

Treatment of symptomatic vitreomacular adhesion (VMA).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Provides pharmacologic (nonsurgical) option for patients with less severe symptomatic VMA to receive treatment at an earlier stage and potentially avoid surgical complications.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Ocriplasmin Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer by intravitreal injection only into the affected eye by a qualified physician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Must be diluted prior to intravitreal administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Remove vial from freezer and allow contents to thaw at room temperature for several minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Once completely thawed, dilute with 0.2 mL of sterile, preservative-free 0.9% sodium chloride for injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Gently swirl to mix contents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to intravitreal administration, withdraw entire contents of vial (0.4 mL) through a sterile 19-gauge needle into a 1-mL syringe (both provided by manufacturer<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> ) using aseptic technique.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discard needle and replace with sterile 30-gauge needle (also provided by manufacturer<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> ) for intravitreal injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> To obtain appropriate dose (0.125 mg), carefully expel air bubbles and excess drug until plunger tip is aligned with the line that marks 0.1 mL on the syringe.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use immediately because drug product contains no preservatives.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Inject under controlled aseptic conditions (including use of sterile gloves, sterile drape, and sterile eyelid speculum [or equivalent]) following adequate anesthesia and administration of a broad-spectrum anti-infective agent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

To administer intravitreally, insert 30-gauge injection needle 3.5–4 mm posterior to the limbus, aiming toward the center of vitreous cavity in order to deliver intended dose to the mid-vitreous.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid injecting into the horizontal meridian.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for elevation of IOP immediately following intravitreal injection; monitoring may include evaluation of optic nerve head perfusion or tonometry.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> A sterile paracentesis needle should be available.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use each vial only for treatment of a single eye; discard any unused portion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If contralateral eye requires treatment, use a new vial; change sterile field, syringe, gloves, drape, eyelid speculum, and injection needle before administering into the other eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

Repeated administration in the same eye is not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Intravitreal injection: 0.125 mg (0.1 mL of diluted solution) into the affected eye once as a single dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Repeated doses not recommended because of possible increased risk of lens subluxation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> (See Lens Subluxation under Cautions.)

If contralateral eye requires treatment, wait >7 days after initial injection before administering to contralateral eye because monitoring of postinjection course (including potential for decreased vision) in injected eye is necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

Dosage modification not required based on age or gender.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Ocriplasmin

Contraindications

  • Manufacturer states none known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states none known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

A decrease of ≥3 lines of best corrected visual acuity reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> The majority of cases were attributed to progression of VMA with traction; many such cases required surgical intervention.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor patients appropriately.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Intraocular inflammation or infection, intraocular hemorrhage, and increased IOP reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Most cases of intraocular inflammation were mild and transient.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for elevation of IOP immediately following intravitreal injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Administration under Dosage and Administration.) Instruct patients to promptly report any symptoms suggestive of endophthalmitis or retinal detachment (e.g., eye pain or redness, photophobia, blurred or decreased vision).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Lens subluxation reported in at least one patient who received an intravitreal dose of 0.175 mg (1.4 times the recommended dose).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Lens subluxation also reported in animals receiving a single dose that produced vitreous concentrations 1.4 times those achieved with the recommended dose; in monkeys receiving a second intravitreal dose 28 days after the first dose, lens subluxation was reported in 100% of treated eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Repeated administration in the same eye is not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Retinal detachment and retinal tear (without detachment) reported following ocriplasmin treatment; occurred before, during, or after vitrectomy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dyschromatopsia, described as yellowish vision, reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In approximately half of such cases, electroretinographic changes also reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

As with all therapeutic proteins, potential for immunogenicity exists; however, immunogenicity to ocriplasmin not evaluated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether ocriplasmin is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Caution if used in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No substantial differences in efficacy or safety relative to younger patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Ocriplasmin Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

The theoretical average vitreous concentration achieved immediately after intravitreal injection is approximately 29 mcg/mL (0.125 mg-dose delivered to 4.3 mL of vitreous volume).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Systemic concentrations expected to be low or undetectable following intravitreal injection because of the small dose (0.125 mg) administered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Undergoes autolysis in vitreous.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> In systemic circulation,<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> rapidly inactivated by protease inhibitor α2-antiplasmin or α2-macroglobulin of the endogenous protein catabolism pathway.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following intravitreal injection in a limited number of patients undergoing vitrectomy, mean vitreous concentrations declined rapidly.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> At 24 hours postinjection, mean vitreous concentrations were <3% of the theoretical vitreous concentration achieved immediately after injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Endogenous protein catabolism.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Frozen at or below −20°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Protect from light.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Store in original package until use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Proteolytic enzyme; exhibits activity against protein components (e.g. collagen, fibronectin, laminin) of the vitreous body and the vitreoretinal interface.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

  • Promotes liquefaction of the vitreous body and detachment of the vitreous from the retina (posterior vitreous detachment [PVD]) by dissolving the protein matrix responsible for VMA.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Risk of intraocular inflammation or infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of patients informing their ophthalmologist immediately if change in vision occurs or if the treated eye becomes red, sensitive to light, or painful.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Possible risk of temporary visual impairment after intravitreal injection;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> importance of avoiding driving a vehicle or operating heavy machinery until visual impairment has resolved.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If visual impairment persists or worsens, importance of seeking care from an ophthalmologist.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
