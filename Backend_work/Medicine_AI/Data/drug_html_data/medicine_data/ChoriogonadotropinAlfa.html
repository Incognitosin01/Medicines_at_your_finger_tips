Introduction

Gonad-stimulating hormone; biosynthetic (recombinant DNA-derived) form of human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Uses for Choriogonadotropin Alfa

Female Infertility

Used in conjunction with other infertility agents (e.g., gonadotropin-releasing hormone agonist, FSH) for induction of final follicular maturation and early luteinization in ovulatory, infertile women during assisted reproductive technology (ART) programs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Use in patients with tubal obstruction only if participating in ART programs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Choriogonadotropin alfa (r-hCG) is equivalent to urinary-derived hCG with regard to number of oocytes recovered, fertilized oocytes or embryos, and live births.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Used in conjunction with follicle-stimulating agent to induce ovulation in anovulatory, infertile women in whom anovulation is functional and not due to primary ovarian failure.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Choriogonadotropin alfa (r-hCG) is similar to urinary-derived hCG with regard to ovulation rates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Choriogonadotropin Alfa Dosage and Administration

Evaluate partner’s infertility.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

  • When ultrasound assessment and serum estradiol concentrations show sufficient follicular maturation, administer choriogonadotropin alfa 1 day after last dose of follicle-stimulating agent to complete final follicular maturation and induce ovulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of mild to moderate uncomplicated ovarian enlargement; may be accompanied by abdominal distention and/or pain, but generally regresses without treatment within 2–3 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Careful monitoring of ovarian response recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If ovaries are abnormally enlarged during controlled ovarian stimulation, withhold choriogonadotropin alfa during current course of therapy to minimize risk of OHSS.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html> (See Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome under Cautions.)

Risk of potentially severe OHSS, characterized by apparent dramatic increase in vascular permeability that may result in rapid accumulation of fluid in peritoneal cavity, thorax, and potentially, pericardium.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

May progress rapidly (within 24 hours to several days).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Initial manifestations include severe pelvic pain, nausea, vomiting, and weight gain.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> Other symptoms include abdominal pain/distention, diarrhea, severe ovarian enlargement, dyspnea, and oliguria.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html> Hypovolemia, hemoconcentration, electrolyte imbalances, ascites, hemoperitoneum, pleural effusions, hydrothorax, acute pulmonary distress, and thromboembolic events may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Transient liver function test abnormalities, which may be accompanied by morphologic changes (as detected by liver biopsy), reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Occurs most often after completion of gonadotropin therapy, reaching maximum severity after 7–10 days; usually resolves spontaneously with onset of menses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> Monitor patients for ≥2 weeks after hCG administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>l</sup></body></html> OHSS may be more severe and protracted if pregnancy occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

If severe OHSS develops, discontinue therapy, hospitalize patient, and consult clinician experienced in management of OHSS or fluid and electrolyte imbalances.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Multiple ovulations resulting in multiple gestations reported in 30.9 or 13.3% of women during ART programs or ovulation induction, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Risk of multiple births correlates with number of embryos transferred.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Potential for arterial thromboembolism exists.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May cause fetal harm; exclude pregnancy before initiating treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Animal studies indicate adverse effects on pregnancy outcomes and/or labor.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications under Cautions.)

Administer only under supervision of qualified clinicians experienced in fertility disorders and interpretation of indices of ovulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor follicular development (e.g., using transvaginal ultrasound, serum estradiol concentrations) to correctly identify follicular maturation, determine timing of choriogonadotropin alfa administration, detect ovarian enlargement, and minimize risks of OHSS and multiple gestation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Choriogonadotropin Alfa Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following sub-Q administration, absolute bioavailability is 40%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Peak serum concentrations attained in 12–24 hours; detectable in serum for 10 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Following midcycle administration, stimulation of luteal-phase progesterone production persists for approximately 10 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Median apparent volume of distribution is 21.4 L in women undergoing in vitro fertilization.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Urinary-derived hCG extensively metabolized, principally in the liver and kidneys, to active metabolites; urinary-derived choriogonadotropin alfa (r-hCG) exhibits similar pharmacokinetics as hCG.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>k</sup></body></html>

Excreted in urine principally as metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Biphasic; median terminal half-life is 29.2 hours in women undergoing in vitro fertilization.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics not evaluated in patients with renal or hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
