Warnings/Precautions

Drugs that Prolong the QT Interval

Possible additive effects on corrected QT (QTc)-interval prolongation with drugs known to prolong the QT interval or cause torsades de pointes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid concomitant use; discontinue use of such drugs or consider use of alternative drugs without QT-interval prolongation potential.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If concomitant use cannot be avoided, monitor ECGs and serum electrolyte concentrations more frequently (i.e., prior to initiation of inotuzumab ozogamicin therapy, after initiation of any drug known to prolong the QTc interval, and periodically during therapy as clinically indicated).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Prolongation of QT Interval under Cautions.)

Hepatotoxic Drugs

Because hepatic VOD and liver function test abnormalities are associated with inotuzumab ozogamicin therapy, some experts recommend avoiding concomitant therapy with other drugs known to cause hepatotoxicity in patients receiving inotuzumab ozogamicin and high-dose conditioning regimens with alkylating agents, if possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> (See Hepatotoxicity, including Hepatic VOD, under Cautions.)

Inotuzumab Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Mean peak plasma concentrations of inotuzumab ozogamicin increase with repeated exposure.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Plasma concentrations of unconjugated N-acetyl-γ-calicheamicin usually are undetectable.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Steady-state concentrations of inotuzumab ozogamicin attained by the fourth cycle of therapy; estimated systemic accumulation of the antibody-drug conjugate is 5.3-fold.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether inotuzumab ozogamicin or its metabolites are distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Calicheamicin derivative: Approximately 97%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

N-acetyl-γ-calicheamicin dimethylhydrazide is primarily metabolized by nonenzymatic reduction in vitro.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Antibody-drug conjugate: 12.3 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Mild hepatic impairment (total bilirubin concentration not exceeding the ULN with AST concentration exceeding the ULN, or total bilirubin concentration >1 times but not >1.5 times the ULN with any AST concentration) does not affect clearance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Moderate or severe hepatic impairment (total bilirubin concentration >1.5 times the ULN with any AST concentration) does not appear to affect clearance, but data are limited.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Renal impairment (Clcr 15–89 mL/minute) does not affect clearance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Age, gender, and race do not have clinically important effects on pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
