Uses for Clidinium

Peptic Ulcer Disease

Used in fixed combination with chlordiazepoxide as adjunctive therapy in the treatment of peptic ulcer disease;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> however, no conclusive data that antimuscarinics aid in the healing, decrease the rate of recurrence, or prevent complications of peptic ulcers.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

With the advent of more effective therapies for the treatment of peptic ulcer disease, antimuscarinics have only limited usefulness in this condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

GI Motility Disturbances

Used in fixed combination with chlordiazepoxide in the treatment of functional GI motility disturbances (e.g., irritable bowel syndrome).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

Has limited efficacy in treatment of GI motility disturbance and should only be used if other measures (e.g., diet, sedation, counseling, amelioration of environmental factors) have been of little or no benefit.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Acute Enterocolitis

Used in fixed combination with chlordiazepoxide in the treatment of acute enterocolitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> However, antimuscarinics should be used with extreme caution in patients with diarrhea or ulcerative colitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)

Clidinium Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally 3 or 4 times daily before meals and at bedtime.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as clidinium bromide; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

As with other antimuscarinics, higher than recommended dosage may be required for therapeutic effect.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Clidinium bromide is commercially available in the US only in fixed combination with chlordiazepoxide hydrochloride.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> Fixed-ratio combination preparations do not permit individual titration of dosages.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Usual maintenance clidinium bromide dosage is 2.5 or 5 mg (1 or 2 capsules of clidinium bromide in fixed combination with chlordiazepoxide hydrochloride) 3 or 4 times daily administered before meals and at bedtime.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Clidinium

Drugs with Anticholinergic Effects

Additive adverse effects resulting from cholinergic blockade (e.g., xerostomia, blurred vision, constipation).<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Advise of possibility of increased anticholinergic effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Orally Administered Drugs

Potential pharmacokinetic interaction (altered GI absorption of various drugs).<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Antimuscarinics may inhibit GI motility, delay gastric emptying, and prolong GI transit time.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Clidinium Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Incompletely absorbed from the GI tract (apparently from the intestines) because completely ionized.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following oral administration, antisecretory activity occurs within <1 hour.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following oral administration, antisecretory activity persists for ≤hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Does not readily penetrate the CNS or the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Not known whether clidinium bromide crosses the placenta or is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Principally in the liver to its 3-hydroxy alcohol.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Limited data; in 2 adults, approximately 36% of dose excreted in urine within 7 days of oral administration, with 90% of urinary excretion occurring within the first day; 20–46% eliminated in feces.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Biphasic; in 2 adults, initial half-life was 2.4 hours and terminal half-life was 20 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Clidinium bromide-chlordiazepoxide hydrochloride fixed combination: Tight, light-resistant container at 25°C (may be exposed to 15–30°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Has pronounced antispasmodic and antisecretory effect on the GI tract.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

  • Competitively inhibits acetylcholine or other cholinergic stimuli at autonomic effectors innervated by postganglionic cholinergic nerves and, to a lesser extent, on smooth muscles that lack cholinergic innervation.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

  • At usual doses, antimuscarinics principally antagonize cholinergic stimuli at muscarinic receptors and have little or no effect on cholinergic stimuli at nicotinic receptors. <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

  • Antimuscarinics also have been referred to as anticholinergics (cholinergic blocking agents), but this term is appropriate only when it describes the antagonism of cholinergic stimuli at any cholinergic receptor, whether muscarinic or nicotinic.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

  • Antimuscarinics also have been referred to as parasympatholytics since the antagonized functions principally are under the parasympathetic division of the nervous system.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Potential for hyperthermia and heat prostration; avoid exposure to high environmental temperature and use with caution when febrile.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of drowsiness or blurred vision; exercise caution when performing activities requiring mental alertness (e.g., driving a motor vehicle, operating machinery) or when performing other hazardous work.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>
