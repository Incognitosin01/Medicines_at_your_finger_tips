Uses for Fluticasone (Systemic, Oral Inhalation)

Asthma

Fluticasone used for long-term prevention of bronchospasm in patients with asthma.<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>35</sup></body></html>

In corticosteroid-dependent patients, fluticasone may permit a substantial reduction in the daily maintenance dosage of the systemic corticosteroid and gradual discontinuance of corticosteroid maintenance dosages.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>35</sup></body></html>

Fluticasone/salmeterol fixed combination is used only in patients with asthma not responding adequately to long-term asthma controller therapy (e.g., inhaled corticosteroids) or whose disease severity clearly warrants initiation of treatment with both an inhaled corticosteroid and a long-acting β2-adrenergic agonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>51</sup></body></html>

Once asthma control achieved and maintained, assess patient at regular intervals and step down therapy (e.g., discontinue fluticasone/salmeterol), if possible without loss of asthma control, and maintain patient on long-term asthma controller therapy (e.g., inhaled corticosteroids).<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>51</sup></body></html>

Do not use fluticasone/salmeterol in patients with asthma adequately controlled on low or medium dosage of inhaled corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>51</sup></body></html>

COPD

Fixed combination with salmeterol as the oral inhalation powder (Advair Diskus) is used for maintenance treatment of airflow obstruction in patients with COPD, including chronic bronchitis and/or emphysema; also used to reduce exacerbations of COPD in patients with a history of exacerbations.<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Fluticasone/salmeterol is not indicated for relief of acute bronchospasm.<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>
