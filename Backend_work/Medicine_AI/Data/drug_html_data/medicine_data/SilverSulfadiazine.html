Uses for Silver Sulfadiazine

Prevention and Treatment of Burn Infections

Silver sulfadiazine cream is used topically as adjunct therapy to prevent and treat wound sepsis in second- and third-degree burns after resuscitative measures (e.g., control of shock and pain, correction of electrolyte imbalance) have been instituted.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>l</sup></body></html>

Control of bacterial growth may prevent conversion of deep, second-degree (partial-thickness) wounds to third-degree (full-thickness) wounds by sepsis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Concomitant administration of appropriate systemic anti-infective agents may be necessary if infection present or suspected.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Although controlled, comparative studies are lacking, silver sulfadiazine and mafenide are considered by many clinicians to be among the topical anti-infective agents of choice in burn patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Mafenide appears to penetrate burn eschar better than silver sulfadiazine and may be more effective in minimizing the growth of bacteria and early treatment of wound sepsis.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Unlike mafenide, a carbonic anhydrase inhibitor, silver sulfadiazine does not alter acid-base balance and does not have a limited duration of therapy and area of topical application.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>f</sup></body></html>

Softening action of silver sulfadiazine cream may aid in eschar removal and preparation of wound for grafting. <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Silver Sulfadiazine Dosage and Administration

General

  • Topically apply 1% silver sulfadiazine cream after appropriate regimens for burn care have been promptly instituted, including control of shock and pain.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>l</sup></body></html>

Administration

Apply 1% cream topically to cleansed, debrided burn wounds under sterile conditions (e.g., using a sterile-gloved hand).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Topical cream should not be applied to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

If possible, bathe patient daily to aid in debridement.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Dressings usually not required but may be used.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as silver sulfadiazine; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Apply 1% cream in a thickness of approximately 16 mm (1/16 inch) 1 or 2 times daily; cover burn area with cream at all times.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Reapply cream to any areas from which it has been removed by patient activity and reapply immediately following hydrotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Continue applying cream as long as there is a possibility of infection unless a clinically important adverse reaction occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Usually continue until healing is progressing well or site is ready for grafting.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

When applied to extensive areas of the body and/or tissue damage is present, sulfadiazine may be absorbed systemically and produce adverse reactions characteristic of the sulfonamides, including hematologic effects (e.g., agranulocytosis, aplastic anemia, thrombocytopenia, leukopenia, hemolytic anemia), dermatologic and hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., Stevens-Johnson syndrome, exfoliative dermatitis), adverse GI effects, hepatitis and hepatocellular necrosis, adverse CNS effects, and toxic nephrosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>l</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>m</sup></body></html> Skin discoloration (including argyria) also reported with topical application of silver sulfadiazine cream.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Possible fungal proliferation in and below eschar; however, low reported incidence of fungal superinfection.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Transient leukopenia, principally manifested as decreased ANC, reported in an average of 20% of patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>l</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>m</sup></body></html> Maximal WBC count decrease occurs within the first 2–4 days after initiating therapy, and WBC count usually returns to normal within 2–3 days after onset of leukopenia regardless of whether silver sulfadiazine therapy is continued or discontinued.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>m</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

Hemolysis may occur in patients with glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Possible sensitivity reactions (e.g., blood dyscrasias, skin reactions [dermatitis, rashes, facial edema, pruritus], jaundice, hepatitis).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>k</sup></body></html> If allergic reaction occurs, consider discontinuance of drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Possible cross-sensitivity with other sulfonamides and related compounds (e.g., sulfonylureas, carbonic anhydrase inhibitors, thiazides, loop diuretics).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications.)

In patients with extensive burns, monitor serum sulfonamide concentrations, carefully monitor renal function, and examine urine for sulfonamide crystals.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Absorption of propylene glycol (contained in silver sulfadiazine cream) may affect serum osmolality, which may affect interpretation of some laboratory test results.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>l</sup></body></html>

Silver Sulfadiazine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Silver sulfadiazine itself does not appear to be absorbed following topical administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> When in contact with body tissues and fluids, silver sulfadiazine slowly reacts with sodium chloride, sulfhydryl groups, and protein, resulting in sulfadiazine release.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Sulfadiazine may then be systemically absorbed from the site of application, particularly when silver sulfadiazine is applied to extensive areas and/or to damaged tissue.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>l</sup></body></html>
