Uses for Gallium Nitrate

Hypercalcemia

Used in conjunction with adequate hydration for the management of symptomatic hypercalcemia associated with malignant neoplasms in patients whose hypercalcemia is not adequately managed with conventional methods of treatment (e.g., hydration alone);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

More conservative measures than gallium therapy (e.g., hydration alone or combined with diuretics) generally are used for mild or asymptomatic hypercalcemia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Bladder Cancer

Has been used alone<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> and in combination with other antineoplastic agents<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> for the treatment of advanced or metastatic bladder carcinoma†; however, other agents are preferred.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Gallium Nitrate

Contraindications

  • Severe renal impairment (Scr >2.5 mg/dL).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Severe renal impairment (Scr >2.5 mg/dL).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Renal function abnormalities reported in clinical trials with gallium; hypercalcemia in cancer patients is commonly associated with impaired renal function.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Establish adequate hydration and satisfactory urine output (e.g., 2 L daily) before initiating therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Maintain adequate hydration throughout treatment period.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid overhydration in patients with compromised cardiovascular status.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitoring of Scr during gallium therapy is strongly recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discontinue gallium if Scr >2.5 mg/dL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Concomitant use of gallium with other nephrotoxic drugs may increase the risk of renal insufficiency in patients with cancer-related hypercalcemia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Boxed Warning.)

Asymptomatic or mild to moderate hypocalcemia (6.5–8 mg/dL, corrected for albumin) may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Manufacturer recommends daily monitoring of serum calcium concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If hypocalcemia occurs, discontinue gallium; short-term calcium replacement may be necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Transient hypophosphatemia may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Manufacturer recommends twice-weekly monitoring of serum phosphorus concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Increased BUN and Scr, hypocalcemia, transient hypophosphatemia, decreased serum bicarbonate, decreased BP. <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
