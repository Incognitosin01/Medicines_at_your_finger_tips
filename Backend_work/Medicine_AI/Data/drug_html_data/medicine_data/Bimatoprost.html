Introduction

Uses for Bimatoprost

Ocular Hypertension and Glaucoma

Bimatoprost 0.01 or 0.03% ophthalmic solution (Lumigan or generic formulations, respectively): Reduction of elevated IOP in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Bimatoprost 0.03% at least as effective as latanoprost 0.005% in controlling diurnal IOP;<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> may be more effective than timolol 0.5% in reducing IOP in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

When selecting an initial ocular hypotensive agent, consider extent of the required IOP reduction, coexisting medical conditions, and drug characteristics (e.g., dosing frequency, adverse effects, cost).<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> With single-agent regimens, the reduction in IOP is approximately 25–33% with topical prostaglandin analogs; 20–25% with topical β-adrenergic blocking agents, α-adrenergic agonists, or miotic (parasympathomimetic) agents; 20–30% with oral carbonic anhydrase inhibitors; 18% with topical rho kinase inhibitors; and 15–20% with topical carbonic anhydrase inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html>

A prostaglandin analog frequently is considered for initial therapy in the absence of other considerations (e.g., contraindications, cost considerations, intolerance, adverse effects, patient refusal) because of relatively greater activity, once-daily administration, and low frequency of systemic adverse effects; however, ocular adverse effects can occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>134</sup></body></html>

Goal is to maintain an IOP at which visual field loss is unlikely to substantially reduce quality of life during the patient's lifetime.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html>

Reduction of pretreatment IOP by ≥25% shown to slow progression of primary open-angle glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> Set an initial target IOP (based on extent of optic nerve damage and/or visual field loss, baseline IOP at which damage occurred, rate of progression, life expectancy, and other considerations) and reduce IOP toward this goal.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> Adjust target IOP up or down as needed over course of disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html>

Combination therapy with drugs from different therapeutic classes often required to control IOP.<html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>133</sup></body></html>

Hypotrichosis of Eyelashes

Bimatoprost 0.03% ophthalmic solution (Latisse): Used to increase eyelash growth, including lash length, thickness, and darkness, in individuals with hypotrichosis of the eyelashes.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Effect on lash growth is expected to abate following discontinuance of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Increased pigmentation of the iris and periorbital tissue (eyelid) reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Pigmentation expected to increase as long as bimatoprost is administered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Following discontinuance of therapy, pigmentation of the iris is likely to be permanent, while pigmentation of periorbital tissue reportedly is reversible in some patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Long-term effects of increased pigmentation unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increased pigmentation of the iris may not be evident until after several months to years of bimatoprost therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May continue therapy in patients who develop noticeably increased iris pigmentation; however, examine these patients regularly.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible gradual change in eyelashes and vellus hair in the treated eye, including increased length, thickness, pigmentation, and number of eyelashes and/or misdirected growth of eyelashes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Usually reversible upon discontinuance of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Studies in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension indicate that administering bimatoprost or other prostaglandin analogs more than once daily may paradoxically reduce the IOP-lowering effect of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

In studies in patients with eyelash hypotrichosis, bimatoprost reduced IOP, although not to a clinically important extent, in patients with or without elevated IOP.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

In patients receiving prostaglandin analogs, including bimatoprost (e.g., Lumigan 0.01%, generic bimatoprost 0.03%), for reduction of elevated IOP, concomitant use of bimatoprost (Latisse) for treatment of eyelash hypotrichosis may interfere with achieving the desired IOP reduction.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Patients receiving prostaglandin analogs for IOP reduction should receive bimatoprost (Latisse) for treatment of eyelash hypotrichosis only after consultation with their clinician and with monitoring for changes in IOP.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

In patients receiving bimatoprost for treatment of eyelash hypotrichosis, hair growth may occur in areas where the drug solution comes in repeated contact with the skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Apply bimatoprost only to the skin of the upper eyelid margin at the base of the lashes; carefully blot any excess solution from the eyelid margin to avoid runoff onto the cheek or other skin areas.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with active intraocular inflammation (e.g., uveitis); may exacerbate inflammation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Macular edema, including cystoid macular edema, reported in patients receiving bimatoprost for reduction of elevated IOP.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Use with caution in aphakic patients, in pseudophakic patients with a torn posterior lens capsule, or in patients with known risk factors for macular edema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Bacterial keratitis reported with use of multiple-dose containers of topical ophthalmic preparations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Containers were inadvertently contaminated by patients, most of whom had concurrent corneal disease or disruption of the ocular epithelial surface.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Improper handling of ophthalmic solutions can result in contamination of the solution by common bacteria known to cause ocular infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Reuse of the sterile, disposable, single-use applicators provided with Latisse ophthalmic solution also increases the potential for contamination and infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Serious damage to the eye and subsequent loss of vision may result from using contaminated ophthalmic solutions or applicators.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Remove contact lenses prior to topical application of each dose to the eyes or eyelid margins, since the preparations contain benzalkonium chloride, which may be absorbed by and cause discoloration of soft contact lenses; may reinsert lenses 15 minutes after the dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

In animal studies, abortion, early delivery, reduced fetal body weight, and increased fetal and pup mortality observed at high exposure levels.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

No adequate and well-controlled studies in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Postmarketing experience has revealed no increase in risk of major birth defects or spontaneous abortions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Use only if the potential benefits justify possible risk to the fetus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Distributed into milk in animals following high-dose IV administration; animal data lacking at clinically relevant doses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Not known whether bimatoprost distributes into human milk after topical application to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consider developmental and health benefits of breast-feeding along with the mother's clinical need for bimatoprost and any potential adverse effects of the drug on the breast-fed infant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Not recommended for treatment of open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension in pediatric patients <16 years of age because of potential safety concerns related to increased pigmentation following long-term use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Pigmentation under Cautions.)

In a controlled study in 71 pediatric patients 5–17 years of age, improvement in overall eyelash prominence was observed with bimatoprost 0.03% therapy in 73% of adolescents with hypotrichosis not associated with a contributory medical condition and 85 or 44% of pediatric patients with hypotrichosis associated with chemotherapy or alopecia areata; response rates for vehicle recipients with these respective conditions were 7, 100, and 33%.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> No new safety concerns identified.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in safety and efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Bimatoprost Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following once-daily topical application of bimatoprost 0.03% ophthalmic solution to the eyes (cornea and/or conjunctival sac) for 2 weeks, peak blood concentrations were attained within 10 minutes and were below the lower limit of detection within 1.5 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Steady-state blood concentrations were achieved during the first week of dosing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reduction in IOP generally occurs within 4 hours after topical application to the eyes and peaks within 8–12 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Moderately distributed into body tissues with a steady-state volume of distribution of 0.67 L/kg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In human blood, bimatoprost resides mainly in the plasma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Bimatoprost is distributed into milk in animals; it is not known whether the drug distributes into milk in humans.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

88%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Undergoes oxidation, N-deethylation, and glucuronidation to form various metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Approximately 67% excreted in urine and 25% excreted in feces after IV administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

45 minutes after IV administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
