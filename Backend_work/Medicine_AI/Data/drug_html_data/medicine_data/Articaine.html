Uses for Articaine

Dental Anesthesia

Local, infiltrative, or regional (i.e., nerve block) anesthesia in simple and complex dental and periodontal procedures.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Anesthetic activity comparable to that of lidocaine, mepivacaine, and prilocaine.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Articaine

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to local anesthetics of the amide type or to sodium metabisulfite.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to local anesthetics of the amide type or to sodium metabisulfite.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Injection contains epinephrine, which may cause tissue necrosis or systemic toxicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider usual precautions associated with epinephrine administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Accidental intravascular injection may result in confusion, seizures, CNS or cardiorespiratory depression, coma, and/or respiratory arrest.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Aspirate prior to administration to guard against intravascular injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Should be used only by clinicians who are sufficiently knowledgeable in the diagnosis and management of dose-related toxicity and other acute emergencies that might arise.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Resuscitative equipment and drugs must be available for immediate use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Articaine injection contains sodium metabisulfite, which may cause allergic-type reactions (including anaphylaxis and life-threatening or less severe asthmatic episodes) in certain susceptible individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Toxic plasma concentrations of local anesthetics (resulting from systemic absorption) associated with adverse CNS effects (e.g., restlessness, anxiety, tinnitus, lightheadedness, dizziness, disorientation, excitement, blurred vision, tremors, depression, drowsiness).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Carefully monitor level of consciousness after each local anesthetic injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Toxic plasma concentrations of local anesthetics (resulting from systemic absorption) associated with adverse cardiovascular effects (e.g., reduced myocardial contractility, peripheral vasodilation, depressed cardiac conduction and excitability).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Possible atrioventricular block, ventricular arrhythmia, cardiac arrest, and, rarely, death.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Carefully monitor cardiovascular and respiratory vital signs after each local anesthetic injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cardiac arrhythmias may occur in patients receiving potent general anesthetics; use with caution in such patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Articaine hydrochloride injection contains epinephrine; risk of exaggerated vasoconstrictor response in patients with hypertension or peripheral vascular disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Risk of ischemic injury or necrosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with heart block or other cardiovascular disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Articaine Pharmacokinetics

Peak plasma concentrations achieved approximately 25 minutes following single dose and 48 minutes following 3 doses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

1–6 minutes following submucosal injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Average onset of anesthesia following articaine administration appears to be similar to that of prilocaine<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> but slightly faster than that of other local anesthetics (e.g., lidocaine).<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

  • Local anesthetics block the generation and conduction of nerve impulses by increasing the threshold for electrical excitation, slowing the propagation of the nerve impulse, and reducing the rate of rise of the action potential.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing their clinician if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
