Administration

For solution compatibility information, see Compatibility under Stability.

Administer only by IV infusion through a central venous line (e.g., central venous catheter or peripherally inserted central catheter [PICC]) using an infusion pump; do not use an inline filter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Daunorubicin component may cause severe tissue necrosis if extravasation occurs; do not administer IM or sub-Q.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following administration, flush IV line with 0.9% sodium chloride injection or 5% dextrose injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Must reconstitute commercially available powder for injection and further dilute prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Storage under Stability.)

Do not mix with any other drug or administer any other drug simultaneously in the same IV line.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Determine number of vials to reconstitute based on indicated dose of daunorubicin component.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to reconstitution, allow vials to equilibrate to room temperature for 30 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute vial containing 44 mg of daunorubicin and 100 mg of cytarabine with 19 mL of sterile water for injection to provide a colloidal dispersion containing 2.2 mg of daunorubicin and 5 mg of cytarabine per mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> Using a timer, swirl each vial for 5 minutes (gently invert every 30 seconds).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not heat, vortex, or vigorously shake.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Allow reconstituted drug to rest for 15 minutes; mix again with 5 gentle inversions prior to further dilution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reconstituted daunorubicin/cytarabine liposomal dispersion should be opaque, purple, homogeneous, and free of visible particulates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Further dilute reconstituted drug prior to IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dilute appropriate dose in an IV bag containing 500 mL of 0.9% sodium chloride injection or 5% dextrose injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Mix diluted solution by gently inverting IV bag.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Storage under Stability.)

Diluted solution should be a translucent, deep purple, homogeneous dispersion that is free of visible particulates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Discard any unused portions of reconstituted and diluted solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer by IV infusion over 90 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as a fixed-combination preparation containing a 1:5 molar ratio of daunorubicin to cytarabine coencapsulated in liposomes (daunorubicin/cytarabine liposomal).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Each single-dose vial contains 44 mg of daunorubicin and 100 mg of cytarabine coencapsulated in liposomes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Calculate dosage of the fixed combination based on the daunorubicin component.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Complete course consists of 1–2 cycles of induction therapy and 1–2 cycles of consolidation therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Induction therapy: Daunorubicin 44 mg/m2 and cytarabine 100 mg/m2 as the fixed liposomal combination on days 1, 3, and 5 of first induction cycle; if complete remission not achieved and unacceptable toxicity does not occur, may administer a second induction cycle 2–5 weeks after first induction cycle on days 1 and 3 at same dosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consolidation therapy (administer 5–8 weeks from start of last induction cycle): Daunorubicin 29 mg/m2 and cytarabine 65 mg/m2 as the fixed liposomal combination on days 1 and 3; may administer second consolidation cycle 5–8 weeks following initiation of first consolidation cycle if disease progression or unacceptable toxicity does not occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a dose is missed, administer as soon as possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Adjust dosage schedule accordingly to maintain treatment interval between cycles.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a mild hypersensitivity reaction occurs, immediately interrupt infusion and initiate supportive treatment; upon resolution of symptoms, reduce rate of infusion by 50%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider premedication with an antihistamine and/or corticosteroid prior to subsequent infusions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a moderate hypersensitivity reaction occurs, immediately interrupt infusion and initiate supportive treatment; do not resume infusion upon resolution of symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For subsequent infusions, may administer at same rate following administration of a premedication regimen (i.e., antihistamine and/or corticosteroid).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a severe or life-threatening hypersensitivity reaction occurs, permanently discontinue therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Initiate supportive treatment and monitor patient.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Do not substitute the fixed liposomal combination of daunorubicin and cytarabine (daunorubicin/cytarabine liposomal) with other daunorubicin and/or cytarabine preparations (e.g., daunorubicin hydrochloride injection, daunorubicin citrate liposomal injection, cytarabine injection, cytarabine liposomal injection).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Boxed Warning.)

Pharmacokinetic properties, formulation, and dosage of the fixed-combination liposomal preparation differ from those of single-entity preparations (including their liposomal and nonliposomal forms).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Confirm correct drug, dose, and formulation prior to preparation and administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Serious or fatal hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylactic reactions, reported in patients receiving daunorubicin and cytarabine as single agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor for manifestations of hypersensitivity reactions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If a hypersensitivity reaction occurs, initiate appropriate treatment and supportive care.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Reduce infusion rate, temporarily interrupt infusion, or discontinue therapy based on severity of the reaction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage Modification for Toxicity under Dosage and Administration.) If a severe or life-threatening hypersensitivity reaction occurs, permanently discontinue therapy and monitor patient.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Serious or fatal hemorrhage associated with prolonged severe thrombocytopenia reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Fatal CNS hemorrhage in absence of disease progression reported rarely.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Most common hemorrhagic event was epistaxis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If hemorrhage occurs, monitor CBCs until resolution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Administer platelet transfusions if necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Daunorubicin/cytarabine liposomal contains the anthracycline daunorubicin, and cardiotoxicity is a known risk of anthracycline therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Previous therapy with other anthracycline agents, preexisting heart disease, previous radiation therapy to the mediastinal region, or concomitant use of other cardiotoxic drugs may increase risk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> With conventional daunorubicin, increased incidence of heart failure observed with total cumulative dosage >550 mg/m2 (or >400 mg/m2 in patients who have received radiation therapy to the mediastinal region).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Calculate lifetime cumulative anthracycline exposure prior to each cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Include any previous or concomitant therapy with other anthracycline agents, such as doxorubicin, or related compounds in calculation.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Do not use in patients who have reached the maximum cumulative limit.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Obtain baseline ECG and assess cardiac function (including left ventricular ejection fraction [LVEF]) with echocardiogram or multigated radionuclide angiography (MUGA) prior to initiation of therapy; do not use in patients with baseline LVEF below limit of normal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Repeat echocardiogram or MUGA prior to initiation of consolidation therapy and as clinically indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Evaluate risk versus benefit of initiating or continuing therapy in patients with impaired cardiac function.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reconstituted daunorubicin/cytarabine liposomal contains 5 mg/mL of copper gluconate (14% of which is elemental copper).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Safety in patients with Wilson disease or other copper-related metabolic disorders not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Maximum theoretical total exposure of copper following induction and consolidation therapy is 106 mg/m2.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In patients with Wilson disease, use only if potential benefit outweighs risk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If used in such patients, monitor total serum copper, serum non-ceruloplasmin-bound copper, and 24-hour urine copper excretion and perform neuropyschological assessments periodically.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consult with a clinician who has expertise in managing acute copper toxicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If manifestations of acute copper toxicity occur in any patient, discontinue therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Extravasation of daunorubicin, a component of daunorubicin/cytarabine liposomal, can produce severe local tissue necrosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See IV Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

May cause fetal harm based on animal findings and anecdotal reports in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

DAUNOrubicin and Cytarabine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following administration as the fixed liposomal combination, pharmacokinetics of daunorubicin and cytarabine are dose proportional over the dose range of 1.3–59 mg/m2 and 3–134 mg/m2, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following administration of the fixed liposomal combination by IV infusion over 90 minutes on days 1, 3, and 5 in adults, accumulation ratio of daunorubicin and cytarabine are 1.3 and 1.4, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Patients with serum bilirubin concentrations ≤3 mg/dL: Pharmacokinetics of either drug not substantially altered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Hepatic Impairment under Cautions.)

Patients with mild or moderate renal impairment (Clcr 30–89 mL/minute): Pharmacokinetics of either drug not substantially altered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Renal Impairment under Cautions.)

Age, gender, race, body weight, body mass index, and leukocyte count do not substantially affect systemic exposure of either drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether daunorubicin or cytarabine is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Daunorubicin: Catalyzed by aldo-keto reductase and carbonyl reductase enzymes to active metabolite daunorubicinol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cytarabine: Metabolized by cytidine deaminase to inactive metabolite 1-β-d-arabinofuranosyluracil (ara-U, uracil arabinoside).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Daunorubicin: Approximately 9% of a dose eliminated in urine as unchanged drug and daunorubicinol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cytarabine: Approximately 71% of a dose eliminated in urine as unchanged drug and 1-β-d-arabinofuranosyluracil.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Daunorubicin: 31.5 hours (>99% of daunorubicin in plasma remaining encapsulated in liposomes).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cytarabine: 40.4 hours (>99% of cytarabine in plasma remaining encapsulated in liposomes).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Fixed combination of daunorubicin (an anthracycline) and cytarabine (an antimetabolite) coencapsulated in liposomes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Daunorubicin exhibits antimitotic and cytotoxic activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Intercalates into DNA and inhibits topoisomerase II (by stabilizing the complex between this enzyme and DNA), resulting in single-strand and double-strand breaks in DNA.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Also may inhibit polymerase activity, affect regulation of gene expression, and be involved in free radical damage to DNA.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

  • Liposomal delivery system employed to optimize therapeutic efficacy of the combination regimen; liposomes control release of the drug combination such that fixed synergistic drug ratios established in vitro are maintained in vivo following drug administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Cytarabine is a cycle-phase specific antineoplastic agent; cytotoxic effect occurs only during S phase of cell division.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Appears to inhibit DNA polymerase.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Liposomal delivery system employed to optimize therapeutic efficacy of the combination regimen; liposomes control release of the drug combination such that fixed synergistic drug ratios established in vitro are maintained in vivo following drug administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Formulated in a fixed 1:5 daunorubicin to cytarabine molar ratio, which has been shown to provide the greatest synergistic effect with the least antagonism.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

In addition to maintaining favorable drug ratios in plasma, preclinical studies also indicate that liposomes penetrate and sustain high concentrations of the drugs at the tumor site (specifically the bone marrow).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>
