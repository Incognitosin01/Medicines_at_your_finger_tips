Uses for Formoterol

Bronchospasm in Asthma

Formoterol: Used concomitantly with long-term asthma controller therapy (e.g., inhaled corticosteroids) for prevention of bronchospasm in patients with reversible obstructive airway disease (e.g., asthma).<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Monotherapy with long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists (e.g., formoterol) increases the risk of asthma-related death and may increase the risk of asthma-related hospitalization in children and adolescents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>41</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>51</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>54</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>55</sup></body></html> Use of formoterol alone for treatment of asthma without concomitant use of long-term asthma controller therapy (e.g., inhaled corticosteroids) is contraindicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>41</sup></body></html> (See Asthma-related Death and Serious Asthma-related Events under Cautions.)

Formoterol in fixed combination with budesonide or mometasone (formoterol/budesonide, formoterol/mometasone): Treatment of asthma only in patients not responding adequately to long-term asthma controller therapy (e.g., inhaled corticosteroids) or whose disease severity clearly warrants initiation of treatment with both an inhaled corticosteroid and a long-acting β2-adrenergic agonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>54</sup></body></html>

Once asthma control achieved and maintained, assess patient at regular intervals and step down therapy (e.g., discontinue formoterol or formoterol/budesonide) if possible without loss of asthma control, and maintain patient on long-term asthma controller therapy (e.g., inhaled corticosteroids).<html><head></head><body><sup>41</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html>

Do not use formoterol/budesonide in patients with asthma adequately controlled on low or medium dosage of inhaled corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html>

Formoterol alone or in fixed combination with budesonide or mometasone not indicated for relief of acute bronchospasm; do not initiate in patients with rapidly deteriorating or potentially life-threatening episodes of asthma.<html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>54</sup></body></html> (See Acute Exacerbations of Asthma or COPD under Cautions.)

Bronchospasm in COPD

Formoterol, formoterol/budesonide, or formoterol in fixed combination with glycopyrrolate (formoterol/glycopyrrolate): Long-term maintenance treatment of bronchospasm or airflow obstruction associated with COPD, including chronic bronchitis and emphysema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>55</sup></body></html>
