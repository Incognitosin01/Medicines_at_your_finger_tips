Introduction

Antineoplastic agent; an inhibitor of mitogen-activated extracellular signal-regulated kinase (MEK) 1 and MEK2 in cells with b-Raf serine-threonine kinase (BRAF) V600E or V600K mutations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Uses for Trametinib Dimethyl Sulfoxide

Melanoma

In combination with dabrafenib as adjuvant therapy following complete resection of melanoma with BRAF V600E or V600K mutation and nodal involvement.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Alone or in combination with dabrafenib for treatment of unresectable or metastatic melanoma in selected patients with BRAF V600E or V600K mutation<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> (designated an orphan drug by FDA as monotherapy or when used in combination for this use).<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

FDA-approved in vitro diagnostic test (e.g., THxID BRAF kit) required to confirm presence of BRAF V600E or V600K mutation prior to initiation of monotherapy or combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Not recommended for use in patients with melanoma who have experienced disease progression following treatment with a BRAF inhibitor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

NSCLC

Used in combination with dabrafenib for treatment of metastatic non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) in patients with BRAF V600E mutation<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (designated an orphan drug by FDA when used in combination for this use).<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

FDA-approved diagnostic test (e.g., THxID BRAF kit) required to confirm presence of BRAF V600E mutation prior to initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Anaplastic Thyroid Cancer

Used in combination with dabrafenib for treatment of locally advanced or metastatic anaplastic thyroid cancer in patients with BRAF V600E mutation when no satisfactory locoregional treatment options are available<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (designated an orphan drug by FDA when used in combination for this use).<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

When combination therapy with trametinib includes use of dabrafenib, cautions, precautions, and contraindications of dabrafenib also must be considered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

New primary cutaneous and noncutaneous malignancies are a known class effect of BRAF inhibitors (i.e., dabrafenib, encorafenib, vemurafenib).<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> Cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma, new primary melanoma, basal cell carcinoma, and noncutaneous malignancies reported in patients receiving trametinib in combination with dabrafenib.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Perform dermatologic evaluations prior to initiation of and every 2 months during therapy, then for up to 6 months following discontinuance of combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor patients closely for signs or symptoms of new noncutaneous malignancies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage Modification for Toxicity under Dosage and Administration.)

Hemorrhage (sometimes fatal), including intracranial or GI hemorrhage, has occurred during trametinib/dabrafenib combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If hemorrhagic events occur, dosage modification and/or treatment discontinuance may be necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For grade 3 hemorrhagic events, interrupt trametinib therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If improvement observed, trametinib may be resumed at a reduced dosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If no improvement observed, permanently discontinue trametinib.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For grade 4 hemorrhagic events, permanently discontinue trametinib.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Colitis and GI perforation, sometimes fatal, has occurred with monotherapy and trametinib/dabrafenib combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients closely for manifestations of colitis and GI perforation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Venous thromboembolism (VTE) has occurred during trametinib/dabrafenib combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Selective inhibitor of mitogen-activated extracellular signal-regulated kinase (MEK) 1 and 2 activation and kinase activity in cells with BRAF V600E or V600K mutations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

  • Inhibits BRAF V600 mutation-positive melanoma cell growth in vitro and in vivo by decreasing cell proliferation, causing cell cycle arrest, and inducing apoptosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

  • MEK proteins are upstream regulators of the extracellular signal-related kinase (ERK) pathway, which promotes cellular proliferation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Approximately 50% of cutaneous melanomas carry a BRAF mutation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> Most common BRAF mutation is the substitution of glutamic acid for valine at codon 600 (BRAF V600E); mutation involving substitution of lysine for valine at codon 600 (BRAF V600K) occurs less frequently.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • BRAF V600 mutations result in activation of BRAF pathway that includes MEK 1 and 2.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • BRAF V600E mutation activates the mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) and ERK signal transduction pathway, which enhances cell proliferation and tumor progression (e.g., metastasis).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Combination therapy with a BRAF inhibitor (i.e., dabrafenib, encorafenib, vemurafenib) and an MEK inhibitor (i.e., binimetinib, cobimetinib, trametinib) results in complete inhibition of the MAPK/ERK pathway.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

    Combination therapy with trametinib and dabrafenib resulted in greater growth inhibition of melanoma cell lines and prolonged inhibition of tumor growth in melanoma xenografts testing positive for BRAF V600 mutations in vitro.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of advising patients to read the manufacturer's patient information before beginning treatment and each time the prescription is refilled.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of taking trametinib at least 1 hour before or 2 hours after a meal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of taking a missed dose as soon as it is remembered, but only if it can be taken at least 12 hours before the next scheduled dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of proper storage of trametinib (e.g., storing tablets in original bottle at 2–8°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of not removing desiccant from the bottle, and not storing tablets in pill boxes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of new primary cutaneous and noncutaneous malignancies with trametinib/dabrafenib combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting clinician promptly if dermatologic changes (i.e., new lesions, changes to existing lesions) or signs and/or symptoms of other malignancies occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of intracranial and GI hemorrhage with trametinib/dabrafenib combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting clinician promptly if signs and/or symptoms of unusual bleeding or hemorrhage occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of colitis or GI perforation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting clinician promptly if unusual bleeding, diarrhea, abdominal pain or tenderness, fever, or nausea occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of DVT and PE with trametinib/dabrafenib combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting clinician promptly if sudden onset of breathing difficulty, leg pain, or swelling occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of cardiomyopathy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of immediately contacting clinician if manifestations of heart failure occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of visual disturbances that may lead to blindness.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting clinician if vision changes occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of interstitial lung disease (or pneumonitis).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of immediately contacting clinician if cough or dyspnea occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of serious febrile reactions with trametinib/dabrafenib combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting clinician if fever develops.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of skin toxicities (possibly requiring hospitalization).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting clinician if progressive or intolerable rash occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of hypertension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of monitoring BP regularly during therapy and of contacting clinician if manifestations of hypertension occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of diarrhea (sometimes severe).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting clinician if severe diarrhea occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of fetal harm if taken during pregnancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of female patients using highly effective contraception during treatment and for 4 months after discontinuance of trametinib.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting clinician if pregnancy is suspected or confirmed during treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of serious adverse reactions in nursing infants of women receiving trametinib.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of discontinuing breast-feeding during therapy and for 4 months after the last dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising women of reproductive potential that trametinib may reduce female fertility.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and dietary or herbal supplements, as well as any concomitant illnesses (e.g., cardiovascular disease).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
