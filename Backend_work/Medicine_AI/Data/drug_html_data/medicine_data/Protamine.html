Uses for Protamine

Heparin Overdosage

Treatment of severe heparin overdosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html>

Do not use for minor bleeding during heparin therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>123</sup></body></html> (See Boxed Warning.) Heparin withdrawal usually corrects minor overdosage or bleeding within a few hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>123</sup></body></html>

Heparin Neutralization during Extracorporeal Circulation

Neutralization of heparin administered during extracorporeal circulation† in arterial and cardiac surgery or dialysis procedures.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>122</sup></body></html>

Heparin Neutralization in Pregnant Women Near Delivery

Neutralization of anticoagulant effect of heparin to reduce risk of bleeding near delivery† in pregnant women receiving heparin therapy who go into spontaneous labor.<html><head></head><body><sup>125</sup></body></html>

Low Molecular Weight Heparin Overdosage

Has been used for treatment of low molecular weight (LMW) heparin (e.g., dalteparin, enoxaparin, tinzaparin [no longer commercially available in the US]) overdosage†.<html><head></head><body><sup>114</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>121</sup></body></html> However, neutralization of an LMW heparin is not complete even with multiple doses of protamine.<html><head></head><body><sup>114</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>121</sup></body></html> (See Actions.)
