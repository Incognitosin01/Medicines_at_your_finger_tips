Uses for Olodaterol

Bronchospasm in COPD

Olodaterol alone or in fixed combination with tiotropium used for long-term maintenance treatment of airflow obstruction associated with COPD, including chronic bronchitis and emphysema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Not indicated for the treatment of acute bronchospasm (i.e., as rescue therapy for treatment of acute episodes of bronchospasm).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Other Uses

Not indicated for treatment of asthma†; safety and efficacy in asthma not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Olodaterol Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Absolute bioavailability approximately 30%, principally due to absorption of drug delivered to the lungs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Oral bioavailability is low (<1%).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Peak concentrations reached within 10–20 minutes following oral inhalation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Steady-state concentrations achieved after 8 days of once-daily dosing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Accumulation of olodaterol up to 1.8-fold higher following repeated dosing compared with single-dose administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Onset of bronchodilation observed within 5 minutes following first dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Bronchodilation generally persists for 24 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Olodaterol concentrations increased by approximately 40% in patients with severe renal impairment compared with patients with normal renal function.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Extensively distributed into tissue.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Distributed into milk in rats.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> Not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Approximately 60%; binding independent of drug concentration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Extensively metabolized by direct glucuronidation and O-demethylation followed by conjugation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

UGT 2B7, 1A1, 1A7, and 1A9 involved in direct glucuronidation of the drug; O-demethylation mediated by CYP2C9 and 2C8, with negligible contribution by CYP3A4.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Unconjugated demethylated metabolite binds to β2-receptors; not detectable in plasma after repeated inhalation of recommended dosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Following oral administration, approximately 9 and 84% of a radiolabeled dose excreted in urine and feces, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Following oral inhalation, approximately 5–7% of an administered dose is excreted as unchanged drug in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Effective half-life of 7.5 hours in patients with COPD receiving orally inhaled olodaterol 5 mcg daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Provide copy of the manufacturer's patient information (medication guide) and instructions for use to all patients each time drug is dispensed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> Importance of instructing patients to read the medication guide prior to initiation of therapy and each time prescription is refilled.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients that long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists (including olodaterol) increase the risk of asthma-related death and that olodaterol is not indicated for the treatment of asthma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not using olodaterol to relieve acute COPD symptoms; do not use extra doses for such symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients that if a dose is missed, to take the dose as soon as it is remembered; importance of not taking more than one dose (2 inhalations) in a 24-hour period.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of all patients being provided with and instructed in the use of a short-acting, inhaled β2-agonist as treatment for acute COPD symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of contacting a clinician immediately if symptoms worsen, the short-acting β2-agonist becomes less effective or more inhalations than usual are required to relieve symptoms, or clinically important decrease in lung function occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of patients not discontinuing therapy without medical supervision, since symptoms may recur after discontinuance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>
