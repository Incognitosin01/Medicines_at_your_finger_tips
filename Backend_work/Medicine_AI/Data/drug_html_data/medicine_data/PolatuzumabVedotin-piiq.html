Uses for Polatuzumab Vedotin-piiq

Non-Hodgkin Lymphoma

Used in combination with bendamustine and rituximab for treatment of relapsed or refractory diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL), not otherwise specified, in patients who have received at least 2 prior therapies;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> designated an orphan drug by FDA for treatment of this cancer.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Dosage

1.8 mg/kg by IV infusion on day 1 of each 21-day cycle for a total of 6 cycles; administer in combination with bendamustine (90 mg/m2 IV on days 1 and 2 of each cycle) and rituximab (375 mg/m2 IV on day 1 of each cycle).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a dose is missed, administer the dose as soon as possible; adjust schedule of administration to maintain a 21-day interval between polatuzumab vedotin doses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Risk of peripheral neuropathy (mainly sensory, but also motor and sensorimotor); may occur as early as the first cycle of therapy and appears to be a cumulative effect.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Median time to onset is 2 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for manifestations of peripheral neuropathy (e.g., hypoesthesia, hyperesthesia, paresthesia, burning sensation, neuropathic pain, weakness, gait disturbance).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If such symptoms occur, temporary interruption of therapy, dosage reduction, or drug discontinuance may be required.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage Modification for Toxicity under Dosage and Administration.)

Infusion-related reactions (e.g., fever, chills, flushing, dyspnea, hypotension, urticaria), sometimes severe, reported; may occur as late as 24 hours following infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Premedicate with an antihistamine and antipyretic prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor patient during and for at least 90 minutes following completion of initial infusion and for at least 30 minutes following completion of each subsequent infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Risk of severe cytopenias (e.g., neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, anemia).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor for signs and symptoms of infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Antifungal and antiviral prophylaxis for Pneumocystis jiroveci and herpes virus infections recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Neutropenia, thrombocytopenia, anemia, peripheral neuropathy, fatigue, diarrhea, pyrexia, decreased appetite, pneumonia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Polatuzumab Vedotin-piiq

MMAE (the cytotoxic component of polatuzumab vedotin) is a substrate of CYP3A4.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> MMAE does not inhibit CYP isoenzymes 1A2, 2B6, 2C8, 2C9, 2C19, or 2D6 and does not induce major CYP isoenzymes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

MMAE is a substrate of P-glycoprotein (P-gp), but does not inhibit P-gp.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Drugs Affecting Hepatic Microsomal Enzymes

Potent inhibitors of CYP3A: Possible increased systemic exposure of MMAE and increased risk of toxicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor patients closely for signs of toxicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potent inducers of CYP3A: Possible decreased systemic exposure of MMAE.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Drugs Metabolized by Hepatic Microsomal Enzymes

Substrates of CYP3A: Pharmacokinetic interaction not expected.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
