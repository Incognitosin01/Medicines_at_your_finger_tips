Uses for Flurandrenolide

Corticosteroid-responsive Dermatoses

Relief of inflammatory and pruritic manifestations of corticosteroid-responsive dermatoses.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

Generally most effective in acute or chronic dermatoses (e.g., seborrheic or atopic dermatitis, localized neurodermatitis, anogenital pruritus, psoriasis, late phase of allergic contact dermatitis, inflammatory phase of xerosis).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Topical therapy generally preferred over systemic therapy; fewer associated adverse systemic effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Topical therapy generally only controls manifestations of dermatoses; eliminate cause if possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Topical efficacy may be increased by using a higher concentration or occlusive dressing therapy. (See Administration with Occlusive Dressing under Dosage and Administration.)<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Response may vary from one topical corticosteroid preparation to another.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Anti-inflammatory activity may vary considerably depending on the vehicle, drug concentration, site of application, disease, and individual patient.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Flurandrenolide 0.05% ointment is considered to have high-range potency.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Flurandrenolide 0.05% cream is considered to have medium-range potency.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Flurandrenolide Pharmacokinetics

Topically applied flurandrenolide can be absorbed through normal intact skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

Only minimal amounts of topical corticosteroid reach the dermis and subsequently the systemic circulation after application to most normal skin areas; more absorption occurs from the scrotum, axilla, eyelid, face, and scalp than from the forearm, knee, elbow, palm, and sole.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Topical corticosteroids and metabolites are excreted by the kidneys and, to a lesser extent, in bile.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Ointment has high-range corticosteroid activity; cream has medium-range corticosteroid activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Precise mechanism of action for topical anti-inflammatory activity is unknown; therapeutic benefit in the management of corticosteroid-responsive dermatoses mediated primarily through anti-inflammatory, antipruritic, and vasoconstrictive actions.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

  • Anti-inflammatory effects may occur through stabilization of cellular and lysosomal membranes, preventing release of proteolytic enzymes.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

  • Decreases inflammation by stabilizing leukocyte lysosomal membranes, preventing release of destructive acid hydrolases from leukocytes; inhibiting macrophage accumulation in inflamed areas; reducing leukocyte adhesion to capillary endothelium; reducing capillary wall permeability and edema formation; decreasing complement components; antagonizing histamine activity and release of kinin from substrates; reducing fibroblast proliferation, collagen deposition, and subsequent scar tissue formation; and possibly by other mechanisms as yet unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Evaporation of water from lotion vehicle produces cooling effect.<html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

  • Tape is vehicle and occlusive dressing; retains insensible perspiration resulting in hydration of stratum corneum and improved drug diffusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> Tape protects skin and acts as mechanical splint for fissures; also provides sustained action by preventing drug removal.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>
