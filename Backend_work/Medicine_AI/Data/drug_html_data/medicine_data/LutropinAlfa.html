Introduction

Gonad-stimulating hormone; a biosynthetic (recombinant DNA origin) form of naturally occurring human LH.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Uses for Lutropin Alfa

Female Infertility

Used in conjunction with recombinant FSH (follitropin alfa) to stimulate follicular development in anovulatory, infertile women with hypothalamic or pituitary insufficiency (hypogonadotropic hypogonadism)<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> and profound LH deficiency (LH <1.2 international units [IU]/L).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Lutropin Alfa Dosage and Administration

General

  • Should be prescribed by clinicians experienced in infertility treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Prior to initiating therapy with lutropin alfa, perform a thorough gynecologic and endocrinologic evaluation; assess pelvic anatomy and rule out early pregnancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Determine that serum LH concentrations are <1.2 IU/L, FSH concentrations <5 IU/L, and a negative progestin challenge test (i.e., a lack of withdrawal bleeding with progesterone administration).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Perform a thorough diagnostic evaluation in patients who demonstrate abnormal uterine bleeding and other signs of endometrial abnormalities.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Evaluate partner’s infertility.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

  • Administer lutropin alfa in conjunction with recombinant follitropin alfa daily until follicular maturation (as determined by serum estradiol concentrations and ovary ultrasound examinations) occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • When ultrasound assessment and serum estradiol concentrations show sufficient follicular maturation, discontinue lutropin alfa and follitropin alfa; administer hCG 1 day after the last dose of lutropin alfa and follitropin alfa to complete final follicular maturation and induce ovulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Do not administer hCG if the ovaries show an excessive response to treatment with gonadotropins because of an increased risk of ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome under Cautions.)

  • Encourage couple to have daily sexual intercourse beginning the day prior to administration of hCG, until ovulation occurs (as determined by a rise in basal body temperature, an increase in serum progesterone, and menstruation following a shift in basal body temperature).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Adequate Patient Evaluation and Monitoring under Cautions.)

  • Safety and efficacy of concomitant administration of lutropin alfa and other recombinant or urinary human follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) preparations unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer by sub-Q injection, generally into the abdomen;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> may be self-administered by patient.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Administer lutropin alfa and follitropin alfa as separate injections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute vial containing 82.5 units of lutropin alfa sterile lyophilized powder with 1 mL of sterile water for injection (provided by manufacturer).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Gently swirl vial until lyophilized powder dissolves; do not shake.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Following reconstitution, total extractable amount per 82.5 unit vial is 75 units.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer immediately following reconstitution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Dosage of lutropin alfa is expressed in terms of international units (IU, units) of LH activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

75 units daily in conjunction with follitropin alfa (75–150 units daily) until follicular maturation occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See General under Dosage and Administration.)

If stimulation of ovulation is unsuccessful, individualize dosage of lutropin alfa and follitropin alfa administered in subsequent cycles based on woman’s response in the preceding cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of mild to moderate uncomplicated ovarian enlargement; may be accompanied by abdominal distension and/or pain but generally regresses without treatment within 2–3 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Careful monitoring of ovarian response recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If ovaries are abnormally enlarged (i.e., development of ≥3 follicles with a mean diameter of ≥15 mm) and/or if excessive estradiol production (>1100 pg/mL) occurs following the last dose of lutropin alfa and follitropin alfa, withhold hCG administration during the current course of therapy to minimize the risk of development of ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Ovarian Hyperstimulation Syndrome under Cautions.)

Risk of potentially severe OHSS, characterized by an apparent dramatic increase in vascular permeability that may result in rapid accumulation of fluid in the peritoneal cavity, thorax, and potentially, the pericardium.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

OHSS may progress rapidly (within 24 hours to several days) and is initially manifested by severe pelvic pain, nausea, vomiting, and weight gain.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Other symptoms include abdominal pain/distension, diarrhea, dyspnea, and oliguria.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Hypovolemia, hemoconcentration, electrolyte imbalances, ascites, hemoperitoneum, pleural effusions, hydrothorax, acute pulmonary distress, and thromboembolic events may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Transient liver function test abnormalities, which may be accompanied by morphologic changes (as detected by liver biopsy) have been reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

OHSS occurs most often after completion of gonadotropin therapy; develops rapidly, reaching maximum severity after 7–10 days; and usually resolves spontaneously with the onset of menses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> OHSS may be more severe and protracted if pregnancy occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Discontinue therapy with lutropin alfa and follitropin alfa if ovaries are abnormally enlarged or abdominal pain occurs; withhold hCG administration and advise patient to avoid sexual intercourse.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If severe OHSS occurs, discontinue therapy, hospitalize patient, and consult a clinician experienced in the management of OHSS or fluid and electrolyte imbalances.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Multiple ovulations resulting in multiple gestations reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Potential for arterial thromboembolism exists.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer only under the supervision of qualified clinicians experienced in fertility disorders and in interpretation of indices of ovulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor follicular development (e.g., using ovarian ultrasound, serum estradiol concentrations) in order to correctly identify follicular maturation, determine timing of hCG administration, detect ovarian enlargement, and minimize risks of OHSS and multiple gestation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Obtain clinical confirmation of ovulation from direct and indirect indices of progesterone production, including a rise in basal body temperature, increase in serum progesterone, and menstruation following a shift in basal body temperature.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Sonographic evidence of ovulation includes findings of fluid in the cul-de-sac, ovarian stigmata, collapsed follicle, and a secretory endometrium.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Lutropin Alfa Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Mean absolute bioavailability is 56%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following sub-Q administration, maximum serum concentrations reached after 4–16 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

<5% of dose excreted renally as unchanged drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Biphasic; terminal half-life is approximately 18 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Store refrigerated or at room temperature (2–25°C); protect from light.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use immediately after reconstitution; discard unused portion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Stimulates (in combination with FSH) the development of a potentially competent follicle to indirectly prepare the reproductive tract for implantation and pregnancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Stimulates theca cells in the ovaries to secrete androgens; increases estradiol secretion by the follicles.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Substitutes for the endogenous LH surge responsible for ovulation and stimulates late maturation of the ovarian follicle, resumption of oocytic meiosis, initiation of rupture of the preovulatory ovarian follicle, and oocyte expulsion. <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Induces and maintains the corpus luteum.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>
