Uses for Aluminum Acetate

Dermatologic Conditions

Used topically as self-medication (OTC use) for the temporary relief of minor skin irritations (e.g., insect bites; athlete’s foot; poison ivy, poison oak, or poison sumac; rashes due to soaps, detergents, cosmetics, or jewelry).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Helps relieve itching and has a cooling and drying effect; particularly useful on wet or weeping lesions.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Used topically for the relief of swelling associated with minor bruises† and for reducing foot sweat†.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Aluminum acetate preparations also have been used topically for the relief of a variety of other skin-related conditions, such as eczema†<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> , diaper rash†<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> , acne†<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> , and pruritus ani†.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Aluminum Acetate Dosage and Administration

Apply aluminum acetate solution topically to the affected area of the skin as a wet dressing or compress or as a soak.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Aluminum Acetate

Consult with a clinician prior to use with other topical or systemic medications.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Collagenase

Potential pharmacologic interaction (decreased efficacy of topical collagenase); avoid concomitant use.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Aluminum Acetate Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Substantial systemic absorption from intact or denuded skin, wounds, or mucous membranes appears unlikely following topical application.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Stability
