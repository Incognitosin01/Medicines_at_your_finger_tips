Uses for Factor X (Human)

Hereditary Factor X Deficiency

On-demand treatment and control of bleeding episodes in patients with hereditary factor X deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html>

Perioperative management of bleeding in patients with mild hereditary factor X deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html>

Designated an orphan drug by FDA for treatment of hereditary factor X deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy for perioperative management of bleeding in patients with moderate or severe hereditary factor X deficiency undergoing major surgery† not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Hereditary factor X deficiency is a rare but serious bleeding disorder manifested by a lifelong bleeding tendency that typically includes umbilical bleeding during the neonatal period, easy bruising, mucosal bleeding, hemarthrosis, hematomas, intracranial hemorrhage, and GI bleeding.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Women of reproductive age with factor X deficiency also may experience menorrhagia and postpartum hemorrhage.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Factor X (human) is a specific factor X concentrate and does not contain substantial amounts of other clotting proteins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> Alternative treatment options such as other plasma-derived products (e.g., fresh frozen plasma, prothrombin complex concentrates [PCCs]) have disadvantages for factor X-deficient patients because in addition to factor X, they contain other plasma proteins that may contribute to adverse events.<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Factor X (Human) Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer by IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May be self-administered if clinician determines that the patient and/or their caregiver is competent to safely administer the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not mix with any other drugs or solutions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to reconstitution, allow drug and manufacturer-supplied sterile water for injection diluent to warm to room temperature.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute single-use vials of lyophilized drug by adding 2.5 or 5 mL of sterile water for injection to vial containing approximately 250 or 500 units, respectively, of factor X (human) using transfer device provided by manufacturer; resulting solution contains approximately 100 units of factor X (human) activity per mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> Swirl vial gently until powder is completely dissolved.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If more than 1 vial is required to obtain a dose, may pool reconstituted contents of multiple vials; use a separate transfer set to reconstitute each vial.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer reconstituted solutions promptly or within 1 hour.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

10 mL/minute; do not exceed 20 mL/minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Dosage expressed in international units (IU, units) of factor X activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Each vial contains approximately 250 or 500 units of factor X (human); actual number of units indicated on each vial and carton.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Generally, 1 unit/kg of factor X (human) increases plasma factor X levels by 2 units/dL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Dose and duration of therapy dependent upon severity of the factor X deficiency, the location and extent of bleeding, and the patient's clinical condition and individual response.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pediatric patients ≥12 years of age: 25 units/kg, repeat dose every 24 hours until bleeding stops.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pediatric patients ≥12 years of age: Dose based upon the degree of deficiency of blood coagulation factor X, desired factor X plasma level, and the patient's body weight.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> The desired factor X increase is the difference between the patient's plasma factor X level and the desired level.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use the following calculations for administering the drug:<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Approximate increase in factor X level expected from a given dosage:

Estimated increment of factor X (units/dL or % of normal) = [total dose (units) ÷ body weight (kg)] × 2

Dose required to achieve desired factor X levels:

Dose required (units) = body weight (kg) × desired factor X increase (units/dL) × 0.5

Presurgery dose in pediatric patients ≥12 years of age: Administer loading dose to increase plasma factor X levels to 70–90 units/dL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Postsurgery dose in pediatric patients ≥12 years of age: Repeat dose as necessary to maintain plasma factor X levels ≥50 units/dL until patient no longer at risk of bleeding due to surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Measure post-infusion plasma factor X levels before and after surgery to ensure that hemostatic levels are achieved and maintained.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Factor X (Human)

Contraindications

  • Known life-threatening hypersensitivity reactions to factor X (human) or any component in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known life-threatening hypersensitivity reactions to factor X (human) or any component in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Hypersensitivity reactions (including anaphylaxis) may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html>

Discontinue administration immediately and institute appropriate treatment if early manifestations of hypersensitivity reactions occur; such reactions include angioedema, infusion site inflammation (e.g., burning, stinging, erythema), chills, cough, dizziness, fever, flushing, generalized urticaria, headache, hives, hypotension, lethargy, musculoskeletal pain, nausea, pruritus, rash, restlessness, tachycardia, chest tightness, tingling, vomiting, and wheezing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Neutralizing antibodies (inhibitors) against factor X may occur in patients receiving factor X (human).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> Presence of inhibitors may manifest as inadequate response to treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for possible development of inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If expected plasma factor X activity levels not attained or bleeding not controlled with an appropriate dose, measure factor X inhibitory antibody concentrations using an appropriate assay (e.g., Nijmegen-Bethesda inhibitor assay).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Factor X (human) is prepared from human blood and may carry risk of transmitting infectious agents, including viruses and, theoretically, the Creutzfeldt-Jakob disease (CJD) agent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> Possibility that unknown infectious agents exist in such products.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Report any suspected infections associated with factor X (human) to Bio Products Laboratory at 866-398-0825 or to FDA at 800-FDA-1088 or [Web].<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor plasma factor X activity by performing a validated test (e.g., one-stage clotting assay) to confirm that adequate factor X levels have been achieved and maintained with factor X (human) therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If expected plasma factor X activity levels not attained or bleeding not controlled with an appropriate dose, measure factor X inhibitory antibody concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Immunogenicity under Cautions.)

No adequate and well-controlled studies of factor X (human) in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Animal reproductive and development studies with factor X (human) lacking.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use during pregnancy only when clearly needed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether factor X (human) is distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> Use with caution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy in patients <12 years of age not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Important to individualize dosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of instructing patients to read the manufacturer's patient information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of patient not attempting to infuse factor X (human) unless instructed how to do so by clinician or hemophilia center.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of patients immediately informing a clinician of any early signs or symptoms of hypersensitivity, including angioedema, infusion site inflammation (burning, stinging, erythema), chills, cough, dizziness, fever, flushing, generalized urticaria, headache, hives, hypotension, lethargy, musculoskeletal pain, nausea, pruritus, rash, restlessness, tachycardia, chest tightness, tingling, vomiting, or wheezing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients that the development of neutralizing antibodies (inhibitors) to factor X is a possible complication of treatment with factor X (human) and of advising patients to contact their healthcare provider for further treatment and/or assessment if they experience a lack of clinical response to factor X (human).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and herbal supplements, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
