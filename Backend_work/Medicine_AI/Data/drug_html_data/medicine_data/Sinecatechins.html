Introduction

Antioxidant; a green tea extract consisting predominantly of catechins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Uses for Sinecatechins

External Genital and Perianal Human Papillomavirus Warts

Treatment of external genital and perianal warts (condylomata acuminata) caused by human papillomavirus (HPV) in immunocompetent patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

CDC recommends that external HPV genital warts be treated with a self-administered topical therapy (imiquimod, podofilox, sinecatechins), a topical therapy administered by a health-care provider (podophyllum resin, trichloroacetic acid [TCA], bichloroacetic acid [BCA]), or a surgical technique (cryotherapy, electrosurgery, surgical excision).<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Should not be used for treatment of urethral, intravaginal, cervical, rectal, or intra-anal HPV disease; safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Sinecatechins Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the skin as a 15% ointment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> For external use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid contact with the eyes; do not administer orally, intravaginally, or intra-anally.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not apply to open wounds.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Wash hands before and after application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Apply a thin layer to each wart with the finger(s), dabbing to ensure complete coverage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not use bandages, occlusive dressings, or wrappings.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Uncircumcised males treating warts under the foreskin should retract the foreskin and clean the area daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Washing ointment from the treated area prior to the next application is not necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If bathing or washing the treatment area, apply ointment afterwards.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Dosage

Apply a 0.5-cm strand of ointment to each wart 3 times daily, dabbing with fingers to ensure complete coverage and leaving a thin film on warts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Continue therapy until all warts have completely cleared or for a maximum of 16 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy of multiple treatment courses not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

Maximum of 16 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Sinecatechins

Contraindications

  • No known contraindications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No known contraindications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Safety and efficacy not established for treatment of urethral, intravaginal, cervical, rectal, or intra-anal HPV disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not apply to open wounds.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Local skin reactions occur frequently.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> (See Common Adverse Effects under Cautions.) Continue therapy when severity of the local skin reaction is acceptable.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In case of severe local reactions (i.e., pruritus), wash area with mild soap and water to remove the ointment; withhold further doses and notify clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid exposure of the genital and perianal area to sunlight or ultraviolet light; effects of such light not studied.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy in immunosuppressed patients not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether topical sinecatechins is distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consult a clinician before using in children <12 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Erythema, pruritus, burning, pain/discomfort, erosion/ulceration, edema, induration, vesicular rash.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Sinecatechins

No formal drug interaction studies to date.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Sinecatechins Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Pharmacokinetics after topical application not fully characterized.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Data suggest that systemic exposure to catechins following repeated topical application of sinecatechins ointment probably is less than that observed after single ingestion of 400 mL of green tea.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether distributed into milk following topical application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Before dispensing to patient: 2–8°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> After dispensing, store refrigerated or at temperatures <25°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • A botanical drug product; a partially purified fraction of the water extract of green tea leaves from Camellia sinensis (L.) O Kuntze.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Mechanism of action in treatment of external genital and perianal HPV warts is unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Antioxidant activity demonstrated in vitro; the clinical importance of this finding is unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Green tea catechins may have immunostimulant, antitumor, and potent antiviral properties that may contribute to the therapeutic effect of sinecatechins ointment.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Consists principally of catechins (85–95% by weight), including more than 55% epigallocatechin gallate (EGCg); other catechin derivatives present include epicatechin (EC), epigallocatechin (EGC), epicatechin gallate (ECg), gallocatechin gallate (GCg), gallocatechin (GC), catechin gallate (Cg), and catechin (C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Also contains gallic acid, caffeine, and theobromine (together account for 2.5% of total drug substance) plus undefined botanical constituents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of reading manufacturer’s patient information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of using only as directed by clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

    Importance of applying externally only; importance of avoiding contact with the eyes or application into the vagina or anus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients that sinecatechins ointment is not a cure for external genital and perianal warts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> New warts may appear during therapy and also should be treated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Local skin reactions are common.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dermatologic Effects under Cautions.) If severe skin reactions occur, contact clinician, wash treatment area with mild soap and water, and withhold further application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients to avoid sexual (genital, anal, or oral) contact while the ointment is on the skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> The ointment must be washed off carefully before having protected sexual contact, since the ointment may weaken condoms and vaginal diaphragms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of advising patients that the effect of sinecatechins on the transmission of genital and perianal warts is unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising female patients using tampons that a tampon should be inserted before applying the ointment; avoid accidental application of the ointment into the vagina if the tampon is changed while the ointment is on the skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising uncircumcised males to retract the foreskin and clean the area daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients to avoid exposure of the genital and perianal area to sunlight or ultraviolet light.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients not to bandage, cover, or wrap the treatment area.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Topical Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

  • Importance of advising patients that the ointment may stain clothing or bedding.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
