What is Preparation H Medicated Wipes?

Witch hazel is a plant also known as Avellano de Bruja, Café du Diable, Hamamelis, Hamamélis, Hamamélis de Virginie, Hamamelis virginiana, Hazel, Noisetier des Sorcières, Snapping Tobacco Wood, Spotted Elder, Virginian Witch Hazel, Winter Bloom, and other names. Witch hazel is a liquid distilled from dried leaves, bark, and twigs of this plant.

Preparation H Medicated Wipes (for the skin) has been used in alternative medicine as a possibly effective aid in treating hemorrhoids, minor bleeding, and skin irritation.

Preparation H Medicated Wipes has also been used to treat eczema. However, research has shown that this medicine may not be effective in treating this condition.

Other uses not proven with research have included treating bruises or varicose veins.

It is not certain whether Preparation H Medicated Wipes is effective in treating any medical condition. Medicinal use of this product has not been approved by the FDA. This product should not be used in place of medication prescribed for you by your doctor.

Preparation H Medicated Wipes may also be used for purposes not listed in this product guide.

Warnings

Follow all directions on the product label and package. Tell each of your healthcare providers about all your medical conditions, allergies, and all medicines you use.

Before taking this medicine

Before using Preparation H Medicated Wipes, talk to your healthcare provider. You may not be able to use this product if you have certain medical conditions.

Preparation H Medicated Wipes is thought to be possibly safe when used on a child's skin. Ask a healthcare provider about using Preparation H Medicated Wipes on a child.

Ask a doctor before using this product if you are pregnant or breast-feeding.

How should I use Preparation H Medicated Wipes?

When considering the use of an alternative medicine, seek the advice of your doctor. You may also consider consulting a practitioner who is trained in the use of natural medicines.

If you choose to use Preparation H Medicated Wipes, use it as directed on the package or as directed by your doctor, pharmacist, or other healthcare provider. Do not use more of this product than is recommended on the label.

Do not take by mouth. Topical medicine is for use only on the skin. Witch hazel may cause stomach upset or liver problems when taken by mouth.

Preparation H Medicated Wipes is available in different forms, such as liquid, gel, soap, or topical pad.

Call your doctor if the condition you are treating with Preparation H Medicated Wipes does not improve, or if it gets worse while using this product.

Store Preparation H Medicated Wipes as directed on the label.

What happens if I miss a dose?

Since Preparation H Medicated Wipes is used when needed, you may not be on a dosing schedule. Skip any missed dose if it's almost time for your next dose.

What happens if I overdose?

An overdose of witch hazel topical is not expected to be dangerous. Seek emergency medical attention or call the Poison Help line at 1-800-222-1222 if anyone has accidentally swallowed the medication.

What should I avoid while using Preparation H Medicated Wipes?

Follow your healthcare provider's instructions about any restrictions on food, beverages, or activity.

Preparation H Medicated Wipes side effects

Get emergency medical help if you have signs of an allergic reaction: hives; difficult breathing; swelling of your face, lips, tongue, or throat.

Although not all side effects are known, Preparation H Medicated Wipes is thought to be likely safe for most people when used as directed.

This is not a complete list of side effects and others may occur. Call your doctor for medical advice about side effects. You may report side effects to FDA at 1-800-FDA-1088.

What other drugs will affect Preparation H Medicated Wipes?

Witch hazel used on the skin is not likely to be affected by other drugs you use. But many drugs can interact with each other. Tell each of your healthcare providers about all medicines you use, including prescription and over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, and herbal products.
