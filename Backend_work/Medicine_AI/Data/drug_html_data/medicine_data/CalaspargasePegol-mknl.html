Introduction

Antineoplastic agent; conjugate of monomethoxy polyethylene glycol (mPEG) and Escherichia coli-derived asparaginase.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Uses for Calaspargase Pegol-mknl

Acute Lymphocytic (Lymphoblastic) Leukemia (ALL)

Component of combination chemotherapy for treatment of childhood and young adult ALL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Asparaginase preparations used in induction and/or intensification (consolidation) regimens prior to maintenance therapy; CNS-directed therapy (for prophylaxis of CNS involvement) also required.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

In childhood ALL, combination therapy with an asparaginase preparation, a corticosteroid (dexamethasone or prednisone), and vincristine, with or without an anthracycline (daunorubicin or doxorubicin), is used as an induction regimen.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> Some clinicians reserve 4-drug induction regimens for those with high-risk childhood ALL, while others use such regimens for all patients with childhood ALL regardless of presenting features.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> Multiple-drug induction regimens produce complete remission in ≥95% of children with ALL.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

In adults, induction regimens typically include an anthracycline, vincristine, and prednisone; some regimens also add other drugs (e.g., an asparaginase preparation, cyclophosphamide).<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Such induction regimens produce complete remission in about 60–90% of adults with ALL.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Adolescents and young adults appear to have better outcomes with use of pediatric-based treatment regimens for ALL instead of traditional adult treatment regimens.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Grade 3 or 4 hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylaxis, reported with calaspargase pegol therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Other hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., angioedema, lip or eye swelling, erythema, hypotension, bronchospasm, dyspnea, pruritus, rash) also reported with other asparaginase preparations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer calaspargase pegol in a setting with resuscitation equipment and other agents necessary to treat anaphylaxis (e.g., epinephrine, oxygen, IV corticosteroids, antihistamines).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Observe patients for 1 hour following infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If a serious hypersensitivity reaction occurs, discontinue the drug and initiate appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

Pancreatitis reported with calaspargase pegol therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Hemorrhagic or necrotizing pancreatitis also reported with other asparaginase preparations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Measure serum amylase and/or lipase concentrations to identify early signs of pancreatic inflammation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If pancreatitis is suspected, withhold calaspargase pegol therapy; if confirmed, permanently discontinue therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

Serious thrombotic events (e.g., sagittal sinus thrombosis) reported with calaspargase pegol therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Discontinue the drug in patients experiencing serious thrombotic events.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

Hemorrhagic events associated with increased PT, increased PTT, and hypofibrinogenemia reported with calaspargase pegol therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Evaluate coagulation parameters (e.g., PT, PTT, fibrinogen) in patients with signs and symptoms of hemorrhage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider the need for appropriate clotting factor replacement therapy in patients with severe or symptomatic coagulopathy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

Hepatotoxicity and abnormal liver function, including elevations in aminotransferase and bilirubin (direct and indirect) concentrations and reduced serum albumin and plasma fibrinogen concentrations, can occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor bilirubin and aminotransferase concentrations at least weekly during treatment cycles including calaspargase pegol and for 6 weeks after last dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If serious hepatotoxicity occurs, discontinue calaspargase pegol and initiate supportive care.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

May cause fetal harm based on animal findings.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Studies in pregnant rabbits suggest that asparagine depletion may harm animal offspring.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Confirm pregnancy status prior to initiating calaspargase pegol therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid pregnancy during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Women of childbearing potential should use effective methods of contraception, including a barrier method, while receiving calaspargase pegol and for ≥3 months after last dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid concomitant use of oral contraceptives and calaspargase pegol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.) If used during pregnancy or if patient becomes pregnant, apprise of potential fetal hazard.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potential for immunogenicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Although development of anti-drug antibodies reported,<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> immunogenic potential of calaspargase pegol-mknl not fully established.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Calaspargase Pegol-mknl Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following IV administration of calaspargase pegol-mknl, pharmacokinetics of plasma asparaginase activity are nonlinear.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following IV administration of a single 2500-unit/m2 dose, peak plasma concentration attained after approximately 1 hour, generally near the end of the 1-hour infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Steady-state concentrations achieved around the fourth dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Rapidly depletes plasma asparagine (i.e., concentrations below the lower limit of quantification within 5 minutes after the dose) in the induction phase.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Plasma asparagine concentrations remained less than the assay limit of quantification for >18 days following a single 2500-unit/m2 dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Serum trough asparaginase activity of ≥0.1 units/mL correlates with asparagine depletion in CSF and serum; has been established as a surrogate measure of asparaginase efficacy.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Steady-state serum trough asparaginase activity for calaspargase pegol-mknl 2500 units/m2 every 3 weeks comparable to that for pegaspargase 2500 units/m2 every 2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether calaspargase pegol is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Asparaginase does not appear to cross blood-brain barrier; however, CSF asparagine depletion occurs as a result of plasma asparagine depletion following treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Unlikely to be renally filtered.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Approximately 16 days;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> about 2.5–3 times longer than asparaginase activity half-life of pegaspargase.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>
