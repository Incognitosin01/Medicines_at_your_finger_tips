Introduction

Antivenom; equine IgG F(ab′)2 fragments capable of binding and neutralizing venom toxins of Centruroides scorpions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Uses for Centruroides Immune F(ab′)2

Scorpion Envenomation

Treatment of clinical signs of scorpion envenomation;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

May shorten time to resolution of envenomation symptoms,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> possibly eliminating need for treatment in an intensive care unit.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

In the US, Centruroides sculpturatus (commonly known as the bark scorpion) is the only scorpion considered potentially dangerous to humans;<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> found mainly in Arizona, but may be found in parts of California, New Mexico, Texas, Nevada, and northern Mexico.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Consider consultation with experts experienced in treating Centruroides envenomation (e.g., Arizona Poison and Drug Information Center in Tucson, Banner Good Samaritan Poison and Drug Information Center in Phoenix).<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Centruroides Immune F(ab′)2 Dosage and Administration

General

  • Initiate treatment as soon as possible after a scorpion sting in patients who develop clinically important signs of scorpion envenomation (e.g., loss of muscle control, roving or abnormal eye movements, slurred speech, respiratory distress, excessive salivation, frothing at the mouth, vomiting).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Monitor closely during and for up to 1 hour after completion of IV infusion to confirm resolution of clinically important signs of envenomation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Severe hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylaxis, may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Patients with known allergies to equine protein are at increased risk for anaphylactic reactions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Patients who previously received Centruroides (scorpion) immune F(ab′)2 (equine) or another equine antivenom or antitoxin may be at increased risk for severe hypersensitivity reactions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor closely for hypersensitivity during antivenom infusion; IV epinephrine, corticosteroids, and diphenhydramine should be readily available.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

If anaphylactic reaction occurs, immediately discontinue antivenom infusion and initiate appropriate emergency medical care.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Delayed hypersensitivity or serum sickness reactions may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Mild symptoms may include pruritus, nausea, urticaria, low-grade fever, and malaise; severe manifestations include persistent urticaria, vomiting, arthralgia, myalgia, syncope, and angioedema.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Monitor for manifestations of delayed allergic reactions or serum sickness (e.g., rash, fever, myalgia, arthralgia) at follow-up visits.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Treat such reactions as necessary;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> treatment is generally symptomatic (e.g., antihistamines, analgesics, antipyretics, corticosteroids).<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Prepared from equine plasma;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> potentially may transmit infectious agents, including viruses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Several steps in manufacturing process (e.g., pepsin digestion, ammonium sulfate precipitation/heat treatment, nanofiltration) reduce risk of transmission of viruses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Contains trace amounts of cresol (<0.41 mg per vial) from manufacturing process.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Cresol used as an injectable excipient has resulted in localized reactions and generalized myalgia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use with caution in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Efficacy and safety in pediatric patients comparable to that in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Has been used in children ranging from younger than 1 month to 18 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Centruroides Immune F(ab′)2 Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following IV infusion, clinically important manifestations of scorpion envenomation generally resolve within 4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Approximately 6.6 days in healthy (nonenvenomed) adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> Additional study needed to determine pharmacokinetics in patients with scorpion envenomation.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Room temperature (up to 25°C); may be exposed to temperatures up to 40°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discard partially used vials.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Centruroides (scorpion) immune F(ab′)2 (equine) is a polyvalent preparation of venom-specific F(ab′)2 fragments of equine IgG that bind and neutralize Centruroides venom toxins, facilitating redistribution of the venom away from target tissues and elimination from the body.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Manufactured from plasma of horses immunized with venom of C. noxius, C. limpidus limpidus, C. limpidus tecomanus, and C. suffusus suffusus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Although C. sculpturatus venom not used in manufacturing process, in vitro studies demonstrate similar binding affinities and high degree of cross-reactivity among venoms of the various Centruroides species, including C. sculpturatus.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

  • Standardized by ability to neutralize Centruroides scorpion venom in mice (mouse LD50 neutralizing units).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Each commercially available vial will neutralize ≥150 mouse LD50 units of Centruroides venom.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
