Administration

Administer by nasal inhalation using a metered-dose spray pump.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Shake spray pump gently immediately prior to priming or use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Prior to initial use, prime spray pump by actuating 8 times.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Prime pump after a period of nonuse (i.e., ≥4 days) by actuating once or until a fine spray appears.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Clear nasal passages prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Tilt the head slightly forward; insert spray tip into one nostril, keeping bottle upright.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Point the tip away from the nasal septum.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Pump the drug into one nostril while holding the other nostril closed and concurrently inspiring through the nose.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Repeat procedure for the other nostril.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Wipe applicator with a clean tissue after use.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

To unblock nasal applicator, remove dust cap and gently pull upward to free applicator.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Wash dust cap and applicator in warm water, dry, replace applicator on bottle, and reprime the pump.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Dosage

After initial priming, nasal spray pump delivers 50 mcg of ciclesonide per metered spray.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Each bottle containing 12.5 g of ciclesonide nasal spray delivers 120 metered sprays, after which the correct amount of drug in each spray cannot be assured.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Symptomatic relief usually is evident within 24–48 hours after initiation of therapy; further symptomatic improvement may occur over 1–2 weeks in seasonal allergic rhinitis or 5 weeks in perennial allergic rhinitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Once symptoms are controlled, gradually reduce dosage to the lowest effective level.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children ≥6 years of age: 100 mcg (2 sprays) in each nostril once daily (total dosage: 200 mcg daily).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Ciclesonide (EENT)

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to ciclesonide or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to ciclesonide or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Carefully monitor patients being switched from prolonged systemic corticosteroids to intranasal corticosteroids since corticosteroid withdrawal symptoms (e.g., joint pain, muscular pain, lassitude, depression), acute adrenal insufficiency, and/or severe symptomatic exacerbation of asthma or other clinical conditions may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In patients with asthma or other clinical conditions requiring long-term systemic steroids, rapid decreases in systemic corticosteroid dosages may cause severe symptom exacerbations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Although risk with intranasal use is unknown, consider the possibility that corticosteroid-induced immunosuppression could occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid exposure to varicella and measles in previously unexposed patients and those who have not been properly immunized.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If exposure to varicella or measles occurs in susceptible patients, consider administering varicella zoster immune globulin (VZIG) or immune globulin (IG), respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider treatment with an antiviral agent if varicella develops.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Immediate hypersensitivity reactions or contact dermatitis may occur rarely.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients hypersensitive to other corticosteroids; cross-sensitivity may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Exceeding recommended dosages or use in patients who are particularly sensitive to corticosteroid effects may cause systemic corticosteroid effects (e.g., menstrual irregularities, acneiform lesions, cushingoid features).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If systemic effects occur, slowly discontinue drug therapy, according to accepted procedures for discontinuing oral corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Rarely, localized candidal infections of the nose and/or pharynx reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Treat suspected infections appropriately; may require discontinuance of ciclesonide therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Periodically examine nasal passages for signs of mucosal changes during long-term therapy (several months or longer).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid use until healing occurs in patients with recent nasal septal ulcers, nasal surgery, or nasal trauma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use cautiously, if at all, in patients with clinical or asymptomatic Mycobacterium tuberculosis infections of the respiratory tract, untreated local or systemic fungal or bacterial infections, ocular herpes simplex, or systemic viral or parasitic infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increased IOP, glaucoma, and cataracts reported rarely with other intranasal corticosteroids; such effects not reported with ciclesonide.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Closely monitor patients with a change in vision or a history of glaucoma and/or cataracts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
