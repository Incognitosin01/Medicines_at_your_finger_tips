Uses for Gatifloxacin (EENT)

Bacterial Ophthalmic Infections

Topical treatment of bacterial conjunctivitis caused by susceptible Staphylococcus aureus, S. epidermidis, Streptococcus mitis group, S. oralis, S. pneumoniae, or Haemophilus influenzae.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Many cases of mild, acute bacterial conjunctivitis resolve spontaneously without anti-infective therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> Although topical ophthalmic anti-infectives may shorten time to resolution and reduce severity and risk of complications,<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> avoid indiscriminate use of topical anti-infectives.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Treatment of acute bacterial conjunctivitis generally is empiric;<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> use of a broad-spectrum topical ophthalmic antibacterial usually recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> In vitro staining and/or cultures of conjunctival material may be indicated in diagnosis and management of recurrent, severe, or chronic purulent conjunctivitis or when acute conjunctivitis does not respond to initial empiric topical treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Gatifloxacin (EENT) Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply 0.5% ophthalmic solution topically to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

For topical ophthalmic use only;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> do not inject directly into anterior chamber of the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> (See Administration Precautions under Cautions.)

Avoid contaminating applicator tip with material from eye, fingers, or other source.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Children ≥1 year of age: Instill 1 drop of 0.5% solution in the affected eye(s) every 2 hours while awake (up to 8 times daily) on day 1, then 1 drop 2–4 times daily while awake on days 2 through 7.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Gatifloxacin (EENT)

Contraindications

  • Hypersensitivity to gatifloxacin, other quinolones, or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Hypersensitivity to gatifloxacin, other quinolones, or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylactic reactions, angioedema (e.g., pharyngeal, laryngeal, or facial edema), dyspnea, urticaria, and itching, reported in patients receiving gatifloxacin ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Stevens-Johnson syndrome reported rarely.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Hypersensitivity reactions have occurred following a single dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Immediately discontinue at first sign of rash or hypersensitivity reaction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For topical ophthalmic use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Corneal endothelial cell injury may occur if gatifloxacin ophthalmic solution is introduced directly into anterior chamber of the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prolonged use may result in overgrowth of nonsusceptible organisms, including fungi.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

If superinfection occurs, discontinue gatifloxacin ophthalmic solution and institute appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Whenever clinical judgment dictates, examine patient with the aid of magnification (e.g., slit lamp biomicroscopy) and, if appropriate, fluorescein staining.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Do not wear contact lenses during treatment with gatifloxacin ophthalmic solution or if any signs or symptoms of bacterial conjunctivitis are present.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Data not available regarding use of gatifloxacin 0.5% ophthalmic solution in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Distributed into milk in rats after oral administration;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> not known whether distributed into human milk following topical application to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Consider benefits of breast-feeding and importance of the drug to the woman;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> also consider potential adverse effects on the breast-fed child from the drug or underlying maternal condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children <1 year of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in safety and efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Worsening of conjunctivitis, eye irritation, dysgeusia, eye pain.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Gatifloxacin (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Serum concentrations of gatifloxacin were undetectable (<5 ng/mL) after topical application to one eye of gatifloxacin 0.5% ophthalmic solution in an escalated dosage regimen (2 drops initially, then 2 drops 4 times daily for 7 days, and then 2 drops 8 times daily for 3 days).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

15–25°C (may be exposed to 15–30°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Protect from freezing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Contains benzalkonium chloride as a preservative.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>
