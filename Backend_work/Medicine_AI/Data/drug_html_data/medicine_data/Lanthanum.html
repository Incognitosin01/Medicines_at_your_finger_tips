Introduction

Phosphate binder used to reduce the intestinal absorption of phosphates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Uses for Lanthanum

Hyperphosphatemia

Reduction of serum phosphorus in patients with end-stage renal disease (ESRD).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Reductions in serum phosphorus concentrations are similar to those achieved with alternative phosphate binders (e.g., calcium salts, sevelamer).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Risk of hypercalcemia apparently is less than with calcium (e.g., calcium carbonate) salts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Lanthanum Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally in divided doses with or immediately after meals (in order to bind dietary phosphates efficiently).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Chew tablets completely before swallowing; do not swallow intact tablets.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as lanthanum carbonate; dosage expressed in terms of lanthanum.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Initially, 750 mg–1.5 g daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adjust dosage at 2- to 3-week intervals until serum phosphorus concentration is acceptable; generally titrated in increments of 750 mg daily in clinical studies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Dosage of 1.5–3 g daily usually is required to reduce serum phosphorus concentrations to <6 mg/dL;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> dosages up to 3.75 g daily have been studied.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor serum phosphorus concentrations as needed during titration and regularly thereafter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Lanthanum

Contraindications

  • No known contraindications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No known contraindications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Safety and efficacy not established in active peptic ulcer disease, ulcerative colitis, Crohn’s disease, or bowel obstruction; use with caution in patients with these disorders.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Abdominal radiographs performed in patients taking lanthanum may have the typical radiopaque appearance of a radiograph performed using an imaging agent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No differences in fracture or mortality rates were observed between patients receiving lanthanum and those receiving alternative therapy for up to 3 years in clinical studies; however, data are insufficient to conclude lanthanum has no effect on fracture or mortality rates beyond 3 years of use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether lanthanum is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Caution if used in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children <18 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Deposited in developing bone (including the growth plate) of animals in long-term studies;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> although growth abnormalities in animals were not observed, the consequences of deposition in developing bone of pediatric patients are unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No substantial differences in safety and efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Lanthanum Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Information regarding the mass balance of lanthanum in humans after oral administration is not available.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Minimally absorbed from the GI tract; bioavailability <0.002%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Minimal effect on systemic lanthanum concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Mean plasma concentrations of the drug remain low (≤1.1 ng/mL) for up to 2 years of use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Plasma concentrations increase minimally with increasing dosages in the recommended range.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Distributed into bone; bone lanthanum concentrations increase over time (e.g., 4.5 years of administration).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Does not appear to cross the blood-brain barrier in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

>99%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Not metabolized.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In animals, excreted principally in the feces (94–99%), mainly as unabsorbed drug; systemically absorbed lanthanum is eliminated in the feces, principally via biliary excretion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Minimally excreted in urine in healthy individuals (renal clearance of IV lanthanum chloride [not commercially available in the US] is <2% of total plasma clearance).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Plasma: 53 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Bone: 2–3.6 years.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In lanthanum-treated patients with ESRD, no quantifiable amounts were present in the dialysate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

25°C (may be exposed to 15–30°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Protect from moisture.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Dissociates in the acidic environment of the upper GI tract to release trivalent lanthanum ions, which bind dietary phosphate released during digestion, thereby forming highly insoluble lanthanum phosphate complexes that are excreted fecally.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

  • Lanthanum ions have a high affinity for phosphate; the drug binds about 97% of available phosphates in vitro at pH 3–5 (corresponding to that of gastric fluid) when in twofold molar excess to phosphates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Reduces phosphate absorption, serum phosphorus concentrations, and serum calcium times phosphorus product (Ca × P).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>
