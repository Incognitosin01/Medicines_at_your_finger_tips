Introduction

Uses for Pentosan

Interstitial Cystitis

Symptomatic relief of bladder pain or discomfort associated with interstitial cystitis;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Pentosan Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer with water ≥1 hour before or 2 hours after meals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as pentosan polysulfate sodium; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

100 mg 3 times daily<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> for 3 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> If after 3 months no improvement and no dose-limiting adverse effects occur, may continue therapy for another 3 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states that if no improvement of pain is observed by 6 months, the clinical benefits and risks of continued therapy are unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> However, data from a long-term clinical study indicate overall continued symptomatic improvement (e.g., pain, urgency, urinary frequency, nocturia) during 1–2 years of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Some clinicians recommend a dosage of 200 mg twice daily†; this dosage appears to be effective and promotes greater patient compliance.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Pentosan

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to pentosan polysulfate, structurally related compounds, or any ingredient in formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to pentosan polysulfate, structurally related compounds, or any ingredient in formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Pentosan polysulfate is weak anticoagulant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Rectal hemorrhage and bleeding complications of ecchymosis, epistaxis, and gum hemorrhage reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Evaluate patients at increased risk for hemorrhage including those undergoing invasive procedures, with signs and symptoms of coagulopathy, or receiving concomitant drugs that affect hemostasis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

Delayed immunoallergic thrombocytopenia similar to heparin-induced thrombocytopenia with symptoms of thrombosis and hemorrhage reported with sub-Q, IM, or sublingual administration of a different formulation of pentosan polysulfate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with history of heparin-induced thrombocytopenia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Carefully evaluate patients with thrombocytopenia prior to initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Thrombocytopenia and elevations in PT and partial thromboplastin time (PTT) reported in patients with elevated liver function test results.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> Such effects not observed in healthy men receiving ≤1.2 g of pentosan polysulfate sodium daily (a dosage greater than the recommended 100 mg 3 times daily) for 8 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Carefully evaluate patients with diseases such as aneurysms, hemophilia, GI ulcerations, polyps, or diverticula prior to initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Mild and usually transient elevations (<2.5 times ULN) of serum aminotransferases, alkaline phosphatase, γ-glutamyl transpeptidase, and LDH concentrations reported in about 1.2% of patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Such abnormalities usually occur 3–12 months after initiation of therapy and generally not associated with jaundice or other clinical signs and symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> These elevations may remain unchanged or rarely progress with continued use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Alopecia, primarily alopecia areata (limited to single area on scalp), reported; may occur within first 4 weeks of initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category B.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pentosan Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following oral administration of radiolabeled pentosan polysulfate (as solution), approximately 6% absorbed systemically, with peak levels of plasma radioactivity achieved at a median of 2 hours (range, 0.6–120 hours) after dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Early or mild interstitial cystitis: Pain relief occurs within 6–8 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Moderate to severe interstitial cystitis: In majority of patients, pain relief occurs in approximately 6 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Pain relief may persist for >29 months (in some patients).<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Effect of food on absorption of pentosan polysulfate unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> In clinical trials, pentosan polysulfate was administered with water 1 hour before or 2 hours after meals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

In animals, distributed into uroepithelium of GU tract, with lower amounts distributed into liver, spleen, lung, skin, periosteum, and bone marrow.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Small amounts distributed into RBCs in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether pentosan polysulfate is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Orally absorbed pentosan polysulfate undergoes partial desulfation in liver and spleen and partial depolymerization in kidneys to form large number of metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Desulfation and depolymerization pathways can become saturated with continued dosing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following oral administration of radiolabeled 300- or 450-mg dose (as solution), 84 or 58%, respectively, of dose was excreted in feces as unchanged drug; about 6% of dose was excreted in urine, mainly as desulfated and depolymerized metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following oral administration of radiolabeled 300- or 450-mg dose (as solution), mean half-life for plasma radioactivity was 27 or 20 hours, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

15–30°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Semisynthetic low molecular weight heparinoid is a uroprotective agent structurally similar to naturally occurring glycosaminoglycans.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

  • The main cause of interstitial cystitis appears to be a defective mucous glycosaminoglycans layer of the bladder that may cause increased bladder epithelial permeability.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Such permeability allows movement of irritating urine solutes into interstitium and causes tissue injury.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

  • Although the mechanism of action of pentosan polysulfate sodium in the management of interstitial cystitis has not been fully elucidated, the drug appears to replenish the defective mucous (glycosaminoglycans) layer and restore bladder integrity (by adhering to surface of bladder); acts as a buffer to control cell permeability and prevent irritating solutes from reaching epithelial cells.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

  • Weak anticoagulant following oral administration compared with heparin (1/15 activity of heparin); may increase bleeding times.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

  • Given parenterally (parenteral formulation not commercially available in US), inhibited generation of factor Xa.<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

  • In ex vivo study, inhibits thrombin-induced platelet aggregation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
