Introduction

Eptinezumab-jjmr is a calcitonin gene-related peptide (CGRP) antagonist.

Uses for Eptinezumab-jjmr

Eptinezumab-jjmr has the following uses:

Eptinezumab-jjmr is indicated for the preventive treatment of migraine in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Eptinezumab-jjmr

Contraindications

Eptinezumab-jjmr is contraindicated in patients with serious hypersensitivity to eptinezumab-jjmr or to any of the excipients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Hypersensitivity reactions, including angioedema, urticaria, facial flushing, and rash, have occurred with eptinezumab-jjmr in clinical trials. Most hypersensitivity reactions occurred during infusion and were not serious, but often led to discontinuation or required treatment. Serious hypersensitivity reactions may occur. If a hypersensitivity reaction occurs, consider discontinuing eptinezumab-jjmr, and institute appropriate therapy. <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Risk Summary: There are no adequate data on developmental risks associated with the use of eptinezumab-jjmr in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No adverse developmental effects were observed following administration of eptinezumab-jjmr to pregnant animals at doses greater than those used clinically.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In the U.S. general population, the estimated background risk of major birth defects and miscarriages in clinically recognized pregnancies is 2–4% and 15–20%, respectively. The estimated rate of major birth defects (2.2–2.9%) and miscarriage (17%) among deliveries to women with migraine are similar to rates reported in women without migraine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Published data have suggested that women with migraine may be at increased risk of preeclampsia and gestational hypertension during pregnancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Animal Data: When eptinezumab-jjmr (0, 75, or 150 mg/kg) was administered weekly to female rats and rabbits by intravenous injection throughout organogenesis, no adverse effects on embryofetal development were observed. The higher dose tested (150 mg/kg) is 30 times the maximum recommended human dose (MRHD) of 300 mg, on a body weight basis (mg/kg).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

When eptinezumab-jjmr (0, 75, or 150 mg/kg) was administered weekly to female rats throughout pregnancy and lactation, no adverse effects on pre- and postnatal development were observed. The higher dose tested (150 mg/kg) is 30 times the MRHD, on a mg/kg basis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Risk Summary: There are no data on the presence of eptinezumab-jjmr in human milk, the effects on the breastfed infant, or the effects on milk production. The developmental and health benefits of breastfeeding should be considered along with the mother’s clinical need for eptinezumab-jjmr and any potential adverse effects on the breastfed infant from eptinezumab-jjmr or from the underlying maternal condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and effectiveness in pediatric patients have not been established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Clinical studies of eptinezumab-jjmr did not include sufficient numbers of patients aged 65 and over to determine whether they respond differently from younger patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

The most common adverse reactions (≥2% and 2% or greater than placebo) were nasopharyngitis and hypersensitivity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

Mechanism of Action

Eptinezumab-jjmr is a humanized monoclonal antibody that binds to calcitonin gene-related peptide (CGRP) ligand and blocks its binding to the receptor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

Advise the patient to read the FDA-approved patient labeling.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Hypersensitivity Reactions

Inform patients that hypersensitivity reactions, including angioedema, urticaria, facial flushing, and rash, can occur. Advise patients to contact their healthcare provider immediately if signs or symptoms of hypersensitivity reactions occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pregnancy

Advise patients to notify their healthcare provider if they become pregnant during treatment or plan to become pregnant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Lactation

Inform patients to notify their healthcare provider if they are breastfeeding or plan to breastfeed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
