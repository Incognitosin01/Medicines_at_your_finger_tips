Introduction

A vitamin A derivative; naturally occurring endogenous retinoid with topical antineoplastic activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Uses for Alitretinoin

AIDS-related Kaposi’s Sarcoma

Treatment of cutaneous lesions associated with AIDS-related Kaposi’s sarcoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Not recommended for treatment of systemic AIDS-related Kaposi’s sarcoma, characterized by ≥10 new lesions in the prior month, symptomatic lymphedema, symptomatic pulmonary Kaposi’s sarcoma, or symptomatic visceral disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No clinical experience to date in patients receiving systemic therapy for Kaposi’s sarcoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Alitretinoin Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Wait 20 minutes after bathing or showering before applying gel; avoid use of occlusive dressings or wrappings.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Avoid contact with healthy skin or mucous membranes (e.g., eyes, nostrils, mouth, lips, vagina, tip of penis, rectum, anus); irritation may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Allow gel to dry for 3–5 minutes before covering treated area with clothing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If possible, do not bathe, shower, or swim for at least 3 hours after application.<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

If application-site toxicity occurs, reduce application frequency.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If severe irritation occurs, discontinue temporarily until manifestations subside.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Initially, apply twice daily in sufficient amounts (to cover only affected areas).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May increase application frequency gradually to 3 and then 4 times daily, according to individual lesion tolerance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In some patients, appreciable response occurred only after ≥14 weeks of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Continue therapy as long as the patient derives benefit<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (has been applied for up to 175 weeks in clinical trials).<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Alitretinoin

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to retinoids or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to retinoids or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

May cause fetal harm; teratogenicity and embryotoxicity demonstrated in animals receiving oral alitretinoin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> No reproduction studies in animals with topical alitretinoin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

No adequate and well-controlled studies in humans.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid pregnancy during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If used during pregnancy, apprise of potential hazard to the fetus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Retinoids associated with photosensitivity; in vitro data indicate alitretinoin may have weak photosensitizing effect.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Minimize exposure of treated areas to natural or artificial (e.g., sunlamps) sunlight.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible severe local skin reactions (e.g., intense erythema, edema, vesiculation).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Patients with cutaneous T-cell lymphoma exhibit less tolerance to these effects than those with Kaposi’s sarcoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category D.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Fetal/Neonatal Morbidity and Mortality under Cautions.)

Not known whether alitretinoin or its metabolites are distributed into milk; discontinue nursing due to risk in nursing infants.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children <18 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Insufficient clinical experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether they respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Rash,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> pruritus,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> exfoliative dermatitis,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> skin disorders (excoriation,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> cracking,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> scabbing,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> crusting,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> drainage,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> eschar,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> fissure,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> oozing<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> ), pain,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> paresthesia,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> edema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Alitretinoin

Metabolized by CYP2C9, CYP3A4, CYP1A1, and CYP1A2.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No data available on interactions between topical alitretinoin and systemically administered drugs for Kaposi’s sarcoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Alitretinoin Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Not substantially absorbed systemically following topical application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Following repeated, multiple-daily-dose topical applications of alitretinoin (9-cis-retinoic acid) in patients with cutaneous lesions associated with Kaposi’s sarcoma, plasma concentrations of 9-cis-retinoic acid were similar to those of naturally occurring 9-cis-retinoic acid found in healthy untreated patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether alitretinoin or its metabolites are distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Alitretinoin metabolized to 4-hydroxy-9-cis-retinoic acid and 4-oxo-9-cis-retinoic acid by CYP2C9, CYP3A4, CYP1A1, and CYP1A2 in vitro; major circulating metabolite in vivo following oral alitretinoin is 4-oxo-9-cis-retinoic acid.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Principally by metabolism; no unchanged drug excreted in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

1.3–2.4 hours following oral administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

25°C (may be exposed to 15–30°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Binds to and activates both intracellular retinoic acid receptors (RAR) (e.g., RARα, RARβ, RARγ) and retinoid X receptors (RXR) (e.g., RXRα, RXRβ, RXRγ) that appear to enhance gene transcription.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

  • Exact mechanism of action in Kaposi’s sarcoma not fully elucidated; appears to affect expression of genes that inhibit cell proliferation, induce cell differentiation, and trigger apoptosis in both healthy and cancer cells.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

  • In vitro, alitretinoin appears to inhibit Kaposi’s sarcoma cell growth.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
