Uses for Fluorides

Dental Caries

Prevent or reduce the incidence of dental caries; slow or reverse the progression of existing dental lesions.<html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>s</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>t</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>v</sup></body></html>

Effect of fluoride is predominantly posteruptive; maintain consistent small amounts of fluoride in saliva and dental plaque.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>113</sup></body></html> (See Actions.)

As a dietary supplement for the prevention of dental caries in children in areas where the concentration of fluoride ion in drinking water is less than optimal.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html>

American Dental Association (ADA) recommends that all patients, including those without dental caries and with no apparent increased risk of dental caries, receive adequate water fluoridation or oral fluoride supplementation, combined with daily use of fluoridated dentifrice and twice-yearly topical application of fluoride.<html><head></head><body><sup>128</sup></body></html>

In addition, ADA and others state that patients at low or slightly increased risk for dental caries (e.g., low socioeconomic status, low levels of parental education, no regular dental care, or without dental insurance or access to dental services) may require sodium fluoride 0.05% mouth rinse or 0.4% stannous fluoride gel.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>128</sup></body></html>

ADA and others recommend that patients at moderate-to-high risk for dental caries (e.g., active dental caries, history of high incidence of caries in older siblings or caregivers, root surfaces exposed by gingival recession, impaired ability to maintain oral hygiene, malformed enamel or dentin, low salivary buffering capacity, wearing of orthodontic appliances, or reduced salivary flow from medications, radiation treatment, or disease) receive additional exposure to fluorides (e.g., home or professionally applied products).<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>128</sup></body></html>

CDC supports fluoridation of the public water supply as the most cost-effective means of providing optimal levels of fluoride to large segments of the population.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html>

Optimal concentration of fluoride ion in drinking water is approximately 1 ppm (range 0.7–1.2 ppm), depending on the annual mean maximum daily temperature of the area.<html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>w</sup></body></html> In warm climates where more water is likely to be ingested, concentrations should be at the lower end of the range.<html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html>

In communities where the concentration of fluoride in the water supply is >2 ppm, use an alternative source of drinking water for children ≤8 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html>

Because consumption of commercially available beverages (including bottled water) in the US and Canada is displacing the consumption of tap or well water, the relative importance of fluoridation of local community water on fluoride intake may be affected.<html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> However, current recommendations in children continue to be based on the fluoride concentrations in local drinking water.<html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html>

ADA and others recommend that in areas where oral fluoride supplements are necessary, children 6 months to 16 years of age receive daily fluoride supplements to provide maximum benefit to both deciduous and permanent teeth.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Efficacy of topically applied fluoride varies according to the concentration of fluoride ion in the preparation, method and frequency of application, and the duration of use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Acidulated phosphate fluoride gels (alone or in combination with a saliva substitute) have been used to control dental decay after xerostomia-producing radiation therapy of tumors of the head and neck.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stannous fluoride gels have been used to prevent decalcification in orthodontic patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stannous fluoride gels have been used to protect against postirradiation caries.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Desensitizing Dentin

Topically to desensitize exposed root surfaces of teeth.<html><head></head><body><sup>f</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Bone Diseases

Has been used to increase bone density and relieve bone pain in the treatment of various metabolic and neoplastic bone diseases†.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Has been used in conjunction with calcium and vitamin D or calcium and estrogen to reduce skeletal fracture rates in osteoporosis†; further study is needed.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Has been used alone or in conjunction with calcium carbonate as an adjunct in the management of bone lesions in multiple myeloma†.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Has been used to reduce bone pain in metastatic prostatic carcinoma†.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Has been reported to stabilize the progression of hearing loss in a limited number of patients with otosclerosis†.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Has been used to increase spinal (but not femoral neck) bone mineral density (BMD) in corticosteroid-induced osteoporosis†;<html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html> however, further studies are needed and other therapies are preferred.<html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html>
