Uses for Procyclidine

Parkinsonian Syndrome

Adjunctive treatment of all forms of parkinsonian syndrome.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

May be used as monotherapy for mild to moderate symptoms or in combination with other antiparkinsonian agents for severe symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Relieves muscle rigidity better than it does tremor; however, tremor, fatigue, weakness, and sluggishness frequently are relieved.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Drug-Induced Extrapyramidal Reactions

Control of extrapyramidal reactions induced by antipsychotic agents (e.g., phenothiazines).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Procyclidine Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally, preferably after meals.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as procyclidine hydrochloride; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Adjust dosage carefully according to individual requirements and response.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Younger and postencephalitic patients require and tolerate a higher dosage than geriatric patients or those with arteriosclerosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Initially, 2.5 mg 3 times daily after meals.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> As tolerated, gradually increase to 5 mg 3 times daily or the minimum dosage needed to control symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> If needed, administer an additional 5-mg dose at bedtime.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

If bedtime dosage is not tolerated, total daily dosage may be administered in 3 divided doses.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Gradually substitute 2.5 mg 3 times daily for all or part of original drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Increase procyclidine dose as needed while decreasing other drug until complete replacement achieved. <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Initially, 2.5 mg 3 times daily; increase by 2.5-mg increments until symptoms controlled.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Usual dosage: 10–20 mg daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations at this time.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Procyclidine

Contraindications

  • Angle-closure glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

  • Known hypersensitivity to procyclidine or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Angle-closure glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to procyclidine or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Safe use during pregnancy has not been established.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Caution in tachyarrhythmias because antimuscarinics block vagal inhibition of the SA nodal pacemaker.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Possible decreased tone and amplitude of contractions of the ureters and bladder with resultant urinary retention;<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> use with caution in patients with prostatic hypertrophy.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Use with caution and carefully monitor hypotensive patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Possible mental confusion, disorientation, agitation, hallucinations, and psychotic-like symptoms, particularly in older patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Possible increased ocular tension.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Contraindicated in patients with angle-closure glaucoma; however, can be administered safely to patients with open-angle glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> (See Warnings under Cautions.)

Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Possible increased incidence of adverse CNS effects compared with younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See CNS Effects under Cautions.)

Common Adverse Effects

Dry mouth, blurred vision, nausea, vomiting, epigastric distress, constipation, lightheadedness, muscular weakness.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Exhibits atropine-like action and exerts antispasmodic effects on parasympathetic-innervated peripheral structures including smooth muscle.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Potential for drug to impair mental alertness or physical coordination; use caution when driving or operating machinery until effects on individual are known.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and dietary or herbal supplements, as well as concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information. (See Cautions.)
