Tacrolimus (Systemic) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Incomplete and variable absorption following oral administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Absolute bioavailability of oral tacrolimus is approximately 17% in adult renal transplant patients, 22% in adult hepatic transplant patients, and 31% in pediatric hepatic transplant patients 0.7–13.2 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Food can reduce both the rate and extent of oral absorption of tacrolimus capsules; effect is most pronounced with high-fat meals (e.g., 400 kcal, 34% fat).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Lack of direct correlation between blood tacrolimus concentrations and efficacy; however, high trough blood concentrations are possibly associated with increasing incidence of adverse effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Typical whole blood trough tacrolimus concentrations for hepatic transplant patients: 5–20 ng/mL (for months 1–12 posttransplant); long term, posttransplant patients are maintained at the lower end of this range.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Typical whole blood trough tacrolimus concentrations for renal transplant patients: 7–20 ng/mL (for months 1–3 posttransplant) and 5–15 ng/mL (for months 4–12 posttransplant).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Crosses the placenta and is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Average whole blood-to-plasma concentration ratio is 35 (range: 12–67).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Approximately 99% (principally albumin and α-1-acid glycoprotein).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Extensively metabolized by CYP enzymes (principally CYP3A) to several metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Activity of tacrolimus is related principally to the parent drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Eliminated principally in feces (92%).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following IV administration, elimination half-life of approximately 34 hours in healthy individuals, 19 hours in renal transplant patients, and 12 hours in hepatic transplant patients (including those 0.7–13.2 years of age).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In healthy individuals, half-life values are similar after IV and oral administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In patients with severe hepatic impairment (mean Child-Pugh score >10), mean clearance is substantially decreased;<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> clearance is not substantially altered in those with mild hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

In patients with renal impairment, clearance is similar to that in healthy individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
