Uses for Flurbiprofen (EENT)

Inhibition of Intraoperative Miosis

Prophylactically before ocular surgery (e.g., cataract extraction)<html><head></head><body><sup>74</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> to prevent or reduce intraoperative miosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>74</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html>

Postoperative Ocular Inflammation

Has been used for prevention and management of postoperative ocular inflammation† associated with argon laser trabeculoplasty and cyclocryotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>75</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>78</sup></body></html>

Cystoid Macular Edema

Has been used for prevention of postoperative cystoid macular edema† associated with cataract extraction.<html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>120</sup></body></html>

Inhibition of Corneal Neovascularization

Has been reported to inhibit corneal neovascularization† induced by chemical or thermal burns or prolonged use of contact lenses in preliminary research in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>79</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>80</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>81</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>121</sup></body></html>

Flurbiprofen (EENT) Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>74</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of the solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>94</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as flurbiprofen sodium; dosage expressed in terms of flurbiprofen sodium.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>113</sup></body></html>

1 drop of 0.03% solution into the eye(s) undergoing surgery beginning 2 hours before the surgery; repeat at approximately 30-minute intervals for a total of 4 drops per affected eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Flurbiprofen (EENT)

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to flurbiprofen sodium or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

May inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>30</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>33</sup></body></html>

May cause increased bleeding of ocular tissues (including hyphemas) when used in conjunction with ocular surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with underlying bleeding tendencies or in those receiving drugs known to prolong bleeding time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

Possible cross-sensitivity with aspirin and other NSAIAs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Use with caution in patients with history of hypersensitivity to these drugs (severe, nearly fatal anaphylactic reaction to oral flurbiprofen reported)<html><head></head><body><sup>86</sup></body></html> and in whom asthma, rhinitis, or urticaria is precipitated by aspirin or other NSAIAs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>
