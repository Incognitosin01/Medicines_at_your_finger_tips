Introduction

Bronchodilator; a synthetic tertiary amine long-acting antimuscarinic agent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Uses for Revefenacin

COPD

Long-term maintenance treatment of reversible bronchospasm associated with COPD, including chronic bronchitis and emphysema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Not indicated for treatment of acute episodes of bronchospasm (i.e., as rescue therapy) or acute exacerbations of COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Revefenacin Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer by oral inhalation via nebulization only using a standard jet nebulizer (with mouthpiece) connected to an air compressor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer once daily at the same time every day.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Store single-dose vials of revefenacin oral inhalation solution in the foil pouch provided; remove immediately before use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For specific information on administration of the oral inhalation solution via nebulization, consult the manufacturer’s instructions for use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In clinical trials, a Pari LC Sprint nebulizer with a Pari Trek S compressor was used to deliver revefenacin oral inhalation solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Safety and efficacy of the drug delivered from a nebulizer system without a compressor not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not mix with other inhalation solutions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not administer inhalation solution orally or by injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Each 3-mL single-dose vial of revefenacin oral inhalation solution contains 175 mcg of revefenacin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dilution not required prior to administration by nebulization.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Amount of drug delivered to the lungs depends on patient factors, type of nebulization system used, and compressor performance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

175 mcg (entire contents of 1 single-dose vial) once daily via nebulization.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Contraindications

Warnings/Precautions

Immediate hypersensitivity reactions may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If such a reaction occurs, discontinue drug immediately and consider alternative therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not initiate in patients with acutely deteriorating or substantially worsening COPD, which may be life-threatening.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Indicated only for maintenance treatment of bronchospasm associated with COPD; do not use for treatment of acute bronchospasm (i.e., as rescue therapy).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Treat acute symptoms with an inhaled, short-acting β2-agonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Failure to respond to a previously effective dosage of revefenacin or to a supplemental short-acting, inhaled β2-agonist may indicate deterioration of COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Immediately reevaluate the patient and treatment regimen.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not increase revefenacin dosage in such situations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible life-threatening paradoxical bronchospasm may occur upon inhalation of revefenacin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If paradoxical bronchospasm occurs, discontinue drug immediately and consider alternative therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with narrow-angle glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May worsen signs and/or symptoms of narrow-angle glaucoma (e.g., ocular pain or discomfort, blurred vision, visual halos, colored images in association with red eyes from conjunctival congestion and corneal edema).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Use with caution in patients with urinary retention.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May worsen signs and symptoms of urinary retention (e.g., difficulty passing urine, dysuria) associated with prostatic hyperplasia or bladder neck obstruction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

No effect on QT interval observed in healthy individuals following single dose of revefenacin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No adequate and well-controlled studies in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Advise women to contact their clinician if they become pregnant while using revefenacin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In animal reproduction studies, subcutaneous administration of the drug to pregnant rats and rabbits during the period of organogenesis produced no evidence of fetal harm at approximately 209 and 694 times the exposure at the maximum recommended human dose, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Placental transfer of revefenacin and its active metabolite observed in pregnant rabbits.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distributed into milk in rats; not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Data not available on effects of the drug on the breast-fed child or on milk production.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in safety and efficacy relative to younger adults, but increased sensitivity cannot be ruled out.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Revefenacin

Revefenacin is a substrate of P-glycoprotein (P-gp) and breast cancer resistance protein (BCRP).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Revefenacin and its active metabolite are not inhibitors of P-gp or BCRP.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Revefenacin and its active metabolite do not inhibit CYP1A2, 2B6, 2C8, 2C9, 2C19, 2D6, or 3A4/5 and do not induce CYP1A2, 2B6, or 3A4/5.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

The active metabolite of revefenacin is a substrate of organic anion transporting polypeptide (OATP) 1B1 and OATP1B3.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Inhibitors of OATP1B1 and OATP1B3 may increase systemic exposure of the active metabolite; concomitant use with revefenacin not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Revefenacin and its active metabolite are not inhibitors of uptake membrane transporters, including OATP1B1, OATP1B3, organic anion transporter (OAT)1, OAT3, or organic cation transporter (OCT)2.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Revefenacin Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following oral administration, absolute bioavailability is <3%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Following oral inhalation, peak plasma concentrations of revefenacin and its active metabolite reached within 14–41 minutes after initiation of nebulization.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following repeated dosing of orally inhaled revefenacin, steady-state concentrations achieved within 7 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Peak FEV1 effect: 2–3 hours post oral inhalation dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Distributed into milk in rats; not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In vitro protein binding of drug and its active metabolite: 71 and 42%, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized principally via hydrolysis of the primary amide to a carboxylic acid forming its major active metabolite.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Conversion to active metabolite occurs rapidly; plasma exposures of active metabolite exceed those of revefenacin by approximately 4- to 6-fold (based on AUC).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Active metabolite formed by hepatic metabolism possesses activity at target muscarinic receptors that is lower (approximately one-third to one-tenth) than that of revefenacin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following IV administration, 54% of drug recovered in feces and 27% in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Approximately 19% recovered in feces as active metabolite.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following single radiolabeled oral dose, 88% recovered in feces and <5% in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following oral inhalation, minimal renal excretion (<1%) of drug and its active metabolite in patients with COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following once-daily oral inhalation in patients with COPD, terminal half-life of drug and active metabolite is 22–70 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Moderate hepatic impairment: No increase in peak concentrations of revefenacin and a 1.5-fold increase in peak concentrations of its active metabolite.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> AUCs of revefenacin and its active metabolite increased 1.2- and up to 4.7-fold, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Severe hepatic impairment: Not evaluated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Severe renal impairment (Clcr <30 mL/minute): For revefenacin, 1.5- and 2.3-fold increase in peak concentrations and AUC, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For the active metabolite, up to 2- and 2.5-fold increase in peak concentrations and AUC, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

End-stage renal disease: Not evaluated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No clinically important differences in exposure based on age, gender, weight, and smoking status.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of adequate understanding of proper storage and inhalation techniques, including use of a standard jet nebulizer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
