Uses for Ipratropium (EENT)

Common Cold

Symptomatic relief of rhinorrhea associated with the common cold in adults and children ≥5 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Does not relieve nasal congestion or sneezing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Seasonal Allergic Rhinitis

Symptomatic relief of rhinorrhea associated with seasonal allergic rhinitis in adults and children ≥5 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Does not relieve nasal congestion, sneezing, or itching eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Perennial Rhinitis

Symptomatic relief of rhinorrhea associated with allergic and nonallergic perennial rhinitis in adults and children ≥6 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Does not relieve nasal congestion, sneezing, or postnasal drip.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Ipratropium (EENT) Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer in each nostril 2–4 times daily using spray pump supplied by the manufacturer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Prime pump before first dose with 7 sprays; if used regularly, no further priming required.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> If not used for >24 hours, reprime with 2 sprays; if not used for >7 days, reprime with 7 sprays.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Intranasal spray pump delivers 0.07 mL of solution per actuation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Ipratropium bromide 0.03% nasal spray delivers 21 mcg of ipratropium bromide per spray and about 345 sprays per bottle.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Ipratropium bromide 0.06% nasal spray delivers 42 mcg of ipratropium bromide per spray and about 165 sprays per bottle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children 5–11 years of age: 84 mcg (2 sprays of the 0.06% solution) in each nostril 3 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children ≥12 years of age: 84 mcg (2 sprays of the 0.06% solution) in each nostril 3 or 4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children ≥5 years of age: 84 mcg (2 sprays of the 0.06% solution) in each nostril 4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children ≥6 years of age: 42 mcg (2 sprays of the 0.03% solution) in each nostril 2 or 3 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

84 mcg (2 sprays of the 0.06% solution) in each nostril 3 or 4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

84 mcg (2 sprays of the 0.06% solution) in each nostril 4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

42 mcg (2 sprays of the 0.03% solution) in each nostril 2 or 3 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Ipratropium (EENT)

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to ipratropium or any ingredient in the formulation, or to atropine or its derivatives.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to ipratropium or any ingredient in the formulation, or to atropine or its derivatives.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Immediate hypersensitivity reactions, including urticaria, angioedema, rash, bronchospasm, and oropharyngeal edema, may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Caution in patients with angle-closure glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Ocular pain or precipitation or worsening of angle-closure glaucoma reported following inadvertent contact of the drug with the eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Caution in patients with prostatic hypertrophy or bladder neck obstruction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Category B.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Not known whether ipratropium is distributed into milk following intranasal administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Caution advised.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Safety established based on studies in children.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Efficacy based on extrapolation from studies in adults with perennial rhinitis and adults and adolescents with common cold or seasonal allergic rhinitis and on the likelihood that the disease course, pathophysiology, and the drug’s effect in children are similar to those in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy of 0.03% nasal spray not established in children <6 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy of 0.06% nasal spray not established in children <5 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Nasal dryness, epistaxis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Ipratropium (EENT)

Limited systemic absorption of intranasal ipratropium minimizes potential for interactions with systemically administered drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Antimuscarinic Agents

Potential for additive pharmacologic effect with other antimuscarinic agents, including orally inhaled ipratropium.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Ipratropium (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

<20% following intranasal administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Common cold: 1 hour.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Perennial rhinitis: 1 day.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Distribution

0–9% (albumin and α1-acid glycoprotein).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized to ester hydrolysis products (tropic acid and tropane).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Following IV administration, approximately 50% of dose excreted in urine as unchanged drug within 24 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Following intranasal administration at recommended dosages, mean percentage of dose excreted in urine as unchanged drug generally <10%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

1.6 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

15–30°C; avoid freezing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Nonselective competitive antagonist at muscarinic receptors.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • When applied locally, inhibits secretions from serous and seromucous glands lining the nasal mucosa.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Ipratropium nasal spray has not produced appreciable changes in pupillary diameter, heart rate, or blood pressure in children or adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Ipratropium nasal spray does not alter physiologic nasal functions such as sense of smell, ciliary beat frequency, mucociliary clearance, or air conditioning capacity of nose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Provide careful instruction on priming the pump and administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>
