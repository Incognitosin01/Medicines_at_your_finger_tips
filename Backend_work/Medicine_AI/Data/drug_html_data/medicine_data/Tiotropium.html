Uses for Tiotropium

COPD

Tiotropium alone (as oral inhalation powder or solution) or tiotropium/olodaterol fixed-combination therapy (as oral inhalation solution): Long-term maintenance treatment of reversible bronchospasm associated with COPD, including chronic bronchitis and emphysema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html>

Tiotropium alone (as oral inhalation powder or solution): Reduction of COPD exacerbations in patients with a history of exacerbations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html>

A long-acting bronchodilator (e.g., orally inhaled salmeterol, formoterol, tiotropium) or an inhaled corticosteroid recommended for maintenance monotherapy in patients with moderate to severe COPD (e.g., FEV1 30 to <80% of predicted<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> or, alternatively, <60% of predicted)<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> who have persistent symptoms not relieved by as-needed therapy with a selective, short-acting inhaled β2-adrenergic agonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> Maintenance therapy with long-acting bronchodilators in such patients more effective and more convenient than regular therapy with short-acting bronchodilators.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> Insufficient data to favor one maintenance monotherapy over another in patients with moderate to severe COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> In selected patients with inadequate response, may use a combination of several long-acting bronchodilators, such as tiotropium, and a long-acting β2-adrenergic agonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

In patients with severe to very severe COPD (e.g., FEV1 <30 to <50% of predicted), some clinicians recommend addition of an inhaled corticosteroid to one or more long-acting bronchodilators, given separately or in fixed combination;<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> however, benefits of combination therapy over monotherapy not consistently established.<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> If inadequate response or limiting adverse effects occur, may consider the addition or substitution of extended-release oral theophylline.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Tiotropium alone (as oral inhalation powder or solution) or tiotropium/olodaterol fixed-combination therapy (as oral inhalation solution): Not indicated for initial treatment of acute episodes of bronchospasm or acute deterioration of COPD;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> a drug with a more rapid onset of action (e.g., a short-acting β-adrenergic agonist) preferred.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Asthma

Tiotropium alone (as oral inhalation solution): Long-term maintenance treatment of reversible bronchospasm associated with asthma.<html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html>

Tiotropium oral inhalation solution: Not indicated for treatment of acute episodes of bronchospasm.<html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html>

Tiotropium/olodaterol fixed-combination therapy: Not indicated for treatment of asthma.<html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html>

Tiotropium Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Oral inhalation powder: Following inhalation, absolute bioavailability is 19.5%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Most of a dose is swallowed<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> and minimally absorbed into systemic circulation;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> the fraction reaching the lungs appears to be readily absorbed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> Peak plasma concentrations following oral inhalation attained within 7 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Steady state achieved after 7 days of daily administration, with no accumulation thereafter, in patients with COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
