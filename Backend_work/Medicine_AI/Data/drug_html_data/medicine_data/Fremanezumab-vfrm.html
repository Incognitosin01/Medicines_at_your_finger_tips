Introduction

Antimigraine agent; recombinant fully humanized IgG2a monoclonal antibody that inhibits calcitonin gene-related peptide (CGRP) receptor function.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Uses for Fremanezumab-vfrm

Preventive Treatment of Migraine

Preventive treatment of migraine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Substantially reduces the monthly average number of migraine or headache days and acute antimigraine agent- or headache medication-use days in patients with episodic or chronic migraine compared with placebo.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

The American Headache Society (AHS) states that fremanezumab and other CGRP antagonists offer a number of advantages over some oral migraine preventive therapies, including no need for dosage escalation, rapid onset of therapeutic activity, demonstrated efficacy in patients in whom prior preventive treatments have failed as well as in those concurrently receiving oral preventive treatments, minimal risk of adverse drug interactions, and favorable overall tolerability profiles.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> However, because of the relatively high cost of CGRP antagonists, AHS recommends that CGRP antagonists be considered for use only in patients who are unable to tolerate oral preventive therapies and/or who have had an inadequate response to a 6-week trial of ≥2 oral preventive therapies (e.g., topiramate, valproate, β-adrenergic blocking agents, tricyclic antidepressants, SNRIs).<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Fremanezumab-vfrm Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer by sub-Q injection only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Commercially available in single-use prefilled syringes containing 225 mg of the drug in 1.5 mL of solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May be administered by a healthcare professional or caregiver or self-administered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer by sub-Q injection into the abdomen, anterior thigh, or back of upper arm; avoid injections within 2 inches of the navel, knee, or groin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May administer multiple injections of the drug (i.e., to achieve a 675-mg dose) at same body site but not at exact location of previous injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not administer concomitantly with other parenteral drugs at the same injection site.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid injection into areas where the skin is tender, bruised, erythematous, or indurated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to use, provide patients and/or caregivers with proper training on how to prepare and administer fremanezumab-vfrm using the prefilled syringes, including aseptic technique.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consult manufacturer's labeling for detailed instructions regarding sub-Q administration using the prefilled syringes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to sub-Q administration, remove prefilled syringe(s) from the refrigerator and allow to sit at room temperature for 30 minutes; protect from exposure to direct sunlight.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not warm syringes using a heat source (e.g., microwave, hot water).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not use the prefilled syringe if the solution is cloudy or discolored or contains particles.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prefilled syringes are intended for single use only; discard after use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

225 mg once monthly or 675 mg every 3 months (quarterly).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Administer 675-mg doses as 3 consecutive injections of 225 mg each.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Administration under Dosage and Administration.) Gradual dosage titration not necessary; may initiate therapy with either the 225-mg monthly dose or 675-mg quarterly dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

When switching dosage regimens, administer the first dose of the new dosage regimen on the next scheduled date of administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a dose is missed, administer the missed dose as soon as possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May then schedule subsequent doses once monthly (225-mg doses) or every 3 months (675-mg doses) from the date of the last administered dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

When initiating therapy with fremanezumab or another CGRP antagonist in patients already receiving a preventive treatment for migraine, AHS recommends adding the CGRP antagonist to the existing antimigraine regimen and avoiding making other changes until the clinical efficacy of the CGRP antagonist is determined.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Patients who respond to CGRP antagonist therapy usually respond following the first 3 injections.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> Therefore, AHS recommends assessing clinical efficacy of fremanezumab after 3 months (if using the monthly dosage regimen) or 6 months (if using the quarterly dosage regimen) of treatment; continue therapy only if treatment benefits have been observed by that time.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Fremanezumab-vfrm

Contraindications

Warnings/Precautions

Hypersensitivity reactions, including rash, pruritus, drug hypersensitivity, and urticaria, reported during clinical trials.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Most reactions were mild to moderate in severity; however, some resulted in drug discontinuance or required corticosteroid therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Most reactions occur within hours to 1 month after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a hypersensitivity reaction occurs, consider discontinuing the drug and initiate appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications under Cautions and see also Advice to Patients.)

Manufacturer states that the prefilled syringe cap for fremanezumab-vfrm injection is not made with natural rubber latex.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potential for immunogenicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Antibodies to fremanezumab, including neutralizing antibodies to the drug, reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Available data do not demonstrate an effect of anti-fremanezumab antibody development on efficacy or safety of the drug; however, data are too limited to make definitive conclusions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No adequate data on the developmental risk associated with use of fremanezumab in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider long half-life of the drug if used in pregnant women or those planning to become pregnant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No adverse developmental effects observed when pregnant rats and rabbits received fremanezumab sub-Q at dosages resulting in systemic exposures up to approximately 2–3 times the exposure from the maximum recommended human dosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Estimated rates of major birth defects and miscarriage among deliveries to women with migraine (2.2–2.9 and 17%, respectively) are similar to rates reported in women without migraine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Possible increased risk of preeclampsia in women with migraine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Effects on the breast-fed infant and on milk production also unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider developmental and health benefits of breast-feeding, mother's clinical need for fremanezumab, and potential adverse effects on the breast-fed infant from the drug or underlying maternal condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states that safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pending further clinical experience with CGRP antagonists in pediatric patients, the Pediatric and Adolescent Headache special interest group of the AHS recommends limiting use of CGRP antagonists (e.g., erenumab, fremanezumab, galcanezumab) mainly to postpubertal adolescents† with relatively frequent migraines (i.e., ≥8 headache days per month) who have moderate to severe disability associated with migraine (e.g., PedMIDAS score ≥30) and in whom ≥2 preventive therapies have failed.<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> For younger pediatric patients† with severe chronic migraine that is refractory to multiple preventive therapies, consider CGRP antagonists only in carefully selected patients with close monitoring (e.g., pubertal status, bone health, linear growth, weight, body mass index [BMI], infectious complications).<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Hepatic impairment not expected to substantially affect pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> (See Special Populations under Pharmacokinetics.)

Renal impairment not expected to substantially affect pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> (See Special Populations under Pharmacokinetics.)

Fremanezumab-vfrm Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Median time to peak concentrations is 5–7 days following a single sub-Q dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Steady-state concentrations achieved in approximately 6 months following sub-Q administration of 225 mg monthly or 675 mg quarterly.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Median systemic accumulation is approximately 2.3- or 1.2-fold following 225-mg once-monthly or 675-mg once-quarterly dosage regimens, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Exhibits dose-proportional pharmacokinetics over a sub-Q dose range of 225–900 mg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics in patients with migraine are similar to those in healthy individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Antimigraine effects of CGRP antagonists, including fremanezumab, usually evident following 1–3 monthly sub-Q injections or 1–2 quarterly sub-Q injections in responding patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>
