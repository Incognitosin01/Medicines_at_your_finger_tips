Uses for Factor IX (Recombinant), Fc fusion protein

Hemophilia B

On-demand treatment and control of hemorrhagic episodes in patients with hemophilia B (congenital factor IX deficiency, or Christmas disease).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>156</sup></body></html> Designated an orphan drug by FDA for such use.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Maintenance of hemostasis in patients with hemophilia B undergoing surgery (i.e., perioperative management of bleeding).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Routine prophylaxis (i.e., administration at regular intervals on an ongoing basis) to reduce frequency of hemorrhagic events.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Such prophylactic therapy with factor IX concentrates considered the current standard of care for patients with hemophilia B.<html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>176</sup></body></html> Decreases frequency of spontaneous musculoskeletal hemorrhage, preserves joint function, and improves quality of life.<html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>185</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>186</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Dose (potency) expressed in terms of international units (IU, units) of factor IX activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Potency determined by an aPTT-based one-stage clotting assay calibrated against a WHO standard.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> Administration of 1 unit/kg of factor IX (recombinant), Fc fusion protein increases factor IX levels by approximately 1%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Estimate dose required to achieve a particular percentage increase in plasma factor IX level using the following formula:

Dose (units) = body weight (in kg) × desired factor IX increase (in units/dL or % of normal) x reciprocal of recovery (in units/kg per units/dL)<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Factor IX (Recombinant), Fc fusion protein

Contraindications

  • Known history of hypersensitivity (e.g., anaphylaxis) to factor IX (recombinant), Fc fusion protein or other components of the preparation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk for development of inhibitors (neutralizing antibodies) to factor IX following treatment with any factor IX preparation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> Reported in less than 5% of patients with hemophilia B receiving factor IX concentrates;<html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> not reported in principal efficacy study with factor IX (recombinant), Fc fusion protein.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients regularly for development of inhibitors with appropriate clinical observation and laboratory tests.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> (See Laboratory Monitoring under Cautions.) Suspect presence of inhibitors if expected factor IX levels not achieved or bleeding not controlled with recommended dose, particularly in those who previously achieved a response.<html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html>

Because of an association between inhibitor development and allergic reactions, evaluate for presence of inhibitors in any patient experiencing a hypersensitivity reaction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Hypersensitivity Reactions under Cautions.) Increased risk of anaphylaxis following re-exposure to factor IX (recombinant), Fc fusion protein in patients with inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consultation with a hemophilia treatment center strongly recommended for patients with inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html>

Thromboembolic complications reported with use of factor IX products, particularly when administered by continuous IV infusion through a central venous catheter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Administer factor IX (recombinant), Fc fusion protein by direct IV (“bolus”) injection only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Rate of Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

Monitor factor IX levels (using aPTT-based one-stage clotting assay) to guide dosing and assess therapeutic response.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> Results can vary based on type of aPTT reagent; a kaolin-based reagent is likely to result in an underestimation of factor IX activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Monitor for development of inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Perform appropriate laboratory test (i.e., Bethesda assay) to confirm presence of an inhibitor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> (See Development of Inhibitors to Factor IX under Cautions.)

Risk of hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylaxis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Closely observe patient for early signs and symptoms of hypersensitivity (e.g., angioedema, chest tightness, hypotension, rash, nausea, vomiting, paresthesia, restlessness, dyspnea, wheezing, urticaria, and pruritus).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If a hypersensitivity reaction occurs, immediately discontinue drug and initiate appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Factor IX (Recombinant), Fc fusion protein Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Exhibits dose-proportional pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Rapid onset of action with peak plasma concentrations attained immediately after a dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Mean recovery of factor IX approximately 1 unit/dL for each 1 unit/kg of factor IX (recombinant), Fc fusion protein administered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Compared with adults, patients <12 years of age appear to have lower incremental recovery of factor IX.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In one study, incremental recovery was approximately 0.6, 0.7, or 0.9 units/dL per each unit/kg administered in pediatric patients 2–5 years, 6–11 years, or 12–17 years of age, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether factor IX (recombinant), Fc fusion protein is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Following administration of a single 50-unit/kg dose in patients with hemophilia B, mean terminal half-life was 86.52 hours; average time to reach 1% factor IX activity was 11.5 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Compared with adults, patients <12 years of age appear to have higher body weight-adjusted clearance and shorter half-life.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In one study, half-life was approximately 66, 72, or 84 hours in patients 2–5 years, 6–11 years, or 12–17 years of age, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In a comparative pharmacokinetic analysis, terminal half-life of factor IX (recombinant), Fc fusion protein was substantially longer than that of a conventional recombinant factor IX preparation (82 versus 33.8 hours).<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>
