Gonadotropin, Chorionic Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following single injection in hypogonadotropic men, onset and peak stimulation of testosterone production (as indicated by plasma testosterone concentrations) occur at 24 and 80 hours, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Following single injection in prepubertal and early pubertal boys with cryptorchidism, peak stimulation of testosterone production occurs at 2–5 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>kk</sup></body></html>

Following midcycle administration, stimulation of luteal-phase progesterone production (as indicated by serum progesterone concentrations) persists for approximately 1 week.<html><head></head><body><sup>n</sup></body></html> (See Actions.)

Following single injection in prepubertal boys with cryptorchidism, stimulation of testosterone production persists for ≥6 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>kk</sup></body></html>

Bioavailability is less in obese infertile women than in nonobese women.<html><head></head><body><sup>u</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Distributed mainly into testes and ovaries; smaller amounts may be distributed into proximal tubules of renal cortex.<html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

Not known if distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Extensively metabolized, principally in liver and kidneys, to active metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Excreted in urine (20%) as metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Biphasic; terminal half-life is about 23–33 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>u</sup></body></html>
