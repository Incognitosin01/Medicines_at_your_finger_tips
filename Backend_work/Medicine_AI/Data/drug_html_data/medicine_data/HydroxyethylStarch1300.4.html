Introduction

Third-generation hydroxyethyl starch (HES) (tetrastarch); a nonprotein synthetic colloid used as a plasma volume expander.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html>

Uses for Hydroxyethyl Starch 130/0.4

Hypovolemia

Prevention or treatment of hypovolemia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not a substitute for RBCs or coagulation factors in plasma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Comparable with 6% hetastarch in 0.9% sodium chloride injection as a plasma volume expander in patients undergoing major elective orthopedic surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Provides no mortality benefit over normal saline (0.9% sodium chloride injection) when used in patients admitted to the intensive care unit (ICU) and may be associated with an increased risk of severe renal injury (i.e., requirement for renal replacement therapy).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Contraindicated in critically ill adults, including those with sepsis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications and also see Increased Mortality and Severe Renal Injury under Cautions.)

Warnings/Precautions

Increased mortality and/or severe renal injury requiring renal replacement therapy reported in critically ill adults, including patients with sepsis, receiving various HES preparations.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Occurred within the recommended dosage range (30–90 g [500–1500 mL] daily).<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Considered by FDA to be class effects of HES.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

HES solutions are contraindicated in critically ill adults, including those with sepsis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Avoid use in patients with preexisting renal dysfunction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Discontinue use at first sign of renal injury.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Monitor renal function for >90 days in hospitalized patients receiving HES.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylactic and anaphylactoid reactions (e.g., mild influenza-like symptoms, bradycardia, tachycardia, bronchospasm, noncardiac pulmonary edema), reported very rarely.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a hypersensitivity reaction occurs, discontinue therapy immediately; initiate appropriate treatment and supportive measures and continue until symptoms have resolved.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Hemodilution, resulting in dilution of blood components (e.g., coagulation factors, other plasma proteins) and decreased hematocrit, reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Hematologic Effects and Coagulopathy under Cautions.) Avoid fluid overload.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cardiac Insufficiency under Dosage and Administration.)

Administration of high dosages may result in decreased concentrations of coagulation factors, other plasma proteins, and hematocrit.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Depending on dosage administered, disturbances of blood coagulation beyond hemodilution effects also can occur rarely.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor coagulation parameters in patients receiving prolonged therapy or as clinically indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increased bleeding reported in patients undergoing open heart surgery with cardiopulmonary bypass who received HES.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Excess bleeding occurred irrespective of molecular weight or molar substitution of the HES preparation, and, therefore, is considered by FDA to be a class effect of HES.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Monitor coagulation status of patients undergoing open heart surgery in association with cardiopulmonary bypass.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Discontinue HES at first sign of coagulopathy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

Transient elevations in serum amylase concentrations may occur; such elevations may obscure diagnosis of pancreatitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Hypotension, shock, and circulatory collapse reported very rarely.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Causal relationship to drug not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor fluid balance, electrolyte concentrations, renal function, acid-base balance, and coagulation parameters in patients receiving prolonged therapy or as clinically indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor liver function.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

Hydroxyethyl Starch 130/0.4 Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

No substantial plasma accumulation observed following daily administration of 500 mL of a 10% solution containing hydroxyethyl starch 130/0.4 (not commercially available in the US<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> ) for 10 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Plasma volume expansion maintained for at least 6 hours following IV infusion of a 500-mL dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Renal impairment: In patients with Clcr <50 mL/minute, AUC increased by 73% compared with patients with Clcr >50 mL/minute; peak concentration unaffected by renal impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Patients undergoing hemodialysis: No data available.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetic data not available for patients with hepatic impairment, pediatric patients, or geriatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Elimination

Following administration of single or multiple 500-mL doses, approximately 62 or 70% of the dose, respectively, is excreted in urine as hydroxyethyl starch within 72 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
