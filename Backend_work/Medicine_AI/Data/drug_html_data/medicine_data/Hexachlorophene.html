Uses for Hexachlorophene

Surgical Hand Antisepsis

Used by health-care personnel as a preoperative hand scrub to prevent spreading of cutaneous microorganisms from hands and forearms during surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Antiseptic Skin Cleanser

Used topically as a disinfectant by health-care personnel, food handlers, and other individuals who are in a position to spread infection from their hands.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Used topically to control cutaneous gram-positive infections where other infection control procedures have been unsuccessful.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Use only as long as necessary for infection control.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Not indicated for bathing infants as prophylaxis against staphylococcal infections because of serious adverse effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See Pediatric Use under Cautions.)

Cautions for Hexachlorophene

Contraindications

  • Application to burned or denuded skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Use as an occlusive dressing, wet pack, or lotion.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Routine prophylactic total body bathing.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Use as a vaginal pack or tampon, or on any mucous membranes.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Known primary light sensitivity to halogenated phenol derivatives. <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Cross-hypersensitivity under Cautions.)

  • Known hypersensitivity to hexachlorophene or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Possibility of rapid and extensive absorption of hexachlorophene following topical application to generalized dermatologic conditions (e.g., lesions of ichthyosis congenita, dermatitis of Letterer-Siwe syndrome) or burns; may result in toxic serum concentrations and CNS toxicity including potentially fatal neurotoxicity (e.g., CNS stimulation, irritation, seizures).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Rinse thoroughly after each use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Monitor closely and discontinue promptly if signs or symptoms of cerebral irritability occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Possible cross-sensitivity of hexachlorophene and halogenated phenol derivatives; use not recommended in individuals who have demonstrated primary light sensitivity to halogenated phenol derivatives.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Avoid contact with the eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> If contact occurs, rinse thoroughly.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Apply to the head and periorbital skin areas only in responsive patients with unanesthetized eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Rinse thoroughly after use, especially from sensitive areas (e.g., scrotum, perineum).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Harmful if swallowed, especially to infants and children.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Do not pour into measuring cups, medicine bottles, or similar containers since they may be mistaken for baby formula or other medications.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Not known whether hexachlorophene is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Use not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Use with caution and only when necessary on infants.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Infants, especially premature infants or those with dermatoses, are at greater risk of absorption and subsequent systemic toxicity (i.e., CNS stimulation, convulsions).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Discontinue at the first signs of neurotoxicity (stimulation of the CNS, convulsions, clonic muscular contractions, decerebrate rigidity) or dermatitis. <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Neurotoxicity under Cautions.)

Dermatitis, irritability, generalized clonic muscular contractions, and decerebrate rigidity reported in infants following topical application of hexachlorophene.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Do not use routinely for bathing infants.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Positive correlation between hexachlorophene baths and brain lesions in premature infants.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Response in patients ≥65 years of age does not appear to differ from that in younger adults; however, use with caution due to greater frequency of dermatologic disease and peripheral circulatory disease and decreased propensity for wound healing. <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Dermatitis, photosensitivity, redness and/or mild scaling or dryness of skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Hexachlorophene Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Absorbed from GI tract and intact and denuded skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Approximately 3% of a topical dose absorbed systemically.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Rapid absorption may occur following topical application to burned or inflamed skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Accumulates on the skin during the first 3 or 4 days of repeated use; concentration on skin remains relatively constant thereafter.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following repeated daily application, residual drug is retained on skin for several days.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Residual drug may be removed by cleansing with non-hexachlorophene-containing soaps or detergents or ethanol or isopropyl alcohol.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Concentrations of ≥0.5 mcg/mL have been reported following use of a 3% hexachlorophene preparation as a surgical scrub for hands and forearms 5 times daily for 10 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Serum concentrations of ≥1 mcg/mL in animals associated with CNS toxicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See Neurotoxicity under Cautions.)

Possible greater absorption in infants, especially premature infants or those with dermatoses.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Serum concentrations of 0.009–4.35 mcg/mL have been reported in neonates bathed daily in hexachlorophene preparations for 1–56 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Hexachlorophene crosses the placenta.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Distributed into milk in rats; not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Elimination

6.1–44.2 hours in infants.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Tight, light-resistant, nonmetallic containers at ≤25°C. <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Potent bacteriostatic activity against staphylococci and other gram-positive bacteria.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Exact mechanism(s) of action unknown, but at low concentrations appears to interrupt bacterial electron transport and inhibit membrane-bound enzymes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Higher concentrations rupture bacterial membranes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Cumulative antibacterial action develops with repeated use.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Rebound bacterial growth occurs following discontinuance.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
