Uses for Poractant Alfa

Respiratory Distress Syndrome (RDS)

Treatment (rescue) of RDS (hyaline membrane disease) in premature neonates (designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Poractant Alfa Dosage and Administration

General

  • Observe clinical status and monitor systemic oxygenation frequently; decrease inspired oxygen concentrations and ventilator pressures gradually to prevent hyperoxia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Following completion of dosing procedure, resume usual ventilator management and clinical care.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not suction airways for 1 hour after dosing unless substantial obstruction occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Experience of Supervising Clinician under Cautions.)

Administration

Administer only by intratracheal instillation using specialized techniques.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consult manufacturer’s labeling or specialized references for guidelines on administration techniques.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Allow drug to reach room temperature before administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Gently invert vial to obtain a uniform suspension; do not shake.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Contains no preservatives; discard unused portion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as poractant alfa; dosage expressed in terms of phospholipids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Each mL of the commercially available formulation contains 80 mg of phospholipids (including 54 mg of phosphatidylcholine, of which 30.5 mg is dipalmitoyl phosphatidylcholine) and 1 mg of surfactant proteins (SP-B, SP-C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Premature neonates: 2.5 mL/kg (200 mg/kg) of birthweight.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer up to 2 repeat doses (1.25 mL/kg of birth weight), given at 12-hour intervals, if neonate remains intubated and respiratory manifestations of RDS persist or worsen.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

100 or 200 mg/kg, given as a single dose within 10 minutes of birth.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

Premature neonates: Total dosage (initial and repeat doses) should not exceed 5 mL/kg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Safety and efficacy not established for administration of >3 doses (1 initial and 2 repeat doses), administration more frequently than every 12 hours, and initiation of therapy >15 hours after diagnosis of RDS.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Poractant Alfa

Contraindications

Warnings/Precautions

Use only by clinicians experienced in resuscitation, stabilization, and general care of premature neonates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Therapy can rapidly affect oxygenation and lung compliance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Perform frequent clinical and laboratory assessments; modify oxygen and ventilatory support in response to respiratory changes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Transient episodes of decreased oxygen saturation reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If this occurs, discontinue administration and initiate appropriate measures to alleviate the condition; following stabilization, resume therapy and monitor appropriately.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Transient episodes of bradycardia and hypotension reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If these occur, discontinue administration and initiate appropriate measures to alleviate the condition; following stabilization, resume therapy and monitor appropriately.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Transient endotracheal tube blockage, reflux of surfactant into endotracheal tube, and airway obstruction reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If these occur, discontinue administration and initiate appropriate measures to alleviate the condition; following stabilization, resume therapy and monitor appropriately.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Correction of acidosis, hypotension, anemia, hypoglycemia, and hypothermia recommended prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Therapy expected to reduce severity of RDS but will not eliminate morbidity and mortality associated with other complications of prematurity (e.g., pneumonia, septicemia, intracranial hemorrhage, patent ductus arteriosus).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy in conjunction with investigational therapies for RDS (e.g., high-frequency ventilation) not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not intended for use in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not intended for use in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Natural porcine lung extract containing mostly phospholipids and small amounts of neutral lipids, fatty acids, and surfactant-associated proteins (SP-B, SP-C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

  • Endogenous pulmonary surfactant reduces alveolar surface tension and increases alveolar stability.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Poractant compensates for surfactant deficiency in premature neonates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Increases alveolar stability and pulmonary compliance, decreases pulmonary edema and work required for breathing, and facilitates uniform alveolar recruitment on inspiration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>
