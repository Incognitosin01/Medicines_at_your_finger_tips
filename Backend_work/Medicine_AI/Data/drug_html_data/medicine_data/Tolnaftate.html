Treatment of tinea pedis (athlete’s foot) and tinea manuum† (hand ringworm) caused by E. floccosum, M. canis, M. audouinii, T. mentagrophytes, T. rubrum, or T. tonsurans.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Available for self-medication (OTC use) for treatment of tinea corporis,<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> tinea cruris,<html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> or tinea pedis and for prevention of reinfection of tinea pedis.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html>

Topical antifungals usually effective for treatment of uncomplicated tinea corporis and tinea cruris.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html> An oral antifungal may be necessary when tinea corporis or tinea cruris is extensive, dermatophyte folliculitis is present, infection is chronic or does not respond to topical therapy, or patient is immunocompromised because of coexisting disease or concomitant therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html>

Pityriasis (Tinea) Versicolor

Tolnaftate Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the infected skin as a 1% cream, aerosol liquid spray, aerosol powder spray, powder, or solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Avoid contact with eyes, nose, mouth, and other mucous membranes.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Do not apply to scalp or nail infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html>

Clean affected areas with soap and water and dry thoroughly prior to applying preparation.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

A small amount of the cream or powder or 2 or 3 drops of the solution should be rubbed gently into affected areas.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Alternatively, a thin layer of aerosol liquid spray or aerosol spray powder should be applied.<html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Although pruritus, burning, and soreness may be relieved within 24–72 hours after initiation of therapy, complete the full course of treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

When treating tinea pedis, pay special attention to spaces between toes.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> Also, wear well-fitting, ventilated shoes and change shoes and socks at least once daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html>

Actions and Spectrum

  • Thiocarbamate antifungal structurally and pharmacologically unrelated to other commercially available antifungals.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • May be fungistatic or fungicidal against susceptible organisms.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Mechanism of antifungal activity unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Appears to distort the hyphae and stunt mycelial growth in susceptible fungi.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Dermatophytes: Active in vitro against Epidermophyton floccosum, Microsporum audouinii, M. canis, M. gypseum, M. japonicum, Trichophyton mentagrophytes, T. rubrum, T. schoenleinii, and T. tonsurans.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
