Introduction

Relatively selective histamine H1-receptor antagonist<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> with mast-cell stabilizing properties.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Uses for Epinastine

Allergic Conjunctivitis

Prevention of ocular itching associated with allergic conjunctivitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Epinastine Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not for injection or oral use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as epinastine hydrochloride; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children ≥3 years of age: 1 drop of a 0.05% solution in each eye twice daily for up to 8 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Continue therapy throughout period of exposure (i.e., until pollen season is over or until exposure to offending allergen is terminated), even in absence of symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contamination of the solution with common bacteria that can cause ocular infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Serious damage to the eye and subsequent loss of vision may result from using contaminated ophthalmic solutions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of delaying insertion of contact lenses for at least 10 minutes after epinastine instillation, since benzalkonium chloride preservative may be absorbed by soft lenses; importance of not wearing contact lenses in the presence of ocular redness.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Not indicated for contact lens-related ocular irritation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of reporting any worsening of symptoms or new-onset ocular pain/discomfort.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinician if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information. (See Cautions.)
