Introduction

Biosynthetic (recombinant DNA origin) preparation of porcine blood coagulation factor VIII.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Uses for Antihemophilic Factor (Recombinant), Porcine Sequence

Acquired Hemophilia A

Treatment and prevention of bleeding episodes in patients with acquired hemophilia A, a condition caused by the development of autoantibodies (inhibitors) to antihemophilic factor (blood coagulation factor VIII).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Because of reduced cross-reactivity (about 5–10%) with human factor VIII inhibitors, may be useful in situations where human antihemophilic factor concentrates are ineffective.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Designated an orphan drug by FDA for treatment and prevention of bleeding in patients with inhibitor antibodies to human coagulation factor VIII.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states that safety and efficacy not established in patients with baseline anti-porcine factor VIII inhibitor titers >20 Bethesda Units.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states that drug not indicated for treatment of patients with congenital hemophilia A or von Willebrand disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Antihemophilic Factor (Recombinant), Porcine Sequence Dosage and Administration

General

  • Individualize dosage and duration of therapy based on location and severity of bleeding, target factor VIII levels, and patient's clinical and pharmacokinetic (e.g., in-vivo recovery, half-life) response.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Monitor factor VIII activity (by the one-stage clotting assay) after each dose is given to ensure that adequate levels of factor VIII are attained and maintained.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Laboratory Monitoring under Cautions.) Careful control of dose is especially important in cases of life-threatening bleeding or major surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • If administered dose is ineffective in achieving expected factor VIII levels or bleeding not controlled, consider possibility that inhibitors may have developed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Development of Inhibitors to Porcine Factor VIII under Cautions.)

Administration

Administer by slow IV injection or slow IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Rate of Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

Reconstitute lyophilized powder with manufacturer-supplied prefilled diluent syringe.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May require reconstitution of more than one vial to obtain required dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to reconstitution, allow drug vial and diluent to warm to room temperature.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> After addition of diluent, gently swirl vial until powder is completely dissolved.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Resultant solution should be clear and colorless; discard if particulate matter or discoloration observed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer within 3 hours after reconstitution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not administer reconstituted solution in the same tubing or container with other drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consult manufacturer's labeling for specific instructions on reconstitution and preparation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer at a rate of 1–2 mL per minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Dosage and potency expressed in terms of international units (IU, units) of antihemophilic factor activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Potency is determined by a one-stage clotting assay calibrated using WHO standard.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer recommends initial dose of 200 units/kg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adjust subsequent dose and dosing frequency (every 4–12 hours recommended by manufacturer) based on individual clinical response and postinfusion levels of factor VIII to maintain the following target trough factor VIII levels:<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Minor or moderate bleeding (e.g., superficial muscle without neurovascular compromise, joint bleed): 50–100% of normal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Major bleeding (e.g., moderate to severe intramuscular, retroperitoneal, GI, or intracranial bleeding): 100–200% of normal to control acute bleeding episode, then maintain at 50–100% of normal if subsequent dosing required.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not exceed plasma factor VIII levels of 200% of normal (200 units/dL) at any time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Antihemophilic Factor (Recombinant), Porcine Sequence

Contraindications

  • Life-threatening hypersensitivity reactions to antihemophilic factor (recombinant), porcine sequence or any of its components (e.g., hamster protein).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Life-threatening hypersensitivity reactions to antihemophilic factor (recombinant), porcine sequence or any of its components (e.g., hamster protein).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of development of inhibitory antibodies (inhibitors) to porcine factor VIII.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Reported in approximately 26% of patients in principal efficacy study.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for development of inhibitors with appropriate clinical observation and laboratory tests.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Laboratory Monitoring under Cautions.) Suspect presence of inhibitors if patient fails to respond to adequate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider other therapeutic options if inhibitors suspected and lack of response present.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor factor VIII activity (with one-stage clotting assay) after each dose to confirm that adequate levels have been attained and maintained.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Obtain plasma factor VIII level 30 minutes and 3 hours after initial dose, and 30 minutes after each subsequent dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor for development of inhibitors to porcine factor VIII.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Perform appropriate laboratory test (i.e., modified version of Bethesda assay [Nijmegen]) to confirm presence of an inhibitor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Development of Inhibitors to Porcine Factor VIII under Cautions.)

Risk of hypersensitivity reactions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Contains trace amounts of baby hamster kidney (BHK) proteins, which may stimulate antibody production and cause hypersensitivity reactions; anti-BHK antibodies not detected in principal clinical study.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Observe for manifestations of hypersensitivity (e.g., angioedema, chest tightness, dyspnea, hypotension, wheezing, urticaria, pruritus, anaphylaxis).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If a hypersensitivity reaction occurs, immediately discontinue drug and initiate appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into human milk; use with caution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Acquired hemophilia A occurs very rarely in children.<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in efficacy and safety observed between geriatric patients ≥65 years of age and younger adults in principal clinical study; however, small number of patients in this age group precludes definitive conclusions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
