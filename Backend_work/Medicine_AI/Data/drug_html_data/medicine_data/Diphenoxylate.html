Introduction

Antiperistaltic antidiarrhea agent; synthetic phenylpiperidine-derivative opiate agonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Uses for Diphenoxylate

Diarrhea

Adjunctive therapy in the management of diarrhea.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Symptomatic treatment of mild or uncomplicated travelers’ diarrhea, including that occurring in adult travelers with HIV infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> Should not be used in travelers with severe diarrhea or with high fever or blood in the stools; these travelers may benefit from short-term treatment with an anti-infective (e.g., a fluoroquinolone).<html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html>

Ineffective for prevention of travelers’ diarrhea; may increase incidence of travelers’ diarrhea.<html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html>

Diphenoxylate Dosage and Administration

Administration

In children 2–12 years of age, administer as oral solution using only the calibrated measuring device provided by the manufacturer.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Do not administer in children <2 years of age because of the narrow range between therapeutic and toxic doses in this age group.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Discontinue if symptoms of travelers’ diarrhea persist >48 hours or worsen.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as diphenoxylate hydrochloride; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Commercially available only in combination with atropine sulfate (in subtherapeutic quantity to discourage deliberate overdosage).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Children 2–12 years of age: Initially, 0.3–0.4 mg/kg daily, given in 4 divided doses.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Diphenoxylate

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to diphenoxylate or atropine.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

  • Obstructive jaundice.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

  • Diarrhea associated with pseudomembranous enterocolitis or enterotoxin-producing bacteria.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to diphenoxylate or atropine.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Obstructive jaundice.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Diarrhea associated with pseudomembranous enterocolitis or enterotoxin-producing bacteria.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Do not use in patients with diarrhea caused by poisoning until the toxic material is eliminated from the GI tract by gastric lavage or cathartics.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Do not use in patients with high fever or blood in stools.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html>

Overdosage may result in severe respiratory depression and coma, possibly leading to permanent brain damage or death.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Adhere strictly to dosage recommendations.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Respiratory depression is possible up to 30 hours after ingestion and may recur despite initial response to an opiate antagonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Because diphenoxylate is structurally similar to meperidine, consider the possibility of hypertensive crisis if diphenoxylate and MAO inhibitors are used concomitantly.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Appropriate fluid and electrolyte replacement therapy is recommended, when indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> If severe dehydration or electrolyte imbalance is present, withhold diphenoxylate until appropriate corrective therapy has been initiated.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Drug-induced inhibition of peristalsis may result in fluid retention in the intestine, which may further aggravate dehydration and electrolyte imbalance; may mask fluid and electrolyte depletion in treatment of acute enteritis, especially in young children.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Antiperistaltic agents may prolong and/or worsen diarrhea resulting from some infections (e.g., those caused by Shigella, Salmonella, toxigenic Escherichia coli) and from pseudomembranous enterocolitis associated with broad spectrum antibiotics; do not use in these conditions.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Toxic megacolon reported with agents that inhibit intestinal motility or prolong intestinal transit time in some patients with acute ulcerative colitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Carefully observe patients with acute ulcerative colitis; discontinue promptly if abdominal distention occurs or other adverse symptoms develop.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Hepatic coma reported in patients with cirrhosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Use with extreme caution in patients with cirrhosis, advanced hepatorenal disease, or abnormal liver function tests results.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Contraindicated in patients with jaundice.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Ensure accuracy of prescription; similarity in spelling of Lomotil (fixed combination of diphenoxylate hydrochloride and atropine sulfate) and Lamictal (lamotrigine, an anticonvulsant agent) may result in errors.<html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html>

Commercially available only in combination with atropine sulfate.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Consider the cautions, precautions, and contraindications associated with atropine.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Evidence of physical dependence not reported with recommended dosages.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Possibility of dependence when given in high dosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Not recommended for children <2 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Use particular caution in young children due to greater variability of responses to the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Consider nutritional status and degree of dehydration.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> (See Fluid and Electrolyte Replacement Therapy under Cautions.)

Common Adverse Effects

Nausea, vomiting, abdominal discomfort or distention, sedation, dizziness, pruritus, anorexia, restlessness or insomnia, confusion, headache.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>
