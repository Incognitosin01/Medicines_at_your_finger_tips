Warning

    Thromboembolic Risks, Ischemic Risks, Cardiac Arrest, and Sudden Death

  • Treatment with factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo has been associated with serious and life-threatening adverse effects (e.g., arterial and venous thromboembolic events, ischemic events [MI and ischemic stroke], cardiac arrest, sudden death).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Monitor for manifestations of thromboembolic events and initiate anticoagulation when medically appropriate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Monitor for signs and symptoms that precede cardiac arrest and provide appropriate medical treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Thromboembolic Risks, Ischemic Risks, Cardiac Arrest, and Sudden Death under Cautions.)

Specific reversal agent for anticoagulant effects of apixaban or rivaroxaban; a recombinant modified human factor Xa protein.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> Also known as andexanet alfa.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Uses for Factor Xa (recombinant), Inactivated-zhzo

Reversal of Apixaban or Rivaroxaban Anticoagulation

Used for the reversal of apixaban or rivaroxaban anticoagulation in patients with life-threatening or uncontrolled bleeding; designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Accelerated approval of factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo for this indication based on the change from baseline in anti-factor Xa activity following administration in healthy individuals;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> an improvement in hemostasis has not been established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Continued FDA approval for this indication may be contingent on the results of studies demonstrating an improvement in hemostasis in patients receiving the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states that safety and efficacy of factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo not established in, and the drug is not indicated for, the treatment of bleeding related to any factor Xa inhibitor other than apixaban or rivaroxaban.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer as a direct IV (“bolus”) injection followed by a continuous infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Initiate continuous infusion within 2 minutes after the direct IV injection of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute vials containing 100 or 200 mg of factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo with 10 or 20 mL of sterile water for injection, respectively, using a 20-mL (or larger) syringe and a 20-gauge (or higher) needle to provide a solution containing 10 mg/mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Gently swirl vials (do not shake) to aid reconstitution (typical dissolution time for each vial is 3–5 minutes).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute all required vials for a dose in succession to reduce total reconstitution time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Direct IV injection: Transfer appropriate amount of solution from the reconstituted vial(s), using a 60-mL (or larger) syringe with a 20-gauge (or higher) needle, into an empty polyolefin or PVC IV bag (≤250 mL).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Continuous IV infusion: Transfer appropriate amount of solution from the reconstituted vial(s), using more than one 40- to 60-mL syringe or an equivalent 100-mL syringe with a 20-gauge (or higher) needle, into an empty polyolefin or PVC IV bag (≤250 mL).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Administer drug IV using an inline 0.2- or 0.22-μm polyethersulfone or equivalent low protein-binding filter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Direct IV injection: Target rate 30 mg/minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Continuous IV infusion low-dose regimen: 4 mg/minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Continuous IV infusion high-dose regimen: 8 mg/minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

High-dose regimen: Direct IV injection of 800 mg followed within 2 minutes by a continuous IV infusion of 8 mg/minute for up to 120 minutes (960 mg).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Life-threatening thromboembolic and ischemic complications, including sudden death, reported following administration of factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Safety of factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo not established in patients who have experienced a thromboembolic event or disseminated intravascular coagulation (DIC) within 2 weeks prior to the life-threatening bleeding event requiring treatment with the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety of factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo also not established in patients who have received prothrombin complex concentrates, recombinant factor VIIa, or whole blood products within 7 days prior to the bleeding event.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for manifestations of arterial and venous thromboembolic events, ischemic events, and cardiac arrest.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Initiate anticoagulation when medically appropriate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Provide appropriate treatment for cardiac arrest as needed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reversing effects of factor Xa inhibitor therapy increases the risk of thromboembolic events; resume anticoagulant therapy as soon as medically appropriate following treatment with factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

A rapid and substantial decrease in anti-factor Xa activity corresponding to a direct IV (“bolus”) injection dose of the drug observed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> This decrease was sustained throughout continuous IV infusion of the drug; anti-factor Xa activity returned to placebo concentrations approximately 2 hours after completion of a direct IV injection or continuous IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Thereafter, the anti-factor Xa activity decreased at a rate similar to the clearance of the factor Xa inhibitor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Tissue factor pathway inhibitor (TFPI) activity in plasma returned to pretreatment levels approximately 96 hours following factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy of repeat doses of factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potential for immunogenicity with use of all therapeutic proteins, including factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Low titers of anti-factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo antibodies observed in patients receiving the drug (generation 1 product).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> None of these anti-factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo antibodies were neutralizing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Development of antibodies cross-reacting with factor X or factor Xa not observed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No adequate and well-controlled studies of factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Animal reproductive and developmental studies with factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo lacking.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Safety and efficacy of the drug during labor and delivery not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo is distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider benefits of breast-feeding and the clinical need for factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo in the woman along with any potential adverse effects on the breast-fed infant from the drug or from the underlying maternal condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in efficacy or safety between geriatric and younger patients; however, increased sensitivity of some older individuals cannot be ruled out.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Adverse effects reported in patients in clinical trials: Urinary tract infections,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> pneumonia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adverse effects reported in healthy individuals: Infusion-related reactions (e.g., flushing, feeling hot, cough, dysgeusia, dyspnea).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of informing patients that reversing the effects of factor Xa inhibitor therapy increases the risk of thromboembolic events; arterial and venous thromboembolic events, including ischemic events, cardiac events, and sudden death, have been observed within 30 days following administration of factor Xa (recombinant), inactivated-zhzo.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information. (See Cautions.)
