Introduction

Synthetic analog of gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH, luteinizing hormone-releasing hormone, gonadorelin); used as an antineoplastic agent and for its endocrine effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Uses for Goserelin

Prostate Cancer

Also used as an adjunct to radiation therapy in patients with stage III [C]) prostate cancer.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Goserelin Dosage and Administration

General

Determine serum testosterone concentrations periodically in patients with prostate cancer in whom the anticipated clinical or biochemical response to goserelin has not been achieved.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Reduction in serum prostate specific antigen (PSA) concentrations may provide information about duration of progression-free status in men with prostate cancer.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Do not rely solely on PSA concentrations to monitor response because decreases in PSA concentration may occur independent of tumor response.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Antibody formation and acute anaphylactic reactions reported with GnRH agonists, including goserelin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Expected hormonal changes increase the risk for pregnancy loss and fetal harm when administered to a pregnant woman; increased risk of pregnancy loss demonstrated in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Women of childbearing potential should avoid pregnancy and use an effective nonhormonal method of contraception during therapy and for 12 weeks following the last dose of goserelin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> Continuous use of goserelin usually inhibits ovulation and stops menstruation; however, prevention of pregnancy is not ensured.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Exclude pregnancy before initiating therapy in women with benign gynecologic conditions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If a patient with endometriosis or undergoing endometrial thinning becomes pregnant during therapy, discontinue the drug and advise patient about potential fetal hazard.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

If used during pregnancy in women with advanced breast cancer, apprise of potential fetal hazard.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible worsening of signs and/or symptoms of prostate or breast cancer (e.g., increased bone pain) and/or development of new manifestations due to transient increases in serum testosterone (in men) or estrogen (in women) concentrations during the initial weeks of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Concomitant therapy with an antiandrogen (e.g., bicalutamide, flutamide, nilutamide) 1 week before and during the first few weeks of goserelin therapy may minimize disease flare in men with prostate cancer.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Spinal cord compression and/or ureteral obstruction reported in men with prostate cancer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> If spinal cord compression or renal impairment secondary to ureteral obstruction develops, institute standard treatment of these complications; consider immediate orchiectomy in extreme cases.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Caution in patients at particular risk of developing spinal cord compression or ureteral obstruction; observe closely during the first month of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Treat spinal cord compression or ureteral obstruction before initiating goserelin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>
