Introduction

Ferrous fumarate, ferrous gluconate, ferrous sulfate, carbonyl iron, and polysaccharide-iron complex are used orally in the prevention and treatment of iron deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Uses for Iron Preparations, Oral

Prevention and Treatment of Iron Deficiency

Prevention and treatment of iron deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Not indicated for treatment of anemia resulting from causes other than iron deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Iron Preparations, Oral Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally between meals (e.g., 1 hour before or 2 hours after a meal).<html><head></head><body><sup>201</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>203</sup></body></html>

For patients who have difficulty tolerating oral iron supplements, administer smaller, more frequent doses; start with a lower dose and increase slowly to the target dose; try a different form or preparation; or take with or after meals<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> or at bedtime.<html><head></head><body><sup>149</sup></body></html>

Do not exceed recommended dosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Iron Preparations, Oral

Contraindications

  • Primary hemochromatosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Peptic ulcer, regional enteritis, or ulcerative colitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Primary hemochromatosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Peptic ulcer, regional enteritis, or ulcerative colitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Children <12 years of age and pregnant or nursing women should consult a health professional before using iron-containing preparations.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Possible fatal poisoning in children <6 years of age;<html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>148</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> keep iron-containing preparations out of reach of children.<html><head></head><body><sup>148</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

If accidental overdosage occurs, immediately contact a clinician or a poison control center.<html><head></head><body><sup>148</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Possible allergic reactions (e.g., bronchial asthma) with Fergon 225-mg tablets, which contain the dye tartrazine (FD&C yellow No. 5).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Use with caution, particularly in patients sensitive to aspirin.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Excess storage of iron with secondary hemochromatosis possible; not recommended for treatment of hemolytic anemias (unless an iron-deficient state also exists) or for patients receiving repeated blood transfusions.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Do not administer for >6 months except in patients with continued bleeding, menorrhagia, or repeated pregnancies.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Possible increased pathogenicity of certain microorganisms,<html><head></head><body><sup>134</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>138</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>139</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>140</sup></body></html> including possible adverse effects on prognosis in HIV-infected individuals;<html><head></head><body><sup>138</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>139</sup></body></html> not recommended for use in individuals without documented iron deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>138</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>139</sup></body></html>

When used in fixed combination with other agents, consider the cautions, precautions, and contraindications associated with the concomitant agents.

Distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Constipation, diarrhea, dark stools, nausea, epigastric pain.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Iron Preparations, Oral Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Absorption dependent upon the form of iron administered (e.g., ferrous form more readily absorbed), the dose, degree of erythropoiesis, diet, and iron stores.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> GI absorption of iron increases in iron-deficient individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html>

Oral bioavailability of iron can vary from <1% to >50%,<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> and the principal factor controlling GI iron absorption is the amount of iron stored in the body.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html>

Symptoms of anemia usually improve within a few days; normal hemoglobin values usually attained in 2 months unless blood loss continues.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Food decreases absorption of inorganic iron.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See Food under Interactions.)

Distribution

Exists in humans almost exclusively complexed to protein or in heme molecules.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Crosses the placenta and distributes into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Occurs in a virtually closed system.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Most iron liberated by destruction of hemoglobin is conserved and reused by the body.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Blood loss greatly increases iron loss.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Room temperature (15–30°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Protect from excessive heat and moisture.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Corrects erythropoietic abnormalities caused by a deficiency of iron.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Does not stimulate erythropoiesis, nor does it correct hemoglobin disturbances not caused by iron deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Corrects erythropoietic abnormalities caused by a deficiency of iron.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Does not stimulate erythropoiesis, nor does it correct hemoglobin disturbances not caused by iron deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
