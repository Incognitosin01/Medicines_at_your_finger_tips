Uses for Selenium Sulfide

Dandruff or Seborrheic Dermatitis

Treatment of dandruff and seborrheic dermatitis of the scalp.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Relieve symptoms (itching and flaking of the scalp) associated with dandruff.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Dandruff usually responds to treatment, but tends to relapse if therapy is discontinued.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Untreated dandruff is a cosmetic problem; generally no medical disability results.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Pityriasis (Tinea) Versicolor

Treatment of pityriasis (tinea) versicolor caused by Malassezia furfur (Pityrosporum ovale).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Topical antifungals usually effective; an oral antifungal (with or without a topical antifungal) may be necessary in patients who have extensive or severe infections or those who have failed to respond to or have frequent relapses with topical therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>f</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html>

Selenium Sulfide Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply lotion or shampoo topically to the skin; apply shampoo or lotion (as a shampoo) topically to the hair and scalp.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

For external use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Avoid contact with eyes; do not apply to broken skin or to infected or inflamed areas.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> (See Systemic Toxicity under Cautions.)

Shake lotion well before use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Lotion may damage jewelry; remove all jewelry before using.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

For treatment of dandruff and seborrheic dermatitis of the scalp: Massage into wet scalp.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Wait 2–3 minutes, then thoroughly rinse scalp.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Repeat application and rinse procedures.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Wash hands thoroughly following use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

If used before or after bleaching, tinting, or permanent waving of the hair, rinse hair with cool water for at least 5 minutes after applying lotion or shampoo.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

For treatment of tinea versicolor: Apply sufficient quantity topically to the affected skin; apply a small amount of water concurrently to form a lather.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Allow to remain on the skin for 10 minutes following application, then rinse skin thoroughly.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Children ≥2 years of age: Initially, apply 5–10 mL of 2.5% lotion (as a shampoo) twice weekly for 2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> For maintenance, apply once every 1–4 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Children ≥2 years of age (as self-medication): Apply a small amount of 1% lotion as a shampoo once or twice weekly.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> For maximum control, use every time patient usually shampoos hair and scalp.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Children ≥2 years of age: Apply 2.5% lotion to affected areas once daily for 7 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Initially, apply 2.25% shampoo or 5–10 mL of 2.5% lotion (as a shampoo) twice weekly for 2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> For maintenance, apply once every 1–4 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

For self-medication, apply a small amount of 1% lotion as a shampoo once or twice weekly.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> For maximum control, use every time patient usually shampoos hair and scalp.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Apply 2.25% shampoo or 2.5% lotion to affected areas once daily for 7 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Oiliness or dryness of the hair and scalp may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Rebound oiliness of the scalp reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Discoloration of various shades of natural and dyed hair has occurred;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> minimize or prevent by rinsing hair thoroughly after treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Diffuse hair loss reported; resolved 1–2 weeks after discontinuance of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Hypersensitivity reactions have occurred.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> If irritation or sensitivity occurs, discontinue drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Risk of systemic toxicity following topical application to damaged skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Do not use if acute inflammation or exudation present; risk of increased absorption.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Do not apply to damaged skin (e.g., open lesions or acute inflammation or exudation of the scalp).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Contact with eyes, genital area, or in skin folds may cause burning and irritation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> If contact with the eyes occurs, rinse eyes thoroughly with water; thoroughly rinse genital area and skin folds following topical application of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Skin irritation, increased hair loss, discoloration of hair, oiliness or dryness of hair and scalp.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Selenium Sulfide Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Not absorbed following topical application of the 1% lotion to intact skin of the scalp during usual shampooing conditions.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Percutaneous absorption may occur following topical application to damaged skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Systemic Toxicity under Cautions.)

Distribution

Not known whether topical selenium sulfide crosses the placenta or is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Tight container at 15–30°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Protect from heat; do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

15–30°C; do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Actions and Spectrum

  • Antibacterial and mild antifungal activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

  • Mechanism of action in the treatment of dandruff and seborrheic dermatitis not fully determined.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Cytostatic (antimitotic) action results in a decreased rate of cell turnover in cells with normal or higher than normal turnover rates.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

  • Active against Pityrosporum ovale, a yeast-like fungus that is part of the normal flora of the scalp.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • A definite causal relationship between P. ovale and the development of dandruff and seborrheic dermatitis not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • May be converted into selenium and sulfide ions when absorbed into epithelial tissue; selenium ions may block enzyme systems involved in the growth of epithelial tissue.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Local irritant effect.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>
