Introduction

Cholinergic agonist; binds to muscarinic receptors.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Uses for Pilocarpine Hydrochloride

Radiation-induced Dry Mouth

Symptomatic treatment of radiation-induced dry mouth (xerostomia) in patients with head and neck cancer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Dry Mouth Secondary to Sjögren Syndrome

Symptomatic treatment of dry mouth in patients with Sjögren syndrome.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

There are few comparative studies of pilocarpine and cevimeline (a similar muscarinic agonist).<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> Although both drugs can increase salivary flow and improve symptoms of dry mouth, adverse effects may differ based on differences in selectivity and affinity for muscarinic receptors.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> (See Actions.)

Pilocarpine Hydrochloride Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally 3–4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as pilocarpine hydrochloride; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Initially, 5 mg 3 times daily; titrate dosage based on therapeutic response and tolerance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Usual dosage range is 15–30 mg daily given in divided doses (not to exceed 10 mg per dose).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adverse effects are dose related; use lowest effective and tolerated dosage for maintenance therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

At least 12 weeks of continuous therapy may be necessary to achieve therapeutic benefit.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Recommended dosage is 5 mg 4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> To minimize incidence of sweating, some clinicians recommend initiating therapy at a low dosage (e.g., 2 mg once or twice daily), then gradually increasing up to 5 mg 3 or 4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

In clinical studies, a therapeutic effect was observed by 6 weeks of treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

Do not exceed 10 mg per dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

Mild hepatic impairment: No dosage adjustment required.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Moderate hepatic impairment: Reduce initial dosage to 5 mg twice daily regardless of indication; adjust subsequent dosage based on response and tolerability.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Hepatic Impairment under Cautions.)

Severe hepatic impairment: Use not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer makes no specific dosage recommendations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer makes no specific dosage recommendations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Pilocarpine Hydrochloride

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to pilocarpine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Uncontrolled asthma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Patients in whom miosis is undesirable (e.g., those with acute iritis, angle-closure glaucoma).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to pilocarpine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Uncontrolled asthma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Patients in whom miosis is undesirable (e.g., those with acute iritis, angle-closure glaucoma).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of altered cardiac conduction and/or heart rate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Patients with clinically important cardiovascular disease may be unable to compensate for transient changes in hemodynamics or heart rhythm induced by pilocarpine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution and under close medical supervision in patients with a history of cardiovascular disease (e.g., angina pectoris, MI).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Blurred vision reported with ophthalmic formulations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May result in impaired depth perception and decreased visual acuity, especially at night and in patients with central lens changes; may impair ability to drive at night or perform hazardous activities in reduced lighting.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

May increase airway resistance, bronchial smooth muscle tone, and bronchial secretions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use with caution and under close medical supervision in patients with controlled asthma, chronic bronchitis, or COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible exaggeration of parasympathomimetic effects (e.g., headache, visual disturbance, lacrimation, sweating, respiratory distress, GI spasm, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, AV block, tachycardia, bradycardia, hypotension, hypertension, shock, mental confusion, cardiac arrhythmia, tremors).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Sweating is the most common adverse effect.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> Excessive sweating may cause dehydration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Use with caution in patients with known or suspected cholelithiasis or biliary tract disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Contraction of gallbladder or biliary smooth muscle could precipitate complications (e.g., cholecystitis, cholangitis, biliary obstruction) in patients with cholelithiasis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May increase ureteral smooth muscle tone, and theoretically cause renal colic or ureteral reflux in patients with nephrolithiasis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dose-related CNS effects reported; use caution in patients with underlying cognitive or psychiatric disturbances.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No adequate and well-controlled studies in pregnant women; fetotoxicity and developmental abnormalities reported in animal studies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use during pregnancy only if potential benefit justifies potential risk to the fetus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether pilocarpine is distributed into human milk; discontinue nursing or the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

In patients ≥65 years of age with head and neck cancer, adverse effects generally similar to those observed in younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In patients ≥65 years of age with Sjögren syndrome, increased incidence of urinary frequency, diarrhea, and dizziness reported compared with younger patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Clearance decreased in patients with hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Special Populations under Pharmacokinetics.) Dosage reduction required in patients with moderate hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Hepatic Impairment under Dosage and Administration.)

Pharmacokinetics not substantially altered in patients with renal impairment (mean Clcr 25.4 mL/minute; range 9.8–40.8 mL/minute).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Radiation-induced dry mouth: Sweating, nausea, rhinitis, diarrhea, chills, flushing, urinary frequency, dizziness, asthenia, headache, dyspepsia, lacrimation, edema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dry mouth secondary to Sjögren syndrome: Sweating, urinary frequency, nausea, flushing, rhinitis, diarrhea, headache, flu syndrome, dyspepsia, dizziness.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pilocarpine Hydrochloride Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

In healthy individuals, peak plasma concentrations achieved in approximately 1 hour.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In healthy individuals, onset of effect approximately 20 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In healthy individuals, duration of effect approximately 3–5 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Exerts a broad spectrum of pharmacologic effects with predominant muscarinic action.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

  • In sufficient dosages, can increase exocrine (e.g., salivary, sweat) gland secretion; also may increase bronchial smooth muscle tone and enhance tone and motility of GI, urinary tract, gallbladder, and biliary duct smooth muscle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

  • Dose-related smooth muscle stimulation of the intestinal tract may cause increased tone, increased motility, spasm, and tenesmus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • May have paradoxical effects on the cardiovascular system (e.g., hypotension and hypertension, bradycardia and tachycardia).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Risk of visual disturbances, especially at night; advise patients to use caution when driving at night or performing hazardous activities in reduced lighting.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of dehydration if excessive sweating occurs; advise patients to consult their clinician if they experience excessive sweating but cannot drink enough liquids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
