Uses for Bromfenac

Postoperative Ocular Inflammation and Pain

Management of ocular inflammation and pain associated with cataract extraction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Bromfenac Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of the solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Do not administer while wearing contact lenses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Dosage

Available as bromfenac sodium sesquihydrate; dosage expressed in terms of bromfenac.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

1 drop of a 0.09% solution in the affected eye(s) twice daily, beginning 24 hours after cataract surgery and continuing through the first 2 weeks of the postoperative period.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Bromfenac

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to bromfenac sodium or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to bromfenac sodium or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

May inhibit platelet aggregation and prolong bleeding time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May cause increased bleeding of ocular tissues (including hyphemas) when used in conjunction with ocular surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with underlying bleeding tendencies or in those receiving drugs known to prolong bleeding time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible cross-sensitivity with aspirin, phenylacetic acid derivatives, and other NSAIAs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use with caution in patients with history of hypersensitivity to these drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Formulation contains sodium sulfite, which may cause allergic-type reactions (including anaphylaxis and life-threatening or less severe asthmatic episodes) in certain susceptible individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May slow or delay wound healing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

Use may result in keratitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In some susceptible patients, continued use may result in epithelial breakdown, corneal thinning, corneal erosion, corneal ulceration, or corneal perforation; these events may be sight-threatening.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If manifestations of corneal epithelial breakdown occur, discontinue therapy immediately and monitor for corneal health.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Patients with complicated ocular surgeries, corneal denervation, corneal epithelial defects, diabetes mellitus, ocular surface diseases (e.g., dry eye syndrome), rheumatoid arthritis, or repeat ocular surgeries within a short period of time may be at increased risk for developing adverse corneal effects that may become sight-threatening.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use with caution in these patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use >24 hours prior to surgery or use beyond 14 days postoperatively may precipitate or exacerbate adverse corneal effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid use in late pregnancy (i.e., third trimester) because of known effects on the fetal cardiovascular system (possible premature closure of the ductus arteriosus).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into milk following ophthalmic administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Use with caution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children <18 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No substantial differences in safety and efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Abnormal ocular sensation, conjunctival hyperemia, ocular redness or irritation (e.g., pruritus, burning, stinging, pain), headache, iritis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Bromfenac Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Actions

  • Inhibits synthesis of prostaglandins in body tissues by inhibiting cyclooxygenase (COX), including both COX-1 and COX-2 isoenzymes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Ocular effects associated principally with inhibition of ocular prostaglandin synthesis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>
