Introduction

Co-trimoxazole Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim are rapidly and well absorbed from the GI tract following oral administration of the fixed combination preparation (co-trimoxazole).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Peak serum concentrations of both sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim are attained within 1–4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Co-trimoxazole contains a 1:5 ratio of trimethoprim to sulfamethoxazole, but the trimethoprim:sulfamethoxazole ratio in serum after administration of the fixed-combination preparation is about 1:20 at steady-state.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Widely distributed into body tissues and fluids, including sputum, aqueous humor, middle ear fluid, bronchial secretions, prostatic fluid, vaginal fluid, and bile.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

In patients with uninflamed meninges, trimethoprim and sulfamethoxazole concentrations in CSF are about 50 and 40%, respectively, of concurrent serum concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Both sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim readily cross the placenta and are distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Sulfamethoxazole is approximately 70% and trimethoprim is approximately 44% bound to plasma proteins.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Presence of sulfamethoxazole decreases protein binding of trimethoprim.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Both sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim are metabolized in the liver.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Both sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim are rapidly excreted in urine by glomerular filtration and active tubular secretion.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> In adults with normal renal function, approximately 45–85% of a sulfamethoxazole dose and 50–67% of a trimethoprim dose are excreted in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Only small amounts of trimethoprim are excreted in feces via biliary elimination.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Serum half-lives of sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim are approximately 10–13 and 8–11 hours, respectively, in adults with normal renal function.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Patients with impaired renal function: Half-lives of both sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim may be prolonged.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

In adults with Clcr≤10 mL/minute, serum half-life of trimethoprim may increase to >26 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> In adults with chronic renal failure, sulfamethoxazole half-life may be 3 times that in patients with normal renal function.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Actions and Spectrum

  • A fixed combination of sulfamethoxazole and trimethoprim; both drugs are folate-antagonists and sequentially inhibit enzymes of the folic acid pathway in susceptible bacteria.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Sulfamethoxazole inhibits formation of dihydrofolic acid from p-aminobenzoic acid and trimethoprim inhibits formation of tetrahydrofolic acid (the metabolically active form of folic acid).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Sulfamethoxazole is bacteriostatic and trimethoprim usually is bactericidal; the fixed combination generally is bactericidal when synergism is achieved.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Susceptibility to trimethoprim is more critical to efficacy of co-trimoxazole than susceptibility to sulfamethoxazole.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Co-trimoxazole is active against many organisms resistant to sulfamethoxazole but susceptible to trimethoprim.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Spectrum of activity includes many gram-positive and -negative aerobic bacteria and some protozoa.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Inactive against most anaerobic bacteria and inactive against fungi and viruses.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Gram-positive aerobes: Active against Staphylococcus aureus (including penicillinase-producing strains), Streptococcus pneumoniae, S. pyogenes (group A β-hemolytic streptococci), and some strains of enterococci (e.g., Enterococcus faecalis).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Also active against Nocardia,<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> but Bacillus anthracis may be resistant.<html><head></head><body><sup>307</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>308</sup></body></html>
