Uses for Cromolyn (EENT)

Allergic Rhinitis

Self-medication for the symptomatic prevention and treatment of seasonal or perennial allergic rhinitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> Provides symptomatic relief of rhinorrhea, nasal congestion, sneezing, and postnasal drip.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Should not use as self-medication for the treatment of sinus infection†, asthma†, or cold symptoms†.<html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html>

Allergic Ocular Disorders

Symptomatic treatment of certain allergic ocular disorders (e.g., vernal keratoconjunctivitis,<html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>208</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> vernal conjunctivitis,<html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>208</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> vernal keratitis).<html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>208</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Has been used for the treatment of giant papillary conjunctivitis†.<html><head></head><body><sup>207</sup></body></html>

Cromolyn (EENT) Dosage and Administration

General

  • For patients with seasonal or perennial rhinitis, continue therapy throughout the period of allergen exposure (e.g., until the pollen season is over or contact with allergen has ceased).<html><head></head><body><sup>215</sup></body></html>

  • If exposure to allergens cannot be predicted, initiate therapy at the first sign of nasal symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>215</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer intranasally or topically to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>206</sup></body></html>

Cromolyn (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Approximately <7 or 0.03% absorbed following an intranasal dose<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> or an ophthalmic dose, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Nasal solution: Improvement may take several days; best effect not seen for 1–2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>215</sup></body></html>

Ophthalmic solution: Improvement usually noted in a few days; treatment for up to 6 weeks sometimes needed to see improvement.<html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>208</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Does not cross most biologic membranes well.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Minimally crosses the placenta (<0.1%) and minimally distributed into milk (<0.001% of a dose) in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Elimination

81 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of providing patient a copy of manufacturer’s patient information.<html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of clinicians instructing patients about proper use, including proper administration of the particular dosage form employed.<html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>208</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html>

  • With nasal solution, importance of informing a clinician of symptoms such as fever, discolored nasal discharge, sinus pain, or wheezing prior to use.<html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of administration of drug at regular intervals.<html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>208</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>215</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of avoiding exposure to allergens.<html><head></head><body><sup>215</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients with seasonal or perennial allergic rhinitis to begin therapy before (e.g., ≤1–2 weeks before), and to continue therapy at regular intervals during, the period of allergen exposure.<html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>215</sup></body></html>

  • With intranasal cromolyn, importance of advising patients with allergic rhinitis to discontinue therapy and consult a clinician if symptoms worsen, new symptoms occur, symptoms do not begin to improve within 2 weeks of treatment initiation, or if treatment is required for longer than >12 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>215</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of discontinuing drug and contacting a clinician if shortness of breath, wheezing, hives or swelling of the mouth or throat, or chest tightness occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>215</sup></body></html>
