Warnings/Precautions

Myelosuppression (anemia, leukopenia, pancytopenia, thrombocytopenia) reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Toxicity studies in adult and juvenile dogs and rats indicate myelosuppression (bone marrow hypocellularity/decreased hematopoiesis; decreased extramedullary hematopoiesis in spleen and liver; decreased levels of circulating erythrocytes, leukocytes, and platelets) and lymphoid depletion in thymus, lymph nodes, and spleen.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor CBCs weekly during linezolid treatment, especially in those receiving the drug for >2 weeks and in those who have preexisting myelosuppression, are receiving concomitant drugs associated with bone marrow suppression, or have a chronic infection that was or is being treated with concomitant anti-infective therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consider discontinuing linezolid if myelosuppression develops or worsens.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Hematologic parameters generally have increased toward pretreatment values following discontinuance of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Peripheral and optic neuropathies reported in adults and children receiving linezolid, principally in those receiving the drug for >28 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> Optic neuropathy progressing to loss of vision reported when linezolid used for >28 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> Blurred vision reported in some patients who received the drug for <28 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If patient experiences symptoms of visual impairment (e.g., changes in visual acuity or color vision, blurred vision, visual field defect), promptly perform an ophthalmic evaluation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor visual function in all patients receiving linezolid for extended periods of time (i.e., ≥3 months).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In addition, monitor visual function in all patients reporting a new visual symptom, regardless of length of linezolid treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If peripheral or optic neuropathy occurs, weigh potential benefits versus risks of continued linezolid treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Serotonin syndrome (including some fatalities) reported in patients receiving linezolid concomitantly with serotonergic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>27</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> Signs and symptoms of serotonin syndrome include mental changes (confusion, hyperactivity, memory problems), muscle twitching, excessive sweating, shivering, shaking, diarrhea, loss of coordination, and/or fever.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>

Most reported cases occurred in patients receiving linezolid concomitantly with SSRIs or SNRIs.<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> FDA has not concluded whether concomitant use of linezolid with other drugs with lesser degrees of serotonergic activity (e.g., tricyclic antidepressants, MAO inhibitors) is associated with a risk comparable to that reported with SSRIs or SNRIs.<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Unless considered clinically appropriate and patients can be carefully monitored for signs and/or symptoms of serotonin syndrome or neuroleptic malignant syndrome-like (NMS-like) reactions, do not use linezolid in patients with carcinoid syndrome or in patients receiving SSRIs, tricyclic antidepressants, serotonin 5-HT1 receptor agonists (triptans), meperidine, bupropion, or buspirone.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

In cases when patient is already receiving a serotonergic antidepressant or buspirone and urgent treatment with linezolid is required (e.g., life-threatening infection caused by MRSA or vancomycin-resistant E. faecium) and alternatives to linezolid are not available and potential benefits of the drug outweigh risks of serotonin syndrome or NMS-like reactions, stop the serotonergic antidepressant promptly and administer linezolid.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> Monitor for manifestations of serotonin syndrome or NMS-like reactions (e.g., hyperthermia, rigidity, myoclonus, autonomic instability, mental status changes that include extreme agitation progressing to delirium and coma) for 2 weeks (5 weeks if fluoxetine was taken) or until 24 hours after last linezolid dose, whichever comes first.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> Also monitor for symptoms associated with discontinuance of the antidepressant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In an investigational study in seriously ill patients with intravascular catheter-related infections†, mortality was higher in patients receiving linezolid than in patients receiving a comparator anti-infective (vancomycin, oxacillin, dicloxacillin); patients also could receive concomitant therapy for gram-negative infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> There was no difference in mortality between linezolid and comparator regimens in patients with only gram-positive bacteria identified in the baseline culture; mortality was higher in linezolid-treated patients with gram-negative bacterial infections, mixed gram-positive and gram-negative infections, or no pathogen identified at baseline.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> Causality not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Advise patients that antibacterials (including linezolid) should only be used to treat bacterial infections and not used to treat viral infections (e.g., the common cold).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of completing full course of therapy, even if feeling better after a few days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that skipping doses or not completing the full course of therapy may decrease effectiveness and increase the likelihood that bacteria will develop resistance and will not be treatable with linezolid or other antibacterials in the future.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that linezolid may be taken orally without regard to meals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • If using the oral suspension, importance of not shaking the bottle vigorously and gently inverting the bottle 3–5 times to resuspend the drug prior to administration of each dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients of the potential risk of serotonin syndrome, particularly if linezolid is used concomitantly with MAO inhibitors, SSRIs, SNRIs, tricyclic antidepressants, or other serotonergic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> Importance of immediately contacting clinician if signs and symptoms of serotonin syndrome develop (e.g., confusion, hyperactivity, memory problems, muscle twitching, excessive sweating, shivering, shaking, diarrhea, loss of coordination, fever).<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> Importance of not discontinuing serotonergic drugs without first consulting clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients, particularly those with diabetes mellitus, that hypoglycemic reactions (diaphoresis and tremulousness) and low blood glucose concentrations may occur during linezolid treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting a clinician to obtain proper treatment if such reactions occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of avoiding large quantities of foods or beverages with high tyramine content during linezolid treatment;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> this includes foods or beverages that have been aged, fermented, pickled, or smoked to improve flavor (e.g., aged cheeses, fermented or air-dried meats, sauerkraut, soy sauce, tap beer, red wine).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider that tyramine content of any protein-rich food may be increased if stored for long periods or improperly refrigerated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise individuals with phenylketonuria that the oral suspension contains aspartame, which is metabolized in the GI tract to provide 20 mg of phenylalanine per 5 mL of suspension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of notifying clinician of any history of hypertension or seizures.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of notifying clinician if any change in vision occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that diarrhea is a common problem caused by anti-infectives and usually ends when the drug is discontinued.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of contacting a clinician if watery and bloody stools (with or without stomach cramps and fever) occur during or as late as 2 months or longer after the last dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy including prescription drugs (e.g., antidepressants) and OTC drugs (e.g., pseudoephedrine), as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
