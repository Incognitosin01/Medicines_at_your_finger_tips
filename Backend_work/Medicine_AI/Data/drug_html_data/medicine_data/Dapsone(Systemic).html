Uses for Dapsone (Systemic)

Leprosy

Treatment of leprosy (Hansen's disease) in conjunction with other anti-infectives.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>216</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>217</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>218</sup></body></html>

WHO and US National Hansen's Disease Program (NHDP) recommend multidrug therapy (MDT) for treatment of all forms of leprosy, including multibacillary leprosy and paucibacillary leprosy.<html><head></head><body><sup>216</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>217</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>218</sup></body></html>

MDT regimens can rapidly kill Mycobacterium leprae, render patient noninfectious after only a few days of treatment, delay or prevent emergence of resistant M. leprae, and reduce risk of relapse after treatment discontinuance.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>216</sup></body></html> MDT regimens do not enhance rate of clearance of dead bacilli from the body;<html><head></head><body><sup>217</sup></body></html> such clearance may take years<html><head></head><body><sup>216</sup></body></html> and depends largely on individual's immune response, which may be defective in leprosy patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>217</sup></body></html> Reactive episodes reported in leprosy patients receiving treatment appear to be due to destruction of M. leprae and immune responses to released bacterial antigens.<html><head></head><body><sup>216</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>217</sup></body></html> (See Leprosy Reactional States under Cautions.)

For treatment of multibacillary leprosy (i.e., ≥6 lesions or skin smear positive) in adults, WHO recommends a 12-month MDT regimen of dapsone (once daily), rifampin (once monthly), and clofazimine (once daily and once monthly).<html><head></head><body><sup>217</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>218</sup></body></html> For treatment of paucibacillary leprosy (i.e., 1–5 lesions) in adults, WHO recommends a 6-month MDT regimen of dapsone (once daily) and rifampin (once monthly).<html><head></head><body><sup>217</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>218</sup></body></html>

For US patients, NHDP recommends more prolonged treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>216</sup></body></html> NHDP recommends that adults with multibacillary leprosy (i.e., those who are skin smear positive and/or have biopsy indicating more advanced disease) receive a 24-month MDT regimen of dapsone (once daily), rifampin (once daily), and clofazimine (once daily), and that adults with paucibacillary leprosy (i.e., those who are skin smear negative without evidence of more advanced disease on biopsy) receive a 12-month MDT regimen of dapsone (once daily) and rifampin (once daily).<html><head></head><body><sup>216</sup></body></html> Clofazimine (no longer commercially available in US) may be obtained from NHDP under an investigational new drug (IND) protocol for treatment of leprosy.<html><head></head><body><sup>216</sup></body></html>

Treatment of leprosy is complicated and should be undertaken in consultation with a specialist familiar with the disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> In the US, clinicians should contact NHDP at 800-642-2477 on weekdays from 9:00 a.m. to 5:30 p.m. Eastern Standard Time or via email at nhdped@hrsa.gov for assistance with diagnosis or treatment of leprosy or assistance obtaining clofazimine for treatment of leprosy.<html><head></head><body><sup>216</sup></body></html>

Dermatitis Herpetiformis

Treatment of dermatitis herpetiformis.<html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Gluten-free diet recommended for all patients with dermatitis herpetiformis; strict adherence to such a diet can result in slow resolution of skin lesions (may take months to years) and improvement in GI symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> Dapsone, used as an adjunct to gluten-free diet,<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> usually results in prompt decrease in pruritus and resolution of skin lesions in responsive patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> Dapsone has no effect on GI component of dermatitis herpetiformis.<html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>
