FOR EXTERNAL TOPICAL USE ONLY. NOT FOR OPHTHALMIC USE.
Rx Only

ACTIVE INGREDIENTS

Each gram contains Trypsin USP NLT 90 USP units, Balsam Peru 87.0 mg, Castor Oil USP 788.0 mg/g

INACTIVE INGREDIENTS

Glyceryl Monostearate, Glycine, L-Proline, Taurine and White Petrolatum.

ACTION

Balsam Peru is an effective capillary bed stimulant used to increase circulation in the wound site area. Also, Balsam Peru has a mildly bactericidal action. Castor Oil is used to promote epithelialization by reducing premature epithelial desiccation and cornification. Also, it can act as a protective covering and aids in the reduction of pain. Trypsin is intended for debridement of eschar and other necrotic tissue. It appears that in many instances removal of wound debris strengthens humoral defense mechanisms sufficiently to retard proliferation of local pathogens.

INDICATIONS

To aid in the care of wound management, including decubitus ulcers, varicose ulcers and dehiscent wounds.

USES

Trypsin Complex Ointment is easy to apply and quickly reduces odor frequently accompanying a decubitus ulcer. The wound may be left open or appropriate dressing applied. As a suggestion, keep in mind wounds heal poorly in the presence of hemoglobin or zinc deficiency. Trypsin Complex Ointment can relieve pain and aid in the management of healing.

Warning

Do not apply to fresh arterial clots. Avoid contact with eyes. Keep this and all medications out of the reach of children. In case of accidental overdose seek professional assistance or contact a Poison Control Center immediately. Use only as directed. When applied to a sensitive area, a temporary stinging sensation may be noted.

DOSAGE

Apply a thin film of Trypsin Complex Ointment a minimum of twice daily or as often as necessary. Wound may be left unbandaged or appropriate dressing applied. To remove, wash gently with appropriate cleanser.

How is Trypsin Complex Ointment Supplied

Trypsin Complex Ointment is supplied in 60 gram tubes. NDC 51991-124-22

Dispense in original container.

Store Trypsin Complex Ointment at 25° C (77° F); excursions permitted to 15° - 30° C (59° - 86° F). See USP Controlled Room Temperature. Avoid freezing.
