Introduction

Antineoplastic agent; a nitrosourea-derivative alkylating agent.

Uses for Carmustine

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of myelosuppression (e.g., thrombocytopenia, leukopenia) following IV carmustine; effects are delayed and cumulative.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> (See Boxed Warning.) Thrombocytopenia generally more severe than leukopenia but both considered dose-limiting toxicities.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Anemia reported less frequently and is less severe.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Following IV administration, thrombocytopenia and leukopenia occur at approximately 4 and 5–6 weeks, respectively, and persist for 1–2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Repeated dosing associated with more severe and more prolonged myelosuppression.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with depressed platelet, leukocyte, or erythrocyte counts.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

With IV carmustine, risk of dose-related, sometimes fatal, pulmonary toxicity (characterized by pulmonary infiltrates and/or fibrosis).<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> (See Boxed Warning.) Risk factors include prolonged therapy (with cumulative doses >1400 mg/m2) and history of lung disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Risk of delayed-onset pulmonary fibrosis (occurred up to 17 years after treatment during childhood and early adolescence); possible reduction of pulmonary function or death.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> (See Pediatric Use under Cautions.)

Perform pulmonary function tests prior to initiation of and frequently during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Patients with baseline forced vital capacity (FVC) or pulmonary diffusion capacity for carbon monoxide (DLCO) <70% of predicted value are particularly at risk.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Risk of secondary malignancies following long-term use of nitrosoureas.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Intracerebral mass effect unresponsive to corticosteroids reported, including one case leading to brain herniation.<html><head></head><body><sup>124</sup></body></html> Brain edema with mass effect (due to tumor recurrence, intracranial infection, or necrosis) may necessitate reoperation and, in some cases, removal of wafer or wafer remnants.<html><head></head><body><sup>124</sup></body></html>

Risk of seizures; median time to onset is 3.5 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>124</sup></body></html>

Formation of tumor bed cyst unresponsive to high-dose corticosteroids reported; required reoperation for drainage following implantation of carmustine wafers.<html><head></head><body><sup>136</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients closely for potential complications of craniotomy (e.g., seizures, intracranial infections, abnormal wound healing, brain edema).<html><head></head><body><sup>124</sup></body></html>

Possible fetal harm; teratogenicity and embryotoxicity demonstrated in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Avoid pregnancy during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> If used during pregnancy or if patient becomes pregnant, apprise of potential fetal hazard.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>124</sup></body></html>

Rapid IV infusion may result in intensive flushing of the skin and suffusion of the conjunctiva; these effects occur within 2 hours and persist for 4 hours after IV administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Also associated with intense pain and burning at injection site; thrombosis is rare.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

With intracranial wafer implant, wound dehiscence; delayed wound healing; subdural, subgaleal, or wound effusions; and CSF leak reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>124</sup></body></html>

With intracranial wafer implant, abscess, meningitis, and pneumonia reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>124</sup></body></html> Sepsis reported but causal relationship not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>124</sup></body></html>

With IV carmustine, dose-related nausea and vomiting reported within 2 hours and persisting for 4–6 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Premedication with antiemetics may diminish or prevent.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>
