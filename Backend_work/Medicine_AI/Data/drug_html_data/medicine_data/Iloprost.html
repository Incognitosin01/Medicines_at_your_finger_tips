Uses for Iloprost

Pulmonary Arterial Hypertension (PAH)

Management of PAH (WHO group 1 pulmonary hypertension) to improve a composite end point consisting of exercise tolerance, symptoms (NYHA/WHO functional class), and lack of clinical deterioration;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> has been designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Efficacy established principally in patients with NYHA/WHO functional class III or IV PAH (idiopathic, heritable, or associated with connective tissue diseases).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Recommended as one of several treatment options for management of PAH in patients with NYHA/WHO functional class III or IV symptoms who are not candidates for calcium-channel blocker therapy or in whom such therapy failed.<html><head></head><body><sup>38</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>40</sup></body></html>

Individualize choice of PAH therapy; consider factors such as disease severity, route of administration, potential adverse effects, costs of treatment, clinician experience, and patient preference.<html><head></head><body><sup>38</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>40</sup></body></html>

In patients with inadequate response to initial monotherapy, may consider combination therapy with an endothelin-receptor antagonist, phosphodiesterase (PDE) type 5 inhibitor, or soluble guanylate cyclase stimulator (added sequentially).<html><head></head><body><sup>40</sup></body></html> Such combination therapy may provide additive and/or synergistic benefits by targeting different pathophysiologic pathways of the disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>40</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Monitor vital signs, especially BP, closely during initiation of iloprost.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Do not initiate in patients with SBP <85 mm Hg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Administer 6–9 times daily at minimum intervals of 2 hours while awake according to clinical response.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Duration of acute benefits may be <2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

Maximum dosage evaluated: 45 mcg daily (5 mcg 9 times daily).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consider increasing dosing interval (e.g., to 3–4 hours between doses depending on patient's response at the end of each dosing interval) in patients with moderate to severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh class B or C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Hepatic Impairment under Cautions.)
