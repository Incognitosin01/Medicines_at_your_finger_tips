Uses for Esketamine

Treatment-resistant Depression

Used intranasally for treatment (in combination with an oral antidepressant) of treatment-resistant depression.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Although there are various definitions of treatment-resistant depression, the condition is often defined as the failure of ≥2 trials of first-line antidepressants given in an adequate dosage for an adequate duration of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>301</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>307</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>309</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>315</sup></body></html>

Intranasal esketamine substantially improved depressive symptoms as measured by the Montgomery-Asberg Depression Rating Scale (MADRS) within 24 hours of administration in a short-term clinical trial and prolonged time to relapse in a longer-term maintenance trial.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Administration

Intended for patient self-administration under the direct supervision of a healthcare provider.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> A treatment session consists of intranasal administration of esketamine and post-administration observation under supervision.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Instruct patients on use of the nasal spray device and advise them to read the patient instructions for use provided by the manufacturer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Esketamine nasal solution is commercially available in kits containing either 2 (56-mg dose kit) or 3 (84-mg dose kit) stoppered glass vials within a nasal spray device.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Each device delivers 14 mg of esketamine per spray and delivers 2 sprays (1 spray per nostril) per device.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Therefore, a 56-mg dose requires 2 devices, and an 84-mg dose requires 3 devices.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

An indicator on the device displays one green light for each spray remaining in the device.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Prior to administration, a healthcare provider should confirm the number of nasal spray devices required for the dose and check that each device indicator displays 2 green dots prior to use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

To prevent loss of the drug, do not prime esketamine nasal spray devices before use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Before the initial spray from the first device at each treatment session, blow the nose to clear the nasal passages.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> During administration, patients should recline their head to about 45 degrees to help keep the solution inside the nose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Insert the tip of the device straight into one nostril; the nose rest should touch the skin between the nostrils.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> While holding the other nostril closed, concurrently inspire through the nose while pushing on the plunger to activate the spray.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Following actuation of the spray, sniff gently to keep the solution in the nose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Repeat this procedure for the other nostril.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If liquid drips out of the nostril, dab the nose with a tissue.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Repeat procedure for each device until the full dose has been administered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Following administration of each device (i.e., 2 sprays), patients should rest in a comfortable position (preferably semi-reclined) for 5 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following use, the healthcare provider should check each device to ensure that both sprays have been delivered; if a green dot appears in the device indicator, the patient should spray again into the second nostril.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Properly dispose of used devices in accordance with the facility's procedures and federal, state, and local regulations for controlled substance disposal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Abuse and Misuse under Cautions.)

Dosage

Available as esketamine hydrochloride; dosage expressed in terms of esketamine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer in combination with an oral antidepressant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In short- and long-term efficacy trials, approximately one-third of patients received 56-mg doses and two-thirds received 84-mg doses of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Esketamine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Rapidly absorbed following intranasal administration; peak concentrations attained in 20–40 minutes following the last nasal spray of a treatment session.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Absolute bioavailability: Approximately 48% following intranasal administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Exposure increases with dose at intranasal dosages of 28–84 mg; peak concentrations and AUC increase in a less than dose-proportional manner between 28 mg and 56 or 84 mg and in a nearly dose-proportional manner between 56 and 84 mg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interpatient variation in peak plasma concentrations and exposure: 27–66 and 18–45%, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Intrapatient variation in peak plasma concentrations and exposure: Approximately 15 and 10%, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Accumulation not observed during twice-weekly administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increases in BP peak approximately 40 minutes following intranasal administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increases in BP last approximately 4 hours following intranasal administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Geriatric patients: Increased peak plasma concentrations and AUC.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Mild hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh class A): Slightly increased peak plasma concentrations and exposure; not considered clinically important.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh class B): Peak plasma concentrations and exposure increased by approximately 8% and 103%, respectively, compared with individuals with normal hepatic function.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Mild to severe renal impairment (Clcr 5–77 mL/minute; not requiring dialysis): Slightly increased systemic exposure; not considered clinically important.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Crosses the blood-brain barrier; brain-to-plasma ratio of noresketamine (the principal active metabolite of esketamine) is 4–6 times lower than that of esketamine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Approximately 43–45%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized principally by CYP2B6 and CYP3A4 and, to a lesser extent, by CYP2C9 and CYP2C19 to its principal active metabolite noresketamine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Noresketamine also metabolized via CYP-mediated pathways and certain metabolites undergo glucuronidation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Excreted in urine (≥78%) and feces (≤2%) as metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

<1% excreted unchanged in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following intranasal administration, plasma esketamine and noresketamine concentrations decline in a biphasic manner, with a rapid initial phase (2–4 or 4 hours for esketamine or noresketamine, respectively) and a longer elimination phase (mean terminal half-life of 7–12 or 8 hours for esketamine or noresketamine, respectively).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
