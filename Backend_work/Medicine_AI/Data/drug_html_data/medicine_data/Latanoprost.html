Uses for Latanoprost

Ocular Hypertension and Glaucoma

Appears to be more effective than unoprostone (not available in US), as effective as travoprost, and slightly less effective than bimatoprost in reducing IOP in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.<html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>77</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>78</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>79</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>80</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>81</sup></body></html>

May be more effective or at least as effective as twice-daily administration of timolol 0.5% in reducing IOP in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>30</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>33</sup></body></html> Appears to be more effective than thrice-daily administration of dorzolamide 2%.<html><head></head><body><sup>86</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>87</sup></body></html>

When selecting an initial ocular hypotensive agent, consider extent of the required IOP reduction, coexisting medical conditions, and drug characteristics (e.g., dosing frequency, adverse effects, cost).<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> With single-agent regimens, the reduction in IOP is approximately 25–33% with topical prostaglandin analogs; 20–25% with topical β-adrenergic blocking agents, α-adrenergic agonists, or miotic (parasympathomimetic) agents; 20–30% with oral carbonic anhydrase inhibitors; 18% with topical rho kinase inhibitors; and 15–20% with topical carbonic anhydrase inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html>

Goal is to maintain an IOP at which visual field loss is unlikely to substantially reduce quality of life during the patient's lifetime.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of not exceeding once-daily dosing; more frequent administration may decrease IOP-lowering effect of latanoprost.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
