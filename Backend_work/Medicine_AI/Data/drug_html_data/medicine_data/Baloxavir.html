Introduction

Antiviral; prodrug of baloxavir, a polymerase acidic (PA) endonuclease inhibitor active against influenza A and B.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Uses for Baloxavir

Treatment of Seasonal Influenza A and B Virus Infections

Treatment of acute, uncomplicated influenza caused by influenza A or B viruses in adults and adolescents ≥12 years of age who have been symptomatic for ≤48 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Efficacy if administered >48 hours after symptom onset not evaluated.<html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html>

Data not available to date regarding use for treatment of severe or complicated influenza in either hospitalized patients or outpatients.<html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> Data also not available to date regarding use for prophylaxis of influenza.<html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html>

CDC, Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP), IDSA, and AAP recommend that antiviral treatment be initiated as soon as possible in all individuals with suspected or confirmed influenza who require hospitalization or have severe, complicated, or progressive illness (regardless of vaccination status or underlying illness).<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html> Early empiric antiviral treatment also recommended in individuals with suspected or confirmed influenza of any severity if they are at high risk of developing influenza-related complications because of age or underlying medical conditions (regardless of vaccination status).<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html> This includes children <5 years of age (especially those <2 years of age), adults ≥65 years of age, individuals of any age with certain chronic medical or immunosuppressive conditions, women who are pregnant or ≤2 weeks postpartum, individuals <19 years of age receiving long-term aspirin therapy, American Indians, Alaskan natives, morbidly obese individuals with body mass index (BMI) ≥40, and residents of any age in nursing homes or other long-term care facilities.<html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html>

CDC, ACIP, IDSA, and AAP also state that empiric antiviral treatment can be considered in previously healthy, symptomatic individuals with suspected or confirmed influenza who are not known to be at increased risk of developing severe or complicated illness (regardless of vaccination status), if such treatment can be initiated within 48 hours of illness onset.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html> Although these individuals typically do not require treatment, early empiric antiviral treatment might provide some benefit (e.g., shortened duration of illness).<html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html> Use clinical judgment to make decisions regarding use of antivirals in such individuals; antivirals not needed in those already beginning to recover.<html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html>

If indicated, initiate antiviral treatment as soon as possible after illness onset (ideally within 48 hours);<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html> do not delay initiation of treatment while waiting for laboratory confirmation.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html>

For treatment of suspected or confirmed severe, complicated influenza in hospitalized patients or outpatients, oseltamivir is the preferred antiviral because of lack of data regarding use of other influenza antivirals (zanamivir, peramivir, baloxavir marboxil) in such patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> Oseltamivir also preferred antiviral for treatment of suspected or confirmed influenza in women who are pregnant or ≤2 weeks postpartum.<html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>142</sup></body></html>

For treatment of suspected or confirmed acute, uncomplicated influenza in otherwise healthy outpatients, CDC, IDSA, and others state that any age-appropriate influenza antiviral (oseltamivir, zanamivir, peramivir, baloxavir marboxil) can be used if not contraindicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html>

Consider viral surveillance data available from local and state health departments and CDC when selecting an antiviral for treatment of seasonal influenza.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html> Strains of circulating influenza viruses and the antiviral susceptibility of these strains constantly evolve;<html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html> emergence of resistant strains may decrease effectiveness of influenza antivirals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

CDC issues recommendations concerning use of antivirals for treatment of influenza, and these recommendations are updated as needed during each influenza season.<html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> Information regarding influenza surveillance and updated recommendations for treatment of seasonal influenza are available from CDC at [Web].

Baloxavir Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally without regard to meals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid taking with dairy products; calcium-fortified beverages; aluminum-, calcium-, or magnesium-containing antacids; polyvalent cation-containing laxatives; or multivitamins or dietary supplements containing calcium, iron, magnesium, selenium, or zinc.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

Dosage

Adolescents ≥12 years of age weighing 40 to <80 kg: Single 40-mg dose given within 48 hours after symptom onset.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adolescents ≥12 years of age weighing ≥80 kg: Single 80-mg dose given within 48 hours after symptom onset.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adults weighing 40 to <80 kg: Single 40-mg dose given within 48 hours after symptom onset.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adults weighing ≥80 kg: Single 80-mg dose given within 48 hours after symptom onset.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations at this time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Baloxavir

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to baloxavir marboxil or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to baloxavir marboxil or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

When making treatment decisions in patients with suspected influenza, consider possibility of primary or concomitant bacterial infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Serious bacterial infections may begin with influenza-like symptoms or may coexist with or occur as complications of influenza.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> No evidence that baloxavir prevents such complications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If a bacterial infection occurs, treat as appropriate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No evidence that baloxavir is effective for illness caused by any organisms other than influenza viruses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Influenza antivirals are not a substitute for annual vaccination with a seasonal influenza vaccine (influenza virus vaccine inactivated, influenza vaccine live intranasal, influenza vaccine recombinant).<html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>144</sup></body></html>

Although influenza virus vaccine inactivated or influenza vaccine recombinant may be administered to individuals receiving influenza antivirals,<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>134</sup></body></html> such antivirals may inhibit the vaccine virus contained in influenza vaccine live intranasal and decrease efficacy of this influenza vaccine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>134</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

Data not available regarding use of baloxavir marboxil in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No adverse embryofetal effects were observed in reproduction studies in rats and rabbits.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pregnant women are at increased risk for severe complications from influenza,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>142</sup></body></html> which may lead to adverse pregnancy and/or fetal outcomes including maternal death, stillbirths, birth defects, preterm delivery, low birthweight, and small size for gestational age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

CDC states baloxavir marboxil not recommended for treatment of influenza in pregnant women because of lack of safety and efficacy data in such patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>142</sup></body></html> Oseltamivir is the preferred antiviral for treatment of suspected or confirmed influenza in women who are pregnant or ≤2 weeks postpartum.<html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>137</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>142</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributes into human milk, affects milk production, or has any effects on breast-fed infants.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Distributed into milk in rats.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states consider benefits of breast-feeding and importance of baloxavir marboxil to the woman; also consider potential adverse effects on the breast-fed child from the drug or the underlying maternal condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

CDC states baloxavir marboxil not recommended for treatment of influenza in nursing women because of lack of safety data in such patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>142</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients <12 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy in adolescents ≥12 years of age similar to that reported in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Clinical studies to date have not included patients ≥65 years of age;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> insufficient data to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

GI effects (nausea, diarrhea), bronchitis, nasopharyngitis, headache.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Baloxavir

Active metabolite of baloxavir marboxil, baloxavir, is metabolized principally by UGT1A3 and, to a minor extent, by CYP3A4.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Baloxavir marboxil and baloxavir do not inhibit CYP1A2, 2B6, 2C8, 2C9, 2C19, or 2D6 and do not induce CYP1A2, 2B6, or 3A4 in vitro.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Baloxavir marboxil and baloxavir do not inhibit UGT1A1, 1A3, 1A4, 1A6, 1A9, 2B7, or 2B15 in vitro.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Baloxavir does not inhibit organic anion transport polypeptide (OATP) 1B1, OATP1B3, organic cation transporter (OCT) 1, OCT2, organic anion transporter (OAT) 1, OAT3, multidrug and toxin extrusion (MATE) 1, or MATE2K.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Baloxavir marboxil and baloxavir are substrates of P-glycoprotein (P-gp) in vitro.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Baloxavir Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following oral administration of baloxavir marboxil, rapidly and almost completely converted to active metabolite, baloxavir,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> by esterases in GI lumen, liver, and blood.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Median time to peak plasma concentrations of baloxavir after oral administration of baloxavir marboxil is 4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Food decreases peak plasma concentrations and AUC of baloxavir by approximately 48 and 36%, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Baloxavir may form chelates with polyvalent cations (e.g., aluminum, calcium, iron, magnesium, selenium, zinc);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> systemic baloxavir exposures may be decreased if baloxavir marboxil is administered with dairy products, calcium-fortified beverages, or other foods, drugs, or preparations containing polyvalent cations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

Baloxavir and its related metabolites are distributed into milk in rats;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Approximately 93–94% in vitro.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions and Spectrum

  • Baloxavir marboxil is a prodrug of baloxavir, a polymerase acidic (PA) endonuclease inhibitor antiviral.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> Inactive until hydrolyzed in vivo to baloxavir.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Baloxavir selectively inhibits cap-dependent endonuclease activity of the PA subunit of the viral RNA polymerase complex and prevents transcription of influenza viral messenger RNA, which is required for viral replication.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

  • Active in vitro against laboratory and clinical isolates of influenza A and B viruses, and has been active against some influenza strains resistant to neuraminidase inhibitors (oseltamivir, peramivir, zanamivir).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of reading patient information provided by the manufacturer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of initiating baloxavir marboxil as soon as possible after first appearance of influenza symptoms (≤48 hours after symptom onset).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that baloxavir marboxil may be taken with or without food, but should not be taken with dairy products or calcium-fortified beverages or with antacids, laxatives, multivitamins, or dietary supplements containing polyvalent cations (e.g., calcium, iron, magnesium, selenium, zinc).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that influenza antivirals such as baloxavir marboxil may decrease effectiveness of influenza virus vaccine live;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> importance of consulting clinician before receiving the live intranasal influenza vaccine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
