Uses for Iodine

Antisepsis

Prevention of infection in the management of minor, superficial skin wounds,<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> wet wounds, and wet ulcers;<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> reduces microbial load in wound environment.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Cadexomer iodine pad and gel used in the cleansing of wet ulcers and wounds (e.g., venous stasis ulcers, pressure sores, and infected traumatic and surgical wounds).<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> Not effective in cleaning dry wounds.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Has been used to disinfect the skin preoperatively.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Wound Healing

Cadexomer iodine pad and gel used to slow the formation of eschar; keeps lesions soft and pliable.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Iodine Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to affected areas as a solution containing iodine 2%, a tincture containing iodine 2% with alcohol 47%, or as a cadexomer iodine 0.9% pad or gel.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

To avoid irritation, iodine solution or tincture-treated wounds should not be covered tightly with occlusive dressings.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Dermatologic Effects under Cautions.)

For external use only;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> do not use in eyes<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> or mouth.<html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html>

For self-medication, do not apply over large areas of body.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Consult a doctor for deep or puncture wounds, animal bites, or serious burns.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Prior to topical application, cleanse wound and surrounding area with sterile water or saline.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> Gently blot wound surface to remove excess fluid; wound surface should be slightly moist.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

While wearing gloves, remove gauze from one or both sides of the pad and then place pad on wound surface.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> Cover with dry sterile dressing (e.g., gauze); apply compression bandage if appropriate (i.e., for venous stasis ulcers).<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Remove pad from wound site with sterile water or saline; if necessary, soak gauze for a few minutes prior to removal.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> Prior to reapplying pad, gently blot wound surface to remove excess fluid, leaving wound surface slightly moist.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Prior to topical application, cleanse wound and surrounding area with a gentle stream of sterile water or saline; do not dry wound surface.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

While wearing gloves, apply a sufficient amount of gel to entirely cover wound (i.e., 0.3–0.6 cm [0.125–0.25 inch] thick) to a clean, dry sterile gauze and then apply gauze to wound site.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Remove gel from wound site with a gentle stream of sterile water or saline; may use a sterile wet swab if necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> Prior to reapplying gel, gently blot wound surface to remove excess fluid, leaving wound surface slightly moist.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Dosage varies depending on whether the drug is administered as conventional iodine (solution or tincture) or as cadexomer iodine (pad or gel).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Solution or tincture: Apply to affected areas as necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Self-medication (solution or tincture): Apply a small amount to affected areas 1–3 times daily for a maximum of 10 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Cadexomer iodine pad: Apply pad to wound site; change pad 3 times weekly or whenever product color has changed from brown to yellow-gray.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> Reduce number of applications as exudate diminishes at wound site;<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> discontinue therapy when wound is free of exudate.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Cadexomer iodine gel: Apply a sufficient amount of gel to entirely cover wound (i.e., 0.3–0.6 cm [0.125–0.25 inch] thick) to a clean, dry sterile gauze and then apply gauze to wound site.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> Reapply gel 3 times weekly or whenever product color has changed from brown to yellow-gray.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> Reduce number of applications as exudate diminishes at wound site;<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> discontinue therapy when wound is free of exudate.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of cross contamination with using cadexomer iodine pad or tube of gel for >1 patient; limit use of a single pad or tube of gel to one patient to reduce the possibility of cross contamination.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Vapors may cause eye or respiratory tract irritation.<html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html>

Possible slight transient pain within the first hour following application of cadexomer iodine.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> Less frequently, moderate pain may persist for several hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Iodine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Absorbed systemically following application of cadexomer iodine pad or gel.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Following application of cadexomer pad or gel, iodine released slowly over 24–72 hours (depending on amount of wound exudate) from cadexomer beads as they absorb fluid.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Iodide readily crosses the placenta and is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Tight, light-resistant containers at ≤35°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

15–30°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Bactericidal;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> potent and rapidly acting germicide.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Active against gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Highly effective against methicillin-resistant strains of Staphylococcus aureus.<html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

  • Fungicidal, protozoacidal (e.g., trichomonocidal), cysticidal, virucidal, and some sporicidal activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Germicidal mechanism of action not fully determined;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> may form N-iodo derivatives by reacting with basic NH groups on amino acids and nucleotide bases;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> may disrupt protein synthesis by oxidizing the sulfhydryl group of cysteine;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> may sterically hinder hydrogen bonding of tyrosine by reacting with the phenolic group of tyrosine;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> and may disrupt physical properties of lipids by reacting with carbon-carbon double bonds of unsaturated fatty acids.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
