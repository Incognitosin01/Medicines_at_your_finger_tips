Introduction

Antivenin (antivenom); ovine IgG Fab fragments capable of binding and neutralizing venom toxins of Crotalinae (pit vipers, crotalines; formerly known as Crotalidae or crotalids) native to North America.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Uses for Crotalidae Polyvalent Immune Fab

North American Crotaline Snakebite Envenomation

Treatment of envenomation following snakebites involving North American Crotalinae (pit vipers, Crotalinae, crotalines; formerly known as Crotalidae or crotalids).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>42</sup></body></html> Designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Crotalinae subfamily of venomous snakes includes rattlesnakes, copperheads, and cottonmouths or water moccasins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May be effective in management of minimal, moderate, or severe envenomation involving Crotalus atrox (Western diamondback rattlesnake), C. adamanteus (Eastern diamondback rattlesnake), C. scutulatus (Mojave rattlesnake), Agkistrodon piscivorus (cottonmouth or water moccasin), and other North American crotalines.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>42</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>49</sup></body></html>

Consultation with experts experienced in treating snakebites (e.g., regional certified poison control center at 800-222-1222) recommended to guide treatment decisions regarding individual patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer by IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Crotalidae Polyvalent Immune Fab

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to papaya or papain is a contraindication, unless benefits outweigh risks and appropriate drugs and equipment for management of anaphylactic reactions are readily available.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Papain Hypersensitivity under Cautions.)

Known hypersensitivity to papaya or papain is a contraindication, unless benefits outweigh risks and appropriate drugs and equipment for management of anaphylactic reactions are readily available.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Papain Hypersensitivity under Cautions.)

Warnings/Precautions

Contains purified immunoglobulin fragments from blood of sheep immunized with snake venom.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Actions.) Heterologous animal proteins can cause severe acute hypersensitivity reactions (anaphylaxis, anaphylactoid reactions), delayed hypersensitivity reactions (late serum reaction, serum sickness), or possible febrile responses to immune complexes formed by animal antibodies and neutralized venom components.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Skin sensitivity testing not considered necessary and not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Use caution if repeat course is indicated for a subsequent envenomation episode since patients may become sensitized to the antivenin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Acute allergic reactions, including anaphylaxis and anaphylactoid reactions, may occur during or shortly after IV infusion of Crotalidae polyvalent immune Fab (ovine).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>43</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>44</sup></body></html>

Immediate hypersensitivity reactions, including hypotension, tongue swelling, chest discomfort, angioedema, bronchospasm, wheezing, tracheal edema, dyspnea, and/or lip swelling, reported in 0.1–6% of patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>43</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>44</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>49</sup></body></html> Rarely, immediate hypersensitivity reaction appeared to be anaphylactoid reaction related to extremely rapid IV infusion rate (i.e., 640 mL/hour).<html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Monitor closely for signs and symptoms of acute hypersensitivity (e.g., urticaria, pruritus, erythema, angioedema, bronchospasm with wheezing or cough, stridor, laryngeal edema, hypotension, tachycardia).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If anaphylaxis or any severe hypersensitivity reaction occurs, immediately discontinue IV infusion and initiate appropriate therapy (e.g., epinephrine, corticosteroids, maintenance of adequate airway, oxygen, IV fluids, IV antihistamines, albuterol, maintenance of BP) as indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Delayed hypersensitivity or serum reactions may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html>

Late serum reactions (rash, pruritus, urticaria, or serum sickness consisting of severe rash and pruritus) reported in 14% (6/42) of patients in initial clinical trials;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> in other studies, delayed hypersensitivity or serum sickness reported in 5–11% of patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>49</sup></body></html>

Most common signs and symptoms are rash and fever.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Generally mild and respond to treatment with antihistamines and corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Monitor for signs and symptoms of delayed hypersensitivity reactions or serum sickness (e.g., rash, fever, myalgia, arthralgia) for up to 2–3 weeks; initiate appropriate treatment if necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Traces of papain or inactivated papain residues may be present in the Crotalidae polyvalent immune Fab (ovine);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> papain is used in manufacturing process.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Individuals allergic to papain, chymopapain, other papaya extracts, or the pineapple enzyme bromelain may be at risk of hypersensitivity reaction to the antivenin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Patients allergic to dust mites or latex may also be allergic to papain.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications under Cautions.)

Coagulopathic effects, such as thrombocytopenia (platelet counts <150,000/mm3), hypofibrinogenemia (fibrinogen concentrations <150 mg/dL), and prolonged PT and PTT, are common in many snakebite victims (especially those with severe envenomation)<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>40</sup></body></html> and occur because snake venom can activate or inhibit activity of various coagulation factors and interfere with blood coagulation cascade.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>40</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Crotalidae polyvalent immune Fab (ovine) is an antivenin (antivenom) preparation of venom-specific Fab fragments of ovine IgG that bind and neutralize venom toxins of Crotalinae (pit vipers, crotalines; formerly known as Crotalidae or crotalids) native to North America.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Following IV administration, the Fab fragments in Crotalidae polyvalent immune Fab (ovine) bind to venom components, facilitating redistribution of venom away from target tissues and elimination from the body.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Mixture of 4 different monospecific antivenins derived from serum of healthy sheep immunized with one of the following North American snake venoms: C. atrox (Western diamondback rattlesnake), C. adamanteus (Eastern diamondback rattlesnake), C. scutulatus (Mojave rattlesnake), or A. piscivorus (cottonmouth or water moccasin).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Each monospecific antivenin is prepared by fractionating IgG from the ovine serum, digesting it with papain, and isolating venom-specific Fab fragments using ion exchange and affinity chromatography columns and then mixed together.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Standardized by ability to neutralize lethal action of the 4 crotaline venoms following IV injection in mice (mouse LD50 neutralizing units).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> One neutralizing unit is determined as the amount of mixed monospecific Fab proteins necessary to neutralize one LD50 of each of the 4 venoms, where the LD50 is amount of venom that would be lethal in 50% of mice.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Murine lethality tests indicate that the antivenin has cross-neutralizing ability with venoms from some other clinically important North American crotalines, including venoms from C. horridus atricaudatus (Canebrake rattlesnake), A. contortrix contortrix (Southern copperhead), Sistrurus miliarius barbouri (Southeastern pygmy rattlesnake), and C. horridus horridus (Timber rattlesnake), but is less potent against venoms from C. viridis helleri (Southern Pacific rattlesnake) and C. molossus molossus (Mexican black-tailed rattlesnake).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of immediately contacting a clinician if any unusual bruising or bleeding (e.g., nosebleeds, excessive bleeding after brushing teeth, blood in stools or urine, excessive menstrual bleeding, petechiae, excessive bruising or persistent oozing from superficial injuries) occurs after hospital discharge.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Advise patients that such bruising or bleeding may occur for 1 week or longer following initial treatment of envenomation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of immediately contacting a clinician if any manifestations of delayed allergic reactions or serum sickness (e.g., rash, pruritus, urticaria) occur after hospital discharge.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
