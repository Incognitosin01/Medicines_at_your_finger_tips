Uses for Glatiramer

Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

Treatment of patients with relapsing forms of MS (e.g., relapsing-remitting MS [RRMS]).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

Glatiramer acetate is one of several disease-modifying therapies used in the management of RRMS.<html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>77</sup></body></html> Although not curative, these therapies have all been shown to modify several measures of disease activity, including relapse rates, new or enhancing magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) lesions, and disability progression.<html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>78</sup></body></html>

The American Academy of Neurology (AAN) recommends that disease-modifying therapy be offered to patients with RRMS who have had recent relapses and/or MRI activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> Clinicians should consider adverse effects, tolerability, method of administration, safety, efficacy, and cost of the drugs in addition to patient preferences when selecting an appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>77</sup></body></html>

Clinical experience suggests that efficacy of glatiramer acetate is generally similar to that of interferon beta.<html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> Efficacy of glatiramer acetate compared with other disease-modifying therapies (e.g., fingolimod, mitoxantrone, natalizumab) not fully evaluated to date.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>26</sup></body></html>

Glatiramer Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer only by sub-Q injection; do not administer IV.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer initial self-administered dose under supervision of a qualified clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Several preparations of glatiramer acetate are commercially available (e.g., Copaxone, Glatopa and other generic preparations), and there are 2 available dosage strengths.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> The 20-mg/mL injection is administered once daily and the 40-mg/mL injection is administered 3 times a week; the 2 dosage strengths are not interchangeable.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Commercially available prefilled syringes intended for single use only; discard unused portion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Allow prefilled syringes to reach room temperature by removing from refrigerator about 20 minutes prior to use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Inject sub-Q into the arm, abdomen, hip, or thigh.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

To minimize risk of localized lipoatrophy and skin necrosis, follow proper injection technique and rotate injection areas and sites with each injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>22</sup></body></html> (See Lipoatrophy and Skin Necrosis under Cautions.)

Dosage

Available as glatiramer acetate; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage recommendations differ based on dosage strength (20 or 40 mg/mL) used.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Dosage strengths are not interchangeable.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If using the 20-mg/mL injection, recommended dosage is 20 mg once daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

If using the 40-mg/mL injection, recommended dosage is 40 mg 3 times a week (administer injections at least 48 hours apart).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations at this time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Glatiramer Pharmacokinetics

Distribution

Following sub-Q injection, some portion of the dose may enter the lymphatic circulation and some may enter systemic circulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Does not appear to cross the blood-brain barrier.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Substantial portion of sub-Q dose appears to be hydrolyzed locally at injection site to small oligopeptides and free amino acids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

2–8°C; protect from light.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May be stored at room temperature (15–30°C) for ≤1 month.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of reading the manufacturer’s patient information prior to beginning glatiramer acetate therapy and each time prescription is refilled.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of clinicians instructing patients and/or caregivers in proper injection techniques (including use of aseptic technique) and about avoiding reuse of syringes and needles and proper disposal of such equipment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of post-injection reaction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of advising patients that glatiramer acetate may cause various symptoms after injection, including flushing, chest pain, palpitations, anxiety, dyspnea, throat constriction, and urticaria.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of informing patients that these symptoms usually are transient and self-limited and do not require specific treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> However, patients should be advised to contact their clinician immediately if any serious reactions (e.g., flushing, chest pain, tachycardia, anxiety, breathing difficulties or tightness in the throat, swelling, rash, hives, itching) occur immediately after injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Patients also should be informed that such reactions may occur early or begin several months after initiation of treatment, and that they may experience one or several episodes of these symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients that they may experience chest pain either as part of a post-injection reaction or in isolation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Patients should be informed that the pain should be transient (usually lasting only for a few minutes).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Some patients may experience more than one such episode, usually beginning at least one month after beginning treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Advise patients to seek medical attention if they experience chest pain of unusual duration or intensity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of lipoatrophy and skin necrosis at injection site.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>22</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> Advise patients to follow proper injection technique and rotate injection areas and sites with each injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of the recommended storage instructions for glatiramer acetate (see Storage under Stability).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of advising patients not to expose the drug to higher temperatures, freezing temperatures, or to intense light.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of patient informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
