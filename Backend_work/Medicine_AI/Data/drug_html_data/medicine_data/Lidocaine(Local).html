For IV regional anesthesia, use 50-mL single-dose vials containing lidocaine hydrochloride 0.5% only.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as lidocaine hydrochloride, as fixed combination containing lidocaine hydrochloride and epinephrine bitartrate or epinephrine hydrochloride, and as lidocaine hydrochloride in dextrose injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>214</sup></body></html> Dosage expressed in terms of lidocaine hydrochloride.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>214</sup></body></html>

When larger dosages (volumes) are required, use preparations containing epinephrine (unless contraindicated).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Use lower dosages than those suggested for healthy adults (see Adults under Dosage and Administration).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Children <10 years of age: 18–20 mg (0.9–1 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 2% solution is adequate for a procedure involving 1 tooth (local infiltration), 2–3 teeth (maxillary infiltration), or teeth in an entire quadrant (mandibular block).<html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html>

Percutaneous: 5–300 mg (1–60 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 0.5 or 1% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

IV regional: 50–300 mg (10–60 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 0.5% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Brachial: 225–300 mg (15–20 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1.5% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Dental: 20–100 mg (1–5 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 2% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Intercostal: 30 mg (3 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Paravertebral: 30–50 mg (3–5 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Pudendal: 100 mg (10 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1% solution (without epinephrine) on each side (for a total dose of 200 mg).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Obstetrical analgesia: 100 mg (10 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1% solution (without epinephrine) on each side (for a total dose of 200 mg).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Cervical (stellate ganglion): 50 mg (5 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Lumbar: 50–100 mg (5–10 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

120–200 mg (3–5 mL) or 1.7–3 mg/kg of lidocaine hydrochloride 4% solution is suggested; a portion of the dose is injected retrobulbarly and the remainder may be used to block the facial nerve.<html><head></head><body><sup>212</sup></body></html>

80–120 mg (2–3 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 4% solution is administered rapidly.<html><head></head><body><sup>212</sup></body></html>

If both transtracheal injection and topical application (oropharyngeal spray) are needed to achieve complete analgesia, combined total dose from transtracheal injection and oropharyngeal spray should be ≤200 mg (5 mL) of 4% solution or ≤3 mg/kg.<html><head></head><body><sup>212</sup></body></html>

Prior to lumbar epidural block, administer test dose to detect accidental intravascular injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> Test doses containing 10–15 mcg epinephrine and 30–45 mg (2–3 mL) of 1.5% lidocaine hydrochloride have been suggested.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> Repeat test dose if patient is moved in a manner that could displace catheter.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Thoracic epidural: 200–300 mg (20–30 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> Dosage is determined by number of dermatomes to be anesthetized (generally 2–3 mL per dermatome).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Lumbar epidural (for anesthesia): 225–300 mg (15–20 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1.5% solution (without epinephrine) or 200–300 mg (10–15 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 2% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> Dosage is determined by number of dermatomes to be anesthetized (generally 2–3 mL per dermatome).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Prior to caudal block, administer test dose to detect accidental intravascular injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> Test doses containing 10–15 mcg epinephrine and 30–45 mg (2–3 mL) of 1.5% lidocaine hydrochloride have been suggested.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> Repeat test dose if patient is moved in a manner that could displace catheter.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Obstetrical analgesia: 200–300 mg (20–30 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Surgical anesthesia: 225–300 mg (15–20 mL) of lidocaine hydrochloride 1.5% solution (without epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

For children >3 years of age with normal lean body mass and normal development, maximum dose is determined by child’s age and weight.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> For example, dosage for a 5-year old child weighing 50 lbs should not exceed 75–100 mg (3.3–4.4 mg/kg or 1.5–2 mg/lb).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> For IV regional anesthesia, maximum 3 mg/kg (1.4 mg/lb), using more dilute solutions (e.g., lidocaine hydrochloride 0.25 or 0.5% solution).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> (See Dilution under Dosage and Administration: Administration.)

For children <10 years of age with normal lean body mass and normal development, maximum dose is determined by using standard pediatric drug formulas (e.g., Clark’s rule).<html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> For example, dosage for a 5-year old child weighing 50 lbs should not exceed 75–100 mg (3.3–4.4 mg/kg or 1.5–2 mg/lb).<html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html>

Maximum 4.5 mg/kg (2 mg/lb) of body weight (without epinephrine) or 7 mg/kg (3.2 mg/lb) of body weight (with epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html>

Maximum 4.5 mg/kg (2 mg/lb) of body weight (up to 300 mg) (without epinephrine) or 7 mg/kg (3.2 mg/lb) of body weight (up to 500 mg) (with epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

IV regional: Maximum 4 mg/kg.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Obstetric or nonobstetric use: Maximum 200 mg (100 mg each side) per 90-minute period.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Maximum recommended dosage should not be administered at intervals of <90 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html>

Dosages >100 mg not required if technique and needle placement are correct.<html><head></head><body><sup>214</sup></body></html>

Maximum 4.5 mg/kg (2 mg/lb) of body weight (up to 500 mg) (without epinephrine) or 7 mg/kg (3.2 mg/lb) of body weight (up to 300 mg) (with epinephrine).<html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html>

Absorption

Systemic absorption dependent upon route of administration, administration site, and presence or absence of epinephrine in formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>214</sup></body></html> Except for intravascular administration, intercostal nerve block results in highest blood levels, and sub-Q administration results in lowest blood levels.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>214</sup></body></html>

Possible accumulation with repeated dosing.<html><head></head><body><sup>211</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>214</sup></body></html>

Longer duration of anesthesia compared with procaine hydrochloride.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Duration of about 100 minutes following lumbar epidural block with 2% solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
