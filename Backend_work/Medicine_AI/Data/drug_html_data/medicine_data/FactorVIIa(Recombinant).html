Uses for Factor VIIa (Recombinant)

Hemophilia A or B with Inhibitors

Factor VIIa (Recombinant) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Circulating factor VIIa concentrations increase approximately 1000-fold following administration of factor VIIa (recombinant).<html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>26</sup></body></html>

Mean maximum factor VII activity observed approximately 10 minutes following single IV infusion in one study.<html><head></head><body><sup>30</sup></body></html>

Factor VII activity decreases rapidly and returns to baseline within 24 hours following IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>30</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Distributes into a volume 2–3 times that of plasma.<html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

2.3 hours (range: 1.7–2.7 hours) in patients with hemophilia A or B and 2.8–3.1 hours in patients with congenital factor VII deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Clearance in children is increased compared with adolescents and adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>

Half-life in children is shorter than that observed in adolescents and adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>
