Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy (REMS):

FDA approved a REMS for belantamab mafodotin-blmf to ensure that the benefits outweigh the risks. The REMS may apply to one or more preparations of belantamab mafodotin-blmf. Further information is available at [Web] and 1-855-209-9188. (See also Restricted Distribution under Cautions.)

Introduction

Belantamab mafodotin-blmf, a B-cell maturation antigen (BCMA)-directed antibody and microtubule inhibitor conjugate, is an antineoplastic agent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Uses for Belantamab Mafodotin-blmf

Belantamab mafodotin-blmf has the following uses:

Belantamab mafodotin-blmf is indicated for the treatment of adult patients with relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma who have received at least 4 prior therapies including an anti-CD38 monoclonal antibody, a proteasome inhibitor, and an immunomodulatory agent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

This indication is approved under accelerated approval based on response rate. Continued approval for this indication may be contingent upon verification and description of clinical benefit in confirmatory trial(s).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Ocular adverse reactions occurred in 77% of the 218 patients in the pooled safety population. Ocular adverse reactions included keratopathy (76%), changes in visual acuity (55%), blurred vision (27%), and dry eye (19%). Among patients with keratopathy (n = 165), 49% had ocular symptoms, 65% had clinically relevant visual acuity changes (decline of 2 or more lines on Snellen Visual Acuity in any eye), and 34% had both ocular symptoms and visual acuity changes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Keratopathy was reported as Grade 1 in 7% of patients, Grade 2 in 22%, Grade 3 in 45%, and Grade 4 in 0.5% per the KVA scale. Cases of corneal ulcer (ulcerative and infective keratitis) have been reported. Most keratopathy events developed within the first 2 treatment cycles (cumulative incidence of 65% by Cycle 2). Of the patients with Grade 2 to 4 keratopathy (n = 149), 39% of patients recovered to Grade 1 or lower after median follow-up of 6.2 months. Of the 61% who had ongoing keratopathy, 28% were still on treatment and 9% were in follow-up; in 24%, the follow-up ended due to death or study withdrawal, or the patients were lost to follow up. For patients in whom events resolved, the median time to resolution was 2 months (range: 11 days to 8.3 months).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

A clinically significant decrease in visual acuity of worse than 20/40 in the better-seeing eye was observed in 19% of the 218 patients and of 20/200 or worse in the better-seeing eye in 1.4%. Of the patients with decreased visual acuity of worse than 20/40, 88% resolved and the median time to resolution was 22 days (range: 7 days to 4.2 months). Of the patients with decreased visual acuity of 20/200 or worse, all resolved and the median duration was 22 days (range: 15 to 22 days).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Conduct ophthalmic examinations (visual acuity and slit lamp) at baseline, prior to each dose, and promptly for worsening symptoms. Perform baseline examinations within 3 weeks prior to the first dose. Perform each follow-up examination at least 1 week after the previous dose and within 2 weeks prior to the next dose. Withhold belantamab mafodotin-blmf until improvement and resume at same or reduced dose, or consider discontinuing permanently based on severity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advise patients to use preservative-free lubricant eye drops at least 4 times a day starting with the first infusion and continuing until end of treatment. Avoid use of contact lenses unless directed by an ophthalmologist.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Changes in visual acuity may be associated with difficulty in driving and reading. Advise patients to use caution when driving or operating machinery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Belantamab mafodotin-blmf is available only through a restricted program under a REMS called the Blenrep REMS because of the risks of ocular toxicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for infusion-related reactions. For Grade 2 or 3 reactions, interrupt the infusion and provide supportive treatment. Once symptoms resolve, resume at a lower infusion rate. Administer premedication for all subsequent infusions. Discontinue belantamab mafodotin-blmf for life-threatening infusion-related reactions and provide appropriate emergency care.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Based on its mechanism of action, belantamab mafodotin-blmf can cause fetal harm when administered to a pregnant woman because it contains a genotoxic compound (the microtubule inhibitor, monomethyl auristatin F [MMAF]) and it targets actively dividing cells.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Risk Summary: Based on its mechanism of action, belantamab mafodotin-blmf can cause fetal harm when administered to a pregnant woman, because it contains a genotoxic compound (the microtubule inhibitor, MMAF) and it targets actively dividing cells. Human immunoglobulin G (IgG) is known to cross the placenta; therefore, belantamab mafodotin-blmf has the potential to be transmitted from the mother to the developing fetus. There are no available data on the use of belantamab mafodotin-blmf in pregnant women to evaluate for drug-associated risk. No animal reproduction studies were conducted with belantamab mafodotin-blmf. Advise pregnant women of the potential risk to a fetus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

The estimated background risk of major birth defects and miscarriage for the indicated population is unknown. All pregnancies have a background risk of birth defect, loss, or other adverse outcome. In the U.S. general population, the estimated background risk of major birth defects and miscarriage in clinically recognized pregnancies is 2% to 4% and 15% to 20%, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Animal Data: Animal reproductive or developmental toxicity studies were not conducted with belantamab mafodotin-blmf. The cytotoxic component of belantamab mafodotin-blmf, MMAF, disrupts microtubule function, is genotoxic, and can be toxic to rapidly dividing cells, suggesting it has the potential to cause embryotoxicity and teratogenicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

There is no data on the presence of belantamab mafodotin-blmf in human milk or the effects on the breastfed child or milk production. Because of the potential for serious adverse reactions in the breastfed child, advise women not to breastfeed during treatment with belantamab mafodotin-blmf and for 3 months after the last dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

The safety and effectiveness of belantamab mafodotin-blmf in pediatric patients have not been established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Of the 218 patients who received belantamab mafodotin-blmf in DREAMM-2, 43% were aged 65 to less than 75 years and 17% were aged 75 years and older. Clinical studies of belantamab mafodotin-blmf did not include sufficient numbers of patients aged 65 and older to determine whether the effectiveness differs compared with that of younger patients. Keratopathy occurred in 80% of patients aged less than 65 years and 73% of patients aged 65 years and older. Among the patients who received belantamab mafodotin-blmf at the 2.5-mg/kg dose in DREAMM-2 (n = 95), keratopathy occurred in 67% of patients aged less than 65 years and 73% of patients aged 65 years and older. Clinical studies did not include sufficient numbers of patients 75 years and older to determine whether they respond differently compared with younger patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

Mechanism of Action

Belantamab mafodotin-blmf is an antibody-drug conjugate (ADC). The antibody component is an afucosylated IgG1 directed against BCMA, a protein expressed on normal B lymphocytes and multiple myeloma cells. The small molecule component is MMAF, a microtubule inhibitor. Upon binding to BCMA, belantamab mafodotin-blmf is internalized followed by release of MMAF via proteolytic cleavage. The released MMAF intracellularly disrupts the microtubule network, leading to cell cycle arrest and apoptosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Belantamab mafodotin-blmf had antitumor activity in multiple myeloma cells and mediated killing of tumor cells through MMAF-induced apoptosis, as well as by tumor cell lysis through antibody-dependent cellular cytotoxicity (ADCC) and antibody-dependent cellular phagocytosis (ADCP).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
