Introduction

Antiparkinsonian; levodopa is the levorotatory isomer of dihydroxyphenylalanine and the metabolic precursor of dopamine, and carbidopa is a decarboxylase inhibitor that inhibits the peripheral decarboxylation of levodopa to dopamine.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Uses for Levodopa/Carbidopa

Parkinsonian Syndrome

Levodopa is used for symptomatic treatment of parkinsonian syndrome (e.g., parkinsonism, idiopathic Parkinson disease [paralysis agitans]); also used for postencephalitic parkinsonism and symptomatic parkinsonian syndrome resulting from carbon monoxide intoxication or manganese intoxication.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>117</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Carbidopa is used in conjunction with levodopa to inhibit decarboxylation of peripheral levodopa and increase the amount of levodopa available for transport to the brain.<html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html>

Levodopa (in combination with carbidopa) is the most effective drug for relieving motor symptoms of parkinsonian syndrome and is considered the drug of choice for this use.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Levodopa provides symptomatic relief (e.g., akinesia, rigidity, tremor) and improves functional ability;<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> however, effectiveness decreases over time and most patients develop motor fluctuations and dyskinesias (drug-induced involuntary movements) with long-term use.<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>157</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>160</sup></body></html>

Strategies for reducing the risk of motor complications associated with long-term levodopa therapy include adjusting dosage of levodopa, adding adjunctive antiparkinsonian agents (e.g., dopamine agonist [e.g., pramipexole, ropinirole, rotigotine], selective monoamine oxidase [MAO]-B inhibitor [e.g., rasagiline, safinamide, selegiline], catechol-O-methyltransferase [COMT] inhibitor [entacapone, tolcapone], amantadine), or initiating therapy with other antiparkinsonian agents first to delay use of levodopa.<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>157</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>160</sup></body></html> Delayed approach is often used in younger patients who have a higher risk of developing motor complications.<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>157</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>160</sup></body></html> Levodopa generally is used for initial therapy in patients >70 years of age, those with cognitive impairment, and those with severe disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html>

Drug-induced Extrapyramidal Effects

Not effective in the management of extrapyramidal effects† induced by antipsychotic agents (e.g., phenothiazines).<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Levodopa/Carbidopa Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally as conventional tablets, extended-release tablets, orally disintegrating tablets, or extended-release capsules.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>117</sup></body></html>

Extended-release tablets: Administer as whole or half tablets; do not chew or crush.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Extended-release capsules: Swallow whole without regard to food; do not chew, divide or crush.<html><head></head><body><sup>117</sup></body></html> In patients with difficulty swallowing, may open capsules and sprinkle entire contents on a small amount (e.g., 1–2 tablespoons) of applesauce; administer mixture immediately and do not store for later use.<html><head></head><body><sup>117</sup></body></html>

Orally disintegrating capsules: Just prior to administration, gently remove tablet from the bottle with dry hands.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Place tablet on tongue to dissolve (usually within seconds) and swallow with saliva.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Administration with water is not necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

Fixed combination levodopa, carbidopa, and entacapone tablets (Stalevo): Do not divide tablets; administer only one tablet per dosing interval.<html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Levodopa/Carbidopa Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Well absorbed from the GI tract; peak plasma levodopa concentrations achieved within 0.5 or 2 hours following administration of conventional tablets or extended-release tablets, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Bioavailability of levodopa from extended-release tablets 70–75% of that from conventional tablets.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Extended-release tablets result in less fluctuation in plasma concentrations between doses than conventional tablets.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html>

Levodopa-carbidopa conventional tablets and orally disintegrating tablets begin to release the drugs within 30 minutes of administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> Pharmacokinetic values for orally disintegrating tablet are similar to those for the conventional tablet.<html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html>
