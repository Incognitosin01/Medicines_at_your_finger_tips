Uses for Pneumococcal Vaccine

Prevention of Pneumococcal Disease

PCV13 (Prevnar 13): Prevention of invasive disease (e.g., pneumonia, meningitis, bacteremia) caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae in infants 6 weeks through 23 months of age, healthy children 2 through 5 years of age, children and adolescents 6 through 18 years of age at increased risk for pneumococcal disease, adults ≥19 years of age at increased risk for pneumococcal disease†, and adults ≥65 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>181</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>199</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>203</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>206</sup></body></html> Provides protection only against the 13 S. pneumoniae serotypes represented in the vaccine (i.e., 1, 3, 4, 5, 6A, 6B, 7F, 9V, 14, 18C, 19A, 19F, 23F).<html><head></head><body><sup>181</sup></body></html>

PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23): Prevention of invasive disease (e.g., pneumonia, meningitis, bacteremia) caused by S. pneumoniae in children 2 through 18 years of age at increased risk for pneumococcal disease, adults ≥19 years of age at increased risk for pneumococcal disease, and adults ≥65 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>129</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>169</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>203</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>206</sup></body></html> Provides protection only against the 23 S. pneumoniae serotypes represented in the vaccine (i.e., 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6B, 7F, 8, 9N, 9V, 10A, 11A, 12F, 14, 15B, 17F, 18C, 19F, 19A, 20, 22F, 23F, 33F).<html><head></head><body><sup>129</sup></body></html>

S. pneumoniae is a major cause of serious or invasive illness and death worldwide.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>166</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>169</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> In the US, pneumococcal pneumonia results in an estimated 175,000 hospitalizations each year (case fatality rate 5–7%); there are >50,000 cases of pneumococcal bacteremia (case fatality rate about 20%) and 3000–6000 cases of pneumococcal meningitis (case fatality rate about 30%) reported annually.<html><head></head><body><sup>166</sup></body></html> Case fatality rates are higher in the elderly (60–80% for pneumococcal bacteremia or meningitis in this age group).<html><head></head><body><sup>166</sup></body></html> In children <5 years of age, S. pneumoniae has been a leading cause of bacterial meningitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>166</sup></body></html>

Epidemiology of pneumococcal disease in the US changed substantially after routine infant and childhood vaccination against the disease was initiated in 2000.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> Overall incidence of invasive pneumococcal disease in children <5 years of age decreased from approximately 99 cases/100,000 population in 1998–1999 to 21 cases/100,000 population in 2008.<html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> Routine infant and childhood vaccination against pneumococcal disease also reduced incidence of invasive pneumococcal disease among unvaccinated individuals of all ages.<html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> Data from 1998–1999 and 2008 indicate that overall rates of invasive pneumococcal disease in individuals 18–49, 50–64, and ≥65 years of age decreased 34, 14, and 37%, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html>

PCV13 (Prevnar 13) is the pneumococcal vaccine recommended by the USPHS Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP), AAP, and others for routine primary and catch-up vaccination in all infants 2 through 23 months of age and healthy children 2 through 5 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>199</sup></body></html> Vaccine antigens in PCV13 (Prevnar 13) are conjugated to a T-cell dependent carrier protein (diphtheria CRM197 protein) and are more immunogenic in infants and young children than the unconjugated antigens contained in PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23).<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html>

PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23) contains 11 additional antigens not contained in PCV13 (Prevnar 13) and PCV13 contains one antigen not found in PPSV23; use of both vaccines provides benefits in terms of immunity against a broader range of serotypes.<html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>203</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> Therefore, ACIP, AAP, and others recommend vaccination with PCV13 (Prevnar 13) and PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23) in children ≥2 years of age, adolescents, and adults ≥19 years of age with certain medical conditions that put them at increased risk for pneumococcal disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>155</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>156</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>199</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>203</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>206</sup></body></html> Vaccination with PCV13 (Prevnar 13) and PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23) also is recommended in all adults ≥65 years of age or older.<html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> (See Vaccination Against Pneumococcal Disease in Groups At Risk under Uses.)

When both PCV13 (Prevnar 13) and PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23) are indicated, give the vaccines sequentially; do not give concurrently.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>199</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>203</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> If possible, give all required doses of PCV13 (Prevnar 13) before PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23).<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>199</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>203</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>204</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>205</sup></body></html> (See Dosage and Administration.)

Actions

  • PCV13 (Prevnar 13): Sterile suspension containing purified capsular polysaccharide antigens extracted from 13 serotypes of S. pneumoniae (i.e., 1, 3, 4, 5, 6A, 6B, 7F, 9V, 14, 18C, 19A, 19F, 23F) and individually conjugated to diphtheria CRM197 protein.<html><head></head><body><sup>181</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html>

  • PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23): Sterile solution containing purified capsular polysaccharide antigens extracted from 23 serotypes of S. pneumoniae (i.e., 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6B, 7F, 8, 9N, 9V, 10A, 11A, 12F, 14, 15B, 17F, 18C, 19F, 19A, 20, 22F, 23F, 33F).<html><head></head><body><sup>129</sup></body></html>

  • Principal mode of transmission of pneumococcal infection is the respiratory route, most commonly through close personal contact with an individual with invasive pneumococcal disease or direct exposure to nasopharyngeal secretions from an infected individual or by autoinoculation in persons carrying the bacteria in their upper respiratory tract.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>166</sup></body></html> S. pneumoniae is a common cause of AOM, sinusitis, and pneumonia and can cause invasive disease, including bacteremia and meningitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> Infection with S. pneumoniae has been a leading cause of bacterial meningitis in the US among children <5 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>166</sup></body></html>

  • Surveillance data from 2008 indicated that the 13 serotypes contained in PCV13 (Prevnar 13) accounted for approximately 61% of US cases of invasive pneumococcal disease occurring in children <5 years of age; serotype 19A accounted for 43% of cases.<html><head></head><body><sup>184</sup></body></html> Surveillance data from 2007–2009 indicated that 49% of cases of invasive pneumococcal disease in immunocompromised children and adolescents 6 through 18 years of age were caused by serotypes contained in PCV13 (Prevnar 13); an additional 23% of cases were caused by serotypes contained in PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23).<html><head></head><body><sup>203</sup></body></html> For adults, surveillance data from 2008 indicated that the 13 serotypes contained in PCV13 (Prevnar 13) accounted for 44–53% of invasive pneumococcal disease and the 23 serotypes contained in PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23) accounted for 66–78% of cases.<html><head></head><body><sup>169</sup></body></html> Data from 2010 indicated that 50% of cases of invasive pneumococcal disease in immunocompromised adults were caused by serotypes contained in PCV13 (Prevnar 13); an additional 21% of cases were caused by serotypes contained in PPSV23 (Pneumovax 23).<html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html>
