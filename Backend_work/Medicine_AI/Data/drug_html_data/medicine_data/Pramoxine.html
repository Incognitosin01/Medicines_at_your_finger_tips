Uses for Pramoxine

Dermatologic Conditions

Self-medication for the temporary relief of pain and itching associated with minor lip or skin irritations (e.g., dermatoses, insect bites, minor burns or sunburns, minor cuts or scrapes, cold sores, hives, rashes due to poison ivy, poison oak, or poison sumac).<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>f</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>k</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Anorectal/Anogenital Disorders

Self-medication for the temporary relief of pain, soreness, burning, itching, or discomfort associated with hemorrhoids and other anorectal or anogenital disorders (e.g., inflammation, irritation, fissures, anogenital pruritus).<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html>
