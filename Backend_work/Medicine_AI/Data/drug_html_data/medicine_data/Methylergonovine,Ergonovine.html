Introduction

Stimulates contractions of uterine smooth muscle; amine ergot alkaloids.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Uses for Methylergonovine, Ergonovine

Postpartum Hemorrhage

Prevention and treatment of postpartum hemorrhage in the presence of uterine atony.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>f</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Administration of parenteral ergot alkaloids in the third stage of labor decreases mean blood loss and incidence of postpartum blood loss of ≥500 mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: A first-line agent for the treatment of postpartum hemorrhage; usually given after oxytocin.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Should not be used for the induction or augmentation of labor.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html> Should not be used in cases of threatened spontaneous abortion.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Test for Variant Angina

Ergonovine maleate: Has been useful in diagnosing variant angina†.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Administered IV to induce coronary artery spasm in patients with suspected variant angina.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine, Ergonovine Dosage and Administration

Administration

Ergonovine maleate: Administer orally or by IM injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html> When used for diagnosis of variant angina†, has been administered IV.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: Administer orally or by IM or slow IV injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Parenteral preparation also has been administered directly into the uterine corpus†.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Do not administer by intra-arterial or periarterial injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: For drug compatibility information, see Compatibility under Stability.

Methylergonovine maleate: Reserve IV administration for severe uterine bleeding or other life-threatening emergency situations.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See IV Administration under Cautions.)

If given IV, monitor BP.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: May be diluted to a volume of 5 mL with 0.9% sodium chloride injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: Administer over at least 1 minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as ergonovine maleate; dosage expressed in terms of ergonovine maleate.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Available as methylergonovine maleate; dosage expressed in terms of methylergonovine maleate.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Ergonovine maleate: To minimize late postpartum bleeding, 0.2–0.4 mg every 6–12 hours until uterine atony has passed (usually 48 hours).<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> Lower dose may be used if severe cramping occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: To control uterine bleeding during the puerperium, 0.2 mg 3 or 4 times daily for up to 1 week.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Ergonovine maleate: 0.2 mg.<html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html> Repeat as necessary; manufacturer states that IM dose rarely needed more frequently than once in 2–4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: 0.2 mg;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> repeat as necessary every 2–4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Ergonovine maleate: 0.1–0.4 mg has been used.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Methylergonovine maleate: Do not routinely administer IV.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Possibility of sudden hypertension and cerebrovascular accident when administered IV.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: If IV administration is considered lifesaving, administer slowly; monitor BP.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See IV Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

Avoid prolonged use; discontinue if ergotism develops.<html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Caution in patients with sepsis, heart disease, venoatrial shunts, mitral valve stenosis, or obliterative vascular disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine and Ergonovine: contraindicated during pregnancy because of the drugs’ uterotonic effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications.)

Methylergonovine maleate: Distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> Use with caution in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: May be administered orally for a maximum of 1 week postpartum to control uterine bleeding.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Postpartum Hemorrhage under Dosage and Administration.)

Safety and efficacy not established in children.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine maleate: Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Use with caution.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Methylergonovine, Ergonovine

Drugs Affecting Hepatic Microsomal Enzymes

Methylergonovine maleate: Interaction possible with drugs that are inhibitors of CYP3A4; potential for vasospasm, cerebral ischemia, and/or ischemia of the extremities.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Concomitant use of ergot alkaloids and HIV protease inhibitors, delavirdine, or efavirenz is contraindicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>l</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>m</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>n</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>o</sup></body></html> Because postpartum hemorrhage due to uterine atony is often managed with methylergonovine, the Perinatal HIV Guidelines Working Group of the Public Health Service Task Force has issued recommendations concerning use of methylergonovine in women receiving certain antiretroviral agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html> For recommendations concerning use of methylergonovine in patients receiving HIV protease inhibitors, delavirdine, or efavirenz, see Specific Drugs under Interactions.

Methylergonovine, Ergonovine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Rapidly absorbed after oral or IM administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Following oral administration of ergonovine, bioavailability reported as 34–117%.<html><head></head><body><sup>k</sup></body></html> Following oral administration of methylergonovine, bioavailability is about 60%.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Oral administration: Uterine contractions occur within 5–15 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

IM injection: Uterine contractions occur within 2–5 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

IV administration: Uterine contractions occur immediately.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Oral administration or IM injection: Uterine contractions persist ≥3 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

IV injection: Uterine contractions persist for 45 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Following IV administration of methylergonovine, rapidly distributed to tissues (i.e., in 2-3 minutes).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized in the liver.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Methylergonovine is excreted principally in feces and bile.<html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> Minimal urinary excretion.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html>

Following IV administration of ergonovine, initial phase (t½α) half-life is about 10 minutes, and the terminal phase (t½β) half-life is about 2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>k</sup></body></html>

Following IV administration of methylergonovine, initial phase (t½α) half-life is about 1–5 minutes, and the terminal phase (t½β) half-life is about 0.5–2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Elimination may be prolonged in neonates.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
