Introduction

Enzymatic debriding agent derived from Clostridium histolyticum.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Uses for Collagenase

Wound Debridement

Used to promote debridement of necrotic tissue in the treatment of severe burns and dermal ulcers including decubitus ulcers.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Collagenase Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply ointment topically to wound.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Do not introduce ointment into major body cavities (e.g., pleural or peritoneal cavities).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Apply ointment directly to the wound or to a sterile gauze pad which is then applied to the wound and properly secured.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Use caution to restrict application to the lesion; avoid applying to healthy skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Local Effects under Cautions.)

Prior to each application, cleanse wound with a gauze pad saturated with 0.9% sodium chloride solution or a compatible cleansing agent to remove necrotic material and follow with a normal saline solution rinse.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Compatibility under Stability.)

Avoid detergents, solutions containing heavy metal ions, or acidic solutions.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> If such materials are used, carefully cleanse site with repeated normal saline solution rinses prior to application.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Enzyme Inactivation under Cautions and see Compatibility under Stability.)

Remove as much loosened detritus as can be done readily with forceps and scissors.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> If clinically indicated, cross-hatch thick eschar with a #10 scalpel blade.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

If infection is present, apply a topical antibiotic powder to the lesion prior to the application of collagenase.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Compatibility under Stability.) If infection does not respond to combined therapy, discontinue collagenase until infection has resolved.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Apply once daily to wound. <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Apply more frequently if the dressing becomes soiled (e.g., due to incontinence).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Discontinue when debridement of necrotic tissue is complete and granulation tissue is well established.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations at this time.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Collagenase

Contraindications

  • Known local or systemic hypersensitivity to collagenase.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Known local or systemic hypersensitivity to collagenase.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Collagenase is potentially irreversibly inactivated at pH <5 and reversibly inactivated at pH ≥8.5; activity can be restored by return of pH to 7.4.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Collagenase can be destroyed by other proteolytic enzymes or by exposure to temperatures >56°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Enzymatic activity is adversely affected by detergents, heavy metal ions (e.g., mercury or silver), and povidone iodine.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Possible risk of systemic bacterial infection;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> closely monitor debilitated patients for evidence of systemic bacterial infection (e.g., fever, chills, tachycardia).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Slight erythema may develop in surrounding tissue, especially if ointment is not confined to lesion being treated.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Not known if distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • An enzymatic debriding agent capable of specifically hydrolyzing peptide bonds of undenatured and denatured collagen.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Liquefies necrotic tissue without damaging granulation tissue.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Contributes towards the formation of granulation tissue and subsequent epithelization of dermal ulcers and severely burned areas.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • May remove substrates necessary for bacterial proliferation or may permit antibodies, leukocytes, and antibiotics better access to the infected area.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
