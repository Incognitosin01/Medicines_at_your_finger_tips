Uses for Difluprednate

Postoperative Ocular Inflammation and Pain

Treatment of inflammation and pain associated with ocular surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Difluprednate Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic emulsion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not for intraocular administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of the preparation container.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not wear contact lenses during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If used with other topical ophthalmic drugs (e.g., β-adrenergic blocking agents, carbonic anhydrase inhibitors, α-agonists, cycloplegics, mydriatics) separate administration by 10 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Initially, 1 drop into the conjunctival sac of the affected eye(s) 4 times daily beginning 24 hours after surgery and continuing for 2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Then decrease to 1 drop twice daily for 1 week.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Thereafter, taper based on response.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations at this time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Difluprednate

  • Active viral diseases of the cornea and conjunctiva (e.g., epithelial herpes simplex keratitis [dendritic keratitis], vaccinia, varicella).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Mycobacterial infection of the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Fungal disease of ocular structures.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Known hypersensitivity to difluprednate, other corticosteroids, or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of glaucoma (with damage to optic nerve), defects in visual acuity and fields of vision, and posterior subcapsular cataract formation with prolonged use of corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Use with caution in glaucoma because IOP may increase.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If used for ≥10 days, monitor IOP routinely, even though monitoring may be difficult in uncooperative patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

In conditions causing thinning of the cornea and sclera, perforations reported with use of topical corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Use of high-dose corticosteroids may delay healing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Use after cataract surgery may delay healing and increase incidence of bleb formation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Risk of secondary ocular infections (bacterial, fungal, or viral) with prolonged use of corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Consider possibility of fungal invasion if persistent corneal ulceration occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Obtain fungal culture when appropriate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In acute purulent conditions, corticosteroids may mask infection or exacerbate existing infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If no improvement after 2 days, reevaluate the patient.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications under Cautions.)

Use of corticosteroids in patients with a history of herpes simplex infections other than epithelial herpes simplex keratitis, in which corticosteroids are contraindicated, requires great caution;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> slit-lamp microscopy is essential.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Initial prescription or renewal of medication order >28 days should be provided only after examination of the patient with the aid of magnification (e.g., slit-lamp biomicroscopy and fluorescein staining where appropriate).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Corneal edema, ciliary and conjunctival hyperemia, eye pain, photophobia, posterior capsule opacification, anterior chamber cells, anterior chamber flare, conjunctival edema, blepharitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Difluprednate Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Corticosteroids are absorbed through the aqueous humor; because only low doses are given, little if any systemic absorption occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Systemically absorbed corticosteroids are distributed into milk; not known whether topical corticosteroids could produce detectable levels in human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Deacetylated to active metabolite.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

15–25°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not freeze; protect from light.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Keep bottle in protective carton when not in use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Derivative of prednisolone;<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> structurally similar to other corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Corticosteroids suppress the inflammatory response to mechanical, chemical, or immunologic agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Corticosteroids inhibit edema, fibrin deposition, capillary dilation, leukocyte migration, capillary proliferation, fibroblast proliferation, deposition of collagen, and scar formation associated with inflammation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Mechanism of ocular effects is unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Corticosteroids may induce phospholipase A2 inhibitory proteins; these proteins may inhibit release of arachidonic acid, thus controlling biosynthesis of potent mediators of inflammation (e.g., prostaglandins, leukotrienes).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contamination of the tip of the container.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not wearing contact lenses during therapy unless specifically directed by a clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of administering other topical ophthalmic preparations 10 minutes apart from difluprednate administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>
