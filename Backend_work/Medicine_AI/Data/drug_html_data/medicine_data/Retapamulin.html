Uses for Retapamulin

Impetigo

Topical treatment of impetigo caused by Staphylococcus aureus (oxacillin-susceptible [methicillin-susceptible] isolates only) or Streptococcus pyogenes (group A β-hemolytic streptococci; GAS).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Although impetigo may be self-limiting, anti-infective treatment usually indicated to reduce duration of symptoms and prevent recurrence or transmission to others.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> Empiric treatment with an appropriate narrow-spectrum anti-infective generally used for initial treatment and considered reasonable for typical cases.<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>43</sup></body></html> Some clinicians suggest in vitro testing (i.e., Gram stain and culture of pus or exudates from skin lesions) to identify causative organism and confirm in vitro susceptibility,<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>43</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>292</sup></body></html> especially if impetigo is extensive and/or failed to respond to initial empiric treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html>

Nonbullous and bullous impetigo have been treated with topical and/or systemic anti-infective therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>43</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> Although comparative efficacy of various regimens not established in well-controlled clinical trials,<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> topical anti-infectives generally used for less extensive disease and systemic anti-infectives generally recommended if impetigo is severe or involves numerous lesions<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>43</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>110</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>292</sup></body></html> or if an outbreak is affecting multiple individuals (e.g., family members, childcare groups, athletic teams).<html><head></head><body><sup>43</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>292</sup></body></html>

When empiric treatment used, select appropriate narrow-spectrum anti-infective based on local patterns of resistance reported for S. aureus and S. pyogenes.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>292</sup></body></html>

Retapamulin Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the skin as a 1% ointment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not apply to eyes or mucous membranes;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> do not administer orally, intranasally, or intravaginally.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Apply thin layer of ointment to affected area.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May be applied to a maximum total treatment area of 100 cm2 in adults or a maximum of 2% of total body surface area (≤100 cm2) in pediatric patients 9 months of age or older.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Treated area may be covered with sterile bandage or gauze dressing, if desired.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Such coverings may protect treated area and prevent accidental transfer of ointment to eyes or other areas and may prevent infants and young children from accidentally touching or licking lesion site.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Wash hands after applying the ointment, unless the hands are being treated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Children ≥9 months of age: Apply thin layer of 1% ointment to affected area twice daily for 5 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Apply thin layer of 1% ointment to affected area twice daily for 5 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

Children ≥9 months: Maximum treatment area is 2% of total body surface area.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Maximum treatment area is 100 cm2.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Retapamulin

Contraindications

Manufacturer states no known contraindications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Local irritation at application site (e.g., pruritus, burning, pain, eczema, erythema) reported following topical application to skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Allergic contact dermatitis reported rarely with topical retapamulin;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> confirmed with patch testing.<html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html>

Postmarketing reports of hypersensitivity, including angioedema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If sensitization or severe local irritation occurs, discontinue the drug, wipe off ointment, and institute appropriate alternative anti-infective therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For external use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use only for topical application to skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not intended for oral, intranasal, ophthalmic, or intravaginal use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established for topical use on mucosal surfaces.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Epistaxis reported when retapamulin was applied to nasal mucosa.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use of anti-infectives may promote selection of nonsusceptible organisms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If superinfection occurs, discontinue the drug and institute appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

To reduce development of drug-resistant bacteria and maintain effectiveness of retapamulin and other anti-infectives, use only for treatment of infections proven or strongly suspected to be caused by susceptible bacteria.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prescribing topical retapamulin in the absence of a proven or strongly suspected bacterial infection unlikely to provide benefit to the patient and increases risk of development of drug-resistant bacteria.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In the absence of culture and in vitro susceptibility testing, consider local epidemiology and susceptibility patterns when selecting anti-infectives for empiric therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No adequate and well-controlled studies evaluating topical retapamulin in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use topical retapamulin only when potential benefits outweigh potential risks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In rats, oral retapamulin associated with maternal toxicity (decreased weight gain, decreased food consumption) and developmental toxicity (decreased fetal body weight, delayed skeletal ossification);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> no treatment-related malformations reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In rabbits, continuous IV infusion of retapamulin (at 8 times estimated maximum achievable human exposure) associated with maternal toxicity (decreased weight gain, decreased food consumption, abortions);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> no treatment-related effects on embryofetal development.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether retapamulin distributed into milk following topical application to skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in infants <9 months of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy for topical treatment of impetigo in pediatric patients ≥9 months of age supported by evidence from adequate and well-controlled studies that included pediatric patients 9 months to 17 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Safety profile in pediatric patients in this age group similar to that reported in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Retapamulin Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Systemic exposure is low following topical application of retapamulin to intact or abraded skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Systemic absorption is enhanced if semi-occlusive or occlusive dressings used at site of application.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Healthy adults: Once-daily topical application of 1% ointment to 800 cm2 of occluded intact skin for up to 7 days resulted in median peak plasma concentrations of 3.5 ng/mL (range: 1.2–7.8 ng/mL) on day 7;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> plasma concentrations generally undetectable on day 1 (lower limit of detection 0.5 ng/mL).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> On day 7, 82% had measurable retapamulin plasma concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Healthy adults: Once-daily topical application of 1% ointment to 200 cm2 of occluded abraded skin for up to 7 days resulted in median peak plasma concentrations of 11.7 ng/mL (range: 5.6–22.1 ng/mL) on day 1 and 9 ng/mL (range: 6.7–12.8 ng/mL) on day 7.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> On day 7, 100% had measurable retapamulin plasma concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adults and children 2–17 years of age: Following twice-daily topical application of 1% ointment, 11% had measurable plasma concentrations (median 0.8 ng/mL);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> maximum plasma concentrations in these adults or children were 10.7 or 18.5 ng/mL, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Infants 2–24 months of age: Following twice-daily topical application of 1% ointment for 5 days, 69% of those 2–9 months of age† and 32% of those 9–24 months of age had measurable plasma concentrations (>0.5 ng/mL).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Among the 29 infants 2–9 months of age, 4 had plasma concentrations ≥26.9 ng/mL and these concentrations exceeded maximum plasma concentrations reported in children 2–17 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Among the 50 infants 9–24 months of age, 1 infant had plasma concentration of 95.1 ng/mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Approximately 94%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that retapamulin ointment is for external use only and should not be swallowed or used in eyes or nose, on mouth or lips, or inside female genital tract.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Inform patients that treated area may be covered with a sterile bandage or gauze dressing, if desired.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Such coverings may protect treated area and prevent accidental transfer of ointment to eyes or other areas and may prevent infants and young children from accidentally touching or licking lesion site.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to wash hands after applying the ointment, unless the hands are being treated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of completing full course of treatment, even if symptoms improve.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of notifying clinician if symptoms do not improve within 3–4 days after starting treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that topical retapamulin may cause reactions at the site of application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of discontinuing use and contacting clinician if application site worsens in irritation, redness, itching, burning, swelling, blistering, or oozing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
