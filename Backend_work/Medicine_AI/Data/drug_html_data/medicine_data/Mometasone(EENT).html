Mometasone (EENT) Dosage and Administration

General

  • For therapeutic effectiveness, use at regular intervals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For therapeutic effectiveness, use at regular intervals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer by nasal inhalation using a a metered-dose nasal spray pump.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Shake inhaler gently immediately prior to use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to initial use, the metered-dose pump spray must be primed with 10 actuations or until a fine spray appears.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Prime pump after a period of nonuse (i.e., >1 week) by actuating twice or until a fine spray appears.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Clear nasal passages prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Tilt the head slightly forward, insert the nasal applicator into one nostril, <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> and point the tip of the applicator away from the nasal septum.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Pump the drug into the nostril<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> while holding the other nostril closed and concurrently inspiring through the nose.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Repeat procedure for the other nostril.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Clean nasal applicator by removing the nasal applicator and plastic cap.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Soak the nasal applicator in cold tap water and/or rinse in warm water and dry.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Rinse the plastic cap under cold water and dry.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Reassemble the nasal applicator with the pump by reinserting the pump stem into the center opening of the applicator.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Reprime the pump by actuating twice or until a fine spray appears.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Mometasone (EENT)

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to mometasone furoate or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to mometasone furoate or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Possible corticosteroid withdrawal symptoms (e.g., joint pain, muscular pain, lassitude, depression), acute adrenal insufficiency, or severe symptomatic exacerbation of asthma or other clinical conditions if prolonged systemic corticosteroid therapy is replaced with topical corticosteroid therapy; careful monitoring recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use particular caution in patients with associated asthma or other conditions that may be exacerbated by too rapid a reduction in systemic corticosteroid dosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Excessive intranasal dosages or use in patients who are particularly sensitive to corticosteroid effects due to recent systemic corticosteroid therapy may cause systemic corticosteroid effects (e.g., menstrual irregularities, acneiform lesions, cushingoid features).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Systemic Corticosteroid Effects under Cautions.)

Increased susceptibility to infections in patients who are taking immunosuppressant drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Certain infections (e.g., varicella [chickenpox], measles) can be serious or even fatal in such patients, particularly in children.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Exposure to varicella and measles should be avoided in previously unexposed patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If exposure to varicella or measles occurs in susceptible patients, consider administering varicella zoster immune globulin (VZIG) or immune globulin (IG), respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider treatment with an antiviral agent if varicella develops.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Rarely, immediate hypersensitivity reactions or wheezing may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Possible growth suppression in children or adolescents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Pediatric Use under Cautions.)

Excessive intranasal dosages or use in patients who are particularly sensitive to corticosteroid effects may increase risk of systemic corticosteroid effects (e.g., hypercorticism, adrenal suppression).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid use of higher than recommended dosages.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If systemic effects occur, slowly reduce dosage and discontinue drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Rarely, localized candidial infections of the nose and/or pharynx have been reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Local treatment of such infections and/or discontinuance of intranasal therapy may be required.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible nasal burning and irritation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Rarely, nasal septum perforation has been reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Periodically examine nasal passages for signs of mucosal changes during long-term therapy (several months or longer).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use not recommended in patients with recent nasal septal ulcers, nasal surgery, or nasal trauma until healing has occurred.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution, if at all, in patients with clinical or asymptomatic Mycobacterium tuberculosis infection of the respiratory tract; untreated fungal, bacterial, or systemic viral infections; or ocular herpes simplex infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No ophthalmologic abnormalities (i.e., elevation in intraocular pressure, cataracts) observed in long-term studies with mometasone furoate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Glaucoma, increased intraocular pressure, and cataracts have been reported rarely with other nasal or inhaled corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients who have a change in vision and those with a history of glaucoma or cataracts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use during pregnancy may result in hypoadrenalism in infants; monitor these infants carefully.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether mometasone furoate is distributed into milk; caution if used in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children <2 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Intranasal corticosteroids may reduce growth velocity in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Routine monitoring of growth (e.g., via stadiometry) recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Titrate dosage to the lowest possible effective level.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adverse effects profiles similar to those in younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Headache, viral infection, pharyngitis, epistaxis/blood-tinged mucus, coughing, upper respiratory tract infection, dysmenorrhea, musculoskeletal pain, sinusitis, vomiting.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Mometasone (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Bioavailability <0.1% administered as a nasal spray; virtually undetectable in plasma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Symptomatic relief may be evident within 5–11 hours after the initial dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Maximum benefit is usually achieved within 1–2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether mometasone furoate crosses the placenta or distributes into breast milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Approximately 98–99%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Swallowed portion of dose is extensively metabolized to multiple derivatives;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> a minor metabolite is formed by CYP3A4.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Excreted as metabolites principally via bile and to a lesser extent in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

5.8 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

25°C (may be exposed to 15–30°C); protect from light.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Inhibits multiple cell types (e.g., mast cells, macrophages, lymphocytes, monocytes, plasma cells) and mediators (e.g., histamine, eicosanoids, leukotrienes, cytokines) involved in inflammation.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
