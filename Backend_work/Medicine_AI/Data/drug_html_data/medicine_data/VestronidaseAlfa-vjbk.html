Introduction

Biosynthetic (recombinant DNA origin) form of human β-glucuronidase, a lysosomal enzyme that catabolizes glycosaminoglycans (GAGs).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Uses for Vestronidase Alfa-vjbk

Mucopolysaccharidosis VII

Replacement therapy in patients with mucopolysaccharidosis VII (MPS VII, Sly syndrome);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> designated an orphan drug by FDA for use in this condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

May improve motor function (e.g., increase walking distance) in some patients; however, effect on CNS manifestations not determined.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Vials contain no preservatives and are intended for single use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Vestronidase alfa-vjbk injection is a colorless to slightly yellow solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Because the drug is a protein solution, slight flocculation (thin translucent fibers) may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discard solution if discoloration or particulate matter is present.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Must dilute commercially available injection concentrate prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Determine number of vials needed based on patient's actual weight and recommended dose of 4 mg/kg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Remove required number of vials from refrigerator and allow them to reach room temperature.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not heat, microwave, or shake the vials.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Using a sufficiently large needle (e.g., 18 gauge), slowly withdraw calculated volume of vestronidase alfa-vjbk from the appropriate number of vials.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Transfer drug to an empty infusion bag.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Dilute with an equal volume of 0.9% sodium chloride injection (1:1 ratio).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> More than a 1:1 dilution ratio may be used if the patient can tolerate additional infusion volume, taking into consideration cardiac function and fluid status.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Gently mix solution; avoid shaking or agitation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use immediately after dilution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If immediate use not possible, may store diluted solution at recommended conditions, but administration should be completed within 42 hours from the time of dilution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Storage under Stability.)

Administer over approximately 4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In the first hour, infuse 2.5% of the total volume;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> increase infusion rate as tolerated to complete infusion over the subsequent 3 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consult manufacturer's prescribing information for recommended infusion rate schedules.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Account for dead space in the IV line to ensure that 2.5% of the total infusion volume is delivered during the first hour of infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Closely observe patients for anaphylaxis during and for ≥60 minutes following administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If a severe hypersensitivity reaction occurs, stop infusion immediately and initiate appropriate treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Hypersensitivity Reactions under Cautions.)

Warnings/Precautions

Hypersensitivity reactions, including life-threatening anaphylaxis, reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Boxed Warning.) Manifestations include respiratory distress, cyanosis, decreased oxygen saturation, and hypotension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Closely observe patients during and for ≥60 minutes after administration; ensure that appropriate medical support is readily available.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

To minimize risk of hypersensitivity reactions, premedicate with a nonsedating antihistamine (with or without antipyretics).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See General under Dosage and Administration.)

If a severe hypersensitivity reaction occurs (e.g., anaphylaxis), stop infusion immediately and initiate appropriate treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider risks and benefits of retreatment following an anaphylactic reaction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

As with all therapeutic proteins, there is a potential for immunogenicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Antibodies (including neutralizing antibodies) to vestronidase alfa-vjbk reported in most patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> However, presence of antibodies does not appear to affect urinary GAG concentrations, a pharmacodynamic measure of response.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

No data on use in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In animal studies, no maternal toxicity or adverse developmental outcomes observed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known if distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Effects of the drug on the breast-fed infant or on milk production also not known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consider known benefits of breast-feeding along with the woman's clinical need for the drug and any potential adverse effects on the breast-fed infant from the drug or underlying maternal condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pediatric patients ≥5 months of age have been treated with vestronidase alfa-vjbk in clinical studies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Clinical trials did not include patients ≥65 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not known if geriatric patients respond differently than younger patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Vestronidase Alfa-vjbk Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Reduction of urinary GAG excretion occurs rapidly within first 2 weeks of treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Serum concentrations appear to increase in an approximately dose-proportional manner in the dose range of 1–4 mg/kg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Serum concentrations in pediatric patients <5 years of age similar to those observed in older children and adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>
