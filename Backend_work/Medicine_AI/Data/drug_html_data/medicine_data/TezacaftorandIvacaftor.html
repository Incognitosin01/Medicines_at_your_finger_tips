Introduction

Combination containing tezacaftor (cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator [CFTR] corrector) and ivacaftor (CFTR potentiator).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Uses for Tezacaftor and Ivacaftor

Cystic Fibrosis

Tezacaftor/ivacaftor combination therapy: Treatment of cystic fibrosis in patients homozygous for F508del mutation in the CFTR gene or having at least 1 mutation in the CFTR gene that is responsive to the combination drug regimen (i.e., E56K, P67L, R74W, D110E, D110H, R117C, E193K, L206W, R347H, R352Q, A455E, D579G, 711+3A→G, E831X, S945L, S977F, F1052V, K1060T, A1067T, R1070W, F1074L, D1152H, D1270N, 2789+5G→A, 3272-26A→G, 3849+10kbC→T).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Use FDA-approved cystic fibrosis mutation test to detect presence of CFTR mutations followed by verification with bidirectional sequencing when recommended by the mutation test instructions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tezacaftor and Ivacaftor Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally with fat-containing food (e.g., eggs, cheese, nuts, whole milk, meats, food prepared with butter or oils) to increase systemic absorption of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Food under Pharmacokinetics.)

Dosage

Available as a kit containing 4 weekly blister cards of 7 tablets containing 100 mg of tezacaftor in fixed combination with 150 mg of ivacaftor copackaged with 7 tablets containing 150 mg of single-entity ivacaftor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children ≥12 years of age: Tezacaftor 100 mg/ivacaftor 150 mg once daily in the morning and single-entity ivacaftor 150 mg once daily in the evening (approximately 12 hours apart).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage adjustment necessary when used concomitantly with moderate or potent inhibitors of CYP3A.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Interactions.)

Warnings/Precautions

Elevated ALT or AST concentrations reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Assess serum ALT and AST concentrations prior to initiation of therapy, every 3 months during the first year, and annually thereafter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In patients with a history of AST or ALT elevations, consider more frequent monitoring.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Closely monitor patients who develop increased ALT, AST, or bilirubin concentrations until abnormalities resolve.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interrupt therapy in patients with ALT or AST elevations >5 times the ULN or in those with ALT or AST elevations >3 times the ULN when associated with elevated bilirubin concentrations >2 times the ULN.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Following resolution of ALT or AST elevations, consider benefits and risks of resuming therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Concomitant use with potent CYP3A inducers substantially decreases systemic exposure of ivacaftor and may decrease exposure of tezacaftor; decreased exposures may reduce therapeutic efficacy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Concomitant use with potent CYP3A inducers not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Interactions.)

Ocular lens opacities (not congenital in nature) reported in pediatric patients receiving tezacaftor/ivacaftor combination therapy or ivacaftor monotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Baseline and follow-up ophthalmologic examinations recommended in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Limited data available regarding use of tezacaftor/ivacaftor combination therapy or its individual components in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Evidence of teratogenicity or adverse effects on fetal development not observed in animals receiving tezacaftor or ivacaftor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> No animal data available with concomitant use of tezacaftor and ivacaftor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Placental transfer of tezacaftor observed in pregnant rats; placental transfer of ivacaftor observed in pregnant rats and rabbits.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Tezacaftor and Ivacaftor

Tezacaftor is a substrate of CYP3A isoenzymes (e.g., CYP3A4, 3A5), P-glycoprotein (P-gp) transport, breast cancer resistance protein (BCRP), and organic anion transporting polypeptide (OATP) 1B1.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Ivacaftor is a sensitive substrate of CYP3A.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In vitro, ivacaftor has potential to inhibit CYP3A and P-gp, and also may inhibit CYP2C9.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Drugs Affecting or Metabolized by Hepatic Microsomal Enzymes

CYP3A substrates: No dosage adjustment needed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potent CYP3A inhibitors: Pharmacokinetic interaction (possible increased tezacaftor and ivacaftor exposures).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Reduce dosage to tezacaftor 100 mg/ivacaftor 150 mg in fixed combination twice weekly, approximately 3–4 days apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not administer single-entity ivacaftor 150 mg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Moderate CYP3A inhibitors: Pharmacokinetic interaction (possible increased tezacaftor and ivacaftor exposures).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Reduce dosage to tezacaftor 100 mg/ivacaftor 150 mg in fixed combination once every other day in combination with single-entity ivacaftor 150 mg once every other day, given on alternate days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potent CYP3A inducers: Pharmacokinetic interaction (decreased ivacaftor exposure; decreased tezacaftor exposure expected).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Concomitant use not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Drugs Affected by P-glycoprotein Transport

P-gp substrates: Pharmacokinetic interaction (possible increased exposure, prolonged therapeutic effect, or increased risk of adverse effects of the substrate drug).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use P-gp substrates with narrow therapeutic index concomitantly with caution; monitor patients appropriately.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tezacaftor and Ivacaftor Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Peak plasma concentrations of tezacaftor and ivacaftor achieved approximately 4 and 6 hours, respectively, after oral administration in the fed state.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Systemic exposure increases with accumulation ratio of approximately 1.5 (for tezacaftor) and approximately 2.2 (for ivacaftor).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Steady-state concentrations of tezacaftor (once daily) and ivacaftor (twice daily) achieved within 8 and 3–5 days, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administration of single dose of fixed combination of tezacaftor/ivacaftor with fat-containing food resulted in systemic exposures of ivacaftor approximately threefold higher when compared with administration in fasting state;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> systemic exposures of tezacaftor similar to those observed with administration in fasting state.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Mild hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh class A): Effect on pharmacokinetics not studied.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Moderate hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh class B): AUCs and peak plasma concentrations of tezacaftor and ivacaftor increased by 36 and 50%, respectively, when compared with healthy individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In a separate study, a twofold increased AUC observed when compared with healthy individuals, but peak plasma concentrations similar between the 2 groups.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Severe hepatic impairment (Child-Pugh class C): Effect on pharmacokinetics not studied.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Systemic exposures expected to be higher than that observed in patients with moderate hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Hepatic Impairment under Dosage and Administration.)

Renal impairment: Not studied in patients with moderate or severe renal impairment or in those with ESRD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Population pharmacokinetic data indicate that mild or moderate renal impairment does not substantially affect clearance of tezacaftor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Renal Impairment under Cautions.)

Pediatric patients 12 to <18 years of age: Based on population pharmacokinetic analyses, exposures of tezacaftor and ivacaftor similar to those observed in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Distributed into milk in rats; not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tezacaftor: Approximately 99% (mainly albumin).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Ivacaftor: Approximately 99% (mainly α1-acid glycoprotein, albumin).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Tezacaftor: Extensively metabolized principally by CYP3A4 and 3A5 to 3 major circulating metabolites (M1, M2, M5).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> M1 has similar potency to the parent drug and is pharmacologically active; M2 has less pharmacologic activity than M1; M5 is inactive.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> M3 is a minor metabolite resulting from direct glucuronidation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Ivacaftor: Extensively metabolized principally by CYP3A4 and 3A5 to 2 major metabolites (M1, M6).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> M1 has approximately one-sixth the potency of ivacaftor; M6 is inactive.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tezacaftor: Mainly excreted unchanged in feces (72% as unchanged drug or M2 metabolite);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> 14% excreted in urine (mainly as M2 metabolite).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Ivacaftor: Mainly excreted in feces (87.8%) after metabolic conversion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Ivacaftor and metabolites minimally excreted in urine (6.6%).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tezacaftor: 15 hours in patients with cystic fibrosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Ivacaftor: 13.7 hours in patients with cystic fibrosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Mutations in the gene encoding CFTR affect quantity or function of this protein at the cell surface resulting in cystic fibrosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>
