Uses for Emedastine

Allergic Conjunctivitis

Symptomatic relief of allergic conjunctivitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Emedastine Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not for injection or oral use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Remove soft contact lenses prior to administration of each dose (since benzalkonium chloride preservative may be absorbed by the lenses); may reinsert lenses 10 minutes after administration if eyes are not red.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If more than one topical ophthalmic drug is used, administer the drugs at least 5 minutes apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of the solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not use if solution is discolored.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as emedastine difumarate; dosage expressed in terms of emedastine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children ≥3 years of age: 1 drop of a 0.05% solution in the affected eye(s) up to 4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

1 drop of a 0.05% solution in the affected eye(s) up to 4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

3–4 hours following oral administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
