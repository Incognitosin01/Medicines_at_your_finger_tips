Uses for Quinupristin/Dalfopristin

Skin and Skin Structure Infections

Treatment of complicated skin and skin structure infections caused by susceptible Staphylococcus aureus (methicillin-susceptible [oxacillin-susceptible] strains) or Streptococcus pyogenes (group A β-hemolytic streptococci, GAS).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

For information on diagnosis and management of skin and skin structure infections, consult current IDSA clinical practice guidelines available at [Web].<html><head></head><body><sup>43</sup></body></html>

Methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus Infections

Has been used as salvage therapy in critically ill patients for treatment of severe infections (e.g., bacteremia, infective endocarditis) caused by methicillin-resistant S. aureus† (MRSA; also known as oxacillin-resistant S. aureus or ORSA) when vancomycin was ineffective.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>26</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>127</sup></body></html>

Some clinicians state quinupristin/dalfopristin is one of several options for treatment of persistent MRSA bacteremia in adults who fail to respond to vancomycin or when the infection is known to be caused by MRSA with reduced susceptibility to vancomycin and daptomycin.<html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html>

For information on treatment of infections caused by MRSA, consult current IDSA clinical practice guidelines available at [Web].<html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html> For information on diagnosis and management of infective endocarditis caused by MRSA, consult current AHA guidelines available at [Web].<html><head></head><body><sup>127</sup></body></html>

Vancomycin-resistant Enterococcus faecium Infections

Has been used for treatment of serious or life-threatening infections caused by susceptible vancomycin-resistant Enterococcus faecium†, including bacteremia, infective endocarditis, intra-abdominal infections, skin and skin structure infections, and urinary tract infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>33</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>34</sup></body></html>
