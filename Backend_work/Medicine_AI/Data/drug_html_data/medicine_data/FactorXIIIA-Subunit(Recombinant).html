Introduction

Biosynthetic (recombinant DNA origin) preparation of human factor XIII A2 homodimer consisting of 2 factor XIII A-subunits.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Uses for Factor XIII A-Subunit (Recombinant)

Congenital Factor XIII A-subunit Deficiency

Routine prophylaxis of bleeding in patients with congenital factor XIII A-subunit deficiency;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>34</sup></body></html> designated an orphan drug by FDA for such use.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Prophylactic therapy with factor XIII concentrate is recommended in all patients with severe (<1% factor XIII activity) deficiency; considered current standard of care.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>34</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>38</sup></body></html>

Factor XIII concentrate currently available in the US as a plasma-derived (Corifact) and a recombinant (Tretten) preparation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>30</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html> Some experts recommend preferential use of recombinant preparations because of potentially superior safety profile with respect to pathogen transmission.<html><head></head><body><sup>30</sup></body></html> When selecting an appropriate factor XIII preparation, consider characteristics of each clotting factor concentrate in addition to individual patient variables.<html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html>

Not effective and should not be used in patients with factor XIII B-subunit deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

Factor XIII A-Subunit (Recombinant) Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer by slow IV injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Rate of Administration under Dosage and Administration.) Do not administer by continuous IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>39</sup></body></html>

Initiate therapy under supervision of a clinician experienced in the treatment of rare bleeding disorders.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May be self-administered in the home setting after appropriate training provided.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not administer in the same IV line with other infusion solutions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>39</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute lyophilized drug with sterile water for injection provided by manufacturer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Prior to reconstitution, allow drug vial and diluent to warm to (but not exceed) room temperature (25°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> To avoid foaming, do not inject diluent directly onto powder.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Gently swirl vial; do not shake.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Resulting solution should be clear and colorless; discard if particulate matter or discoloration observed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May dilute solution with 0.9% sodium chloride injection to facilitate measurement of small doses (volumes) for administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer immediately after reconstitution or within 3 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If not used immediately, store in refrigerator or at room temperature (not to exceed 25°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discard unused portion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consult manufacturer's information for additional details on reconstitution and preparation of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Administer at a rate not exceeding 1–2 mL/minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Factor XIII A-Subunit (Recombinant)

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity reactions to factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant) or any of its components.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity reactions to factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant) or any of its components.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., urticaria, rash, chest tightness, wheezing, hypotension).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If manifestations of anaphylaxis or hypersensitivity occur, immediately discontinue drug and initiate appropriate treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Risk of thromboembolic complications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Although not observed with factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant), has been reported with plasma-derived factor XIII concentrates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

In patients with an underlying risk of thrombosis, monitor for thromboembolic complications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Neutralizing antibodies (inhibitors) to factor XIII may develop after treatment with factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May manifest as inadequate response to treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor for possible development of inhibitory antibodies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If expected plasma factor XIII activity levels not attained or bleeding occurs during factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant) prophylaxis, perform appropriate assay to detect presence of inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Transient, low-titer non-neutralizing antibodies detected in pediatric patients receiving factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant); clinically important effects not observed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use caution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pediatric patients 0–17 years of age have received factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant) in clinical studies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Adverse effects were reported more frequently in children 6 to <18 years of age than in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Immunogenicity under Cautions.)

Actions

  • Biosynthetic (recombinant DNA origin) preparation of human factor XIII A2 homodimer (consists of 2 factor XIII A-subunits).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Congenital factor XIII deficiency is a rare but serious bleeding disorder manifested by a lifelong bleeding tendency that typically includes umbilical bleeding during the neonatal period, delayed soft tissue bleeding, mucosal bleeding, poor wound healing, recurrent miscarriage, and intracranial hemorrhage.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>34</sup></body></html> Predominantly caused by deficiency of the factor XIII A-subunit; factor XIII B-subunit deficiency is extremely rare and associated with milder clinical manifestations.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

  • Patients with congenital factor XIII A-subunit deficiency have decreased plasma levels of endogenous factor XIII A-subunit, resulting in a hemorrhagic tendency.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant) restores normal clot solubility in such patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

  • Factor XIII is a proenzyme (protransglutaminase) that is activated in the presence of calcium by thrombin; once activated, factor XIIIa promotes cross-linking of fibrin and other proteins (α2-plasmin inhibitor, fibrinogen, collagen) to strengthen and stabilize the fibrin clot, protect from fibrinolysis, and enhance platelet adhesion to the site of tissue injury.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Factor XIII circulates in plasma as a heterotetrameric glycoprotein consisting of 2 A-subunits and 2 B-subunits (A2B2).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> The A-subunit is the active catalytic domain; B-subunits function as carrier molecules for A-subunits.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant) binds to free, noncomplexed factor XIII B-subunit to form A2B2.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> In its complexed form, pharmacodynamic and pharmacokinetic (e.g., half-life) properties of factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant) are similar to endogenous factor XIII.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>35</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of patients reporting to their clinician any adverse reactions or other issues occurring after administration of factor XIII A-subunit (recombinant).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
