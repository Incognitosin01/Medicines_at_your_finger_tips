Introduction

Antifungal; tetraene polyene.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Uses for Natamycin

Fungal Ophthalmic Infections

Topical treatment of blepharitis, conjunctivitis, and keratitis caused by susceptible fungi, including keratitis caused by Fusarium solani.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Efficacy not established for use as a single agent for treatment of fungal endophthalmitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use clinical diagnosis, laboratory diagnosis by smear and culture of corneal scrapings, and drug response to guide initial and continued therapy of fungal keratitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Whenever possible, use in vitro susceptibility testing to confirm that causative organism is susceptible to natamycin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Natamycin Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply 5% ophthalmic suspension topically to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For topical ophthalmic use only;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> do not inject directly into eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

To avoid contaminating the ophthalmic suspension, do not allow tip of dispensing dropper to touch any surface.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Shake suspension well prior to each use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Instill 1 drop of 5% ophthalmic suspension into conjunctival sac of affected eye(s) 4–6 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Instill 1 drop of 5% ophthalmic suspension into conjunctival sac of affected eye(s) every 1–2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> After 3–4 days, may decrease frequency to 1 drop 6–8 times daily;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> in many cases, frequency may be gradually decreased every 4–7 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If no signs of improvement after 7–10 days of topical therapy, infection may be caused by pathogens not susceptible to natamycin;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> base continued therapy on clinical reevaluation and additional laboratory studies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If evidence of improvement, continue topical therapy for 14–21 days or until there are no signs or symptoms of active fungal keratitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special populations dosage recommendations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Natamycin

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to natamycin or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to natamycin or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Allergic reactions reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For topical ophthalmic use only;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> not for injection into the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not wear contact lenses if any signs or symptoms of fungal blepharitis, conjunctivitis, or keratitis are present.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Animal reproduction studies not performed to evaluate whether natamycin can cause fetal harm or affect reproduction capacity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use during pregnancy only if clearly needed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in safety and efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Allergic reaction, change in vision, chest pain, corneal opacity, dyspnea, eye discomfort, eye edema, eye hyperemia, eye irritation or pain, foreign body sensation, paresthesia, tearing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Natamycin

No formal drug interaction studies using natamycin 5% ophthalmic suspension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Natamycin Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Systemic absorption not expected following topical application to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following topical application of natamycin 5% ophthalmic suspension to the eye, the drug adheres to cornea in areas of epithelial ulceration and is retained in conjunctival fornices.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Effective natamycin concentrations are attained within corneal stroma, but not in intraocular fluid.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether natamycin is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

2–24°C; do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Protect from light and excessive heat.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions and Spectrum

  • Antifungal antibiotic derived from Streptomyces natalensis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Predominantly fungicidal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Binds to sterols in the fungal cell membrane.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Affects permeability of the membrane barrier, depleting essential cellular constituents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Mechanism of action appears similar to that of amphotericin B and nystatin.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Active in vitro against a variety of yeast and filamentous fungi, including Aspergillus,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Candida,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Cephalosporium,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Fusarium,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> and Penicillium.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Inactive in vitro against gram-positive and gram-negative bacteria.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Advise patients to avoid contaminating tip of dispensing dropper.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients not to wear contact lenses if any signs or symptoms of fungal blepharitis, conjunctivitis, or keratitis are present.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to inform clinician if condition worsens or does not improve after 7–10 days of therapy or if any adverse reactions occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinician of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and dietary or herbal supplements, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
