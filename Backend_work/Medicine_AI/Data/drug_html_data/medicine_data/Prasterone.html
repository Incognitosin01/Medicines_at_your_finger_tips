Introduction

Uses for Prasterone

Dyspareunia

Treatment of moderate to severe dyspareunia, a symptom of vulvar and vaginal atrophy, associated with menopause.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Estrogen is a metabolite of prasterone.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use of exogenous estrogen contraindicated in women with known or suspected history of breast cancer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not studied in women with history of breast cancer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not studied in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Animal reproduction studies not conducted.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states that prasterone is labeled for use only in postmenopausal women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known if distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Effects of drug on breast-fed infant or milk production also unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients; not indicated for use in children.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In placebo-controlled 12-week studies and open-label 52-week clinical trial, 19 and 11% of patients, respectively, were ≥65 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prasterone Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Prasterone: Mean serum trough concentrations increased by 47% from baseline following daily intravaginal administration for 12 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Androgen and estrogen metabolites: Systemic concentrations within expected range for postmenopausal women.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Mean serum trough concentrations of testosterone and estradiol increased by 21 and 19%, respectively, from baseline following daily intravaginal administration for 12 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized by steroidogenic enzymes (e.g., hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase, 5α-reductases, aromatases) into androgens and estrogens.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

5–30°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Store at room temperature or refrigerate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of instructing patients to read the manufacturer's patient information and instructions for use prior to initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of instructing patients to empty their bladder and wash their hands before handling vaginal insert and applicator.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients that vaginal discharge or abnormal Pap smear results may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
