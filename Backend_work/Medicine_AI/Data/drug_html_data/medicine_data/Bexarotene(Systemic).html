Uses for Bexarotene (Systemic)

Cutaneous T-cell Lymphoma

Treatment of skin manifestations of cutaneous T-cell lymphoma (CTCL) in patients who are refractory to at least one prior systemic therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Bexarotene (Systemic) Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally once daily with a meal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Initially, 300 mg/m2 daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

If intolerable adverse effects occur, decrease dosage to 200 mg/m2 daily, then 100 mg/m2 daily, or temporarily discontinue.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> When toxicity is controlled, carefully readjust dosage upward.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

If no tumor response is observed after 8 weeks and the 300-mg/m2 daily dosage is well tolerated, increase to 400 mg/m2 daily with careful monitoring.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Continue as long as benefit is derived from therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Optimum duration is not known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

May cause fetal harm; teratogenicity and embryolethality demonstrated in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Use contraception (2 reliable forms, including at least 1 nonhormonal method) for 1 month before, during, and for 1 month after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Male patients should use condoms during sexual intercourse with women who are or may become pregnant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Lipid abnormalities (e.g., hyperlipidemia, elevated fasting triglycerides and cholesterol, decreased HDL-cholesterol) occur in most patients; usually develop within 2â€“4 weeks and are reversible with cessation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible acute pancreatitis; possibly fatal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Patients with risk factors for pancreatitis (e.g., prior pancreatitis, uncontrolled hyperlipidemia or diabetes mellitus, excessive alcohol consumption, biliary tract disease, or therapy with drugs associated with pancreatic toxicity or known to increase triglyceride concentrations) generally should not receive bexarotene.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Possible elevations in AST and ALT; usually resolve within 1 month following decrease in dosage or discontinuance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Monitor liver function tests at baseline; after 1, 2, and 4 weeks of treatment; and at least every 8 weeks thereafter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider discontinuance if transaminases or bilirubin increase to 3 times ULN.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Possible hypothyroidism.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Consider thyroid supplementation in patients with laboratory evidence of hypothyroidism.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitoring of thyroid function tests recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

New cataracts or worsening of existing cataracts possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Ophthalmologic evaluation recommended if visual difficulties occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with known hypersensitivity to retinoids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Sunburn and skin sensitivity to sunlight possible in patients exposed to direct sunlight.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Minimize exposure to sunlight and artificial UV light.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Category X.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> (See Fetal/Neonatal Morbidity and Mortality and also Contraindications under Cautions.)

Not known whether bexarotene is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Discontinue nursing or the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children <18 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

No substantial differences in safety relative to younger adults, but increased sensitivity cannot be ruled out.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Lipid abnormalities, hypothyroidism, headache, asthenia, rash, leukopenia, anemia, nausea, infection, peripheral edema, abdominal pain, dry skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Protein-bound Drugs

Potential pharmacokinetic interaction (bexarotene displacement by, or bexarotene displacement of, other protein-bound drugs).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Bexarotene (Systemic) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Absorbed following oral administration, with peak plasma concentrations attained within approximately 2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Peak plasma concentrations and AUC (after 75â€“300 mg doses) increased by approximately 48 and 35%, respectively, after a meal containing fat compared with glucose solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Distribution into body tissues and fluids has not been evaluated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

It is not known whether bexarotene is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

>99%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In patients with renal impairment, possible altered protein binding and pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized extensively in the liver; in vitro, metabolized principally via CYP3A4 oxidation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Oxidative metabolites active in vitro; relative contributions of parent drug or metabolites to safety and efficacy unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Bexarotene and its metabolites eliminated principally via biliary excretion; not excreted in urine in appreciable amounts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>
