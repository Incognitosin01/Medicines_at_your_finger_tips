Introduction

Antifungal; azole (triazole derivative).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Uses for Efinaconazole

Onychomycosis

Topical treatment of onychomycosis of the toenails (tinea unguium, ringworm of the nail) caused by Trichophyton rubrum or T. mentagrophytes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Efinaconazole Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply 10% solution topically to affected toenails using flow-through brush applicator supplied by the manufacturer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not administer orally or intravaginally;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> do not apply topically to eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Clean and dry toenails prior to application; do not apply until ≥10 minutes after showering, bathing, or washing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Using the integrated flow-through brush applicator supplied by the manufacturer on the bottle, place 1 drop of solution on each affected toenail.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use attached brush applicator to gently spread solution evenly over entire toenail surface, including cuticle, folds of skin next to sides of toenail, toenail bed, hyponychium (thickened epidermis underneath toenail tip), and undersurface of toenail tip.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For the big toenail, apply second drop at the end of the toenail.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not squeeze applicator bottle while spreading solution over toenails.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Allow solution to dry on the toenails.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Wash hands with soap and water after applying the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid pedicures and use of nail polish and other cosmetic nail products during efinaconazole treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Apply 10% solution to affected toenails once daily for 48 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use amount sufficient to cover entire toenail surface and under toenail tip.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Topical Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Efinaconazole

Contraindications

  • Manufacturer states none.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states none.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

May cause application site irritation (e.g., redness, swelling, burning, itching, blisters);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> no evidence that the drug causes contact sensitization.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

For topical use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not use orally or intravaginally;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> do not apply to eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to treatment of onychomycosis, confirm diagnosis by direct microscopic examination of scrapings from infected toenails mounted in potassium hydroxide (KOH) or by culture.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into human milk following topical application;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> distributed into milk of rats after sub-Q administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use with caution in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in safety and efficacy in older adults relative to younger adults observed in clinical trials, but greater sensitivity of some older individuals cannot be ruled out.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Application site dermatitis,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> ingrown toenail,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> application site vesicles,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> application site pain.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Efinaconazole

Does not inhibit CYP1A2, 2A6, 2C8, 2C9, 2C19, 2D6, 2E1, or 3A4 and does not induce CYP1A2 or 3A4 in vitro at concentrations expected clinically.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No formal drug interaction studies to date.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> Clinically important interactions with CYP inhibitors or inducers unlikely because systemic exposure is low following topical application of efinaconazole to toenails.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Efinaconazole Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following topical application of efinaconazole 10% solution to toenails, low concentrations are absorbed systemically.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Mean peak plasma concentration was 0.67 ng/mL after 28 consecutive days of once-daily topical administration of the 10% solution to all 10 toenails and up to 0.5 cm of skin surrounding each toenail.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> Plasma concentrations of the drug still detectable in some patients 2 weeks after last dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Steady-state concentrations achieved after about 14 days of once-daily topical application.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Following systemic absorption, efinaconazole undergoes oxidative metabolism via multiple CYP isoenzymes, principally CYP2C19 and 3A4.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> Major metabolite (H3) is inactive.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Plasma half-life of 29.9 hours reported following 7 consecutive days of once-daily topical application to all 10 toenails in a study in healthy adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

20–25°C (may be exposed to 15–30°C). <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

The 10% solution contains alcohol and is flammable;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> do not store or use near heat or open flame.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Keep bottle tightly closed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions and Spectrum

  • Triazole-derivative azole antifungal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

  • Presumably exerts antifungal activity by altering cellular membranes, resulting in increased membrane permeability and growth inhibition.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

  • Mechanism of action similar to that of other azole antifungals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> May involve inhibition of 14-α-demethylase in susceptible fungi, which leads to accumulation of C-14-methylated sterols (e.g., lanosterol) and decreased concentrations of ergosterol in fungal cell membranes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> Depletion of ergosterol affects cell membrane integrity and function and leads to fungal cell death.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

  • Spectrum of antifungal activity includes dermatophytes, yeasts, and some other fungi.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Dermatophytes: Active in vitro against Trichophyton mentagrophytes,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> T. rubrum,<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Epidermophyton floccosum,<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Microsporum canis,<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> and M. gypseum.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Other fungi: Active in vitro against Candida albicans,<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Acremonium,<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Aspergillus,<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> and Scopulariopsis.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • In vitro, appears to be as active or more active than ciclopirox, itraconazole, and terbinafine against T. mentagrophytes and T. rubrum.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Antifungal activity not affected by presence of keratin.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> In vitro studies suggest efinaconazole has low binding affinity for keratin and is readily released from keratinized nail plates; this may increase availability at site of nail fungal infection and increase penetration through the nail plate.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of using efinaconazole topical solution only for the condition prescribed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that efinaconazole topical solution is for external use only and to avoid contact with eyes, mouth, or vagina.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to clean and dry toenails prior to application of the drug;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> importance of applying the topical solution once daily using the integrated flow-through brush applicator provided with the bottle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of avoiding pedicures, nail polish, and other cosmetic nail products during efinaconazole treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to wash hands with soap and water following application of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that efinaconazole topical solution is flammable and should not be stored or used near heat or an open flame.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinician if area of application shows signs of persistent irritation (e.g., redness, itching, swelling).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinician of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and dietary or herbal supplements, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing their clinician if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
