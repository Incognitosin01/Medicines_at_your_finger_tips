Uses for Flunisolide (EENT)

Seasonal and Perennial Rhinitis

Symptomatic treatment of seasonal or perennial rhinitis<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> when conventional therapy with antihistamines or decongestants is ineffective or produces intolerable adverse effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Generally provides symptomatic relief of watery rhinorrhea, nasal congestion, sneezing, postnasal drip, and pharyngeal itching;<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> generally does not relieve symptoms of conjunctivitis or those involving the lower respiratory tract<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Appears to provide greater symptomatic relief in allergic rhinitis than in nonallergic rhinitis<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Serious Otitis Media

Has been used intranasally for the treatment of serous otitis media† (eustachian tube dysfunction, middle ear effusion) in children. <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Corticosteroid-dependent Asthma

Although orally inhaled flunisolide is used for the treatment of asthma, the nasal solution is not recommended for this purpose, since the safety of polyethylene glycol in the vehicle of the nasal solution has not been established for this route of administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Flunisolide (EENT) Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer by nasal inhalation using a special nasal inhaler.

Avoid contact with the eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Poor clinical response can result from improper drug administration techniques, poor drug penetration (secondary to marked nasal congestion, presence of nasal polyps, or symptoms originating in the nasal sinuses), or localized infections of the nasal mucosa.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Clear nasal passages prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> If nasal passages are blocked, a topical nasal decongestant can be administered 5–15 minutes before intranasal administration during the first 2–3 days of therapy to ensure adequate penetration of the drug and prevent loss from the nasal passages via excess secretions.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Prior to initial use, the nasal inhaler must be assembled and primed.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

After assembly is complete, tilt the head slightly forward, insert the spray tip into one nostril, and point the tip toward the inflamed nasal turbinates and away from the nasal septum.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Pump the drug into one nostril while holding the other nostril closed and concurrently inspire through the nose.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Repeat procedure for the other nostril.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Advise patients to cleanse the nasal adapter and/or pump in warm water if the holes in the device become clogged.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> If the nasal inhaler is disassembled for any reason (including the cleansing procedure) or not used for 5 days or longer, it must be primed again prior to use.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Dosage

After priming, nasal inhaler delivers about 25 mcg of flunisolide per metered spray and about 200 metered sprays per 25-mL container.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Adjust dosage according to individual requirements and response.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Therapeutic effects of intranasal corticosteroids, unlike those of decongestants, are not immediate.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> This should be explained to the patient in advance to ensure compliance and continuation of the prescribed treatment regimen.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Symptomatic relief is usually evident within 2–3 days of continuous therapy; however, occasionally, up to 2–3 weeks may be required for optimum effectiveness.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Generally assess response to the initial dosage 4–7 days after starting therapy; about two-thirds will experience some relief within this time period.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Once symptoms of seasonal or perennial rhinitis have been controlled, gradually reduce dosage to the lowest effective level.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Discontinue intranasal flunisolide in patients who do not experience clinically important benefit within 3 weeks of initiating therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Children 6–14 years of age: 1 spray (25 mcg) in each nostril 3 times daily or 2 sprays (50 mcg) in each nostril twice daily (total dose: 150–200 mcg/day).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Children 6–14 years of age: 1 spray (25 mcg) in each nostril 3 times daily or 2 sprays (50 mcg) in each nostril twice daily (total dose 150–200 mcg/day).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Usual initial dose is 50 mcg (2 sprays) in each nostril twice daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> When necessary, increase to 50 mcg (2 sprays) in each nostril 3 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Maintenance: 25 mcg (1 spray) in each nostril daily (50 mcg total) may be sufficient.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

No evidence that higher than recommended dosages or increased frequency of administration is beneficial.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Exceeding the maximum recommended daily dosage may only increase the risk of adverse systemic effects (e.g., HPA-axis suppression, Cushing’s syndrome).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Children 6-14 years of age: daily dosage should not exceed 100 mcg (4 sprays) in each nostril (200 mcg total).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Children 6-14 years of age: daily dosage should not exceed 100 mcg (4 sprays) in each nostril (200 mcg total).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Daily dosage should not exceed 200 mcg (8 sprays) in each nostril (400 mcg total).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Daily dosage should not exceed 200 mcg (8 sprays) in each nostril (400 mcg total).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

Cautions for Flunisolide (EENT)

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to flunisolide or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Do not use in the presence of untreated localized nasal mucosa infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to flunisolide or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Do not use in the presence of untreated localized nasal mucosa infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Patients being switched from prolonged systemic corticosteroids to intranasal therapy should be monitored carefully since corticosteroid withdrawal symptoms (e.g., joint pain, muscular pain, lassitude, depression), acute adrenal insufficiency, and/or severe symptomatic exacerbation of asthma or other clinical conditions may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Systemic corticosteroid dosage should be tapered, and patients should be carefully monitored during dosage reduction.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Use cautiously, if at all, in patients with clinical tuberculosis or asymptomatic Mycobacterium tuberculosis infections; untreated fungal, bacterial, or systemic viral or ocular herpes simplex infections; or septal ulcers, trauma, or surgery in the nasal region.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Avoid higher than recommended dosages since suppression of HPA function may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Although risk with intranasal use is unknown, consider the possibility that corticosteroid-induced immunosuppression could occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Avoid exposure to varicella and measles in previously unexposed patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Excessive intranasal dosages or use in patients who are particularly sensitive to corticosteroid effects may cause systemic corticosteroid effects (e.g., Cushing’s syndrome, adrenal suppression).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Temporary or permanent loss of smell may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Rarely, localized candidal infections of the nose and/or pharynx.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Treat suspected local infection appropriately<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> ; may require discontinuance of flunisolide therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Rarely, nasal septal perforations.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Avoid use until healing occurs in patients with recurrent epistaxis, recent nasal septal ulcers, nasal surgery, or nasal trauma.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Temporary or permanent loss of taste may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Flunisolide (EENT)

No reports to date of clinically important drug interactions.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Drugs Affecting Hepatic Microsomal Enzymes

Phenobarbital and other agents that induce hepatic microsomal enzymes may enhance the metabolism of corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Flunisolide (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

About 50% of a nasally inhaled dose is absorbed systemically.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Symptomatic relief usually is evident within 2–3 days of continuous therapy but may take up to 2–3 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Placental distribution in humans is unknown, but flunisolide apparently crosses the placenta in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Distribution into milk unknown, but other corticosteroids are distributed.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

At a plasma concentration of 1–20,000 ng/mL, about 50% bound to plasma albumin.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> No binding to glucocorticosteroid binding globulin.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized via the liver.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Excretion pathway is unknown when inhaled; however, when given systemically, metabolites are excreted in roughly equal portions in feces and urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>PDH</sup></body></html>

Drug absorbed via nasopharyngeal mucosa bypasses extensive first-pass hepatic metabolism.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Plasma half-life: 1–2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Tight, light-resistant container at 15–30°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Discard opened containers after 3 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Potent glucocorticoid and weak mineralocorticoid effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Local anti-inflammatory and vasoconstrictor effects;<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> topical potency similar to triamcinolone acetonide.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • May reduce the number of mediator cells (basophil leukocytes and mast cells) at the epithelial level, number of eosinophils, sensitivity of sensory nerves to mechanical stimuli, secretory response to cholinergic receptor stimulation, and fibroblast activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • May inhibit capillary dilation and permeability.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • May stabilize lysosomal membranes and subsequently prevent the release of proteolytic enzymes.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>
