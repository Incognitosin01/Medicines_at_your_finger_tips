Uses for Lodoxamide

Allergic Ocular Disorders

Symptomatic treatment of certain allergic ocular disorders including vernal keratoconjunctivitis, vernal conjunctivitis, and vernal keratitis (designated an orphan drug by FDA for such use).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Lodoxamide Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not for injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not for subconjunctival injection or introduction directly into anterior chamber of the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contamination of the product.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not wearing soft contact lenses during therapy (since benzalkonium chloride preservative may be absorbed by the lenses).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of contacting clinician if burning or stinging experienced during instillation persists.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs as well as any concomitant illnesses.

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
