Uses for Pemirolast

Allergic Conjunctivitis

Prevention of ocular itching associated with allergic conjunctivitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pemirolast Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not for injection or oral use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of the solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as pemirolast potassium; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Initiate therapy before and continue throughout pollen season.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Children ≥3 years of age: 1 or 2 drops of a 0.1% solution in the affected eye(s) 4 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Symptomatic response (i.e., decreased itching) may be evident within a few days following initiation but frequently requires up to 4 weeks of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Pemirolast Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following topical application to both eyes (0.1% solution 4 times daily for 2 weeks), peak plasma concentrations were attained within about 25 minutes, but were substantially lower than those following a single (10 mg) oral dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

10–15% of topical ophthalmic dose excreted unchanged in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

About 4.5 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

15–25°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Suppresses the release of mediators from cells involved in hypersensitivity reactions, including mast cells, and decreases chemotaxis and activation of eosinophils.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Reportedly blocks calcium channels in mast cell membranes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contamination of the product.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of removing soft contact lenses prior to administration of each dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Delay reinsertion for 10 minutes after administration if eyes are not red; do not wear contact lenses if eye(s) are red.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not indicated for contact lens-related irritation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of reporting any worsening of symptoms or new-onset ocular pain/discomfort.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>
