Warnings/Precautions

Serious and occasionally fatal hypersensitivity and/or anaphylactic reactions reported in patients receiving systemic fluoroquinolones, including systemic moxifloxacin;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> these reactions may occur with first dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> Some hypersensitivity reactions reported with systemic fluoroquinolones have been accompanied by cardiovascular collapse, loss of consciousness, tingling, angioedema (e.g., laryngeal, pharyngeal, facial), airway obstruction, dyspnea, urticaria, and itching.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html>

Immediately discontinue moxifloxacin ophthalmic solution at first sign of rash or hypersensitivity reaction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Serious acute hypersensitivity reactions may require immediate emergency treatment;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> administer oxygen and airway management as clinically indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Prolonged use may result in overgrowth of nonsusceptible organisms, including fungi.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

If superinfection occurs, discontinue moxifloxacin ophthalmic solution and institute appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Whenever clinical judgment dictates, examine patient with the aid of magnification (e.g., slit lamp biomicroscopy) and, if appropriate, fluorescein staining.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Moxifloxacin (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following topical application of moxifloxacin 0.5% ophthalmic solution to the eye, the drug is absorbed systemically and low moxifloxacin concentrations are attained in plasma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Moxeza 0.5% ophthalmic solution: Multiple, bilateral, topical ocular doses applied twice daily for 4 days resulted in peak plasma concentrations of moxifloxacin that were approximately 0.02% of those reported with usual oral dosages of moxifloxacin.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Vigamox 0.5% ophthalmic solution: Bilateral topical doses applied 3 times daily for 5 days resulted in mean steady-state peak plasma concentrations and estimated daily exposures (AUCs) that were 1600 and 1000 times lower, respectively, than those reported with usual oral dosages of moxifloxacin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Half-life of systemically absorbed moxifloxacin is approximately 13 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

2–25°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>
