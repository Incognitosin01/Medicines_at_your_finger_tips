Uses for cycloSPORINE (EENT)

Keratoconjunctivitis Sicca

Used to increase tear production in adults whose tear production presumably is suppressed secondary to ocular inflammation related to keratoconjunctivitis sicca.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increased tear production not observed in patients already receiving topical anti-inflammatory agents or using punctal plugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

cycloSPORINE (EENT) Dosage and Administration

Administration

For ophthalmic use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not for injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not for subconjunctival injection or introduction directly into the anterior chamber of the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic emulsion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of emulsion container.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Invert unit-dose vial a few times before use to obtain a uniform, opaque, white emulsion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Preservative-free emulsion is for single use only in one or both eyes; discard any unused portion immediately after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Remove contact lenses prior to administration; may reinsert lenses 15 minutes after dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

When used concomitantly with artificial tears, administer ophthalmic preparations at least 15 minutes apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

1 drop of a 0.05% emulsion into each eye twice daily, approximately 12 hours apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

cycloSPORINE (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Blood cyclosporin A concentrations were below quantitation limit (0.1 ng/mL) after topical application of cyclosporine 0.05% emulsion to the eye twice daily for up to 12 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> No detectable drug accumulation in blood during 12 months of treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Exact mechanism of action not fully elucidated, but thought to act as a partial immunomodulator with anti-inflammatory effects when administered topically to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

  • Topical application to the eye reduces cell-mediated inflammatory responses associated with inflammatory ocular surface diseases.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

  • Exhibits immunosuppressive activity when administered systemically.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contamination of the product.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of administering ophthalmic emulsion immediately after opening single-use vial and discarding any unused portion immediately after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not wearing contact lenses in presence of decreased tear production.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If contact lenses are worn, importance of removing lenses prior to administration and delaying reinsertion of lenses for 15 minutes after instillation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • If using artificial tears and ophthalmic cyclosporine, importance of allowing at least 15 minutes to elapse between administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
