Uses for Alefacept

Psoriasis

Management of moderate to severe chronic plaque psoriasis in adults who are candidates for systemic therapy or phototherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not use concomitantly with other immunosuppressive agents or in patients currently receiving phototherapy; concomitant use of low-potency topical corticosteroids was permitted in clinical studies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Alefacept Dosage and Administration

Administration

Use only under supervision of a clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer IV or IM; components for either IV or IM reconstitution and administration are supplied with the vials.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Preparations.)

Use strict aseptic technique, since drug product contains no preservative.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not filter reconstituted solution during preparation or administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not admix with other drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer once weekly by rapid IV injection for a 12-week course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Before administration, remove needle used for reconstitution, attach second needle provided by manufacturer, and withdraw 0.5 mL of alefacept solution into syringe.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prepare 2 syringes containing 3 mL of 0.9% sodium chloride injection for use as pre- and post-administration flushes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Prime administration set with 3 mL 0.9% sodium chloride injection and insert into vein, then attach syringe containing alefacept to administration set and administer solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> After alefacept administration, flush with 3 mL 0.9% sodium chloride injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute vial containing 7.5 mg of alefacept with only 0.6 mL of diluent, to provide a solution containing 7.5 mg per 0.5 mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use only the diluent supplied by manufacturer (sterile water for injection in a 10-mL single-use vial).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

With needle pointed toward side of vial, slowly inject diluent into vial.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Swirl contents gently (dissolution generally takes <2 minutes).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> To avoid excessive foaming, do not shake vial and avoid vigorous agitation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Preferably, prepare immediately before use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discard if not used within 4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Storage under Stability.)

Cautions for Alefacept

Contraindications

  • HIV infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> (See Lymphopenia and also see Infectious Complications under Cautions.)

  • Known hypersensitivity to alefacept or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

HIV infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> (See Lymphopenia and also see Infectious Complications under Cautions.)

Known hypersensitivity to alefacept or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of lymphopenia, including dose-dependent reductions in circulating CD4+ and CD8+ T-cell counts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not initiate in patients with CD4+ T-cell counts below normal range.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor CD4+ T-cell counts before initiation of therapy and every 2 weeks throughout the 12-week course of therapy; dosage adjustment may be necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

Do not use in patients with HIV infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Potential for accelerated progression of HIV or increased infectious complications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications under Cautions.)

Possible increased risk of malignancies (e.g., basal or squamous cell skin cancers, melanoma, other solid tumors, lymphomas).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not use in patients with history of malignancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Caution advised when considering use in patients at high risk for malignancy; discontinue if malignancy develops.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible increased risk of infection, including reactivation of latent infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not use in patients with clinically important infections (e.g., HIV infection).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications and also Lymphopenia, under Cautions.) Caution advised if used in patients with chronic infection or history of recurrent infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor carefully for manifestations of infection during and after treatment; discontinue if serious infection develops.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., urticaria, angioedema) reported; if serious hypersensitivity reaction occurs, discontinue immediately and initiate appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not use concomitantly with other immunosuppressive agents or phototherapy (due to potential for excessive immunosuppression).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy of live virus vaccine administration in patients receiving alefacept not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Asymptomatic increases in serum AST and/or ALT concentrations, fatty infiltration of the liver, hepatitis, decompensation of cirrhosis with hepatic failure, and acute hepatic failure reported during postmarketing experience; causal relationship to drug not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Fully evaluate patients with manifestations of hepatic injury.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discontinue alefacept if clinically important hepatic injury develops.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible development of low-titer antibodies to alefacept; relationship between development of antibodies and clinical response or incidence of adverse effects not observed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Long-term immunogenicity remains to be determined.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Interferes with T-cell activation by specifically binding to the antigen CD2 on T cells and inhibiting the interaction between LFA-3 on antigen-presenting cells and CD2.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Potential for reduction of lymphocyte counts, which could increase risk of infection or malignancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of informing clinicians promptly if any signs or symptoms of infection or malignancy occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Necessity of administration under supervision of a clinician, with regular monitoring of T-cell counts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed during or within 8 weeks following discontinuance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For any woman who becomes pregnant, availability of pregnancy registry (see Pregnancy under Cautions).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of serious hepatic injury.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of reporting persistent nausea, anorexia, fatigue, vomiting, abdominal pain, jaundice, easy bruising, dark urine, or pale stools to clinicians.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
