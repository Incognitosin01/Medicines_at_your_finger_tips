Warnings/Precautions

Possible precipitation of heart failure.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid use in patients with decompensated heart failure; may use cautiously in patients with inadequate myocardial function and, if necessary, in patients with well-compensated heart failure (e.g., those controlled with cardiac glycosides and/or diuretics).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adequate treatment (e.g., with a cardiac glycoside and/or diuretic) and close observation recommended if signs or symptoms of impending heart failure occur; if heart failure continues, discontinue therapy, gradually if possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Abrupt discontinuance of therapy is not recommended as it may exacerbate angina symptoms or precipitate MI in patients with CAD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Gradually decrease dosage over a period of about 2 weeks and monitor patients carefully; advise patients to temporarily limit their physical activity during Withdrawal of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If exacerbation of angina occurs or acute coronary insufficiency develops, reinstitute therapy promptly and initiate appropriate measures for the management of unstable angina pectoris.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible bronchoconstriction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Generally should not be used in patients with bronchospastic disease, but may be used with caution in such patients who do not respond to or cannot tolerate alternative treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer the lowest effective dosage (5–10 mg once daily); a bronchodilator (e.g., a β2-adrenergic agonist) should be available.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible risks associated with general anesthesia (e.g., severe hypotension, difficulty maintaining heart beat) due to decreased ability of the heart to respond to reflex β-adrenergic stimuli.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use with caution in patients undergoing major surgery involving general anesthesia; anesthetics used should not cause myocardial depression.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible decreased signs and symptoms of hypoglycemia (e.g., may mask tachycardia but not sweating or dizziness).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with diabetes mellitus receiving hypoglycemic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Signs of hyperthyroidism (e.g., tachycardia) may be masked.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Possible thyroid storm if therapy is abruptly withdrawn; carefully monitor patients having or suspected of developing thyrotoxicosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible increased reactivity to repeated, accidental, diagnostic, or therapeutic challenges with a variety of allergens while taking β-blocking agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Such patients may be unresponsive to usual doses of epinephrine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients
