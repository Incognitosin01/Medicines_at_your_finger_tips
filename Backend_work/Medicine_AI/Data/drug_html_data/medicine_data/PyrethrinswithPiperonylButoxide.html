Introduction

Pediculicide; fixed-combination preparation containing pyrethrins (a pediculicide) and piperonyl butoxide (has little or no insecticidal activity but potentiates that of pyrethrins).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Pyrethrins with Piperonyl Butoxide Pharmacokinetics

Information regarding elimination of pyrethrins and piperonyl butoxide following topical application not available.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Actions and Spectrum

  • Pyrethrins (also known as pyrethrum extract) contains the purified derivatives of pyrethrum flowers (Chrysanthemum cinerariaefolium) and is a complex of substances that includes the alcohols and esters of pyrethrolone and cinerolone, the alcohol of chrysanthemic acid, and the ester of pyrethric acid.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Piperonyl butoxide is a synthetic piperic acid derivative.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Following absorption through the chitinous exoskeleton of arthropods, pyrethrins stimulates the nervous system and blocks nerve impulse transmissions, resulting in paralysis and death.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Piperonyl butoxide has little or no insecticidal activity, but potentiates that of pyrethrins by inhibiting the hydrolytic enzymes responsible for metabolism of pyrethrins in arthropods.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Pyrethrins is active against Pediculus humanus var. capitis (head louse), P. humanus var. corporis (body louse), and Phthirus pubis (pubic or crab louse), and may have some activity against their nits (eggs).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Pyrethrins also is toxic to houseflies, fleas, chiggers, and mosquitoes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Therapeutic failure and resistance to pyrethrins has been reported in P. humanus.<html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html> The prevalence of resistance in the US is unclear.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html>

  • Treatment failures do not necessarily mean resistance is present since failures can also be related to misdiagnosis, noncompliance with the treatment regimen, and reinfestation.<html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html>

Treatment failures do not necessarily mean resistance is present since failures can also be related to misdiagnosis, noncompliance with the treatment regimen, and reinfestation.<html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>116</sup></body></html>
