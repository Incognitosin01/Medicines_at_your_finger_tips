Introduction

Sclerosing agent; nonionic detergent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Uses for Polidocanol

Sclerotherapy

Treatment of uncomplicated spider veins (varicose veins ≤1 mm in diameter) in lower extremity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Treatment of uncomplicated reticular veins (varicose veins 1–3 mm in diameter) in lower extremity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Not studied in varicose veins >3 mm in diameter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Polidocanol Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer only by IV injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid extravasation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Administration Precautions under Cautions.)

Commercially available as 0.5 or 1% solution in single-use ampuls (containing a total of 10 or 20 mg, respectively, in each 2-mL ampul).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Has been administered as an endovenous foam or microfoam;<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> these preparations not commercially available in US and extemporaneous preparation of polidocanol foam not approved by FDA.

Use a syringe (glass or plastic) with a fine needle (typically, 26- or 30-gauge).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Insert needle tangentially into target vein and inject solution slowly while needle is still in vein.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> To prevent vein rupture, only apply gentle pressure during injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

After removing needle from target vein and covering injection site, apply compression in the form of a stocking or bandage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> After treatment session, have patient walk for 15–20 minutes and observe patient to detect any anaphylactic or allergic reaction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Sensitivity Reactions under Cautions.)

Advise patient to maintain compression (stocking or bandage) for 2–3 days after treatment of spider veins or 5–7 days after treatment of reticular veins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For extensive varicosities, longer compression treatment with compression bandages or gradient compression stocking of a higher compression class is recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If small intravaricose blood clots (thrombi) develop, they may be removed by stab incision and thrombus expression (microthrombectomy).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Concentration of polidocanol solution (0.5 or 1%) and injection volume depend on type and size of varicose veins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> After polidocanol treatment, compression is necessary to reduce risk of deep vein thrombosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See IV Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

Spider veins (varicose veins ≤1 mm in diameter): 0.1–0.3 mL (0.5–1.5 mg) of 0.5% solution for each injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not exceed 10 mL (50 mg) per treatment session.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reticular veins (varicose veins 1–3 mm in diameter): 0.1–0.3 mL (1–3 mg) of 1% solution for each injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not exceed 10 mL (100 mg) per treatment session.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Repeat treatments may be necessary if extent of varicose veins requires >10 mL of solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Separate treatment sessions by 1–2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

Maximum 0.3 mL of 0.5 or 1% solution (1.5 or 3 mg, respectively) per injection site; maximum 10 mL per treatment session.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Polidocanol

Contraindications

  • Known allergy (anaphylaxis) to polidocanol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Sensitivity Reactions under Cautions.)

  • Acute thromboembolic diseases.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known allergy (anaphylaxis) to polidocanol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Sensitivity Reactions under Cautions.)

Acute thromboembolic diseases.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Severe allergic reactions (e.g., anaphylactic reactions), including some fatalities, reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Severe reactions more frequent with larger volumes (>3 mL).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use minimal required dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Postmarketing reports of allergic dermatitis, angioedema, generalized urticaria, and asthma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Observe patient for 15–20 minutes after polidocanol treatment session in case an anaphylactic or allergic reaction occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Clinicians should be prepared to provide appropriate treatment of anaphylaxis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Extravasation may result in severe adverse local effects, including tissue necrosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Carefully place IV needle into target vein;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> use smallest effective volume of solution at each injection site.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Overdosage may result in higher incidence of localized reactions such as necrosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

After polidocanol treatment session, apply compression to treatment area with a stocking or bandage and have patient walk for 15–20 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See IV Administration under Dosage and Administration.) Supervise patient during this period in case an anaphylactic or allergic reaction occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Sensitivity Reactions under Cautions.)

Accidental intra-arterial injection may cause severe necrosis, ischemia, or gangrene.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Immediately consult vascular surgeon if occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Inadvertent perivascular injection can cause pain.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If pain is severe, a local anesthetic (without adrenaline) may be injected.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C. <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not use during pregnancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Effects on labor and delivery unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Potential for serious adverse reactions in nursing infants; avoid use in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Injection site reactions including hematoma, irritation, discoloration, pain, pruritus, warmth, neovascularization, thrombosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Polidocanol

No drug interaction studies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Polidocanol Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Rapid onset and offset of local sclerosing effect.<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Following IV injection of 4.5–18 mg in 4 adults, low systemic plasma concentrations were observed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> Peak concentrations occurred 5 minutes after IV injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Not known.<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Not known.<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Mean apparent terminal half-life is 1.5 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

15–30°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use immediately after opening ampul; does not contain a preservative.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • A nonionic detergent consisting of 2 components: a polar hydrophilic (dodecyl alcohol) chain and an apolar hydrophobic (polyethylene oxide) chain.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Used as a sclerosing agent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Has a concentration- and volume-dependent damaging effect on blood vessel endothelium.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Following IV injection, induces local endothelial damage in target vein.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Platelets then aggregate at site of damage and attach to the venous wall; eventually, a dense network of platelets, cellular debris, and fibrin occludes the vessel.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Finally, the occluded vein is replaced with connective fibrous tissue.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Effect on the vein is permanent.<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Advise patients that compression will be applied in the form of a stocking or bandage immediately after polidocanol treatment to reduce the risk of deep vein thrombosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of walking for 15–20 minutes immediately after polidocanol treatment and then daily for next few days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of wearing compression stockings or support hose on the treated legs continuously for 2–3 days after treatment of spider veins or for 5–7 days after treatment of reticular veins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> After this initial period, importance of wearing compression stockings or support hose during daytime for an additional 2–3 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Depending on area treated, compression stockings or support hose should be thigh or knee high to provide adequate coverage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to avoid heavy exercise, sunbathing, long plane flights, and hot baths or sauna for 2–3 days after polidocanol treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and dietary or herbal supplements, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
