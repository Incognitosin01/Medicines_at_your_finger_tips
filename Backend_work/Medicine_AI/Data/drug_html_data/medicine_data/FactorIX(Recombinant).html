Uses for Factor IX (Recombinant)

Hemophilia B

Prevention and control of bleeding episodes in patients with hemophilia B (congenital factor IX deficiency or Christmas disease).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

Maintenance of hemostasis in patients with hemophilia B undergoing surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

Designated an orphan drug by FDA for treatment of hemophilia B.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>

Also used for routine prophylaxis (i.e., administration at regular intervals) to prevent or reduce frequency of bleeding events.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>176</sup></body></html> Such prophylactic therapy currently considered the standard of care for patients with hemophilia B.<html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>176</sup></body></html> Decreases frequency of spontaneous musculoskeletal hemorrhage, preserves joint function, and improves quality of life.<html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>185</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>186</sup></body></html>

Several factor IX concentrates are currently available in the US, including a variety of recombinant and plasma-derived preparations; the Medical and Scientific Advisory Council (MASAC) of the National Hemophilia Foundation recommends preferential use of recombinant factor IX preparations because of their potentially superior safety profile with respect to pathogen transmission.<html><head></head><body><sup>155</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>156</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>176</sup></body></html> Other experts (e.g., World Federation of Hemophilia) state that choice of preparation should be determined by local criteria.<html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> When selecting an appropriate factor IX preparation, consider characteristics of each clotting factor concentrate, individual patient variables, patient/provider preference, and emerging data.<html><head></head><body><sup>155</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>156</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>187</sup></body></html>

Not indicated for the treatment of other coagulation factor deficiencies (e.g., factors II, VII, VIII, X) or for management of hemophilia A in patients with inhibitors to factor VIII.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not indicated for reversal of coumarin-induced anticoagulation or for treatment of bleeding associated with low levels of liver-dependent coagulation factors.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy of factor IX (recombinant) for induction of immune tolerance in patients with hemophilia B not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Dose (potency) expressed in terms of international units (IU, units) of factor IX activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> Potency is determined by an aPTT-based one-stage clotting assay calibrated against a WHO standard.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html>

Estimate initial dose using the following formula:<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html>

Dose (units) = body weight (in kg) x reciprocal of observed recovery (in units/kg per units/dL) × desired factor IX increase (in % of normal or units/dL)

Determine desired factor IX level by the clinical situation and severity of bleeding.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> For recommendations on target factor IX levels for a given clinical situation, see the specific dosage sections below. Incremental recovery should be based on patient's individual recovery determined by serial factor IX activity assays; however, an initial dose may be calculated empirically based on expected recovery values observed in clinical trials.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> In clinical trials, administration of 1 unit/kg BeneFIX generally increased factor IX levels by approximately 0.8% in adults and 0.7% in children <15 years of age; administration of 1 unit/kg Rixubis generally increased factor IX levels by approximately 0.9% in adults and adolescents ≥12 years of age and 0.7% in children <12 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

These calculations and suggested dosage regimens are only approximations and should not preclude appropriate clinical monitoring and individualization of dosage based on the hemostatic requirements of patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> Perform serial assays of factor IX activity (by the one-stage clotting assay) whenever possible to ensure that adequate levels of factor IX have been attained and maintained.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adjust subsequent dosage based on individual clinical and pharmacokinetic (e.g., half-life, incremental recovery) response.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

If calculated dose is ineffective in achieving appropriate factor IX levels, consider possibility that inhibitors may have developed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>171</sup></body></html> (See Development of Inhibitors to Factor IX under Cautions.)

Factor IX (Recombinant) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

In vivo recovery of factor IX following IV administration of 50 units/kg factor IX (recombinant) as the BeneFIX preparation was approximately 28% lower than that achieved following administration of equivalent dose of plasma-derived factor IX; difference presumably due to structural modifications of factor IX (recombinant).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html>

Decreased in vivo recovery in pediatric patients ≤15 years of age compared with patients >15 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> Incremental recovery of Rixubis in pediatric patients <12 years of age approximately 22% lower than in older individuals; mean incremental recovery lower in younger (<6 years of age) versus older (6–12 years of age) pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetic profiles of the 2 currently available preparations of factor IX (recombinant) (BeneFIX and Rixubis) appear to be comparable.<html><head></head><body><sup>27</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Readily diffuses through interstitial fluid; distributes through both intravascular and extravascular compartments.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Circulates in plasma as unbound drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Binds rapidly and reversibly to vascular endothelium.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Not known whether factor IX (recombinant) crosses the placenta or is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Clearance correlates with body weight; generally increases through adolescence, then stabilizes during adulthood.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> Clearance of Rixubis is higher in younger (<6 years of age) versus older (6–12 years of age) pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

Half-life of BeneFIX is approximately 18–24 hours in adults and approximately 20–21 hours in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Half-life of Rixubis is approximately 26.7 hours in adults and adolescents ≥12 years of age; in pediatric patients, half-life is approximately 23 hours for those 6–11 years of age and approximately 28 hours for those < 6 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

BeneFIX: Store at room temperature (≤30°C) or under refrigeration (2–8°C); avoid freezing to prevent damage to diluent syringe.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Rixubis: Store at 2–8°C for up to 24 months; do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html> May store at room temperature (≤30°C) for a period of up to 12 months; once stored at room temperature, do not return to refrigerator.<html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>

May store reconstituted solution of BeneFIX or Rixubis at room temperature prior to administration; use solution within 3 hours of reconstitution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>25</sup></body></html>
