Uses for Loteprednol

Allergic Conjunctivitis

Symptomatic relief of seasonal allergic (hay fever, pollinosis) conjunctivitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Inflammatory Ocular Disorders

Symptomatic relief of corticosteroid-responsive inflammatory conditions of the palpebral and bulbar conjunctiva, cornea, and anterior segment of the globe (i.e., allergic conjunctivitis, acne rosacea keratitis, superficial punctate keratitis, herpes zoster keratitis, iritis, and cyclitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> Use in conjunction with appropriate anti-infective therapy in some cases of conjunctival infection, when the benefits of reducing edema and inflammation outweigh the risks associated with use of topical corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Loteprednol etabonate 0.5% less effective than prednisolone acetate 1% in the management of anterior uveitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html>

Postoperative Ocular Inflammation

Treatment of postoperative ocular inflammation following ocular surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>27</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>

Bacterial Ophthalmic Infections

Used in fixed combination with tobramycin in certain bacterial ocular infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of glaucoma (with damage to optic nerve), defects in visual acuity and fields of vision, and posterior subcapsular cataract formation with prolonged use of corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html> Use with caution in the presence of glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html> Marked elevation in IOP (≥10 mm Hg) reported with loteprednol use; incidence lower than that reported with prednisolone acetate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

In conditions causing thinning of the cornea or sclera, perforations reported with use of topical corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

Risk of secondary ocular infection with prolonged use of corticosteroids.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

In acute purulent ocular conditions, corticosteroids may mask infection or exacerbate existing infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Abnormal/blurred vision, burning on instillation, chemosis, discharge, dry eyes, epiphora, foreign body sensation, itching, injection, photophobia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Adverse systemic events include headache, rhinitis, and pharyngitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Loteprednol Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Limited systemic exposure following topical application to the eye; plasma concentrations usually are undetectable.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In clinical studies in patients with seasonal allergic conjunctivitis, improvement in ocular itching and bulbar conjunctival injection occurred within 2 hours following initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

In clinical studies in patients with contact lens-associated giant papillary conjunctivitis, improvement in papillae, conjunctival injection and itching, and lens intolerance was evident within 1 week of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

In patients with seasonal allergic conjunctivitis, improvement in ocular itching and bulbar conjunctival injection persisted through day 14 of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

In patients with contact lens-associated giant papillary conjunctivitis, improvement in papillae, conjunctival injection and itching, and lens intolerance persisted for up to 6 weeks while therapy was continued.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>
