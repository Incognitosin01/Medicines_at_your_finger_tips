Introduction

Antiemetic; selective, second-generation inhibitor of type 3 serotonergic (5-HT3) receptors.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>

Uses for Palonosetron

Cancer Chemotherapy-induced Nausea and Vomiting

Palonosetron is used IV for the prevention of acute and delayed nausea and vomiting associated with initial and repeat courses of moderately emetogenic cancer chemotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Palonosetron also is used IV for the prevention of acute nausea and vomiting associated with initial and repeat courses of highly emetogenic cancer chemotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For prevention of nausea and vomiting associated with highly emetogenic chemotherapy regimens (including an anthracycline plus cyclophosphamide), ASCO recommends a 3-drug antiemetic regimen consisting of an NK1 receptor antagonist (e.g., either oral aprepitant or IV fosaprepitant), a 5-HT3 receptor antagonist (e.g., dolasetron, granisetron, ondansetron, palonosetron), and dexamethasone.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> ASCO states that fixed-combination netupitant and palonosetron plus dexamethasone is an additional treatment option.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html>

For moderately emetogenic chemotherapy regimens, ASCO recommends a 2-drug antiemetic regimen preferably consisting of palonosetron and dexamethasone.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> If palonosetron is not available, a first-generation 5-HT3 receptor antagonist (preferably granisetron or ondansetron) may be substituted.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html> Limited evidence suggests that aprepitant may be added to this regimen; in such cases, use of any 5-HT3 receptor antagonist is appropriate.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>

For chemotherapy regimens with a low emetogenic risk, ASCO recommends administration of a single dose of dexamethasone prior to chemotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>

For chemotherapy regimens with minimal emetogenic risk, ASCO states that routine antiemetic administration is not necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>28</sup></body></html>

Postoperative Nausea and Vomiting

Palonosetron is used IV for the prevention of postoperative nausea and vomiting for up to 24 hours following surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Efficacy of the drug beyond 24 hours not demonstrated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Routine prophylaxis not recommended in patients in whom there is little expectation that nausea and/or vomiting will occur postoperatively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Recommended for patients in whom nausea and/or vomiting must be avoided postoperatively, even when anticipated incidence is low.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Palonosetron Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer IV.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Visually inspect solution for particulate matter and discoloration prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not mix with other drugs; flush tubing with 0.9% sodium chloride injection before and after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For prevention of cancer chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting in adults, administer IV over a period of 30 seconds.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For prevention of cancer chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting in pediatric patients 1 month to <17 years of age, administer IV over 15 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For prevention of postoperative nausea and vomiting in adults, administer IV over a period of 10 seconds.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as palonosetron hydrochloride; dosage expressed in terms of palonosetron.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pediatric patients 1 month to <17 years of age: 20 mcg/kg (up to a maximum of 1.5 mg) as a single dose administered by IV infusion approximately 30 minutes before administration of chemotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states that a clinical study demonstrated that pediatric patients require a higher dose of palonosetron to prevent cancer chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting compared with adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

0.25 mg as a single dose administered IV approximately 30 minutes before administration of chemotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

0.075 mg as a single dose administered IV immediately before induction of anesthesia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Palonosetron

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to palonosetron or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to palonosetron or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Hypersensitivity reactions, including anaphylaxis, reported in palonosetron-treated patients with or without known hypersensitivity to other 5-HT3 receptor antagonists.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>33</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Serotonin syndrome (in some cases fatal) reported in patients receiving 5-HT3 receptor antagonists.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Most cases were associated with concomitant use of other serotonergic drugs (e.g., SSRIs, SNRIs, MAO inhibitors, mirtazapine, fentanyl, lithium, tramadol, IV methylene blue).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Serotonin syndrome occurring with overdosage of another 5-HT3 receptor antagonist alone (ondansetron) also reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html> The majority of reports of serotonin syndrome related to 5-HT3 receptor antagonist use occurred in a post-anesthesia care unit or infusion center.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manifestations of serotonin syndrome may include mental status changes (e.g., agitation, hallucinations, delirium, coma), autonomic instability (e.g., tachycardia, labile BP, dizziness, diaphoresis, flushing, hyperthermia), neuromuscular symptoms (e.g., tremor, rigidity, myoclonus, hyperreflexia, incoordination), and seizures with or without GI symptoms (e.g., nausea, vomiting, diarrhea).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for emergence of serotonin syndrome, particularly with concomitant use of other serotonergic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If symptoms of serotonin syndrome occur, discontinue palonosetron and initiate supportive treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)
