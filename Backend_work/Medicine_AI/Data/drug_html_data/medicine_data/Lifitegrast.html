Uses for Lifitegrast

Dry Eye Disease

Management of dry eye disease (also known as keratoconjunctivitis sicca).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Lifitegrast Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For topical ophthalmic use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Remove contact lenses prior to administration; may reinsert lenses 15 minutes after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid eye injury or contamination of the solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not touch tip of single-use container to the eye or any other surface.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Sufficient amount of solution in each single-use container for administration of 1 dose into each eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Preservative-free solution is for single use only; discard any unused portion immediately after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Dosage

1 drop of a 5% solution in each eye twice daily, approximately 12 hours apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special population recommendations at this time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Lifitegrast

Contraindications

Manufacturer states none known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

No data available regarding use in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No evidence of teratogenicity in pregnant rats.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Increased incidence of omphalocele in pregnant rabbits.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> No data available regarding effects on breast-fed infant or milk production.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consider developmental and health benefits of breast-feeding along with mother's clinical need for the drug and any potential adverse effects of the drug on the infant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients <17 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in safety or efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Ocular effects: Instillation site irritation, reduced visual acuity, blurred vision, conjunctival hyperemia, irritation, increased lacrimation, discharge, discomfort, pruritus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Nonocular effects: Dysgeusia, headache, sinusitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Lifitegrast Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Rapidly absorbed into ocular tissues following topical application;<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> peak tear concentrations occur at 0.3 hours after twice-daily administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Does not produce clinically important systemic exposure of lifitegrast or systemic inhibition of lymphocyte function.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Following topical administration of lifitegrast 5% solution to the eye twice daily for 180 and/or 360 days, 19% of patients had lifitegrast trough concentrations above the lower limit of quantitation (0.5 ng/mL).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether distributed into milk after topical application to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Cleared from plasma in 1–4 hours after topical application to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

20–25°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid eye injury or contamination of the ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of storing single-use containers in original foil pouch until ready to use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of administering ophthalmic solution immediately after opening the single-use container and discarding any unused portion immediately after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • If contact lenses are worn, importance of removing lenses prior to administration and delaying reinsertion of lenses for 15 minutes after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
