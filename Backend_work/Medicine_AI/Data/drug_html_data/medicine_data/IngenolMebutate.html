Macrocyclic diterpene ester; inducer of cell death.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Uses for Ingenol Mebutate

Actinic Keratosis

Treatment of actinic keratosis lesions on the face and scalp (0.015% gel) or on the trunk and extremities (0.05% gel).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Appears to have similar efficacy as other topical treatments (e.g., fluorouracil, imiquimod, diclofenac) with a shorter duration of treatment (2–3 days versus 4–16 weeks); however, clinical studies that directly compare the safety and efficacy of these drugs are needed.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

When selecting a regimen for treatment of actinic keratoses (e.g., topical drug therapy, photodynamic therapy, cryotherapy), consider the number of lesions, the individual patient's desired outcome (e.g., cosmetic effects, relief of associated symptoms, prevention of skin cancer), and adverse effects associated with the regimen.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Ingenol Mebutate Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically as a 0.015 or 0.05% gel.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For external use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not administer orally, vaginally, or topically to the lips or eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

FDA has received reports of severe eye injuries and skin reactions associated with the application of the drug; some cases were associated with application of the gel jn a manner other than that recommended in the product labeling.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> (See Cautions: Warnings/Precautions and see Advice to Patients.)

Apply evenly to affected area, up to one contiguous area of skin approximately 25 cm2.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Wash hands immediately after applying ingenol mebutate gel.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Allow gel to dry for 15 minutes; avoid washing or touching the treated area for 6 hours after application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> After 6 hours, may wash area with a mild soap.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

0.015% Gel: Apply evenly to affected area once daily for 3 consecutive days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

0.05% Gel: Apply evenly to affected area once daily for 2 consecutive days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

Use only enough gel to cover the treatment area (≤1 single-use tube per application); do not use more often or for longer than instructed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Ingenol Mebutate

Contraindications

  • No known contraindications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No known contraindications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

FDA has warned of reports of severe allergic reactions and herpes zoster (shingles) associated with the use of ingenol mebutate gel.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Allergic reaction may include throat tightness, difficulty breathing, feeling faint, or swelling of the lips or tongue.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Patients who experience a severe allergic reaction should stop using the drug and seek immediate medical attention.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Severe skin reactions (e.g., erythema, crusting, swelling, vesiculation/pustulation, erosion/ulceration) reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Local skin reactions typically occur ≤1 day following treatment initiation, peak in intensity ≤1 week following completion of treatment, and resolve ≤2 weeks following treatment of the face or scalp and ≤4 weeks following treatment of the trunk or extremities.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not apply until skin has healed from any previous drug or surgical treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Patients should stop using the drug and contact their health care professionals if they develop hives, itching, or severe skin rash.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

FDA has received reports of severe eye injuries associated with application of ingenol mebutate gel.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Ocular exposure may result in adverse effects (e.g., severe eye pain, eyelid edema, ptosis, periorbital edema).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in patients <18 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in safety and efficacy reported in patients ≥65 years of age relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Local skin reactions: Erythema, flaking/scaling, crusting, swelling, vesiculation/pustulation, erosion/ulceration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Application site reactions: Pain, pruritus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Ingenol Mebutate

Does not inhibit CYP1A2, 2A6, 2B6, 2C8, 2C9, 2C19, 2D6, 2E1, and 3A4 or induce CYP 1A2, 2C9, and 3A4.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No formal drug interaction studies to date; clinically important interactions following topical application considered unlikely.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Ingenol Mebutate Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Minimally absorbed following application; plasma concentrations of ingenol mebutate and metabolites were below lower limit of quantification (0.1 ng/mL).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

2–8°C (may be exposed to 0–15°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid freezing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Macrocyclic diterpene ester extracted from the sap of the plant Euphorbia peplus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Induces rapid and direct cell death and causes an immune response mediated by specific activation of protein kinase C delta, including neutrophil-mediated oxidative burst and clearance of tumors.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of providing patient a copy of manufacturer's patient information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients that ingenol mebutate gel is for external use only and should not be used in the eyes, mouth, lips, or vagina.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Patients should use ingenol mebutate gel as prescribed by their health care professionals; the drug should not be used on a larger area of skin or for a longer period than instructed in the prescribing information.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients who experience a severe allergic reaction to stop using ingenol mebutate gel and to seek immediate medical attention.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients to wash their hands thoroughly with soap and water following application and to avoid inadvertent transfer of gel to other areas or to another person.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Accidental transfer of drug from the hands, even after washing, has occurred (e.g., through application of make-up, insertion of contact lenses).<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of avoiding contact with eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Risk of ocular problems (e.g., severe eye pain, eyelid edema, ptosis, periorbital edema).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In case of accidental ocular exposure, importance of flushing the eyes with large amounts of water and seeking medical care as soon as possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of instructing patients to allow the treated area to dry for 15 minutes following application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Advise patients to avoid washing or touching the treated area or participating in activities that cause excessive sweating for 6 hours after treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients to keep ingenol mebutate gel out of the reach of children.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of local skin reactions; importance of informing clinician if severe local skin reactions occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and herbal products, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
