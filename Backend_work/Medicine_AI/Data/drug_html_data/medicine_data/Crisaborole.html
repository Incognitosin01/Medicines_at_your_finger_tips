Uses for Crisaborole

Atopic Dermatitis

Topical treatment of mild to moderate atopic dermatitis (eczema) in adults and children ≥2 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Crisaborole Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically as a 2% ointment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For external use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not administer orally, vaginally, or topically to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Apply evenly as a thin layer to affected areas of skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Wash hands after applying, unless hands are being treated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Children ≥2 years of age: Apply thin layer of 2% ointment to affected areas of skin twice daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Apply thin layer of 2% ointment to affected areas of skin twice daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Crisaborole

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to crisaborole or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to crisaborole or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., urticaria) reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Suspect hypersensitivity if severe pruritus, swelling, and erythema occur at site of application or at distant site.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If signs and symptoms of hypersensitivity occur, discontinue immediately and initiate appropriate treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Data are inadequate regarding use of topical crisaborole in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Adverse embryofetal effects not observed in animal studies using oral crisaborole.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether systemically absorbed crisaborole is distributed into human milk, affects human milk production, or affects breast-fed infants.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consider benefits of breast-feeding and importance of the drug to the woman;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> also consider potential adverse effects on the breast-fed infant from the drug or underlying maternal condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children <2 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Application site pain (burning, stinging).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Crisaborole Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Rapidly absorbed systemically following topical application to the skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Following topical application of crisaborole 2% ointment (approximately 3 mg/cm2) twice daily for 8 days in pediatric patients 2–17 years of age with atopic dermatitis, plasma concentrations of the drug were detectable in all patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Mean peak plasma concentration was 127 ng/mL;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> steady-state plasma concentrations were attained by day 8.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reduction of pruritus occurs as early as 8 days after initiation of topical crisaborole.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether systemically absorbed crisaborole is distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

97%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Systemically absorbed drug is rapidly and extensively metabolized to inactive metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Systemically absorbed drug excreted principally in urine as inactive metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

20–25°C (may be exposed to 15–30°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Boron-based benzoxaborole PDE4 inhibitor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

  • PDE4 is a phosphodiesterase expressed in inflammatory cells associated with atopic dermatitis and is involved in metabolism of cyclic adenosine-3',5'-monophosphate (cAMP).<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Selective inhibition of PDE4 results in accumulation of intracellular cAMP, a modulator of inflammatory responses, which can downregulate inflammatory responses by inhibiting expression of inflammatory cytokines, thereby reducing inflammation in patients with atopic dermatitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

  • Crisaborole, unlike other commercially available PDE4 inhibitors, is a boron-based benzoxaborole PDE4 inhibitor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> The drug preferentially inhibits PDE4 and suppresses tumor necrosis factor (TNF; TNF-α), interleukin-2 (IL-2), interleukin-4 (IL-4), interleukin-5 (IL-5), interleukin-13 (IL-13), interleukin-17 (IL-17), interleukin-22 (IL-22), interleukin-23 (IL-23), and interferon-γ secretion from human leukocytes.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

  • Exact mechanism(s) of action of crisaborole in the management of atopic dermatitis not elucidated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Provide patient with a copy of the manufacturer's patient information with each prescription of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that crisaborole should only be used as directed by a clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that crisaborole ointment is for external use only and that contact with the eyes should be avoided;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> importance of not using the drug orally or intravaginally.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of washing hands immediately after applying crisaborole, unless the hands are being treated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to discontinue the drug and immediately contact a clinician if signs or symptoms of hypersensitivity (e.g., hives, itching, swelling, redness) occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
