Administration

Administer by intradermal injection using Mantoux method.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Avoid administering by IV, IM, or sub-Q.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Sub-Q injection lacks diagnostic value and may result in adverse reactions (e.g., general febrile reaction, acute inflammation around old tuberculous lesions) in highly sensitive individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Use tuberculin syringe with short (0.25–0.5 inch) 26- or 27-gauge needle.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Use a separate sterile, single-use disposable syringe and needle for each individual patient to prevent transmission of infectious agents (e.g., HIV, hepatitis virus).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> To avoid contamination of vial content (and subsequent transmission of infectious agents to other individuals), never use the same needle and/or syringe to re-enter multidose vial, even when used for the same individual.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

To prepare dose, wipe vial stopper with suitable germicide (e.g., 70% alcohol) and use aseptic technique to draw exact dose into syringe, taking care to exclude air bubbles.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> .

Prior to intradermal injection, cleanse administration site with 70% alcohol or other antiseptic and allow to dry.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Usual injection site is volar (preferred) or dorsal surface of forearm, approximately 4 inches below elbow.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>121</sup></body></html> Avoid hairy areas, areas with lesions, areas near veins, swollen or red areas,<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>121</sup></body></html> and areas without adequate subcutaneous tissue (e.g., concavities over tendon or bone).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Potential for a drop of blood when needle withdrawn from injection site.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Remove any blood at injection site gently with gauze to avoid squeezing out tuberculin.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Avoid recapping needle following use; properly dispose of used needles and syringes.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

A pale bleb about 6–10 mm in diameter should form at injection site.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> Bleb is absorbed within minutes; no dressing required.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Risk of severe reaction at test site in individuals who previously experienced a severe reaction (e.g., vesiculation, ulceration, necrosis).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Use not recommended in such individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications under Cautions.)

Possible decreased ability to respond to test (resulting in false-negative reaction) in individuals with any condition that impairs or attenuates cell-mediated immunity, including viral infections (e.g., HIV, infectious mononucleosis, influenza, measles, mumps, rubella, varicella), overwhelming TB or tuberculous pleurisy, other bacterial infections (e.g., brucellosis, leprosy, pertussis, typhoid fever, typhus), fungal infections (e.g., blastomycosis), diseases affecting lymphoid organs (e.g., Hodgkin's disease, chronic leukemia, lymphoma, sarcoidosis), or malignancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>
