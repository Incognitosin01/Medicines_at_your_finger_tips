Use: Labeled Indications

Relief of pain and itching: Temporary relief of pain and itching associated with insect bites; sunburn; scrapes; minor cuts, minor skin irritations, and minor burns; or rashes due to poison ivy, poison oak, and poison sumac

Contraindications

Concurrent use with any other product containing diphenhydramine (including oral); use on large area of the body

Dosing: Adult

Relief of pain and itching: Topical: Apply 1% or 2% to affected area up to 3 to 4 times/day

Dosing: Pediatric

Relief of pain and itching: Children â‰¥2 years and Adolescents: Topical cream, gel, or stick: Apply 1% or 2% concentration to affected area up to 3 to 4 times/day

Administration

Topical: Stick: Holding stick straight down, press tip of stick on affected area repeatedly until liquid flows, then dab sparingly.

Warnings/Precautions

Other warnings/precautions:

â€¢ Self-medication (OTC use): Topical products should not be used on large areas of the body, or on chicken pox or measles. Healthcare provider should be contacted if topical use is needed for >7 days. Topical products are not for OTC use in children <2 years of age. Do not use with other products containing diphenhydramine.

Pregnancy Considerations

When administered orally, diphenhydramine crosses the placenta. Diphenhydramine can also be measurable in the serum following topical administration to large areas of the body. Refer to the Diphenhydramine (Systemic) monograph.
