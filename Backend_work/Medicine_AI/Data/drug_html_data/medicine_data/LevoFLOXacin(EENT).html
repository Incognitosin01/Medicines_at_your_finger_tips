Uses for levoFLOXacin (EENT)

Bacterial Ophthalmic Infections

Topical treatment of bacterial conjunctivitis caused by susceptible Staphylococcus aureus, S. epidermidis, Streptococcus pneumoniae, groups C, F, and G streptococci, viridans streptococci, Corynebacterium, Acinetobacter lwoffii, Haemophilus influenzae, or Serratia marcescens.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Many cases of mild, acute bacterial conjunctivitis resolve spontaneously without anti-infective therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> Although topical ophthalmic anti-infectives may shorten time to resolution and reduce severity and risk of complications,<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> avoid indiscriminate use of topical anti-infectives.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Treatment of acute bacterial conjunctivitis generally is empiric;<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> use of a broad-spectrum topical ophthalmic antibacterial usually recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> In vitro staining and/or cultures of conjunctival material may be indicated in diagnosis and management of recurrent, severe, or chronic purulent conjunctivitis or when acute conjunctivitis does not respond to initial empiric topical treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Administration

Apply 0.5% ophthalmic solution topically to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Children ≥6 years of age: Instill 1–2 drops of 0.5% solution in affected eye(s) every 2 hours while awake (up to 8 times daily) on days 1 and 2, then 1–2 drops every 4 hours while awake (up to 4 times daily) on days 3 through 7.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for levoFLOXacin (EENT)

Contraindications

  • Hypersensitivity to levofloxacin, other quinolones, or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Hypersensitivity to levofloxacin, other quinolones, or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Serious and occasionally fatal hypersensitivity and/or anaphylactic reactions reported with systemic fluoroquinolones, including systemic levofloxacin;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> these reactions may occur with first dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> Some hypersensitivity reactions reported with systemic fluoroquinolones have been accompanied by cardiovascular collapse, hypotension or shock, seizures, loss of consciousness, angioedema (e.g., laryngeal, pharyngeal, facial), airway obstruction, dyspnea, urticaria, and itching.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html>

Immediately discontinue levofloxacin ophthalmic solution at first sign of rash or hypersensitivity reaction.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prolonged use may result in overgrowth of nonsusceptible organisms, including fungi.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If superinfection occurs, discontinue levofloxacin ophthalmic solution and institute appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Whenever clinical judgment dictates, examine patient with the aid of magnification (e.g., slit lamp biomicroscopy) and, if appropriate, fluorescein staining.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not wear contact lenses if any signs or symptoms of bacterial conjunctivitis are present.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Data not available regarding use of levofloxacin 0.5% ophthalmic solution in pregnant women;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> use only if potential benefits to the woman justify potential risks to fetus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

levoFLOXacin (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following topical application of levofloxacin 0.5% ophthalmic solution to the eye, the drug is absorbed systemically and low levofloxacin concentrations are attained in plasma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In 15 healthy adults who received topical application of levofloxacin ophthalmic solution for 15 days, mean plasma concentrations of levofloxacin 1 hour after dosing was 0.86 ng/mL on day 1 and 2.05 ng/mL on day 15.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

After topical application of levofloxacin 0.5% ophthalmic solution to the eye for 15 days, maximum mean plasma concentrations of the drug were >1000 times lower than those reported after usually recommended oral dosages of levofloxacin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage
