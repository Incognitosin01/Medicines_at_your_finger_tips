Introduction

Purine nucleoside antimetabolite with immunomodulatory and disease-modifying activity in multiple sclerosis (MS).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Uses for Cladribine (oral)

Multiple Sclerosis (MS)

Used orally for treatment of relapsing forms of MS, including relapsing-remitting disease and active secondary progressive disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Because of substantial risks associated with the drug (e.g., malignancy, teratogenicity), generally reserved for patients with inadequate response to, or unable to tolerate, an alternative MS therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Boxed Warning and also see Cautions.)

Not indicated for use in patients with clinically isolated syndrome.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cladribine is one of several disease-modifying therapies used in the management of relapsing forms of MS.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> Although not curative, these therapies have all been shown to modify several measures of disease activity, including relapse rates, new or enhancing magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) lesions, and disability progression.<html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>77</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>78</sup></body></html>

The American Academy of Neurology (AAN) recommends that disease-modifying therapy be offered to patients with relapsing forms of MS who have had recent relapses and/or MRI activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> Clinicians should consider the adverse effects, tolerability, method of administration, safety, efficacy, and cost of the drugs in addition to patient preferences when selecting an appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>76</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>77</sup></body></html>

Cladribine (oral) Dosage and Administration

General

  • Screen for malignancies according to standard cancer screening guidelines prior to each cladribine treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Malignancies under Cautions.)

  • Screen for HIV, tuberculosis, HBV, and HCV, and evaluate for active infections prior to each treatment course; in patients with active infections, consider delaying therapy until the infection is fully controlled.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Exclude pregnancy in women of childbearing potential prior to each treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Fetal/Neonatal Morbidity and Mortality under Cautions.)

  • Complete any necessary immunizations prior to initiating therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Vaccination against varicella zoster virus (VZV) is recommended in antibody-negative patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Administer live or live attenuated vaccines at least 4–6 weeks prior to initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Perform baseline (within 3 months) MRI of the brain prior to initiating therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Infectious Complications under Cautions.)

  • Perform CBC with differential, including lymphocyte count, prior to each treatment course, at 2 and 6 months after the start of each treatment course, periodically thereafter, and when clinically indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If lymphocyte count <200/mm3 at 2 months, increase monitoring frequency to monthly until 6 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Lymphocytes must be within normal limits prior to initiating the first treatment course and ≥800/mm3 before initiating the second treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Withhold treatment and administer herpes virus prophylaxis in patients with lymphocyte count <200/mm3.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Infectious Complications under Cautions.)

  • Perform liver function tests, including serum aminotransferase, alkaline phosphatase, and total bilirubin concentrations, prior to initiation of each treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer orally once daily without regard to food.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Swallow tablets whole with water; do not chew.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Separate administration of other oral drugs by ≥3 hours during the 4–5 day dosing cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Drugs Affected by Hydroxypropyl Betadex-related Complex Formation under Interactions.)

Observe usual precautions for handling and disposal of cytotoxic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Tablets are not coated and must be swallowed immediately once removed from their packaging.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Handle tablets with dry hands; avoid prolonged contact with the skin and wash hands thoroughly after handling.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Any surface or area that comes in contact with a tablet or a fragment of the tablet must be thoroughly washed with water.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer orally as 2 treatment courses separated by at least 43 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Each treatment course consists of 2 treatment cycles separated by 23–27 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In each treatment cycle, the drug is administered as 1 or 2 tablets once daily over 4–5 consecutive days to obtain the required dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

Dosage

Cumulative dosage of 3.5 mg/kg, administered over 2 yearly treatment courses (1.75 mg/kg per year).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Administer second treatment course at least 43 weeks after the last dose of the first treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

In clinical studies, malignancies reported more frequently with drug than placebo; reported malignancies included metastatic pancreatic carcinoma, malignant melanoma, and ovarian cancer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Boxed Warning.)

Additional cladribine treatment within 2 years after the first 2 treatment courses may increase incidence of malignancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not administer additional cladribine during this time period.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Risk of malignancy in patients who are retreated with cladribine more than 2 years after completion of 2 treatment courses not known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May cause fetal harm.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Boxed Warning.) Embryolethality and teratogenicity observed in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Exclude pregnancy in women of childbearing potential prior to each treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Women and men of reproductive potential must use effective contraception during therapy and for 6 months after the last dose in each treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discontinue therapy if a patient becomes pregnant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications under Cautions.)

Causes a dose-dependent reduction in lymphocyte count.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Lowest absolute lymphocyte counts occur approximately 2–3 months after the start of each treatment course and are further reduced with each additional treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Nadir absolute lymphocyte counts <500/mm3 reported in clinical studies; although less common, lymphocyte counts <200/mm3 also have occurred.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Median time to recovery to ≥800/mm3 was approximately 28 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reduced lymphocyte counts may increase the risk of immunosuppression and infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor patients with lymphocyte counts <500/mm3 for infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If lymphocyte count <200/mm3, withhold therapy and initiate herpes prophylaxis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Infectious Complications under Cautions.)

Obtain CBC with differential, including lymphocyte count, prior to, during, and after each treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See General under Dosage and Administration.)

Lymphocyte counts must be within normal limits before initiating the first treatment course and ≥800/mm3 before initiating the second treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May delay second treatment course for up to 6 months to allow for recovery of lymphocytes to ≥800/mm3; if recovery takes more than 6 months, discontinue cladribine therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Other adverse hematologic effects, including mild to moderate decreases in neutrophil counts, hemoglobin concentrations, and platelet counts, reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Serious cases of thrombocytopenia, neutropenia, and pancytopenia (including documented bone marrow hypoplasia) reported with dosages similar to or higher than recommended for MS; transfusion and treatment with granulocyte-colony stimulating factor (G-CSF) were required.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Obtain CBC with differential, including lymphocyte count, prior to, during, and after treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See General under Dosage and Administration.)

May increase the risk of infections, including life-threatening opportunistic infections and reactivation of latent infections (e.g., herpes virus infections, hepatitis, pyelonephritis, tuberculosis, coccidioidomycosis).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In clinical studies, 2 fatalities occurred from infectious complications (one from tuberculosis and the other from fulminant HBV infection).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Most frequently reported types of herpes viral infections were herpes zoster and oral herpes; higher risk observed when absolute lymphocyte count <500/mm3.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor patients with absolute lymphocyte count <500/mm3 for infections, including herpes virus infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If any signs and symptoms occur, initiate appropriate treatment as clinically indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider delaying or interrupting therapy until resolution of the infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Withhold therapy and administer anti-herpes prophylaxis in patients with lymphocyte count <200/mm3.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Although not reported with oral cladribine, progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy (PML), an opportunistic infection of the brain caused by the JC virus, reported in patients receiving the parenteral formulation of the drug for oncologic indications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Immunocompromised patients are at increased risk of PML.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> At the first sign or symptom suggestive of PML (see Advice to Patients), withhold cladribine therapy and perform an appropriate diagnostic evaluation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> MRI signs of PML may be apparent before clinical manifestations develop.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Obtain baseline MRI within 3 months of initiating first treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Exclude HIV infection, active tuberculosis, and active hepatitis (HBV and HCV) before initiation of each treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider delaying start of therapy in patients with acute infection until the infection is controlled.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Test patients for antibodies to VZV before initiating cladribine and vaccinate patients who are antibody negative.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Administer the vaccine at least 4–6 weeks prior to starting cladribine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not initiate cladribine in patients receiving other immunomodulatory, immunosuppressive, or myelosuppressive agents; increased risk of immunosuppression may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Immunomodulatory, Immunosuppressive, or Myelosuppressive Agents under Interactions.)

Transfusion-associated graft-versus-host disease observed rarely following transfusion of nonirradiated blood in patients treated with cladribine for indications other than MS.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If blood transfusion is required, irradiation of cellular blood components is recommended prior to administration to decrease this risk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consultation with a hematologist is advised.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Serious treatment-related liver injury reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Onset ranged from a few weeks to several months after initiation of cladribine treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In one case, elevated serum aminotransferase concentrations were >20 times the ULN.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> These abnormalities resolved upon treatment discontinuance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Perform liver function tests (serum aminotransferase, alkaline phosphatase, and total bilirubin concentrations) prior to the first and second treatment courses of cladribine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If clinical signs or symptoms suggestive of hepatic dysfunction (e.g., unexplained nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, fatigue, anorexia, jaundice, dark urine) occur, assess serum aminotransferase and total bilirubin concentrations promptly and interrupt or discontinue cladribine as appropriate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a hypersensitivity reaction is suspected, discontinue drug and initiate appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications under Cautions.)

Life-threatening acute cardiac failure with myocarditis reported in one patient with MS in clinical studies; the condition improved after approximately 1 week.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Cases of cardiac failure also reported with parenteral cladribine for indications other than MS.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

May cause fetal harm.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Exclude pregnancy prior to initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discontinue drug if patient becomes pregnant during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Fetal/Neonatal Morbidity and Mortality under Cautions.)

Women of childbearing potential must use effective contraception during cladribine treatment and for 6 months after the last dose in each treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications under Cautions.)

Male patients with partners of childbearing potential should take precautions to prevent pregnancy of their partner by using effective contraception during cladribine treatment and for at least 6 months after the last dose in each treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether cladribine reduces the effectiveness of systemic hormonal contraceptives; women using such hormonal contraceptives should add a barrier method during cladribine treatment and for ≥4 weeks after the last dose in each treatment course.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

Safety and efficacy not established in patients <18 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use not recommended in pediatric patients because of risk of malignancies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Cladribine (oral)

Drugs Affecting or Affected by Hepatic Microsomal Enzymes

Not a substrate of CYP isoenzymes; not expected to inhibit nor induce major CYP isoenzymes to a clinically important extent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetic interactions with drugs affecting or metabolized by CYP isoenzymes not expected.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Drugs Affecting Transporter Systems

Substrate of P-glycoprotein (P-gp), breast cancer resistance protein (BCRP), and equilibrative nucleoside (ENT1) and concentrative nucleoside (CNT3) transport proteins.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Potent ENT1, CNT3, or BCRP inhibitors: Possible alteration of bioavailability, intracellular distribution, and/or renal elimination of cladribine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid concomitant use during the 4–5 day cladribine dosing cycle; consider an alternative drug with minimal or no ENT1, CNT3, or BCRP inhibiting potential.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If this is not possible, reduce dosage of these drugs to minimum effective dosage, separate administration times, and carefully monitor patient.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potent BCRP (e.g., corticosteroids) or P-gp (e.g., rifampin, St. John's wort) inducers: Possible decreased systemic exposure and efficacy of cladribine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Drugs Affected by Hydroxypropyl Betadex-related Complex Formation

Hydroxypropyl betadex, an excipient in the formulation of cladribine tablets, can form complexes with active ingredients of other concomitantly administered drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Bioavailability of other concomitantly administered drugs (particularly drugs with low solubility) may be increased, which may increase risk or severity of adverse reactions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Separate administration of cladribine tablets and other oral drugs by at least 3 hours during the 4–5 day dosing cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Drugs Associated with Hematologic Toxicity

Additive hematologic effects may occur if used concomitantly with other drugs that can affect hematologic profile (e.g., carbamazepine).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> Monitor hematologic parameters.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Immunomodulatory, Immunosuppressive, or Myelosuppressive Agents

Additive immunosuppressive effects may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Concomitant use not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Consider overlapping effects on immune system when used sequentially in patients previously treated with immunomodulatory or immunosuppressive drugs; consider duration and mechanism of the previous drug when initiating cladribine therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Infectious Complications under Cautions.)

Cladribine (oral) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Oral bioavailability is approximately 40%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Peak plasma concentrations occur approximately 0.5 hours (range: 0.5–1.5 hours) following oral administration in the fasted state.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cladribine concentrations increase proportionally over oral dosage range of 3–20 mg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Accumulation not observed with repeated dosing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

High fat meal delayed time to peak concentration by 1 hour and decreased peak concentrations by 29%; no effect on AUC.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Extensively distributed into tissues; intracellular concentrations in lymphocytes are approximately 30–40 times those of extracellular concentrations in vitro.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

May cross blood-brain barrier; in patients with cancer, CSF concentrations are about 25% of plasma concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

20%; independent of concentration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Prodrug that is phosphorylated to its active 2-chlorodeoxyadenosine triphosphate (CdATP) metabolite in lymphocytes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Metabolism in whole blood not fully characterized.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Negligible hepatic enzyme metabolism observed in vitro.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

28.5% excreted unchanged in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Renal clearance exceeds glomerular filtration rate, indicating active renal secretion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Terminal half-life: Approximately 1 day.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Intracellular half-life of phosphorylated metabolites cladribine monophosphate (CdAMP) and CdATP: 15 and 10 hours, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Reduced clearance expected in patients with renal impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
