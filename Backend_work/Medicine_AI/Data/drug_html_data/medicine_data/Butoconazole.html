Uses for Butoconazole

Vulvovaginal Candidiasis

Treatment of uncomplicated vulvovaginal candidiasis (mild to moderate, sporadic or infrequent, most likely caused by Candida albicans, occurring in immunocompetent women).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>30</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>51</sup></body></html> A drug of choice.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>34</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>35</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>36</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>37</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>39</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>40</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>41</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>51</sup></body></html>

Self-medication (OTC use) for treatment of uncomplicated vulvovaginal candidiasis in otherwise healthy, nonpregnant women who have been previously diagnosed by a clinician and are having recurrence of similar symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

Treatment of complicated vulvovaginal candidiasis, including infections that are recurrent (≥4 episodes in 1 year), severe (extensive vulvar erythema, edema, excoriation, fissure formation), caused by Candida other than C. albicans, or occurring in women with underlying medical conditions (uncontrolled diabetes mellitus, HIV infection, immunosuppressive therapy, pregnancy).<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>35</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>36</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>37</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>41</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>46</sup></body></html> Complicated infections generally require more prolonged treatment than uncomplicated infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>49</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>50</sup></body></html>

Optimal regimens for treatment of vulvovaginal candidiasis caused by Candida other than C. albicans (e.g., C. glabrata, C. krusei) not identified.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html> CDC and others state these infections may respond to an intravaginal azole antifungal given for 7–14 days or to a 14-day regimen of intravaginal boric acid (not commercially available in the US).<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>51</sup></body></html>

Butoconazole Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer intravaginally as a cream using the prefilled applicator provided by the manufacturer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

Vaginal cream is for intravaginal administration only and should not be administered orally.<html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html> Contact with the eyes should be avoided.<html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Mycelex-3: Children ≥12 years of age: One applicatorful of 2% cream (approximately 100 mg of the drug) once daily at bedtime for 3 consecutive days.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html> May be used for self-medication.<html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

Gynazole-1: One applicatorful of 2% cream (approximately 100 mg of the drug) as a single dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html>

Mycelex-3: One applicatorful of 2% cream (approximately 100 mg of the drug) once daily at bedtime for 3 consecutive days.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html> May be used for self-medication.<html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

If clinical symptoms persist, tests should be repeated to rule out other pathogens, to confirm the original diagnosis, and to rule out other conditions that may predispose a patient to recurrent vaginal fungal infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use same intravaginal regimen recommended for women without HIV infection; however<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>41</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>46</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>49</sup></body></html> some experts recommend a duration of 3–7 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>49</sup></body></html> Maintenance regimen of an intravaginal azole can be considered for those with recurrent episodes;<html><head></head><body><sup>49</sup></body></html> routine primary or secondary prophylaxis (long-term suppressive or chronic maintenance therapy) not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>49</sup></body></html>

CDC and others recommend an initial intensive regimen (7–14 days of an intravaginal azole or 3-dose regimen of oral fluconazole) to achieve mycologic remission, followed by an appropriate maintenance regimen (6-month regimen of once-weekly oral fluconazole or, alternatively, an intravaginal azole given intermittently).<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>35</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>36</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>37</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>41</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>46</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>48</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>50</sup></body></html>

CDC and others recommend 7–14 days of an intravaginal azole for vulvovaginal candidasis that is severe, caused by Candida other than C. albicans, or occurring in women with underlying medical conditions.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>50</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Butoconazole

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to butoconazole or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to butoconazole or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Butoconazole vaginal cream contains mineral oil that can weaken latex or rubber products (including condoms and vaginal contraceptive diaphragms).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html> Use of such products within 72 hours following intravaginal butoconazole treatment not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to initial use of butoconazole in a woman with signs and symptoms of vulvovaginal candidiasis, confirm the diagnosis by demonstrating yeast or pseudohyphae with direct microscopic examination of vaginal discharge (saline or 10% potassium hydroxide [KOH] wet mount or Gram stain) or by culture.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

Candida identified by culture in the absence of symptoms is not an indication for antifungal treatment since approximately 10–20% of women harbor Candida or other yeasts in the vagina.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html>

If clinical symptoms persist after treatment or recur within 2 months, tests should be repeated to rule out other pathogens, to confirm the original diagnosis, and to rule out other conditions that may predispose a patient to recurrent vaginal fungal infections (e.g., pregnancy, HIV infection).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

Do not use for self-medication in women who have never had a vaginal yeast infection diagnosed by a clinician, in women who are or think they may be pregnant, or in women with diabetes, HIV infection, or HIV exposure.<html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

CDC states that a 7-day regimen of an intravaginal azole antifungal can be used, if necessary, for treatment of vulvovaginal candidiasis in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html>

Not known whether intravaginal butoconazole is distributed into milk; use with caution in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Gynazole-1: Safety and efficacy not established in children.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Mycelex-3: Safety and efficacy not established in children <12 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Vulvar/vaginal burning, itching, soreness and swelling, pelvic or abdominal pain or cramping.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Butoconazole Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following intravaginal administration, only small amounts (1.3–2.2% of a dose) are absorbed systemically;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> peak plasma concentrations usually attained within 12–24 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Crosses placenta in animals following intravaginal administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not known whether drug is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolic fate following intravaginal administration not fully characterized, but systemically absorbed drug appears to be extensively metabolized probably in the liver.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

The systemically absorbed fraction of an intravaginal dose appears to be excreted in urine and feces.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

25°C (may be exposed to 15–30°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid exposure to temperatures >30°<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html> and freezing.<html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of reading and understanding manufacturer’s patient instructions regarding use of applicator for intravaginal administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

  • Not for self-medication in women who have never had a vaginal yeast infection diagnosed by a clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of discontinuing self-medication of vulvovaginal candidiasis and consulting clinician if fever, abdominal pain, or foul-smelling vaginal discharge develops; if symptoms do not improve within 3 days; if condition persists after therapy; or if symptoms recur within 2 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not using latex or rubber products such as condoms or vaginal contraceptive diaphragms within 72 hours following butoconazole treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • If used during menstruation, importance of using sanitary napkins instead of vaginal tampons.<html><head></head><body><sup>47</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, and concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
