Introduction

Mydriatic and cycloplegic; tertiary amine antimuscarinic.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Uses for Homatropine

Ophthalmologic Examination

Used to produce mydriasis and cycloplegia for refraction.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> However, because homatropine has prolonged mydriatic and relatively weak cycloplegic effects, other antimuscarinics may be preferred.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Ophthalmic Inflammation

Management of acute inflammatory conditions (e.g., iridocyclitis) of the uveal tract.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Perioperative Mydriasis

Used for pre- and postoperative states when mydriasis is required.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Axial Lens Opacities

Used as an optical aid in some cases of axial lens opacities.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Homatropine Dosage and Administration

General

  • Prior to initiation of therapy, estimate depth of the angle of the anterior chamber to avoid induction of angle-closure glaucoma in susceptible patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Prior to initiation of therapy, estimate depth of the angle of the anterior chamber to avoid induction of angle-closure glaucoma in susceptible patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Administration

Apply topically to eye(s) as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

For topical ophthalmic use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Not for injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following topical application, apply finger pressure on lacrimal sac for 2–3 minutes to avoid excessive systemic absorption.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as homatropine hydrobromide; dosage expressed in terms of the salt.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

In patients with heavily pigmented irides, higher dosages may be required.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Use only the 2% solution in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

1 drop of a 2% solution in the eye(s) immediately before the procedure; may repeat at 10-minute intervals as necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

1 drop of a 2% solution in the eye(s) 2 or 3 times daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html>

1 or 2 drops of a 2 or 5% solution in the eye(s) immediately before the procedure; may repeat in 5–10 minutes if necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

1 or 2 drops of a 2 or 5% solution in the eye(s) 2 or 3 times daily or, if necessary, up to every 3–4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations at this time.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Homatropine

Contraindications

  • Known or suspected angle-closure (narrow-angle) glaucoma or a predisposition to angle-closure glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Known hypersensitivity to homatropine or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Known or suspected angle-closure (narrow-angle) glaucoma or a predisposition to angle-closure glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to homatropine or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Consider risks and benefits in patients with keratoconus (homatropine may produce fixed dilated pupil), Down’s syndrome, and children with brain damage.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Discontinue if signs or symptoms of sensitivity occur during treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Excessive use, especially in children, geriatric patients, or other susceptible individuals, may produce adverse systemic antimuscarinic effects, including delirium, agitation, and coma.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Parenteral physostigmine is the recommended antidote for systemic anticholinergic symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Use with extreme caution in infants and young children because of increased susceptibility to the systemic effects of the drug in these patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Consider risks and benefits of use in children with brain damage.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

No substantial differences in safety or efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Use cautiously due to increased susceptibility to the systemic effects of the drug in geriatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Homatropine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following topical application to the eye, mydriatic agents are absorbed systemically, principally through the conjunctiva and nasal mucosa.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Following topical application to the eye, maximum mydriasis occurs in about 10–30 minutes, and maximum cycloplegia occurs in about 30–90 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Mydriasis may last 6 hours to 4 days, and cycloplegia persists about 10–48 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Distribution in ocular tissues not fully characterized.<html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html>

Not known whether homatropine is distributed into milk after topical application to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Tight, light-resistant containers at 8–24°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • After topical application to the eye, blocks responses of the sphincter muscle of the iris and the accommodative ciliary muscle of the ciliary body to cholinergic stimulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Produces pupillary dilation (mydriasis) and paralysis of accommodation (cycloplegia).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

  • Cycloplegic activity is less than that of atropine.<html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

  • Mydriatic and cycloplegic actions are of shorter duration than those of atropine or scopolamine and of longer duration than those of cyclopentolate or tropicamide.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>c</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>d</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Advise patients not to drive or engage in hazardous activities while pupils are dilated.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that they may experience sensitivity to light and should protect eyes in bright illumination during dilation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Advise parents to prevent the child from getting the solution into his or her mouth and to wash their own hands and the child’s hands following administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not touching dropper tip to any surface and of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contamination of the product.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of applying finger pressure on the lacrimal sac of the eye for 2–3 minutes following topical administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to discontinue drug and contact prescriber if signs or symptoms of sensitivity occur or if irritation persists or increases.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
