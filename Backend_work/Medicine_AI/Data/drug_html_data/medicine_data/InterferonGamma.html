Introduction

Uses for Interferon Gamma

Chronic Granulomatous Disease

Reduction of the frequency and severity of serious infections in patients with chronic granulomatous disease (designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>22</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html>

Osteopetrosis

Treatment to delay the time to disease progression in patients with severe, malignant osteopetrosis (designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis

Interferon gamma-1b was investigated in patients with idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis† (IPF) with mild-to-moderate lung function impairment in the INSPIRE study; the study was terminated early when interim data analysis indicated lack of benefit in patients receiving the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>26</sup></body></html> Interim analysis also indicated that 14.5% of patients receiving interferon gamma 1-b died compared with 12.7% of those receiving placebo.<html><head></head><body><sup>26</sup></body></html>

Interferon gamma-1b is not approved for use in patients with IPF.<html><head></head><body><sup>26</sup></body></html> FDA suggests that health-care professionals should discuss the results of this trial with their patients receiving the drug for IPF and carefully consider whether they should continue to receive treatment with interferon gamma-1b.<html><head></head><body><sup>26</sup></body></html>

Interferon Gamma Dosage and Administration

General

  • If home use is prescribed, carefully instruct patients and/or their caregivers in appropriate use; provide a puncture-resistant container for proper, safe disposal of used syringes and needles.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer by sub-Q injection 3 times weekly (e.g., Monday, Wednesday, Friday).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Optimum sites for sub-Q injection include right and left deltoid and anterior thigh.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

To minimize risk of flu-like syndrome, administer at bedtime and/or give acetaminophen to prevent or partially alleviate fever and headache.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Vials contain no preservative; discard any residual solution remaining in the vial after administration of the single dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Interferon Gamma

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to interferon gamma-1b, products derived from Escherichia coli, or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to interferon gamma-1b, products derived from Escherichia coli, or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Acute and transient flu-like syndrome or constitutional symptoms (e.g., chills, fever)<html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> that are associated with daily dosages ≥250 mcg/m2 (>10 times the weekly recommended dosage) may exacerbate preexisting cardiac conditions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with preexisting cardiac disease (e.g., arrhythmia, CHF, symptoms of ischemia).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible seizures, decreased mental status, dizziness, and gait disturbance, particularly at daily dosages >250 mcg/m2 (>10 times the weekly recommended dosage).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with known seizure disorders or compromised CNS function.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possibly severe, reversible, dose-limiting neutropenia and thrombocytopenia reported rarely.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with myelosuppression and in those receiving drugs that may be myelosuppressive.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor blood cell and differential counts and platelet counts prior to initiating interferon gamma-1b and at 3-month intervals during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Proteinuria reported rarely.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Perform urinalysis and monitor appropriate blood chemistry tests prior to initiating interferon gamma-1b and at 3-month intervals during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interferon Gamma Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following sub-Q injection, >89% of dose is slowly absorbed; peak plasma concentrations attained 7 hours after dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

5.9 hours following a single sub-Q dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

2–8°C; do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Unentered vials may be exposed to room temperature for up to 12 hours prior to use; discard vials not returned to refrigerator or used within 12 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid shaking or excessively or vigorously agitating vials.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of advising patients not to administer the drug until their clinician has thoroughly trained them on proper administration methods (including aseptic technique) and proper disposal of used needles and syringes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>27</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to notify their clinician if injection site reactions (e.g., persistent lumps, swelling, bruising, signs of infection or inflammation [pus, redness, pain]) occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>27</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of myelosuppression and adverse hepatic effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of taking interferon gamma-1b as prescribed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing their clinician if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
