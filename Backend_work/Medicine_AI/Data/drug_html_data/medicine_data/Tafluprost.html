Introduction

Ocular hypotensive agent; fluorinated analog of prostaglandin F2α (PGF2α).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Uses for Tafluprost

Ocular Hypertension and Glaucoma

Reduction of elevated IOP in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Preservative free; may be useful in patients who are allergic or have adverse events related to preservative-containing ocular hypotensives, have sensitive or dry eyes, or do not adequately respond to or cannot tolerate other therapies (e.g., topical prostaglandin analogs).<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

As effective as timolol 0.5% in reducing IOP in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Not as effective as latanoprost 0.005%<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> and may be less effective than travoprost 0.004%.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Addition of tafluprost 0.0015% to timolol 0.5% therapy may result in additional reduction in IOP.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

When selecting an initial ocular hypotensive agent, consider extent of the required IOP reduction, coexisting medical conditions, and drug characteristics (e.g., dosing frequency, adverse effects, cost).<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> With single-agent regimens, the reduction in IOP is approximately 25–33% with topical prostaglandin analogs; 20–25% with topical β-adrenergic blocking agents, α-adrenergic agonists, or miotic (parasympathomimetic) agents; 20–30% with oral carbonic anhydrase inhibitors; 18% with topical rho kinase inhibitors; and 15–20% with topical carbonic anhydrase inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html>

A prostaglandin analog frequently is considered for initial therapy in the absence of other considerations (e.g., contraindications, cost considerations, intolerance, adverse effects, patient refusal) because of relatively greater activity, once-daily administration, and low frequency of systemic adverse effects; however, ocular adverse effects can occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>134</sup></body></html>

Goal is to maintain an IOP at which visual field loss is unlikely to substantially reduce quality of life during the patient's lifetime.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html>

Reduction of pretreatment IOP by ≥25% shown to slow progression of primary open-angle glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> Set an initial target IOP (based on extent of optic nerve damage and/or visual field loss, baseline IOP at which damage occurred, rate of progression, life expectancy, and other considerations) and reduce IOP toward this goal.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> Adjust target IOP up or down as needed over course of disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html>

Combination therapy with drugs from different therapeutic classes often required to control IOP.<html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>133</sup></body></html>

Tafluprost Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the affected eye(s).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If more than one topical ophthalmic drug is used, administer the drugs at least 5 minutes apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use solution from one single-use container immediately after opening; immediately discard unused portion after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Tafluprost 0.0015% ophthalmic solution: One drop in the conjunctival sac of the affected eye(s) once daily in the evening.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

More frequent dosing may diminish the IOP-lowering effect of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If target IOP not achieved, may initiate additional or alternative ocular hypotensive agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>133</sup></body></html> (See Ocular Hypertension and Glaucoma under Uses.)

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations at this time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Tafluprost

Contraindications

  • Manufacturer states none known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states none known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Increased pigmentation of the iris and periorbital tissue (eyelid) reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Pigmentation expected to increase as long as tafluprost is administered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Following discontinuance of therapy, pigmentation of the iris is likely to be permanent, while pigmentation of periorbital tissue reportedly is reversible in some patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Long-term effects of increased pigmentation unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increased pigmentation of the iris may not be evident until after several months to years of tafluprost therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May continue therapy in patients who develop noticeably increased iris pigmentation; however, examine these patients regularly.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible gradual change in eyelashes and vellus hair in the treated eye, including increased length, color, thickness, shape, and number of eyelashes and/or misdirected growth of eyelashes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Usually reversible upon discontinuance of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with active intraocular inflammation (e.g., iritis/uveitis); may exacerbate inflammation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Macular edema, including cystoid macular edema, reported with prostaglandin F2α analogs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use with caution in aphakic patients, in pseudophakic patients with a torn posterior lens capsule, or in patients with known risk factors for macular edema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use only if potential benefits justify possible risk to the fetus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Women of childbearing potential should use effective contraceptive methods.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tafluprost and/or its metabolites distribute into milk in animals; not known whether the drug or its metabolites distribute into milk in humans.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Caution if used in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use in pediatric patients not recommended because of potential safety concerns related to increased pigmentation following long-term use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Pigmentation under Cautions.)

No overall differences in safety or efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Tafluprost

No formal drug interaction studies to date.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> However, no interactions expected because of limited systemic exposure.<html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Tafluprost Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Prodrug; absorbed through the cornea following ocular instillation and hydrolyzed to active form (tafluprost acid).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tafluprost acid: Peak plasma concentrations occur at a median of 10 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reduction in IOP generally occurs approximately 2–4 hours after ocular instillation and peaks after 12 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Distributed into milk in animals; not known whether the drug or its metabolites distribute into milk in humans.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Crosses the placenta in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Hydrolyzed by esterases in the cornea to biologically active form (tafluprost acid).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Systemically, tafluprost acid is further metabolized via fatty acid oxidation and phase II conjugation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tafluprost acid: Rapidly eliminated from plasma; plasma concentrations are below the limit of quantitation within 30 minutes following ocular instillation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

May maintain at temperatures up to 40°C for ≤2 days during shipping; discard mail-order prescriptions of the drug if not received within 2 days of the dispensing date.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Store cartons and unopened foil pouches at 2–8°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> After pouch is opened, may store single-use containers in opened pouch for up to 30 days at 20–25°C; discard any unused containers 30 days after first opening pouch.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Protect from moisture.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Selective prostanoid FP receptor agonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Mechanism of action not fully elucidated; appears to reduce IOP by increasing uveoscleral outflow.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Potent prostanoid FP receptor agonist;<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> negligible affinity for other prostanoid receptors (e.g., DP, EP2, IP, TP).<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Selective prostanoid FP receptor agonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Mechanism of action not fully elucidated; appears to reduce IOP by increasing uveoscleral outflow.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potent prostanoid FP receptor agonist;<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> negligible affinity for other prostanoid receptors (e.g., DP, EP2, IP, TP).<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of not exceeding once-daily dosing; more frequent administration may decrease IOP-lowering effect of tafluprost.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of administering tafluprost ophthalmic solution immediately after opening single-use container and discarding any unused portion immediately after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of permanent increase in brown pigmentation of the iris; risk of darkening of the skin around the eyes (eyelid), which may be reversible after discontinuance of tafluprost.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of changes in eyelashes and vellus hair in the treated eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Potential for disparity between eyes in length, thickness, pigmentation, or number of eyelashes or vellus hairs and/or direction of eyelash growth.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Eyelash changes usually are reversible after discontinuance of tafluprost.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to immediately contact their clinician for advice regarding continued use of tafluprost ophthalmic solution if they develop a new ocular condition (e.g., trauma, infection), experience a sudden decrease in visual acuity, have ocular surgery, or experience ocular reactions (particularly conjunctivitis and eyelid reactions).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • If using more than one topical ophthalmic preparation, importance of administering the preparations at least 5 minutes apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of following instruction on proper storage of cartons, unopened foil pouches, and opened foil pouches.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Storage under Stability.) Importance of recording the date the foil pouch was opened in the space provided on the pouch and of discarding unused containers after 30 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Women of childbearing potential should use effective contraceptive methods during tafluprost therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
