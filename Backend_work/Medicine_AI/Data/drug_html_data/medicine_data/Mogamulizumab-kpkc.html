Introduction

Antineoplastic agent; recombinant humanized anti-CC chemokine receptor 4 (anti-CCR4) monoclonal antibody.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Uses for Mogamulizumab-kpkc

Cutaneous T-cell Lymphoma (CTCL)

Treatment of relapsed or refractory mycosis fungoides or Sézary syndrome that has progressed following at least 1 prior systemic therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Efficacy based on substantially prolonged progression-free survival and improved overall response rates compared with vorinostat.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Designated an orphan drug by FDA for treatment of cutaneous T-cell lymphoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Mogamulizumab-kpkc Dosage and Administration

General

  • Because of the risk of infusion-related reactions, a premedication regimen (e.g., acetaminophen and diphenhydramine) is recommended prior to the initial mogamulizumab infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If an infusion-related reaction occurs, administer premedication prior to subsequent infusions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administration

For solution compatibility information, see Compatibility under Stability.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer by IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not administer by sub-Q injection or rapid IV administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dilute mogamulizumab-kpkc injection to an appropriate concentration (see Dilution under Dosage and Administration) prior to IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Storage under Stability.)

Administer using a sterile, low-protein-binding 0.22-µm (or equivalent) inline filter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not mix with or administer simultaneously through the same IV line with other drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Withdraw appropriate dose of mogamulizumab-kpkc injection containing 4 mg/mL from appropriate number of vials and inject into a PVC or polyolefin infusion bag containing an appropriate volume of 0.9% sodium chloride injection to achieve a final concentration of 0.1–3 mg/mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Mix the diluted solution by gentle inversion; do not shake.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Immediate administration recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If necessary, may refrigerate diluted solution for ≤4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Storage under Stability.)

Discard any partially used vials or diluted solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer by IV infusion over ≥60 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Cycle 1: 1 mg/kg administered on days 1, 8, 15, and 22 of a 28-day cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Subsequent cycles: 1 mg/kg on days 1 and 15 of each 28-day cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Continue therapy until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May administer within 2 days of scheduled dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> If a dose is missed, administer as soon as possible and then resume dosing schedule.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If grade 2 or 3 rash occurs, interrupt therapy until the toxicity improves to grade 1 or less.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May resume following completion of ≥2 weeks of topical corticosteroid therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dermatologic Effects under Cautions.)

If Stevens-Johnson syndrome or toxic epidermal necrolysis is suspected, interrupt therapy until such diagnosis has been excluded and the toxicity improves to grade 1 or less.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If grade 4 rash occurs or Stevens-Johnson syndrome or toxic epidermal necrolysis is confirmed, permanently discontinue drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If grade 1–3 infusion-related reactions occur, interrupt infusion and provide appropriate treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Upon resolution of the reaction, reduce the infusion rate by at least 50%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If reaction recurs and is unmanageable, discontinue drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Infusion-related Reactions under Cautions.)

If grade 4 infusion-related reactions occur, permanently discontinue drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If an immune-mediated adverse effect is suspected, interrupt or permanently discontinue drug as appropriate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Immune-mediated Events under Cautions.)

Warnings/Precautions

Rash (including drug eruption), sometimes fatal or life-threatening (i.e., Stevens-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis), reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Drug eruption most commonly presented as papular or maculopapular rash, lichenoid, spongiotic dermatitis, granulomatous dermatitis, or morbilliform rash; may also present as scaly plaques, pustular eruption, folliculitis, nonspecific dermatitis, or psoriasiform dermatitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Median time to onset of drug eruption is 15 weeks; also may occur after 31 weeks of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor for skin reactions during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Depending on nature and severity of rash, temporarily withhold or discontinue mogamulizumab therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Therapy Interruption for Toxicity under Dosage and Administration.) If grade 1 rash occurs, consider topical corticosteroid therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If grade 2 or 3 rash occurs, initiate topical corticosteroid therapy and continue for ≥2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consider skin biopsy to distinguish drug eruption from disease progression.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Risk of infusion-related reactions (e.g., chills, nausea, fever, tachycardia, rigors, headache, vomiting), sometimes fatal or life-threatening.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Generally occur more frequently during or shortly after first infusion than during subsequent infusions of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Premedication (e.g., diphenhydramine and acetaminophen) recommended prior to first infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If an infusion-related reaction occurs, administer premedication prior to subsequent infusions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients closely for manifestations of infusion-related reactions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interrupt the infusion if an infusion-related reaction of any severity occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Therapy Interruption for Toxicity under Dosage and Administration.) Provide appropriate treatment and supportive care.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Fatal and life-threatening infections (e.g., sepsis, pneumonia, cellulitis) reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Reactivation of HBV infection reported during postmarketing experience.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Monitor for signs and symptoms of infection and institute prompt treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Immune-mediated adverse events (e.g., myositis, myocarditis, polymyositis, hepatitis, pneumonitis, variant of Guillain Barré syndrome, polymyalgia rheumatica, hypothyroidism), sometimes fatal or life-threatening, reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

If an immune-mediated adverse event is suspected, interrupt or permanently discontinue drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Consider potential benefits versus risks of the drug in patients with a history of autoimmune disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increased risk of transplantation-related complications (i.e., grade 3 or 4 acute graft-versus-host disease [GVHD], steroid-refractory GVHD, death) in patients undergoing allogeneic stem cell transplantation following therapy with mogamulizumab.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Risk of stem cell transplantation-related complications, particularly severe acute GVHD, increased in patients receiving the drug within a short time-frame (approximately 50 days) before allogeneic stem cell transplantation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Some clinicians recommend delaying allogeneic stem cell transplantation until ≥50 days after the last dose of mogamulizumab.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Closely monitor for early manifestations of stem cell transplantation-related complications.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Some clinicians recommend optimization of acute GVHD prophylaxis with antithymocyte globulin or post-transplantation cyclophosphamide.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Potential for immunogenicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Development of anti-mogamulizumab antibodies reported; neutralizing antibodies not detected.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Effects on pharmacokinetics, efficacy, or safety of the drug not observed.<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

No data in pregnant women; no embryofetal toxic or teratogenic effects demonstrated in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

IgG antibodies are known to cross the placenta during pregnancy; in reproduction study in monkeys, mogamulizumab was detected in fetal plasma.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Confirm pregnancy status prior to initiating mogamulizumab therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Not recommended during pregnancy or in women of reproductive potential who are not using contraception.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advise women of reproductive potential to use effective methods of contraception while receiving the drug and for ≥3 months after the last dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether mogamulizumab is distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Potential effects on nursing infants or on milk production not known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consider benefits of breast-feeding along with mother's clinical need for mogamulizumab and any potential adverse effects of the drug or disease on the infant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No overall differences in efficacy observed between geriatric patients (≥65 years of age) compared with younger adults, but some serious or grade 3 or greater adverse effects occurred more frequently in geriatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics, efficacy, and safety not affected by mild hepatic impairment (total bilirubin concentration not exceeding the ULN with AST concentration less than the ULN or total bilirubin concentration exceeding the ULN, but ≤1.5 times the ULN, with any AST concentration) or moderate hepatic impairment (total bilirubin concentration >1.5 times, but ≤3 times ULN, with any AST concentration).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics not studied in patients with severe hepatic impairment (total bilirubin concentration >3 times the ULN with any AST concentration).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics, efficacy, and safety not affected by renal impairment (Clcr <90 mL/minute).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Mogamulizumab-kpkc Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Dose-proportional pharmacokinetics over mogamulizumab-kpkc dose range of 0.01–1 mg/kg.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Steady-state concentrations achieved after 12 weeks (i.e., 8 doses);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> systemic accumulation is 1.6-fold.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics not affected by mild hepatic impairment (total bilirubin concentration not exceeding the ULN with AST concentration less than the ULN or total bilirubin concentration exceeding the ULN, but ≤1.5 times the ULN, with any AST concentration) or moderate hepatic impairment (total bilirubin concentration >1.5 times, but ≤3 times ULN, with any AST concentration).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics not affected by renal impairment (Clcr <90 mL/minute).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Age (range: 22–101 years), sex, ethnicity, CTCL subtype (mycosis fungoides or Sézary syndrome), degree of CCR4 expression, and Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group (ECOG) performance status do not affect pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
