Introduction

Uses for Denileukin

Cutaneous T-cell Lymphoma

Treatment of persistent or recurrent cutaneous T-cell lymphoma (CTCL) in patients whose malignant cells express the CD25 component of the IL-2 receptor<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> (designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use).<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy in patients with CTCL whose malignant cells do not express the CD25 component of the IL-2 receptor not established but currently being studied.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Administration

For solution and drug compatibility information, see Compatibility under Stability.

Administer by IV infusion; not for rapid (i.e., bolus) IV injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not use in-line filter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to administration, thaw denileukin diftitox concentrate at room temperature for 1–2 hours or under refrigeration (2–8°C) for up to 24 hours; do not heat solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Must then be diluted with preservative-free 0.9% sodium chloride injection prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not admix with any other drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use strict aseptic technique and only plastic syringes and plastic IV bags to prepare and/or administer the drug; do not use glass containers.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Compatibility under Stability.)

Add appropriate volume of denileukin diftitox concentrate to an empty IV infusion bag and dilute each mL of the concentrate with no more than 9 mL of the 0.9% sodium chloride injection to provide a solution containing at least 15 mcg/mL, a concentration that must be maintained during all steps of solution preparation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Mix by gentle swirling; do not shake vigorously.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer by IV infusion over at least 15 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If infusion-related adverse effects occur, discontinue infusion or administer the drug over longer periods (e.g., up to 80 minutes), depending on the severity of symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Dosage

9 or 18 mcg/kg daily for 5 consecutive days; repeat every 21 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Optimum duration of therapy not established; only 2% of patients who do not experience at least a 25% decrease in tumor burden prior to the fourth course of treatment subsequently respond.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No special population recommendations at this time.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Denileukin

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to denileukin diftitox, diphtheria toxin, aldesleukin (interleukin-2), or any other ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to denileukin diftitox, diphtheria toxin, aldesleukin (interleukin-2), or any other ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Administer only under supervision of qualified clinicians experienced in use of cytotoxic therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Closely monitor patients in a setting equipped and staffed by health-care personnel appropriately trained in CPR.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Risk of severe and/or fatal capillary leak syndrome (e.g., hypotension, edema, hypoalbuminemia).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Syndrome usually occurs within the first 2 weeks of the infusion and may persist or worsen after completion of the treatment cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with preexisting cardiovascular disease and/or low serum albumin concentrations, since they appear to be at increased risk of developing this syndrome.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Monitor weight, edema, BP, and serum albumin concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Usually self-limiting; initiate treatment only if clinically indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Loss of visual acuity, usually with loss of color vision, reported; occurred with or without retinal pigment mottling.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Although recovery was reported in some affected patients, most patients reported persistent visual impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Potentially fatal, acute hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., hypotension, dyspnea, anaphylaxis) reported in about 69% of patients during or within 24 hours of infusion; about 50% of cases occurred on the first day of dosing, regardless of the treatment cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

If hypersensitivity reaction occurs, discontinue immediately and/or institute appropriate therapy as indicated (e.g., IV antihistamine, epinephrine, corticosteroids).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Have these drugs and resuscitative equipment immediately available during denileukin diftitox therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Perform CBCs, blood chemistries, and renal and hepatic function tests prior to initiation of therapy and at weekly intervals during therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Possible development of antibodies to denileukin diftitox; may inhibit functional activity of denileukin diftitox.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Systemic exposure to denileukin diftitox also may be decreased.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> (See Special Populations under Pharmacokinetics.)

Manufacturer states that presence or absence of antibodies does not correlate with the risk of immediate hypersensitivity reactions associated with IV infusion of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May impair immune function; monitor carefully for development of possibly severe infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Patients with CTCL may be predisposed to cutaneous infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible moderate to severe hypoalbuminemia, usually occurring 1 or 2 weeks after administration of denileukin diftitox.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor serum albumin concentrations prior to initiating each course of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Delay administration of the drug until serum albumin concentrations are at least 3 g/dL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Flu-like syndrome (e.g., fever and/or chills, asthenia, GI effects, myalgia, arthralgia) frequently reported within several hours to days after denileukin diftitox infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Mild to moderate symptoms generally responsive to antipyretics, antiemetics, or antidiarrhea agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Denileukin Pharmacokinetics

Distribution

Distributed into liver and kidneys in rats.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized by proteolytic degradation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Biphasic; terminal half-life is approximately 70–80 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In patients with denileukin diftitox antibodies, clearance may be increased twofold to threefold.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

  • Recombinant DNA-derived cytotoxic protein containing domains of diphtheria toxin fragments A and B (Met1 to Thr387)-His and of interleukin-2 (IL-2; Ala1-Thr133);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> prepared from cultures of genetically modified Escherichia coli and purified using reverse phase chromatography followed by a multistep diafiltration process.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>
