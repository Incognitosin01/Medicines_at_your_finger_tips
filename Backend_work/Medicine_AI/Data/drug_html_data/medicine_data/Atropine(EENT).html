Uses for Atropine (EENT)

Ophthalmologic Examination

Used to produce mydriasis and cycloplegia for refraction (e.g., retina and optic disc examination, measurement of refractive error).<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> However, because of long duration of action relative to that of other anticholinergic agents, atropine is seldom, if ever, used for cycloplegic refraction in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html>

In rare cases, ophthalmic atropine use may be necessary to achieve maximal cycloplegia in pediatric patients, but cyclopentolate is more frequently used.<html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html>

Ophthalmic Inflammation

Management of acute inflammatory conditions (e.g., iridocyclitis) of the iris and uveal tract (e.g., uveitis).<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Amblyopia

Used for cycloplegic effects in the treatment of suppression amblyopia (lazy eye) to reduce the visual acuity of the unaffected eye below that of the amblyopic one and force fixation with the amblyopic eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html>

Excess Accommodation and Convergence

Used to treat patients with a functional excess of accommodation and convergence.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Excessive use in children and patients with a history of susceptibility to belladonna alkaloids may produce systemic manifestations of atropine poisoning (e.g., flushing, skin dryness, rash in children, rapid and irregular pulse, fever, abdominal distention in infants, mental aberration, loss of neuromuscular coordination).<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> If systemic symptoms occur, discontinue the drug and initiate appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Not known whether atropine is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> Caution when used in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy of atropine sulfate 1% ophthalmic solution have not been established in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

Use with extreme caution, if at all, in infants and small children, and in children with spastic paralysis or brain damage because of increased susceptibility to the systemic effects of the drug in these patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Coma and death have been reported in very young patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

No substantial differences in safety or efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

Atropine (EENT) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following topical application to the eye, readily absorbed transconjunctivally.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following topical application to the eye, maximum mydriasis occurs in about 30–40 minutes, and maximum cycloplegia occurs after several hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Mydriasis generally lasts about 7–12 days, and cycloplegia persists for up to 14 days or longer.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Onset of effects may be slower and duration more prolonged in individuals with heavily pigmented irides.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

Distribution

Not known whether atropine is distributed into milk after topical application to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Tightly closed containers at 15–30°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Protect from heat; do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Tight containers at 8–27°C,<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> or as specified by manufacturer. Do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • After topical application to the eye, blocks responses of the sphincter muscle of the iris and the accommodative ciliary muscle of the ciliary body to cholinergic stimulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Produces pupillary dilation (mydriasis) and paralysis of accommodation (cycloplegia).<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Slower onset of mydriatic and cycloplegic action and more prolonged ocular effects than most other anticholinergic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Advise patients not to drive or engage in hazardous activities while pupils are dilated.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that they may experience sensitivity to light and should protect eyes in bright illumination during dilation.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Advise parents to prevent the child from getting the solution or ointment into his or her mouth and to wash their own hands and the child’s hands following administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contamination of the product.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
