Introduction

Vasoconstrictor; synthetic form of endogenous angiotensin II, a peptide hormone of the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Uses for Angiotensin II

Shock

Used to increase BP in patients with septic or other distributive shock.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Substantially and rapidly increases mean arterial pressure (MAP) in patients who remain hypotensive despite fluid and vasopressor therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

May have a catecholamine-sparing effect, but effects on mortality not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Administration

For solution and drug compatibility information, see Compatibility under Stability.

Administer by continuous IV infusion; manufacturer recommends administration via central venous line.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Must dilute commercially available injection concentrate prior to infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dilute with 0.9% sodium chloride injection to final concentration of 5000 or 10,000 ng/mL depending on patient's fluid status.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For patients who are not fluid restricted, prepare concentration of 5000 ng/mL by adding 1 mL (2.5 mg) of the injection concentrate to an infusion bag containing 500 mL of 0.9% sodium chloride injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For fluid-restricted patients, prepare concentration of 10,000 ng/mL by adding 1 mL (2.5 mg) of the injection concentrate to an infusion bag containing 250 mL of 0.9% sodium chloride injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Individualize rate of IV infusion based on BP response without exceeding maximum recommended rates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Prescribing Limits under Dosage and Administration.)

Dosage

Available as angiotensin II acetate; dosage expressed in terms of angiotensin II.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Initially, 20 ng/kg per minute by continuous IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Titrate based on BP response.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May increase infusion rate by increments of up to 15 ng/kg per minute as frequently as every 5 minutes as needed to achieve or maintain target BP.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not exceed maximum dosage of 80 ng/kg per minute during the first 3 hours of treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

When underlying shock has improved, titrate dosage downward by decrements of up to 15 ng/kg per minute every 5 to 15 minutes as tolerated to maintain target BP.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not exceed maximum dosage of 40 ng/kg per minute during maintenance therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

First 3 hours of infusion: Maximum 80 ng/kg per minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Maintenance period: Maximum 40 ng/kg per minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Increased incidence of arterial and venous thromboembolic events, particularly DVT, reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Concurrent use of venous thromboembolism (VTE) prophylaxis recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Insufficient data in pregnant women to determine any drug-associated risks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Animal reproductive studies not conducted.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Septic or other distributive shock is a medical emergency and can be fatal if untreated; a delay in treatment in pregnant women is likely to increase risk of maternal and fetal morbidity and mortality.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into milk, affects milk production, or affects the breast-fed infant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No substantial differences in safety or efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics not expected to be altered by hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics not expected to be altered by renal impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Thromboembolic events (e.g., DVT), thrombocytopenia, tachycardia, fungal infection, delirium, acidosis, hyperglycemia, peripheral ischemia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Angiotensin II Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Steady-state concentrations achieved in 5 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Following IV infusion, median time to reach target MAP approximately 5 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following IV infusion in adults with septic or other distributive shock, serum concentrations of angiotensin II were similar at baseline and at 3 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> However, after 3 hours of treatment, serum concentrations of angiotensin I (precursor to angiotensin II) were reduced by approximately 40%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether angiotensin II is distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized by aminopeptidase A to angiotensin 2-8 (angiotensin III) and by angiotensin-converting enzyme 2 to angiotensin 1-7 in plasma, erythrocytes, and many other major organs (e.g., intestine, kidney, liver, lungs).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Angiotensin 1-7 exhibits opposing actions to angiotensin II and causes vasodilation, natriuresis, and reduced BP.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

<1 minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No substantial pharmacokinetic differences based on gender or age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Clearance not dependent on hepatic or renal function; therefore, pharmacokinetics not expected to be influenced by hepatic or renal impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Potent vasoconstrictor that increases BP in patients with septic or other forms of distributive shock.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Synthetic preparation of endogenous human angiotensin II, a peptide hormone of the RAAS.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Increases BP through direct vasoconstriction of the peripheral vasculature; other effects include aldosterone secretion, vasopressin release, activation of the sympathetic nervous system, cardiac remodeling, and preferential vasoconstrictive effects on renal efferent arterioles.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>
