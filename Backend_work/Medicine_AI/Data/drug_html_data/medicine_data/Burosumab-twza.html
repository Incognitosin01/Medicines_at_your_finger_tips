Introduction

Recombinant fully human IgG1 monoclonal antibody to human fibroblast growth factor 23 (FGF23), a bone-derived hormone that regulates phosphate and vitamin D homeostasis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Uses for Burosumab-twza

X-linked Hypophosphatemia

Management of X-linked hypophosphatemia (previously known as familial vitamin D-resistant rickets) (designated an orphan drug by FDA for use in this condition).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Improves serum phosphorus concentrations and reduces osteomalacia-related bone lesions (e.g., bone histomorphometric parameters, active fractures) in adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Improves serum phosphorus concentrations and radiographic measures of rickets severity in pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Administration

A clinician should administer the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Burosumab-twza injection is a clear to slightly opalescent, colorless to pale brown-yellow solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Discard if solution is discolored or cloudy or if any particles or foreign particulate matter is present.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Binds to the bone-derived hormone FGF23, which regulates phosphate homeostasis, controlling reabsorption of phosphate in the kidney, and also regulates expression of 1α-hydroxylase, the rate-limiting enzyme in renal activation of vitamin D.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

  • In X-linked hypophosphatemia, loss-of-function mutations in the phosphate-regulating endopeptidase homolog X-linked (PHEX) gene lead to excess FGF23 and resultant suppression of renal tubular phosphate reabsorption and renal synthesis of 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D, renal phosphate wasting, and impaired bone mineralization.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Burosumab binds at the N-terminal domain to both intact FGF23 and FGF23 polypeptide fragments, restoring renal phosphate reabsorption and increasing serum concentrations of 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

  • Magnitude and duration of response are dose related.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> Renal tubular phosphate reabsorption, serum phosphorus concentrations, and 1,25-dihydroxyvitamin D concentrations increase linearly with increasing drug exposure.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> Increases in serum phosphorus concentrations return to baseline with drug elimination.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
