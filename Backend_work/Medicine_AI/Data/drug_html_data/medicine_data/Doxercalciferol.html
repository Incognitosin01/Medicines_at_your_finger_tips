Uses for Doxercalciferol

Hyperparathyroidism Secondary to Chronic Renal Disease

Treatment of secondary hyperparathyroidism in patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) undergoing dialysis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Oral doxercalciferol also used in the treatment of secondary hyperparathyroidism in patients with stage 3 or 4 CKD who do not yet require maintenance dialysis (predialysis patients).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Suppresses elevated serum or plasma parathyroid hormone (PTH) concentrations associated with secondary hyperparathyroidism in patients with CKD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Deficient production of biologically active vitamin D metabolites leads to secondary hyperparathyroidism, which contributes to the development of metabolic bone disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Doxercalciferol Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> without regard to meals or by direct IV<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> injection.

Dosage

Individualize doxercalciferol dosage based on serum or plasma intact PTH (iPTH) concentrations, with close monitoring of serum calcium and phosphorus concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

In dialysis patients, measure serum iPTH, calcium, and phosphorus concentrations prior to initiation of the drug and weekly during first 12 weeks of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Measure serum iPTH, calcium, phosphorus, and alkaline phosphatase concentrations periodically thereafter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

In predialysis patients, monitor serum calcium, serum phosphorus, and plasma iPTH concentrations at least every 2 weeks for 3 months after initiation of therapy or after subsequent dosage changes, then monthly for 3 months (once dosage is stabilized), and every 3 months thereafter.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Titrate dosage of doxercalciferol to reduce iPTH concentrations within a target range; specific target ranges recommended by manufacturer are based on the degree of renal impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Doxercalciferol

Contraindications

Tendency toward hypercalcemia.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Evidence of vitamin D toxicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Doxercalciferol injection: Known hypersensitivity to doxercalciferol or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> (See Hypersensitivity Reactions under Cautions.)

Warnings/Precautions

Serious, sometimes fatal, hypersensitivity reactions reported in hemodialysis patients receiving doxercalciferol injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Reactions have included anaphylaxis with angioedema (involving the face, lips, tongue, and airways), hypotension, unresponsiveness, chest discomfort, shortness of breath, and cardiopulmonary arrest.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for hypersensitivity reactions upon initiation of IV doxercalciferol treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> If hypersensitivity reactions occur, discontinue the drug and provide clinically appropriate treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Risk of vitamin D analog toxicity; may require emergency measures.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Acute hypercalcemia may increase risk of cardiac arrhythmias, seizures, and also synergistic inotropic and toxic effects in presence of cardiac glycosides.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Chronic hypercalcemia increases risk of soft-tissue calcification, including vascular calcification.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

If hypercalcemia develops following initiation of doxercalciferol therapy, decrease dosage of doxercalciferol and/or calcium-containing phosphate binders.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Use radiographic evaluation of suspected areas for early detection of calcification.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Do not use vitamin D and its analogs during doxercalciferol therapy; possible additive effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

May occur with vitamin D analog toxicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

In patients with CKD, use calcium-containing or other non-aluminum-containing phosphate binders and a low-phosphate diet to control serum phosphate concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

If hyperphosphatemia develops following initiation of doxercalciferol therapy, decrease dosage of doxercalciferol and/or increase dosage of phosphate binder.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Do not use magnesium-containing antacids concomitantly with doxercalciferol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Do not use doxercalciferol for treatment of nutritional vitamin D deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Evaluate patients for vitamin D deficiency prior to initiation of doxercalciferol therapy; if indicated, vitamin D deficiency should be treated prior to initiating doxercalciferol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible risk of hypercalcemia, hyperphosphatemia, hypercalciuria, and excessive suppression of iPTH concentrations; monitor and adjust dosages routinely to minimize risk of such effects. <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Most patients require doxercalciferol dosage titration as well as adjustment of concomitant therapy (e.g., dietary phosphate binders) to optimize iPTH suppression while maintaining serum calcium and phosphorus within prescribed ranges.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.)

Doxercalciferol Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Absorbed from GI tract, with peak blood concentrations of 1α,25-dihydroxyergocalciferol (major metabolite) usually attained within 11–12 hours after repeated oral dosing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Following IV administration, peak blood concentrations of 1,25-dihydroxyergocalciferol are achieved in about 8 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Hydroxylated to the active moiety, 1α,25-dihydroxyergocalciferol (1α,25-dihydroxyvitamin D2) via the hepatic CYP isoenzyme 27.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Active moiety (1α,25-dihydroxyvitamin D2): 32–37 hours (ranging up to 96 hours).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Temporary increases in mean concentrations of 1α,25-dihydroxyvitamin D2 (major metabolite) occur in patients undergoing hemodialysis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
