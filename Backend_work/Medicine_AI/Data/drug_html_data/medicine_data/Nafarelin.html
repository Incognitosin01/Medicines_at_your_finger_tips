Introduction

Gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) agonist; synthetic decapeptide analog of GnRH (luteinizing hormone-releasing hormone, gonadorelin); structurally related to goserelin, leuprolide, and triptorelin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Uses for Nafarelin

Endometriosis

Palliative treatment of endometriosis (e.g., pain relief, reduction in endometrial lesions).<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Experience limited to women ≥18 years of age treated for 6 consecutive months.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Precocious Puberty

Treatment of central (via activation of the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis) precocious puberty (true precocious puberty, GnRH-dependent precocious precocity, complete isosexual precocity) in children of both sexes<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> (designated as an orphan drug by FDA for this use).<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>
