Administration

Administer orally or by IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>403</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>409</sup></body></html>

Administer without regard to meals.<html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>403</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute vial containing 500 mg or 1 g of acyclovir powder with 10 or 20 mL of sterile water for injection, respectively, to provide a solution containing 50 mg/mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>409</sup></body></html>

Administer by IV infusion at a constant rate over at least 1 hour.<html><head></head><body><sup>409</sup></body></html> Do not administer by rapid IV infusion (over <10 minutes) or rapid IV injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>409</sup></body></html> (See Renal Effects under Cautions.)

Absorption from GI tract is variable and incomplete; 10–30% of an oral dose may be absorbed.<html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>233</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>403</sup></body></html> Peak plasma concentrations usually are attained within 1.5–2.5 hours after oral administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>232</sup></body></html>

Commercially available capsules and oral suspension are bioequivalent.<html><head></head><body><sup>403</sup></body></html>

Food does not appear to affect GI absorption.<html><head></head><body><sup>213</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>403</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Widely distributed into body tissues and fluids including the brain, kidney, saliva, lung, liver, muscle, spleen, uterus, vaginal mucosa, CSF, herpetic vesicular fluid, and semen.<html><head></head><body><sup>266</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized partially to 9-carboxymethoxymethylguanine;<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> also converted intracellularly in cells infected with herpesviruses to acyclovir triphosphate, the pharmacologically active form of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>354</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>403</sup></body></html>

Excreted principally in urine as unchanged drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Adults with normal renal function: initial serum half-life averages 0.34 hour and terminal half-life averages 2.1–3.5 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Renal impairment may reduce clearance.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
