Uses for Ibalizumab-uiyk

Treatment of HIV Infection

Treatment of HIV-1 infection in antiretroviral-experienced (previously treated) adults with multidrug-resistant HIV-1 infection failing their current antiretroviral regimen;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> used in conjunction with other antiretrovirals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Designated an orphan drug by FDA for this indication.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Ibalizumab-uiyk Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer by IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Must not be administered by rapid IV injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Must be diluted prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Should be administered by a trained medical professional.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Observe patient for 1 hour after completion of initial IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> May reduce post-infusion observation time to 15 minutes for subsequent infusions if infusion-associated adverse effects did not occur with initial infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer diluted solution by IV infusion into cephalic vein of the right or left arm.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If cephalic vein not accessible, use another appropriate vein.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

After completion of IV infusion, flush IV administration set with 30 mL of 0.9% sodium chloride injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to dilution, concentrate should appear as a colorless to slightly yellow and clear to slightly opalescent solution;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> do not use if it appears cloudy or discolored or contains particles.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use appropriate number of single-dose vials containing 150 mg of ibalizumab-uiyk per mL to prepare desired dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

To prepare the 2-g loading dose, use 10 single-dose vials of the concentrate; withdraw 1.33 mL from each of the vials (total volume of 13.3 mL) and add to an IV infusion bag containing 250 mL of 0.9% sodium chloride injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

To prepare an 800-mg maintenance dose, use 4 single-dose vials of the concentrate; withdraw 1.33 mL from each of the vials (total volume of 5.32 mL) and add to an IV infusion bag containing 250 mL of 0.9% sodium chloride injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Small residual amount of concentrate may remain in each vial;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> discard this unused portion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer immediately after dilution (see Storage under Stability).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If diluted solution refrigerated, let stand at room temperature for ≥30 minutes (but <4 hours) before administering.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Initial dose (loading dose): Administer by IV infusion over ≥30 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Maintenance doses: Administer by IV infusion over ≥15 minutes, unless infusion-associated reactions occurred with loading dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Antiretroviral-experienced adults with multidrug-resistant HIV-1 infection failing their current antiretroviral regimen: Single loading dose of 2 g followed by maintenance dosage of 800 mg once every 2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If a maintenance dose is missed by ≥3 days beyond scheduled day, administer a 2-g loading dose as soon as possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Then, resume usually recommended maintenance dosage of 800 mg once every 2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

Manufacturer makes no specific dosage recommendations for geriatric patients or for patients with renal or hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Contraindications

Warnings/Precautions

Immune reconstitution syndrome reported in at least 1 HIV-infected patient receiving ibalizumab-uiyk in conjunction with other antiretrovirals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

During initial phase of treatment, HIV-infected patients whose immune systems respond to antiretroviral therapy may develop an inflammatory response to indolent or residual opportunistic infections (e.g., Mycobacterium avium, M. tuberculosis, cytomegalovirus [CMV], Pneumocystis jirovecii [formerly P. carinii]);<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> such response may necessitate further evaluation and treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Potential for immunogenicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In clinical studies evaluating safety and efficacy of ibalizumab-uiyk, all enrolled patients were tested throughout the studies for presence of antibodies against the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Only 1 sample from 1 patient tested positive for anti-ibalizumab antibodies;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> no adverse reaction or reduced efficacy attributed to the low titer of anti-ibalizumab antibodies reported in this patient.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Antiretroviral Pregnancy Registry at 800-258-4263 or [Web].<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html>

Insufficient data to establish whether ibalizumab-uiyk poses a risk if used during pregnancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Animal reproductive toxicology studies not conducted.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monoclonal antibodies, such as ibalizumab-uiyk, are transported across the placenta as pregnancy progresses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Potential exists for transmission of ibalizumab-uiyk from mother to developing fetus.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known if distributed into human milk, affects breast-fed child, or affects milk production.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Human IgG is distributed into milk in humans.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> However, data indicate antibodies in breast milk do not enter neonatal or infant circulatory system in substantial amounts.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Instruct HIV-infected women not to breast-feed because of risk of HIV transmission and risk of adverse effects in the infant.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>202</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not studied in geriatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Ibalizumab-uiyk Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following single dose given by IV infusion over 30–90 minutes, AUC increases in more than dose-proportional manner.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

When recommended dosage regimen (single 2-g loading dose followed by maintenance dosage of 800 mg once every 2 weeks) used, steady-state concentrations achieved after first 800-mg maintenance dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Volume of distribution is 4.8 L (approximately that of serum volume).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

As dosage increased from 0.3 to 25 mg/kg, clearance decreased from 9.54 to 0.36 mL/hour per kg and elimination half-life increased from 2.7 to 64 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Pharmacokinetics not evaluated in patients with hepatic or renal impairment, in pediatric patients, or in geriatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Renal impairment not expected to affect pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Population pharmacokinetic analysis suggests ibalizumab-uiyk concentrations decrease as body weight increases;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> manufacturer states this not expected to affect virologic outcome and dosage adjustments not needed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

2–8°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Protect from light; do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Diluted solutions may be stored under refrigeration (2–8°C) for ≤24 hours or at room temperature (20–25°C) for ≤4 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions and Spectrum

  • Recombinant humanized IgG4 monoclonal antibody active against HIV-1.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> CD4-directed post-attachment HIV-1 inhibitor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

  • Blocks HIV-1 from infecting CD4+ T cells by binding to domain 2 of CD4 and interfering with post-attachment steps required for entry of HIV-1 virus particles into host cells, which prevents viral transmission that occurs via cell-cell fusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

  • Does not interfere with domain 1 binding site for major histocompatibility complex (MHC) class II molecules.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> Does not alter CD4-mediated immune functions or cause immunosuppression.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> Does not interfere with gp120 attachment to CD4.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

  • Decreased susceptibility (i.e., decreased maximum percent inhibition [MPI]) observed in some patients receiving ibalizumab-uiyk and may be associated with genotypic changes in the HIV-1 envelope coding sequence that result in loss of potential N-linked glycosylation sites (PNGS) in the V5 loop of gp120.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Clinical importance of this decreased susceptibility not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Known CD4 polymorphisms (i.e., naturally occurring amino acid substitutions in CD4) appear unlikely to affect ibalizumab-uiyk binding to CD4.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Critical nature of compliance with HIV therapy and importance of remaining under the care of a clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html> Importance of taking as prescribed; do not alter or discontinue antiretroviral regimen without consulting clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of using in conjunction with other antiretrovirals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Antiretroviral therapy is not a cure for HIV infection; opportunistic infections and other complications associated with HIV disease may still occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that early initiation of antiretroviral therapy and sustained decreases in plasma HIV RNA have been associated with reduced risk of progression to acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS) and death.<html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that effective antiretroviral regimens can decrease HIV concentrations in blood and genital secretions and strict adherence to such regimens in conjunction with risk-reduction measures may decrease, but cannot absolutely eliminate, the risk of secondary transmission of HIV to others.<html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html> Importance of continuing to practice safer sex (e.g., using latex or polyurethane condoms to minimize sexual contact with body fluids) and reducing high-risk behaviors (e.g., reusing or sharing needles).<html><head></head><body><sup>200</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of reading patient information provided by the manufacturer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that immune reconstitution syndrome has been reported in a patient receiving ibalizumab-uiyk; importance of immediately informing clinicians of any symptoms of infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and dietary or herbal products, and any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Advise HIV-infected women not to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
