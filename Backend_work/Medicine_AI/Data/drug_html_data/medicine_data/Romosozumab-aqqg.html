Uses for Romosozumab-aqqg

Osteoporosis

Treatment of osteoporosis in postmenopausal women at high risk of fracture, defined as history of osteoporotic fracture or multiple risk factors for fracture.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Treatment of osteoporosis in postmenopausal women following failure of or intolerance to other available osteoporosis therapies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Anabolic effect wanes after 12 months of therapy; limit duration of romosozumab therapy to 12 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Absorption under Pharmacokinetics.) If continued treatment of osteoporosis needed, consider use of a bone resorption inhibitor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In addition to adequate intake of calcium/vitamin D and other lifestyle modifications (e.g., exercise, avoidance of excessive alcohol and tobacco use), experts recommend that pharmacologic therapy for osteoporosis be considered in postmenopausal women and in men ≥50 years of age with high risk of fractures (generally those who have experienced a previous hip or vertebral fracture or who have low bone mineral density [BMD]); pharmacologic therapy also may be considered in postmenopausal women and in men ≥50 years of age with low bone mass, although there is less evidence supporting overall fracture risk reduction in such patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html>

Use of a drug with proven antifracture efficacy is recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html>

Individualize choice of therapy based on potential benefits (with respect to fracture risk reduction) and adverse effects of therapy, patient preferences, comorbidities, and risk factors.<html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>106</sup></body></html>

Some experts recommend romosozumab (for up to 1 year) as a treatment option in postmenopausal women with osteoporosis at very high risk for fracture, such as those with severe osteoporosis (i.e., T-score below −2.5 and fractures) or multiple vertebral fractures, to reduce risk of vertebral, hip, and nonvertebral fractures; following completion of romosozumab treatment course, administer other antiresorptive therapy to maintain bone mineral density (BMD) gains and reduce fracture risk.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> Avoid use in women at high risk for cardiovascular disease or stroke (i.e., history of MI or stroke) pending further characterization of the drug’s cardiovascular risks.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> (See Cardiac Effects under Cautions.)

Romosozumab-aqqg Dosage and Administration

General

  • Patients should receive adequate supplemental calcium and vitamin D during romosozumab therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Patients should receive adequate supplemental calcium and vitamin D during romosozumab therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer by sub-Q injection by health-care professional into abdomen, anterior thigh, or outer aspect of upper arm; do not administer IV or by IM injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Available in single-use prefilled syringes containing 105 mg of romosozumab-aqqg in 1.17 mL of solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Single 210-mg dose requires administration of entire contents of 2 prefilled syringes sequentially at separate sites.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use different injection site each time the drug is administered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If an injection site is used again, avoid site of the previous injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not inject into areas of skin that are tender, bruised, red, or hard.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Avoid scars and stretch marks; avoid injections within 2 inches of navel.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to administration, allow solution to warm at room temperature for ≥30 minutes; do not use other methods to warm the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Solution should appear clear to opalescent and colorless to light yellow; do not use if it is cloudy, discolored, or contains particles.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not shake the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

210 mg once every month for 12 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer a missed dose as soon as it can be rescheduled;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> give subsequent doses once monthly from the date of administered dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Romosozumab-binding antibodies, including neutralizing antibodies, reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Presence of antibodies associated with lower serum concentrations of the drug, but generally not associated with changes in efficacy or safety.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Romosozumab-aqqg Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Exhibits nonlinear pharmacokinetics with exposure increasing in more than dose-proportional manner.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following sub-Q administration, peak plasma concentrations occur at approximately 5 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Steady-state concentrations achieved within 3 months when administered once monthly.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Maximum effect on markers of bone formation (145% increase in procollagen type 1 N-telopeptide [P1NP] concentration) and bone resorption (55% decrease in type 1 collagen C-telopeptide [CTX]) observed 2 weeks following initiation of romosozumab-aqqg therapy in postmenopausal women with osteoporosis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

P1NP concentrations declined to concentrations observed in placebo recipients at 9 months of treatment; following drug discontinuance, concentrations returned to baseline within 12 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

CTX concentrations remained below concentrations observed in placebo recipients throughout 12 months of treatment; following drug discontinuance, concentrations initially increased above baseline, then returned toward baseline by 12 months.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Renal impairment, including end-stage renal disease requiring dialysis: No clinically important differences in pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

End-stage renal disease not requiring dialysis: Effect on pharmacokinetics not known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Body weight: Exposure decreases with increasing body weight.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Age (range: 20–89 years), sex, race, disease state (low bone mass or osteoporosis), and prior exposure to alendronate do not appear to affect pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolic pathway not studied; expected to be catabolized to small peptides and amino acids in similar manner as endogenous IgG.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

12.8 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Exhibits nonlinear pharmacokinetics with clearance decreasing as dose increases.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Humanized monoclonal IgG2 antibody that binds to and inhibits sclerostin; a bone anabolic agent.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

  • Sclerostin (a regulatory factor in bone metabolism with inhibitory effect on bone formation) binds to low-density lipoprotein receptor-related protein (LRP) 5 and LRP6 co-receptors, which prevents Wnt from binding to the frizzled family receptor and LRP co-receptors, leading to down-regulation of the canonical Wnt signaling pathway.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> Down-regulation of this pathway results in inhibition of osteoblast differentiation and decreased bone formation.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

  • Binding of romosozumab to sclerostin results in activation of the canonical Wnt signaling pathway, which increases bone formation and, to a lesser extent, decreases bone resorption.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

  • Causes rapid increase in bone formation markers (e.g., P1NP, osteocalcin, bone-specific alkaline phosphatase [BSAP]) and decrease in bone resorption markers (e.g., CTX).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> (See Absorption under Pharmacokinetics.)
