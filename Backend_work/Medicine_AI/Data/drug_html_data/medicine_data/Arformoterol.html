Uses for Arformoterol

COPD

Long-term treatment of bronchoconstriction associated with COPD, including chronic bronchitis and emphysema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists recommended as maintenance therapy in patients with moderate (e.g., forced expiratory volume in 1 second [FEV1] ≥50 but <80% of predicted) to very severe COPD (e.g., FEV1 <30% of predicted or <50% of predicted plus chronic respiratory failure) who have persistent symptoms not relieved by as-needed therapy with short-acting bronchodilators (e.g., ipratropium, β2-adrenergic agonist).<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Regular treatment with long-acting bronchodilators more effective and convenient than treatment with short-acting bronchodilators.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> Superiority of one long-acting bronchodilator over another currently not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> If inadequate response, may use a combination of long-acting bronchodilators, such as a long-acting inhaled anticholinergic agent (tiotropium) and a long-acting β2-adrenergic agonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

In patients with severe (e.g., FEV1 <50% of predicted, history of repeated exacerbations) to very severe COPD, add regular treatment with an inhaled corticosteroid to long-acting bronchodilator therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> If inadequate response or limiting adverse effects occur, add or substitute extended-release oral theophylline.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Not to be used for immediate relief of acute exacerbations of COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> Use short-acting inhaled β2-agonist intermittently (as needed) for acute symptoms of COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> (See Acute Exacerbations of COPD under Cautions.) Efficacy and safety of long-acting bronchodilators, with or without inhaled corticosteroids, during acute exacerbations of COPD not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Arformoterol

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to arformoterol, formoterol, or any ingredient in formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

All long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists, including arformoterol, contraindicated in patients with asthma without concomitant use of long-term asthma controller therapy; safety and efficacy of arformoterol in patients with asthma† not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Asthma-related Death under Cautions.)

Warnings/Precautions

Increased risk of asthma-related death reported with long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See REMS and also see Boxed Warning.)

  • Arformoterol is the active R, R-enantiomer of racemic formoterol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> Arformoterol is twice as potent as racemic formoterol.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Provide patients with a copy of the manufacturer's patient information (medication guide) each time the drug is dispensed.<html><head></head><body><sup>24</sup></body></html> Importance of instructing patients to read the medication guide prior to initiation of therapy and each time the prescription is refilled.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> (See REMS.)

  • Advise of increased risk of asthma†-related death with long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists, such as arformoterol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> Do not use any of the long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists, including arformoterol, in patients with asthma without concomitant use of long-term asthma controller therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of adherence to dosing schedules, including not exceeding recommended dosage or frequency of use unless otherwise instructed by clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patient that if a dose of arformoterol is missed, the next dose should be taken at the regularly scheduled time; dose should not be doubled.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not discontinuing arformoterol therapy without medical supervision, as symptoms may worsen.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of all patients being provided with and instructed in use of short-acting, inhaled β2-adrenergic bronchodilator as supplemental therapy for acute COPD symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of discontinuing regular use of a short-acting, inhaled β-adrenergic bronchodilator when initiating maintenance therapy with arformoterol and instituting intermittent use of a short-acting bronchodilator (not arformoterol) to relieve acute symptoms of COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not using arformoterol to relieve acute symptoms or exacerbations of COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of contacting clinician immediately if decreased effectiveness occurs and/or breathing problems worsen quickly; do not increase dose or frequency of administration or add therapy with other long-acting, inhaled β2-adrenergic agonists.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of contacting a clinician immediately if short-acting β2-adrenergic agonist becomes less effective (e.g., increased dosage or frequency of administration) for acute symptoms of COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>
