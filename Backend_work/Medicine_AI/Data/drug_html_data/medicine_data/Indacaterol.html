Uses for Indacaterol

COPD

Long-term maintenance treatment of airflow obstruction associated with COPD, including chronic bronchitis and/or emphysema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Other Uses

Interactions for Indacaterol

Metabolized via hydroxylation (mainly by CYP3A4 and, to lesser extent, by CYP1A1 and 2D6), as well as via glucuronidation by UGT1A1.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Low-affinity substrate of efflux transporter P-glycoprotein (P-gp).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Unlikely to substantially inhibit transporter proteins (e.g., multidrug resistance protein [MRP] 2, breast cancer resistance protein [BCRP], human organic cation transporters [hOCT] 1 and 2, human multidrug and toxin extrusion [hMATE] transporters 1 and 2K) in vivo; negligible potential to induce MRP2.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Drugs Affecting or Metabolized by Hepatic Microsomal Enzymes and/or the P-glycoprotein Transport System

Data from studies of potent and specific inhibitors of CYP3A4 and P-gp suggest that systemic clearance of indacaterol is influenced by changes in both CYP3A4 and P-gp activities.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> However, may administer recommended indacaterol dosage (75 mcg once daily) without adjustment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Drugs Metabolized by UGT1A1

Unlikely to cause metabolic interactions by induction of UGT1A1.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Drugs that Prolong the QT Interval

Advice to Patients

  • Provide a copy of the manufacturer's patient information (medication guide) to all patients each time the drug is dispensed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Importance of instructing patients to read the medication guide prior to initiation of therapy and each time the prescription is refilled.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients that if a dose is missed, to take the dose as soon as it is remembered; importance of not taking more than one dose in a 24-hour period.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of all patients being provided with and instructed in the use of a short-acting, inhaled β2-adrenergic agonist as treatment for acute COPD symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not using indacaterol to relieve acute symptoms or exacerbations of COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of contacting a clinician immediately if symptoms worsen, the short-acting inhaled β2-agonist becomes less effective or more inhalations than usual are required, or clinically important decrease in lung function occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients receiving indacaterol not to use additional doses of the drug or other inhaled, long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists because excessive use of sympathomimetic agents may result in substantial cardiovascular effects or death.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of patients not discontinuing therapy without medical supervision, since symptoms may recur after discontinuance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
