Introduction

Ocular hypotensive agent; synthetic analog of prostaglandin F2α (PGF2α).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Uses for Latanoprostene Bunod

Ocular Hypertension and Glaucoma

Reduction of elevated IOP in patients with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Once-daily latanoprostene bunod 0.024% ophthalmic solution appears to be more effective than or at least as effective as twice-daily timolol 0.5% ophthalmic solution in reducing IOP in adults with open-angle glaucoma or ocular hypertension.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

When selecting an initial ocular hypotensive agent, consider extent of the required IOP reduction, coexisting medical conditions, and drug characteristics (e.g., dosing frequency, adverse effects, cost).<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> With single-agent regimens, the reduction in IOP is approximately 25–33% with topical prostaglandin analogs; 20–25% with topical β-adrenergic blocking agents, α-adrenergic agonists, or miotic (parasympathomimetic) agents; 20–30% with oral carbonic anhydrase inhibitors; 18% with topical rho kinase inhibitors; and 15–20% with topical carbonic anhydrase inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html>

A prostaglandin analog frequently is considered for initial therapy in the absence of other considerations (e.g., contraindications, cost considerations, intolerance, adverse effects, patient refusal) because of relatively greater activity, once-daily administration, and low frequency of systemic adverse effects; however, ocular adverse effects can occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>134</sup></body></html>

Goal is to maintain an IOP at which visual field loss is unlikely to substantially reduce quality of life during the patient's lifetime.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html>

Reduction of pretreatment IOP by ≥25% shown to slow progression of primary open-angle glaucoma.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> Set an initial target IOP (based on extent of optic nerve damage and/or visual field loss, baseline IOP at which damage occurred, rate of progression, life expectancy, and other considerations) and reduce IOP toward this goal.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html> Adjust target IOP up or down as needed over course of disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>132</sup></body></html>

Combination therapy with drugs from different therapeutic classes often required to control IOP.<html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>133</sup></body></html>

Latanoprostene Bunod Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the affected eye(s).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Avoid contaminating solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Bacterial Keratitis under Cautions.)

Remove contact lenses prior to administration of each dose;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> may reinsert contact lenses 15 minutes after the dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Use with Contact Lenses under Cautions.)

If more than one topical ophthalmic drug used, administer the drugs at least 5 minutes apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Latanoprostene bunod 0.024% ophthalmic solution: One drop into conjunctival sac of affected eye(s) once daily in the evening.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

More frequent dosing may diminish the IOP-lowering effect of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If target IOP not achieved, may initiate additional or alternative ocular hypotensive agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>130</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>131</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>133</sup></body></html> (See Ocular Hypertension and Glaucoma under Uses.)

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Latanoprostene Bunod

Contraindications

  • Manufacturer states none known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer states none known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Increased pigmentation of the iris and periorbital tissue (eyelid) reported with prostaglandin analogs, including latanoprostene bunod.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Pigmentation expected to increase as long as latanoprostene bunod is administered.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Following discontinuance of the drug, pigmentation of the iris is likely to be permanent, while pigmentation of periorbital tissue likely to be reversible in most patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Long-term effects of increased pigmentation unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increased pigmentation of the iris may not be evident until after several months to years of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May continue latanoprostene bunod in patients who develop noticeably increased iris pigmentation; however, examine such patients regularly.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible gradual change in eyelashes and vellus hair in the treated eye, including increased length, thickness, pigmentation, and number of eyelashes and/or misdirected growth of eyelashes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Usually reversible upon discontinuance of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with a history of intraocular inflammation (e.g., iritis/uveitis); generally do not use in patients with active intraocular inflammation since therapy may exacerbate the condition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Macular edema, including cystoid macular edema, reported with prostaglandin analogs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in aphakic patients, in pseudophakic patients with torn posterior lens capsule, and in those with known risk factors for macular edema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Bacterial keratitis reported with use of multiple-dose containers of topical ophthalmic preparations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> These containers were inadvertently contaminated by patients who, in most cases, had concurrent corneal disease or disruption of the ocular epithelial surface.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Improper handling of ophthalmic solutions can result in contamination of the solution by common bacteria known to cause ocular infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Serious damage to the eye and subsequent loss of vision may result from using contaminated ophthalmic solutions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Remove contact lenses prior to administration of each dose since the preparation contains benzalkonium chloride preservative;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> may reinsert contact lenses 15 minutes after the dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No data available regarding use in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Has caused miscarriages, abortions, and fetal harm in rabbits.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> No treatment-related teratogenic effects observed in rats.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known if distributed into milk following topical application to the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Data not available on effects of the drug on the breast-fed child or on milk production.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Consider benefits of breast-feeding along with importance of the drug to the woman and any potential adverse effects on the breast-fed child from the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use in pediatric patients <16 years of age not recommended because of potential safety concerns related to increased pigmentation following long-term use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Pigmentation under Cautions.)

No overall differences in safety and efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Latanoprostene Bunod Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Prodrug; absorbed through the cornea following ocular instillation and hydrolyzed to active metabolites, latanoprost acid (prostaglandin agonist) and butanediol mononitrate (nitric oxide donor).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Following once-daily topical application of latanoprostene bunod 0.024% to the eyes for 28 days, no quantifiable plasma concentrations of latanoprostene bunod or butanediol mononitrate observed;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> low plasma concentrations of latanoprost acid detected.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Mean time to peak plasma concentrations of latanoprost acid occur approximately 5 minutes following administration of the prodrug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> Mean maximal plasma concentrations of latanoprost acid observed above the lower limit of quantitation (30 pg/mL) were 59.1 and 51.1 pg/mL on days 1 and 28, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Reduction in IOP generally occurs within 1–3 hours after ocular instillation and peaks within 11–13 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether the drug or its metabolites distribute into milk in humans following topical ocular administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Prodrug; metabolized in the eye by cellular esterases to latanoprost acid and butanediol mononitrate.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Elimination of latanoprost acid from human plasma is rapid; plasma concentrations dropped below the lower limit of quantitation (30 pg/mL) in most individuals by 15 minutes following administration of the prodrug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Systemically, latanoprost acid is further metabolized in the liver to the 1,2-dinor and 1,2,3,4-tetranor metabolites via fatty acid β-oxidation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Butanediol mononitrate is metabolized to 1,4-butanediol and nitric oxide; the 1,4-butanediol metabolite is further oxidized to succinic acid and enters the tricarboxylic acid cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Unopened bottles: 2–8°C; protect from light and freezing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> During shipment, may be maintained at up to 40°C for ≤14 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Opened bottles: 2–25°C for 8 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of not exceeding once-daily dosing; more frequent administration may paradoxically decrease IOP-lowering effect of latanoprostene bunod.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contaminating the ophthalmic solution with common bacteria that can cause ocular infections; instruct patients to avoid contact of tip of the container with the eyes or surrounding structures, fingers, or any other surface.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Serious damage to the eye and subsequent loss of vision may result from using contaminated ophthalmic solutions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of patients immediately informing a clinician before continuing therapy if they develop a new ocular condition (e.g., trauma, infection) or ocular reaction (particularly conjunctivitis, eyelid reactions), experience a sudden decrease in visual acuity, or have ocular surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of removing contact lenses prior to administration of each dose since the ophthalmic solution contains benzalkonium chloride preservative; may reinsert contact lenses 15 minutes after the dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • If using more than one ophthalmic preparation, importance of administering the preparations at least 5 minutes apart.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of permanent increase in brown pigmentation of the iris; risk of darkening of the skin around the eyes (eyelid), which usually is reversible after discontinuance of latanoprostene bunod.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Risk of changes in eyelashes and vellus hair in the treated eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Potential for disparity between eyes in length, thickness, pigmentation, or number of eyelashes or vellus hairs and/or direction of eyelash growth.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Eyelash changes usually reversible after discontinuance of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
