Uses for Oxymetazoline

Nasal Congestion

Self-medication for temporary relief of nasal congestion associated with the common cold, hay fever, or other upper respiratory allergies.<html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html>

As effective as other topical vasoconstrictors.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Labeled and has been used for self-medication for temporary relief of nasal congestion associated with sinusitis;<html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> however, efficacy data are lacking and/or controversial.<html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html> In October 2005, FDA issued final rule to remove this indication from labeling of OTC nasal decongestants.<html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html> Compliance date for preparations with annual sales <$25,000 is October 11, 2007; compliance date for all other preparations is April 11, 2007.<html><head></head><body><sup>107</sup></body></html>

Conjunctival Congestion

Self-medication for temporary relief of ocular redness due to minor irritation.<html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html>

Otitic Barotrauma

Has been used for self-medication for symptomatic prevention of otitic barotrauma† (aerotitis [barotitis] media);<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>105</sup></body></html> however, no more effective than placebo.<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html>

Oxymetazoline Dosage and Administration

Administer topically to nasal mucosa or conjunctiva.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html>

Administer nasal solution intranasally as sprays or nasal pumps.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Administer nasal spray or pump into each nostril while head is erect.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Administer ophthalmic solution topically to the conjunctiva.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html>

For self-medication: 1 or 2 drops of a 0.025% ophthalmic solution in the affected eye(s) every 6 hours as needed.<html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Oxymetazoline

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to oxymetazoline or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Known sensitivity to the pharmacologic effects of adrenergic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to oxymetazoline or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Known sensitivity to the pharmacologic effects of adrenergic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Possible irritation of nasal mucosa and adverse systemic effects (particularly in children) with excessive dosage and/or prolonged or too frequent intranasal use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Possible rebound nasal congestion or ocular hyperemia (redness);<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html> avoid prolonged use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Accidental ingestion of imidazoline derivatives (i.e., oxymetazoline, naphazoline, tetrahydrozoline) in children has resulted in serious adverse events requiring hospitalization (e.g., coma, bradycardia, decreased respiration, sedation, somnolence).<html><head></head><body><sup>1001</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>1002</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>1003</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>1004</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>1005</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>1006</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>1007</sup></body></html> (See Pediatric Use under Cautions and also see Advice to Patients.)

With intranasal use, possible headache, hypertension, cardiac irregularities (e.g., palpitation, reflex bradycardia), nervousness, nausea, dizziness, and insomnia.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Use with caution and under direction of a clinician in patients with thyroid disease (e.g., hyperthyroidism), heart disease (including angina), hypertension, advanced arteriosclerotic conditions, or diabetes mellitus; in patients experiencing difficulty in urination secondary to prostatic enlargement; or in patients receiving monoamine oxidase (MAO) inhibitors.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html>

Patients with narrow-angle glaucoma should consult a clinician before using ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>114</sup></body></html>

Not known whether oxymetazoline is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>114</sup></body></html> Use with caution in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>113</sup></body></html>

0.05% nasal solution or 0.025% ophthalmic solution not recommended for self-medication in children <6 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html>

Possible irritation of nasal mucosa and adverse systemic effects (including profound CNS depression) associated with excessive dosage, prolonged or too frequent use, or inadvertent ingestion of nasal solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Nasal solution: transient burning, stinging, sneezing, increased nasal discharge or dryness of nasal mucosa.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html>

Oxymetazoline Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Occasionally, absorption may be sufficient to produce systemic effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following intranasal administration, local vasoconstriction usually occurs within 5–10 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following ocular administration, local vasoconstriction usually occurs within minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following intranasal administration, local vasoconstriction persists for 5–6 hours, with gradual decline over the next 6 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following ophthalmic administration, local vasoconstriction persists for up to 6 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • With intranasal use, importance of discontinuing drug and consulting a clinician if nasal congestion worsens or persists for >3 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html>

  • With ophthalmic use, importance of discontinuing drug and consulting a clinician if ocular pain or visual changes occur or if ocular redness or irritation worsens or persists for >72 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html>

  • Overuse of nasal solution may cause recurrence or exacerbation of nasal congestion;<html><head></head><body><sup>111</sup></body></html> overuse of ophthalmic solution may produce increased redness of the eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>112</sup></body></html>
