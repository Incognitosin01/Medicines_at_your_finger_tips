Uses for Propylhexedrine

Nasal Congestion

Self-medication for temporary relief of nasal congestion associated with the common cold, hay fever, or other allergies.<html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

As effective as other topical vasoconstrictors.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Topical nasal decongestants often preferred for short-term treatment; oral agents preferred for prolonged treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See Overuse under Cautions.)

Reduce swelling and facilitate visualization of nasal and pharyngeal membranes prior to surgery or diagnostic procedures.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Open obstructed eustachian ostia in patients with ear inflammation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Propylhexedrine Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer topically to the nasal mucosa as an inhalation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of the inhaler tip; inhaler is for single-patient use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Warm drug container in the hands prior to use to increase drug volatility.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Inhale vapor through each nostril while head is erect; blow nose thoroughly after 3–5 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Supervise use by children 6–12 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Dosage

The Benzedrex propylhexedrine inhaler delivers 0.4–0.5 mg of the drug in each 800 mL of air.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Children ≥6 years of age: 2 inhalations (0.4–0.5 mg) in each nostril no more than every 2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

If symptoms are not improved after 3 consecutive days, consult a clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Overuse under Cautions.)

Cautions for Propylhexedrine

Warnings/Precautions

Possible irritation of nasal mucosa and adverse systemic effects (particularly in children) with excessive dosage and/or prolonged or too frequent use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Possible rebound congestion (rhinitis, chronic redness and swelling of the nasal mucosa); avoid prolonged use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> (See Advice to Patients.)

Overdosage following oral ingestion may result in psychotic reactions, palpitation, ventricular extrasystoles, and shock.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Possible headache, hypertension, nervousness, and increased ventricular rate.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Use with caution in patients with thyroid disease (e.g., hyperthyroidism), heart disease, hypertension, or diabetes mellitus.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Safety during pregnancy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children <6 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Possible irritation of nasal mucosa and adverse systemic effects associated with excessive dosage, prolonged or too frequent use, or inadvertent ingestion of nasal solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Burning, stinging, sneezing, increase in nasal discharge. <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Propylhexedrine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Occasionally, absorption may be sufficient to produce systemic effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following intranasal administration, local vasoconstriction usually occurs within 0.5–5 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following intranasal administration, local vasoconstriction persists for 30–120 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized in the liver.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Excreted in urine as unchanged drug and metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Tight container at 15–30°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Avoid excessive heat.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Inhaler remains potent for 2–3 months after first use, depending on the degree of use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Structurally and pharmacologically related to amphetamine.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Exact mechanism of action unknown but thought to be similar to amphetamine.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Indirectly stimulates α-adrenergic receptors of the sympathetic nervous system; exerts a minor stimulant effect on β-adrenergic receptors.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Intranasal application constricts dilated arterioles, reduces nasal blood flow and congestion, and may open obstructed eustachian ostia.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Temporarily improves nasal ventilation and aeration.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of replacing or discarding inhaler 2–3 months after first use.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of discontinuing drug and consulting a clinician if nasal congestion worsens or persists for >3 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Overuse of nasal inhaler may cause recurrence or exacerbation of nasal congestion; importance of not using more frequently than every 2 hours or for >3 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of avoiding contamination of the inhaler.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> To minimize risk of spreading infections, do not share inhaler with other individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinician if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
