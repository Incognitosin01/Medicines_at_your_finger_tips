Introduction

Mucolytic agent; biosynthetic (recombinant DNA origin) form of human deoxyribonuclease I (DNase I).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>33</sup></body></html>

Uses for Dornase Alfa

Cystic Fibrosis

Adjunctive therapy in patients with cystic fibrosis to reduce mucus viscosity and enable the clearance of airway secretions to improve pulmonary function (designated an orphan drug by FDA for this use).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>27</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>36</sup></body></html>

Reduces the frequency of respiratory infections requiring parenteral anti-infective therapy in patients with forced vital capacity (FVC) ≥40%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>27</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>36</sup></body></html>

Dornase Alfa Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer by oral inhalation via nebulization once daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer using a recommended nebulizer system; safety and efficacy of dornase alfa inhalation solution administered by a nebulizer system other than those listed below not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use in patients unable to inhale or exhale orally throughout the entire nebulization period.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Oral inhalation solution should not be diluted nor mixed with any other drugs in the nebulizer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Empty the entire contents of the single-use ampul of solution into the nebulizer cup; attach the cup to the inhalation apparatus according to the manufacturer’s instructions.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html>

Place the mouthpiece of the nebulizer in the mouth and turn on the compressor.<html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html> Breathe calmly and evenly through the mouth until the nebulizer stops producing a mist (duration of treatment for full dose is approximately 10–15 minutes).<html><head></head><body><sup>32</sup></body></html>

Clean the nebulizer after use according to the manufacturer’s instructions.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Oral inhalation solution contains no preservatives; once the single-use ampul is opened, use the entire contents or discard the remainder.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Each single-use ampul delivers 2.5 mg (2.5 mL of undiluted solution) to the nebulizer cup.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>35</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

In clinical studies, dosages >2.5 mg twice daily did not provide additional improvement in pulmonary function (e.g., FEV1).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy of daily administration for >12 months of continuous therapy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Dornase Alfa

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to dornase alfa, Chinese hamster ovary cell-derived products, or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Adjunct to and not a replacement for standard therapies (e.g., chest physical therapy, anti-infectives, bronchodilators, oral enzyme supplements, vitamins, oral and/or inhaled corticosteroids, analgesics); continue such therapies during enzyme therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

Category B.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Not known whether dornase alfa is distributed into milk; caution if used in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Limited experience in patients <5 years of age; use only in those in whom there is a potential for benefit in pulmonary function or in risk of respiratory tract infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increased incidence of cough, rhinitis, and rash in children <5 years of age compared with children ≥5 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Pharyngitis, chest pain, voice alteration (e.g., hoarseness), rash, conjunctivitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Dornase Alfa

No formal drug interaction studies to date; however, the manufacturer states that concomitant administration of dornase alfa and other standard therapies for cystic fibrosis is safe and effective.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Dornase Alfa Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following oral inhalation, serum DNase concentrations were not increased above normal endogenous levels.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following oral inhalation, mean sputum DNase concentrations measurable within 15 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Improvement in pulmonary function (FEV1) evident within 8 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether dornase alfa crosses the placenta or is distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

2–8°C; protect from light and excessive heat.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Store ampuls in protective foil pouch to protect from light until used.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Discard if left at room temperature for ≥24 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>
