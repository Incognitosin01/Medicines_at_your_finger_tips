Uses for Nalmefene

Postoperative Opiate Depression

Treatment of opiate-induced depression, including respiratory depression, caused by natural and synthetic opiates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Opiate Overdosage

Management of known or suspected opiate overdosage.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Nalmefene Dosage and Administration

Administration

Principally administered by IV injection; can be administered by IM or sub-Q injection if venous access cannot be established.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

For reversal of postoperative opiate depression, administer blue-labeled commercially available injection containing 100 mcg/mL nalmefene.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

For management of known or suspected opiate overdose, administer green-labeled commercially available injection containing 1 mg/mL nalmefene.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Proper steps should be taken to prevent use of incorrect concentration of injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Administer as an IV bolus.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

When used for treatment of postoperative opiate-induced depression in patients with increased cardiovascular risk, it may be desirable to dilute to a final ratio of 1:1 in 0.9% sodium chloride injection or sterile water for injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cardiovascular Disease under Cautions.)

Dosage

Available as nalmefene hydrochloride; dosage expressed in terms of nalmefene.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Titrate dosage to reverse opiate-induced effects; when adequate opiate reversal has been established, additional doses of nalmefene are not required and may cause unwanted reversal of analgesia or precipitate withdrawal.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Initially, 0.25 mcg/kg given at 2- to 5-minute intervals until the desired response is obtained.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Nalmefene

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to nalmefene or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to nalmefene or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

When used in emergency settings, other resuscitative measures (e.g., maintenance of an adequate airway, artificial respiration, administration of oxygen, vasopressor agents) should be readily available and used first when necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Risk of recurrent respiratory depression in postoperative and overdose patients after initial response to nalmefene.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Carefully monitor patients who have responded to nalmefene since the duration of action of some opiates (e.g., methadone and levo-alpha-acetylmethadol [LAAM]) may exceed that of nalmefene; monitor patients until there is no reasonable risk of recurrent respiratory depression.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Serious adverse cardiovascular effects (e.g., VT and VF, pulmonary edema, hypertension, hypotension, cardiovascular instability) reported in postoperative patients and in emergency department settings following administration of nalmefene; may be associated with excessive doses.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients with preexisting cardiovascular disease or in those receiving potentially cardiotoxic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Reduced dosage recommended in postoperative patients at high risk for cardiovascular complications.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> (See Adults under Dosage and Administration.)

Possible precipitation of acute withdrawal symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Use with extreme caution in patients with known physical dependence on opiates or following surgery involving high doses of opiates.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Observe patients suspected of opiate dependency for symptoms of withdrawal following initial and subsequent nalmefene injections.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Incomplete reversal of buprenorphine-induced analgesia reported in animal models; therefore, nalmefene may not completely reverse buprenorphine-induced respiratory depression.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Possible risk of seizures.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Nalmefene Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Completely absorbed following IM or sub-Q administration; mean bioavailability is about 101.5% and 99.7%, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Therapeutic plasma concentrations occurred 5–15 minutes following administration of a single IM or sub-Q dose.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Duration of opiate antagonist activity is variable and depends on the dose, the half-life and plasma concentration of opiate being reversed, the presence or absence of other drugs affecting the brain or muscles of respiration, and the dose of nalmefene being administered.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Effects persist for not >30–60 minutes following administration of a partial reversing dose (i.e., 1 mcg/kg).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Effects may persist for several hours following administration of a full reversing dose (i.e., 1 mg/70 kg).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Rapidly distributed following IV administration, with 80% of brain opiate receptors blocked within 5 minutes of administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Distributed into milk in rats; not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

45%.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized in the liver, primarily by glucuronide conjugation to nalmefene glucuronide, an inactive metabolite.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Excreted principally in urine, mainly as metabolites, and in feces (17%).<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Biphasic; terminal half-life is approximately 10.8 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

In patients with end-stage renal disease, clearance of nalmefene is reduced by 25–27% compared with that in healthy adults and half-life is increased to around 26.1 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

In patients with hepatic impairment, clearance of nalmefene is reduced by 28.3%.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

In geriatric patients, no substantial differences in clearance, steady-state volume of distribution, or half-life relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>
