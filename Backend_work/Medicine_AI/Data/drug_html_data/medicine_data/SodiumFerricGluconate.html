Uses for Sodium Ferric Gluconate

Iron Deficiency Anemia in Hemodialysis Patients Receiving Epoetin Alfa Therapy

Treatment of iron deficiency anemia in adult and pediatric hemodialysis patients receiving epoetin alfa therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

In patients with chronic kidney disease (CKD) on hemodialysis, IV iron superior to orally administered iron in increasing hemoglobin concentrations and/or minimizing dosage of an erythropoiesis-stimulating agent (ESA) (e.g., epoetin alfa); the National Kidney Foundation Kidney Disease Outcomes Quality Initiative (NKF-KDOQI) guidelines state that the IV route of iron administration is preferred in such patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Limited data suggest that iron sucrose and sodium ferric gluconate injections may be associated less frequently with serious adverse effects (e.g., hypersensitivity reactions) compared with iron dextran injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>101</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established for the prevention and/or treatment of iron deficiency anemia not associated with CKD (e.g., HIV- or cancer-related anemia).<html><head></head><body><sup>44</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>97</sup></body></html>

Sodium Ferric Gluconate Dosage and Administration

Administration

For solution and drug compatibility information, see Compatibility under Stability.

Administer (diluted) by slow IV infusion or (undiluted) by slow IV injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

For IV infusion, dilute recommended adult or pediatric dosage in 100 or 25 mL of 0.9% sodium chloride, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use immediately after dilution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

IV infusion: slowly (e.g., over 1 hour).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

IV injection (adults): slowly, up to 12.5 mg/minute<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> at the end of dialysis.<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Dosage is expressed in terms of mg of elemental iron.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Sodium ferric gluconate injection contains the equivalent of 12.5 mg of elemental iron per mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

1.5 mg/kg (up to 125 mg/dose) administered at or during hemodialysis for 8 sequential dialysis sessions.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

125 mg administered at sequential dialysis sessions or during the dialysis session itself.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Most patients will require a minimum cumulative dose of 1 g of elemental iron, administered over 8 sessions at or during sequential dialysis treatments, to achieve a favorable hemoglobin or hematocrit response.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Monitor iron indices (i.e., transferrin saturation [TSAT], serum ferritin concentrations) periodically, and use results (in conjunction with hemoglobin concentrations and ESA dosage) to guide iron therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> Once patients achieve TSAT levels ≥20% or serum ferritin concentrations ≥100 ng/mL, continue IV iron therapy at the lowest dose necessary to maintain target hematocrit/hemoglobin levels and iron stores within acceptable limits.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prescribing Limits

Maximum dose: 125 mg per session.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Sodium Ferric Gluconate

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to sodium ferric gluconate or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Evidence of iron overload.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Anemias not associated with iron deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to sodium ferric gluconate or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Evidence of iron overload.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Anemias not associated with iron deficiency.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Not easily eliminated from the body (not dialyzable) and accumulation can be toxic; avoid unwarranted therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Excess storage of iron may possibly result in a syndrome similar to hemosiderosis, particularly in patients whose anemia is not attributable to iron deficiency (e.g. those with hemoglobinopathies or other refractory anemias that might be erroneously diagnosed as iron deficiency anemia).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Possible hypotension accompanied by flushing, lightheadedness, malaise, fatigue, weakness, or severe pain in the chest, back, flanks, or groin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Such reactions are not associated with sensitivity and usually resolve within 1–2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> May require volume expansion if symptomatic.

Potentially fatal sensitivity (e.g. anaphylactic or anaphylactoid) reactions possible; use with caution, particularly in patients with a history of allergic reactions to iron dextran.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If serious anaphylactoid reactions occur, institute appropriate resuscitative measures.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category B.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether sodium ferric gluconate is distributed into milk; use with caution in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children <6 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Contains benzyl alcohol; not recommended for use in neonates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults; use caution in dosage selection and adjustment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Titrate dosage carefully.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Sodium Ferric Gluconate Pharmacokinetics

Distribution

Ferric iron in plasma combines with transferrin and is carried to the bone marrow and incorporated into hemoglobin.<html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>27</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>29</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>30</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>31</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Following IV administration, terminal elimination half-life of drug-bound iron was approximately 1 hour in healthy iron-deficient adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Following IV administration of 1.5 or 3 mg/kg, terminal elimination half-life was 2 or 2.5 hours, respectively, in iron-deficient pediatric patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

In vitro, <1% of the iron species in a single dose was removed during hemodialysis periods of up to 270 minutes using membranes with pore sizes corresponding to 12,000–14,000 daltons.<html><head></head><body><sup>99</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

20–25°C (may be exposed to temperatures ranging from 15–30°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not freeze.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
