Uses for Dyclonine

Oral/Throat Analgesia/Anesthesia

Self-medication for the short-term relief of minor sore throat pain and mouth and gum irritation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

In fixed combination with menthol as self-medication for temporary relief of occasional minor irritation, pain, or soreness of mouth or throat, and for cough associated with a cold or inhaled irritants.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html>

Local Anesthesia

Has been used as a local anesthetic agent prior to laryngoscopy, bronchoscopy, esophagoscopy, or endotracheal intubation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> However, oral solutions no longer are commercially available in the US.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Dyclonine Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer orally as a lozenge.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Allow the lozenge to slowly dissolve in the mouth; do not bite or chew it.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Cautions for Dyclonine

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to dyclonine or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to dyclonine or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Use with caution in patients with a known allergy to dyclonine.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Sensitization and allergic reactions are infrequent.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Possible hypersensitivity reactions to anesthetics may manifest as cutaneous lesions with delayed onset, urticaria, swelling, and edema.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Systemic (e.g., CNS, cardiovascular) effects possible with excessive dosage or rapid absorption of dyclonine.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Adverse CNS effects may include excitation and/or depression, nervousness, dizziness, blurred vision, or tremors followed by seizures, unconsciousness, drowsiness, and possibly respiratory arrest.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Adverse cardiovascular effects may include myocardial depression, hypotension, bradycardia, and cardiac arrest.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

If sore throat is severe or persistent (e.g., >2 days) or occurs with or is followed by a high fever, persistent headache, rash, swelling, irritation, and nausea, discontinue the drug and consult a clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

If sore mouth symptoms do not improve after 7 days, discontinue the drug and consult a clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Use with extreme caution in areas with traumatized mucosa and/or when sepsis is present in the region of intended application.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

When used in fixed combination with other agents, consider the cautions, precautions, and contraindications associated with the concomitant agents.

Safety has not been established in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Manufacturers state that pregnant women should consult a clinician prior to use.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Manufacturers state that nursing women should consult a clinician prior to use.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Safety of dyclonine lozenges not established in children <2 years of age; consult a clinician or dentist prior to use in such patients.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Dyclonine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following topical application of a 0.5 or 1% solution of dyclonine (both no longer commercially available in the US) to mucous membranes, local anesthesia occurs within 2–10 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Following topical application of a 0.5 or 1% solution of dyclonine, anesthesia persists for approximately 30 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to consult a clinician prior to use for a chronic cough secondary to smoking, asthma, or emphysema or a cough accompanied by excessive phlegm.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients to discontinue the drug and consult a clinician if sore throat symptoms or cough do not improve after 2 days or if sore mouth symptoms do not improve after 7 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>100</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>
