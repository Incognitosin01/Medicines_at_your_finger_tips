Introduction

Polyethylene glycol (PEG)-conjugated, modified synthetic RNA oligonucleotide and aptamer; selective vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF) antagonist.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Uses for Pegaptanib

Neovascular Age-related Macular Degeneration

Treatment of neovascular (wet) age-related macular degeneration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Pegaptanib Dosage and Administration

General

  • Manufacturer’s prescribing information should be consulted for details on assembly and proper use of the syringes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer’s prescribing information should be consulted for details on assembly and proper use of the syringes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administration

Evaluate patient’s medical history for hypersensitivity reactions to prior intravitreal administration of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications and also see Hypersensitivity Reactions under Cautions.)

Administer by intravitreal injection only into a single affected eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Safety and efficacy of treating both eyes concurrently not determined.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Pegaptanib injection does not need to reach room temperature prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Inject following adequate anesthesia and administration of broad-spectrum anti-infective agents.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Inject under controlled aseptic conditions (including use of sterile gloves, sterile drape, a sterile eyelid speculum [or equivalent]).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients for elevation of intraocular pressure (IOP) and for development of endophthalmitis following intravitreal injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Monitoring for IOP may include evaluation of optic nerve head perfusion immediately after the injection, tonometry within 30 minutes following the injection, and biomicroscopy between 2 and 7 days following injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as pegaptanib sodium; dosage expressed in terms of the free acid of the oligonucleotide without polyethylene glycol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Intravitreal injection: 0.3 mg into the affected eye every 6 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Doses exceeding 0.3 mg do not appear to provide additional therapeutic benefit;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> doses exceeding 1 mg have been associated with decreased efficacy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy of pegaptanib beyond 2 years of therapy have not been established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Special Populations

No dosage adjustment required.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Intravitreal injections of pegaptanib associated with endophthalmitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Monitor patients closely for signs of endophthalmitis (e.g., redness, sensitivity to light, pain, changes in vision) during the week following injection to permit early treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

The most common isolate cultured in patients with endophthalmitis after receiving pegaptanib was coagulase-negative Staphylococcus epidermiditis.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Proper aseptic injection technique should be used to decrease potential for infection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> (See Ophthalmic Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

Risk of retinal detachment and iatrogenic traumatic cataract.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Risk of increased IOP within 30 minutes of intravitreal injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Monitor and manage appropriately IOP and optic nerve head perfusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html> (See Ophthalmic Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

Rare cases of anaphylaxis/anaphylactoid reactions, including angioedema, reported following intravitreal administration procedure with pegaptanib.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Evaluate patient’s medical history for hypersensitivity reactions prior to intravitreal administration of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Pegaptanib

No formal drug interaction studies to date.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Metabolized by nucleases; generally not affected by CYP isoenzymes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No apparent difference in the pharmacokinetic profile of pegaptanib involving patients treated with pegaptanib alone or in combination with photodynamic therapy and verteporfin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> No adverse effects attributed directly to concomitant use of pegaptanib with verteporfin.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Pegaptanib Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Slowly absorbed into systemic circulation from the eye with mean peak plasma concentration usually attained within 1–4 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Distributed into vitreous fluid, retina, aqueous fluid, and kidneys.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Crosses the placenta in mice; not known whether crosses the placenta in humans.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Appears to be metabolized by endo- and exonucleases.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

In animals, excreted principally in urine as unchanged drug and metabolites.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Average: 10 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No dosage adjustment required in those with renal impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

2–8°C; do not freeze or shake vigorously.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May be kept at room temperature for up to 8 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Necessity of advising patients about the risk of developing endophthalmitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of informing their ophthalmologist immediately if changes in vision occur or the treated eye becomes red, sensitive to light, or painful.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Patients should be instructed to immediately report any symptoms suggestive of endophthalmitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> (See Endophthalmitis and Other Serious Ocular Effects under Cautions.)

  • Importance of women informing their clinician if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
