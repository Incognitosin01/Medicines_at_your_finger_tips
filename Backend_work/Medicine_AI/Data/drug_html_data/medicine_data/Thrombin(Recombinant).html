Uses for Thrombin (Recombinant)

Hemorrhage

Used topically as an aid in achieving hemostasis at accessible sites of oozing blood and minor bleeding from capillaries and small venules when control of bleeding using standard surgical measures (e.g., suture, ligature, electrocautery) is ineffective or impractical.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

May be used alone or in conjunction with an absorbable gelatin sponge during various types of surgery (e.g., spinal surgery, liver resection, peripheral arterial bypass surgery).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Do not use for massive or brisk arterial bleeding.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications.)

Thrombin (Recombinant) Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically as a solution; do not inject.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications.) Separate topical thrombin solutions from parenteral preparations to avoid inadvertent injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html> (See Thrombosis under Cautions.) Do not leave reconstituted solutions in syringes as an intermediate step.<html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html>

Apply directly to surfaces of bleeding tissue using a sterile syringe sprayer or spray pump; alternatively, may apply with an absorbable gelatin sponge.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> Consult manufacturer's instructions for proper assembly and use of the syringe sprayer or spray pump (available as part of the spray applicator kit).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

When used in conjunction with an absorbable gelatin sponge, transfer solution to a sterile bowl or basin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> Immerse sponge strips of the desired size in solution to allow complete saturation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> Gently squeeze sponge strips to remove excess drug and apply to bleeding site in a single layer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> Consult manufacturer's information for detailed instructions on use of absorbable gelatin sponge preparations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Using a needle-free transfer device, add 5 or 20 mL of the supplied diluent (0.9% sodium chloride injection) to a vial containing 5000 or 20,000 units, respectively, of lyophilized thrombin alfa (thrombin [recombinant]) to provide a solution containing 1000 units/mL.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Gently swirl vial until powder is completely dissolved.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Draw reconstituted solution into a sterile syringe and apply auxiliary label indicating that the solution is for topical use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html>

Use within 24 hours of reconstitution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Volume of drug required to achieve hemostasis depends on total number of bleeding sites, surface area being treated, and method of application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Hemostasis usually achieved within 10 minutes following a single application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Thrombin (Recombinant) Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Minimally absorbed (<0.37%) following topical application to the liver in a rabbit wound model.<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Time to hemostasis dependent on concentrations of thrombin and fibrinogen present during clot formation.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Rapidly (< 5 minutes) neutralized by endogenous inhibitors in circulation; cleared by liver.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

2–25°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May store reconstituted solution at 2–25°C for up to 24 hours; discard after 24 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Promotes hemostasis principally by converting fibrinogen to fibrin; other mechanisms (e.g., platelet activation and aggregation, activation of factor XIII leading to fibrin crosslinking and clot stabilization) also involved.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

  • Converts fibrinogen directly to fibrin at site of vessel injury without the addition of other substances.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

  • Rapidly inactivated by endogenous inhibitors (e.g., antithrombin III human, heparin cofactor II, α2-macroglobulin) to prevent systemic coagulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

Risk of blood clotting disorders if absorbed systemically; importance of contacting a clinician if any new or unusual symptoms of thrombosis occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>102</sup></body></html>

Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
