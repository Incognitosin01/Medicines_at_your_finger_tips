Introduction

Cellulose derivatives (methylcellulose), calcium polycarbophil, malt soup extract, and psyllium preparations are bulk-forming laxatives.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>f</sup></body></html>

Uses for Bulk-Forming Laxatives

Constipation

Relief of occasional constipation; restore bowel movement regularity.<html><head></head><body><sup>e</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>f</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>g</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>h</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>j</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>k</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>l</sup></body></html>

Initial treatment of choice for simple constipation caused by a low-fiber and/or low-fluid diet.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Bulk-forming laxatives, stool softeners, or mineral oil preferred in conditions in which straining at defecation should be avoided (e.g., MI, vascular diseases, diseases of the anus or rectum, hernias, recent rectal surgery).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Treatment of constipation that occurs following prolonged bed rest or hospitalization.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Treatment of choice for constipation resulting from diminished colonic motor response in geriatric patients;<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html> condition is usually due to psychological or physical laxative dependence.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Bulk-forming laxatives or stool softeners are the preferred treatment for constipation occurring during pregnancy or the puerperium.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Not for use when prompt or thorough bowel evacuation is necessary (e.g., poisonings, radiologic examination, bowel surgery).<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Diarrhea

Increase the bulk of stools in patients with chronic, watery diarrhea; subjective improvement noted but the total water content of the stool was unchanged.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

GI Pain

Reduce intraluminal rectosigmoid pressure and pain in diverticular disease.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>i</sup></body></html>

Hypercholesterolemia†

Psyllium preparations have been used as an adjunct to dietary therapy to reduce elevated serum total cholesterol, LDL cholesterol, apolipoprotein B (apo B) concentrations and the ratio of LDL-cholesterol to HDL-cholesterol in adults with mild to moderate hypercholesterolemia†.<html><head></head><body><sup>108</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>109</sup></body></html>
