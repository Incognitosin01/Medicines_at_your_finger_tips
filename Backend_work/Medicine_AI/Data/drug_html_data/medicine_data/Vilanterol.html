Uses for Vilanterol

Bronchospasm in COPD

Vilanterol/fluticasone or vilanterol/umeclidinium fixed-combination therapy: Long-term maintenance treatment of airflow obstruction associated with COPD, including chronic bronchitis and emphysema.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Vilanterol/fluticasone fixed-combination therapy: Reduction of COPD exacerbations in patients with a history of exacerbations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Vilanterol/fluticasone or vilanterol/umeclidinium fixed-combination therapy: Not indicated for treatment of acute bronchospasm (i.e., as rescue therapy for treatment of acute episodes of bronchospasm).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Other Uses

Vilanterol/umeclidinium fixed-combination therapy: Not indicated for treatment of asthma†; safety and efficacy in asthma not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Vilanterol Dosage and Administration

Administration

Administer vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone by oral inhalation only using a specific preloaded inhaler (Breo Ellipta) that delivers powdered vilanterol/fluticasone in fixed combination from foil-wrapped blisters.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer vilanterol in fixed combination with umeclidinium by oral inhalation only using a specific preloaded inhaler (Anoro Ellipta) that delivers powdered vilanterol/umeclidinium in fixed combination from foil-wrapped blisters.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Administer vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone or umeclidinium once daily at the same time every day.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Before first use of either the Breo Ellipta or Anoro Ellipta inhaler, remove device from foil tray; discard enclosed desiccant out of reach of children and pets.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Write date the tray is opened and inhaler is to be discarded (6 weeks after opening) on label.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Do not open inhaler cover until immediately before use; to avoid wasting doses, do not close cover again until dose is inhaled.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Open cover fully to expose the mouthpiece and expect to hear a click.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> If dose counter does not advance when click is heard, inform clinician that dose is not properly prepared.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Before inhaling dose, exhale completely; do not exhale into mouthpiece of inhaler.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Place mouthpiece between lips and inhale deeply through inhaler with a steady, even breath; do not inhale through nose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Do not block air vent on inhaler during inhalation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Remove inhaler from mouth, hold the breath for about 3–4 seconds (or as long as comfortable), then exhale slowly and gently.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Do not administer another dose even if delivery of dose not perceived.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> After dose is administered, close inhaler by sliding cover over mouthpiece as far as possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

After inhalation of vilanterol/fluticasone in fixed combination, rinse mouth with water without swallowing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Routine cleaning of inhaler is not necessary; may clean mouthpiece with dry tissue if desired.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

When used in fixed combination with fluticasone or umeclidinium, consider the cautions, precautions, contraindications, and interactions associated with fluticasone or umeclidinium.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Increased risk of asthma-related death reported with long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists (e.g., vilanterol) when used as monotherapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> Data from clinical trials also suggest that use of long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists as monotherapy increases the risk of asthma-related hospitalization in children and adolescents.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html>

Large safety study (Salmeterol Multicenter Asthma Research Trial [SMART]) showed an increase in asthma-related deaths in patients receiving salmeterol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Increased risk of asthma-related death with use of salmeterol as monotherapy considered class effect of long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists, including vilanterol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Based on review of 4 clinical trials (3 in adults and adolescents and 1 in children), FDA concluded that there is no clinically important increased risk of serious asthma-related events (i.e., asthma-related hospitalization, intubation, death) associated with the use of fixed-combination therapy with long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists and inhaled corticosteroids compared with use of inhaled corticosteroids alone for the treatment of asthma.<html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> These studies also showed that fixed-combination therapy with long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists and inhaled corticosteroids was more effective in reducing the incidence of asthma exacerbations compared with use of inhaled corticosteroids alone.<html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>22</sup></body></html>

Not known whether death rate is increased in patients with COPD receiving long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists, including vilanterol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Do not initiate vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone or umeclidinium in patients with acutely deteriorating COPD, which may be life-threatening.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Do not use for relief of acute symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Not studied in patients with acute symptoms; do not use extra doses of the drug in such situations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Use a short-acting, inhaled β2-agonist as needed for acute symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

When initiating therapy, discontinue regular use of short-acting oral or inhaled β2-agonists and use only for relief of acute COPD symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Failure to respond to a previously effective dosage of vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone or umeclidinium or to a supplemental short-acting, inhaled β2-agonist may indicate worsening COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Immediately reevaluate the patient and treatment regimen.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Do not use extra or increased dosages in such situations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Possible fatalities and/or adverse cardiovascular effects reported in association with excessive use of inhaled sympathomimetic drugs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Do not use vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone or umeclidinium more frequently or at dosages higher than recommended or concomitantly with other preparations containing long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists, since overdosage may result.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Do not use vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone or umeclidinium with another preparation containing a long-acting β2-agonist for any reason.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Possible acute, life-threatening paradoxical bronchospasm may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> If paradoxical bronchospasm occurs, immediately treat patient with an inhaled, short-acting bronchodilator; discontinue therapy with vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone or umeclidinium; and institute alternative therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Use vilanterol with caution in patients with cardiovascular disorders (e.g., coronary insufficiency, cardiac arrhythmias, hypertension).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Vilanterol Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Rapidly absorbed following oral inhalation;<html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> absolute bioavailability of vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone is approximately 27%, principally due to absorption of drug delivered to the lungs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Oral bioavailability of the swallowed portion of an inhaled dose is low (<2% for vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone) because of extensive first-pass metabolism.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Peak plasma concentrations reached within 5–15 minutes following oral inhalation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Steady-state concentrations achieved after 6 days of once-daily dosing of vilanterol/fluticasone fixed-combination therapy and within 14 days of once-daily dosing of vilanterol/umeclidinium fixed-combination therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Vilanterol/fluticasone in fixed combination: Median time to onset (defined as a 100-mL increase from baseline in FEV1) 16 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Vilanterol/umeclidinium in fixed combination: Median time to onset 27 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Bronchodilation generally persists for 24 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Vilanterol/fluticasone in fixed combination: No clinically important changes in peak concentrations or AUC of vilanterol, but increased exposure of fluticasone observed in patients with mild, moderate, or severe hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Hepatic Impairment under Cautions.)

Vilanterol/umeclidinium in fixed combination: No clinically important changes in peak concentrations or AUC of vilanterol or umeclidinium in patients with moderate hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> (See Hepatic Impairment under Cautions.)

Distribution

Not known whether distributed into human milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Approximately 94%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Extensively metabolized, mainly by CYP3A4.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Excreted mainly as metabolites in urine (70%) and feces (30%) following oral administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Effective half-life for vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone: 21.3 hours following oral inhalation of multiple doses in patients with COPD.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Effective half-life of vilanterol in fixed combination with umeclidinium: 11–19 hours following oral inhalation of multiple doses in healthy individuals.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

No clinically important differences in pharmacokinetics based on age, race, or gender.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Possible increased exposure in patients with severe renal impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • When used in fixed combination with fluticasone or umeclidinium, importance of informing patients of important cautionary information about fluticasone or umeclidinium.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Provide copy of the manufacturer’s patient information (medication guide) and instructions for use to all patients each time drug is dispensed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> Importance of instructing patients to read the medication guide prior to initiation of therapy and each time prescription is refilled.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients that monotherapy with long-acting β2-adrenergic agonists (including vilanterol) increases the risk of asthma-related death.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of adequate understanding of proper storage, preparation, and inhalation techniques, including use of the inhalation delivery systems.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not using vilanterol to relieve acute COPD symptoms; do not use extra doses for such symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of adherence to dosing schedules, including not altering the dose or frequency of use unless otherwise instructed by a clinician.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients that if a dose is missed, to take the dose as soon as it is remembered; importance of not doubling the dose or taking more than one dose in a 24-hour period.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of all patients being provided with and instructed in the use of a short-acting, inhaled β2-adrenergic agonist as treatment for acute COPD symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of discontinuing regular use of short-acting, inhaled β2-agonists when initiating vilanterol-containing preparations and using short-acting, inhaled β2-agonists to relieve acute symptoms.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of contacting a clinician immediately if symptoms worsen, the short-acting inhaled β2-agonist becomes less effective or more inhalations than usual are required, or clinically important decrease in lung function occurs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients receiving vilanterol-containing preparations not to use additional vilanterol or other long-acting, inhaled β2-agonists for any reason.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of patients not discontinuing therapy without medical supervision, since symptoms may recur after discontinuance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of adverse effects associated with β2-agonists (e.g., palpitations, chest pain, rapid heart rate, tremor, nervousness).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients that hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., anaphylaxis, angioedema, rash, urticaria) may occur following administration of vilanterol in fixed combination with fluticasone and instructing patients to discontinue the drug if such reactions occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of advising patients with severe milk protein allergy to avoid use of vilanterol-containing preparations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and dietary or herbal supplements, as well as any concomitant illnesses (e.g., cardiac disease, hypertension, seizures, thyroid disease, diabetes mellitus, allergies to drugs or food).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
