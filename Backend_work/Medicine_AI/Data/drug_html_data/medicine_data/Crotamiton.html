Pediculosis

Has been used for treatment of pediculosis capitis† (head lice infestation).<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html>

Treatment of pediculosis corporis† (body lice infestation).<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> One of several options recommended for treatment of pediculosis corporis in the adjunctive treatment of epidemic (louse-borne) typhus.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> The causative agent of epidemic typhus (Rickettsia prowazekii) is transmitted person-to-person by Pediculus humanus corporis and thorough delousing (especially among exposed contacts of individuals with typhus) is recommended in epidemic situations.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html>

Pruritus

Crotamiton Dosage and Administration

General

  • To avoid reinfestation or transmission of scabies, clothing and bed linen that may have been contaminated by the infested individual during the 3 days prior to treatment should be decontaminated (machine-washed in hot water and dried in a hot dryer or dry-cleaned).<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

  • Items that cannot be laundered or dry-cleaned should be removed from body contact for ≥72 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html>

  • Fumigation of living areas is not necessary and is not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

Administration

Apply topically to skin as 10% cream or lotion.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Do not apply to face, eyes, mouth,<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html> urethral meatus, or mucous membranes.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> For external use only; do not administer orally or intravaginally.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Shake lotion before using.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Before applying crotamiton for the treatment of scabies, the patient should bathe or shower with soap and water,<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html> taking care to scrub and remove scaling or crusted detritus, then towel dry.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Apply thin layer of the cream or lotion uniformly and massage thoroughly into all skin surfaces below the chin (particularly all creases and folds) including the entire trunk and extremities from the neck to the toes (including the soles of the feet).<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html> Trim fingernails short and apply the cream or lotion underneath the fingernails with a toothbrush; wrap the toothbrush in paper and properly discard to prevent potential oral crotamiton exposure and toxicity.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Remove the cream or lotion 48 hours after the last application by bathing.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Sexual contacts of patients receiving treatment for scabies and other individuals (household, family) who have had close personal contact with the patient within the previous month should be examined and treated.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html>

The CDC recommends that scabies epidemics in institutional settings (e.g., nursing homes, hospitals, residential facilities and communities) be managed in consultation with an expert.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> The entire population at risk should be treated during institutional outbreaks.<html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>119</sup></body></html>

Pruritus does not indicate treatment failure and is not usually considered an indication for retreatment;<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> such pruritus results from a hypersensitivity reaction of the host to the mite<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>104</sup></body></html> and may persist for several weeks despite successful treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> Oral antihistamines and/or topical corticosteroids may be used to help relieve pruritus.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html> Topical or oral anti-infectives are indicated for treatment of secondary bacterial infections of excoriated skin lesions.<html><head></head><body><sup>103</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Crotamiton

Contraindications

  • Known sensitivity or allergy to crotamiton or known primary irritation response to other topical medications.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Known sensitivity or allergy to crotamiton or known primary irritation response to other topical medications.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

If severe irritation or a sensitivity reaction occurs following topical application, discontinue and initiate appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Avoid contact with the eyes or mouth since irritation may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Avoid contact with mucous membranes, the vagina, and urethral meatus.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Do not apply to acutely inflamed skin or denuded or weeping skin surfaces until acute inflammation has resolved.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Information regarding distribution into milk not available.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established in children.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in controlled clinical studies in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html> Other clinical experience has not revealed age-related differences in response; however, greater sensitivity of some geriatric individuals cannot be ruled out.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Common Adverse Effects

Primary irritation reactions (e.g., dermatitis, pruritus, rash), allergic sensitivity reactions.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>

Crotamiton Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Absorbed systemically following topical application to skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>117</sup></body></html>

Elimination

In a study in healthy adults using radiolabeled crotamiton 10% lotion applied topically twice daily (0.229 mL applied on an area of the forearm 229 cm2), 4.8–8.8% of the dose (measured as radioactivity) was eliminated in urine.<html><head></head><body><sup>117</sup></body></html>

Following topical application to skin, absorption half-life is 2.7 hours and rate of elimination is 30.9 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>117</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

Room temperature.<html><head></head><body><sup>115</sup></body></html>
