Alcaftadine Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply topically to the eye as an ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> For topical ophthalmic use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Remove contact lenses prior to each dose (since benzalkonium chloride preservative may be absorbed by soft lenses); may reinsert lenses after 10 minutes following administration if eyes are not red.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid contamination of the solution container.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Children ≥2 years of age: 1 drop of a 0.25% solution in each eye once daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

1 drop of a 0.25% solution in each eye once daily.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Alcaftadine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Appears rapidly in systemic circulation following topical application to eye.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Peak plasma concentrations of alcaftadine and its active metabolite occur at a median of 15 minutes and at 1 hour, respectively, after administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Rapid; effect on ocular itching apparent at 3 minutes after conjunctival antigen challenge.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Effect on ocular itching shown to persist for 16 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether distributed into milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Alcaftadine: About 39%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Carboxylic acid metabolite: About 63%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Metabolized by cytosolic, non-CYP enzymes to an active carboxylic acid metabolite.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Carboxylic acid metabolite is eliminated mainly in urine (based on data for oral alcaftadine).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Carboxylic acid metabolite: About 2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Plasma concentrations of alcaftadine and carboxylic acid metabolite are below limit of quantitation by 3 and 12 hours, respectively, after administration of alcaftadine ophthalmic solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of learning and adhering to proper administration techniques to avoid contamination of the solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Keep dropper bottle tightly closed when not in use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of delaying insertion of contact lenses for ≥10 minutes after alcaftadine instillation to prevent absorption of benzalkonium chloride by soft lenses; do not wear contact lenses if eyes are red.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Not indicated for contact lens-related ocular irritation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing clinicians if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
