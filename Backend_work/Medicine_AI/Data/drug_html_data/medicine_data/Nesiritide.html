Introduction

Vasodilator; a biosynthetic (recombinant DNA origin) form of human B-type natriuretic peptide (BNP).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html>

Uses for Nesiritide

Acute Decompensated Heart Failure

Treatment of acutely decompensated heart failure in patients with dyspnea at rest or with minimal activity.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Evidence demonstrating improved outcomes with IV vasodilator therapy in hospitalized patients with heart failure is lacking.<html><head></head><body><sup>524</sup></body></html>

May be considered an adjunct to diuretic therapy for relief of dyspnea in patients hospitalized for acutely decompensated heart failure who do not have symptomatic hypotension.<html><head></head><body><sup>524</sup></body></html>

FDA-approved labeling for treatment of dyspnea in patients with acutely decompensated heart failure based on clinical trials showing reductions in pulmonary capillary wedge pressure (PCWP) and improvement in dyspnea when assessed 3 hours after initiation of the IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> A subsequent placebo-controlled trial in >7000 patients (ASCEND-HF) failed to show meaningful improvement in dyspnea at 6 or 24 hours or meaningful short-term reductions in rates of death or rehospitalization for heart failure when nesiritide was used in conjunction with standard therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html>

Some clinicians state that routine use cannot be recommended in the broad population of patients with acutely decompensated heart failure.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>22</sup></body></html>

Use of intermittent, serial, or scheduled repetitive infusions in an outpatient setting for treatment of severe decompensated heart failure not recommended.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>23</sup></body></html> A randomized, placebo-controlled trial failed to establish efficacy of serial outpatient nesiritide infusions for treatment of severe heart failure.<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer recommends strictly limiting use to patients with acutely decompensated heart failure whose manifestations warrant hospitalization or management in emergency department.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Manufacturer does not recommend use as replacement therapy for diuretics, to improve renal function, and/or to enhance diuresis.<html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> (See Renal Effects under Cautions.)

Administration

Administer IV loading dose followed by continuous IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

For solution and drug compatibility information, see Compatibility under Stability.

Reconstituted solutions contain no preservatives; solutions preferably should be prepared immediately before use.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Prime IV tubing with 5 mL of diluted nesiritide solution prior to connecting the tubing to the patient’s vascular access port and prior to administering the loading dose or starting the infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Withdraw loading dose from the infusion bag containing diluted nesiritide solution and administer through port in IV infusion set; continuous infusion should follow immediately.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Do not administer through a central catheter coated with heparin, since nesiritide binds to heparin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Remove 5 mL of a preservative-free diluent (see Solution Compatibility under Stability) from a prefilled 250-mL infusion bag and add the 5 mL of diluent to a vial labeled as containing 1.5 mg of nesiritide.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Swirl vial gently to ensure dissolution; do not shake.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Following reconstitution, add entire contents of the vial to the original 250-mL infusion bag, to yield a final nesiritide concentration of approximately 6 mcg/mL; invert bag several times to ensure complete mixing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Because of the possibility of dose-related episodes of severe and/or protracted hypotension, do not initiate nesiritide at higher than recommended dosages.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html>

Do not titrate at frequent intervals.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Loading dose of 2 mcg/kg over approximately 60 seconds, followed immediately by continuous infusion of 0.01 mcg/kg per minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Loading dose may not be appropriate for patients with SBP <110 mm Hg or for those who recently received cardiac afterload-reducing agents (e.g., nitroglycerin).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Increase infusion rate no more frequently than every 3 hours with central hemodynamic monitoring, up to maximum dosage of 0.03 mcg/kg per minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If hypotension occurs, reduce dosage or discontinue drug and institute supportive measures (e.g., IV fluids, changes in body position).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> If symptomatic hypotension occurs, discontinue drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

May reinitiate nesiritide without loading dose and with 30% reduction in infusion rate once patient has been stabilized.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Because hypotension may be prolonged (up to hours), a period of observation may be necessary before infusion is restarted.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

In ASCEND-HF trial, incidence of symptomatic hypotension was increased with nesiritide compared with placebo (approximately 7 versus 4%).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

In VMAC trial, incidence of symptomatic hypotension in initial 24 hours of therapy was similar with nesiritide or IV nitroglycerin (4 or 5%, respectively), but more prolonged with nesiritide (mean duration: 2.2 hours) than with nitroglycerin (mean duration: 0.7 hour).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Higher dosages have been associated with increased risk of hypotension.<html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Concomitant use of agents that reduce cardiac afterload or affect renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (e.g., ACE inhibitors, angiotensin II receptor antagonists) increases risk of symptomatic hypotension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Interactions.)

Administer only in settings where BP can be monitored closely and hypotension treated aggressively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If hypotension occurs, institute supportive measures.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Dosage reduction or drug discontinuance may be required.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> (See Dosage Modification for Hypotensive Episodes under Dosage and Administration.)

Contraindicated in patients with persistent SBP <100 mm Hg prior to initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use not recommended in patients with known or suspected low cardiac filling pressures or in those for whom vasodilating agents are not appropriate (e.g., patients with substantial valvular stenosis, restrictive or obstructive cardiomyopathy, constrictive pericarditis, pericardial tamponade, or other conditions in which cardiac output depends on venous return).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Pooled analyses of data from initial controlled clinical trials (approximately 1500–1700 patients) indicated numerical, but not statistically significant, increases in 30-day mortality with nesiritide compared with other therapies (generally nitroglycerin and diuretics).<html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Subsequent pooled analysis of data from 7 clinical trials (approximately 8500 patients), including ASCEND-HF, indicated no increase in 30-day mortality with nesiritide compared with active or placebo controls.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Pooled analysis of data from 6 of these studies (approximately 8400 patients) indicated no increase in 180-day mortality with nesiritide compared with active or placebo controls.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible increased Scr.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> Monitor Scr during and after completion of therapy until concentrations are stable.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Possible azotemia in patients with severe heart failure whose renal function depends on activity of the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

Risk of elevated Scr increased in patients receiving nesiritide infusions initiated at rates >0.01 mcg/kg per minute compared with those receiving standard therapies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Initial pooled analysis of data from 5 clinical trials (1269 patients) suggested 1.5-fold increase in risk of worsening renal function (Scr increase of >0.5 mg/dL) with nesiritide (up to 0.03 mcg/kg per minute) compared with active controls.<html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> However, in ASCEND-HF (>7000 patients), incidence of worsening renal function (decrease in estimated GFR of >25%) was similar with nesiritide (0.01 mcg/kg per minute) or placebo.<html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

Serious hypersensitivity reactions reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> More likely to occur in patients with history of sensitivity to recombinant peptides.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Prior to initiation of nesiritide therapy, ask patients about previous hypersensitivity reactions to other recombinant peptides.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If hypersensitivity reaction occurs, discontinue drug and provide appropriate treatment (e.g., epinephrine, oxygen, IV fluids, antihistamines, corticosteroids, pressor amines, airway management) as clinically indicated.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Nesiritide Pharmacokinetics

Elimination

Binding to cell surface clearance receptors with subsequent cellular internalization and lysosomal proteolysis; proteolytic cleavage of the peptide by endopeptidases (e.g., neutral endopeptidase) on the vascular lumenal surface; and renal filtration.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

Mechanisms involved in elimination not specifically studied in humans.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Biphasic; mean initial-phase and terminal-phase half-lives of approximately 2 and 18 minutes, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Renal impairment does not substantially alter pharmacokinetics.<html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>

Age, gender, race/ethnicity, concentration of endogenous BNP, and severity of heart failure do not substantially affect clearance.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html>
