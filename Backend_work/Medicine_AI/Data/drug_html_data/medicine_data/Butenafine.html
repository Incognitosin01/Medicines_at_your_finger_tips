Uses for Butenafine

Dermatophytoses

Available for self-medication (OTC use) for treatment of tinea corporis, tinea cruris, and tinea pedis.<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Administration

Apply topically to the skin as a 1% cream.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Clean affected areas with soap and water; dry thoroughly prior to applying cream.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> May be applied after bathing.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Cautions for Butenafine

Contraindications

  • Known hypersensitivity to butenafine or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Known hypersensitivity to butenafine or any ingredient in the formulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

For external use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> Use only for topical application to the skin; not for ophthalmic or intravaginal use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Avoid contact with eyes, nose, mouth, and other mucous membranes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

If contact with the eye(s) occurs, wash with large amounts of water; consult a clinician if ocular irritation persists.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Do not use for scalp or nail infections.<html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

If irritation or sensitivity occurs, discontinue drug and initiate appropriate therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Contact dermatitis has been reported rarely.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in patients hypersensitive to allylamine antifungals (e.g., naftifine, terbinafine) since cross-sensitivity may occur.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Prior to use, confirm diagnosis by direct microscopic examination of scrapings from infected tissue mounted in potassium hydroxide (KOH) or by culture.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Category B.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether distributed into milk following topical application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use with caution and avoid applying to the breast of nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Butenafine Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Percutaneous absorption occurs following topical application of 1% cream.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Total amount of dose absorbed into systemic circulation following topical application has not been quantified.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Distribution

Not known whether distributed into milk following topical application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Systemically absorbed drug is metabolized; principal metabolite in urine is formed through hydroxylation at the terminal t-butyl side-chain.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

After absorption through skin, a biphasic decline occurs with estimated half-lives of 35 and >150 hours, respectively.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

5–30°C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>
