Avoid use in patients with overt heart failure; use cautiously in patients with inadequate cardiac function and, if necessary, in patients with well-compensated heart failure.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If heart failure worsens, consider discontinuing therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Importance of taking nebivolol exactly as prescribed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of not interrupting or discontinuing therapy without consulting a clinician;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> patients should temporarily limit physical activity when discontinuing therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • If a dose is missed, importance of patient taking only the next scheduled dose (i.e., the next dose should not be doubled).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of avoiding some activities (e.g., driving, operating machinery) until effects on the patient are known.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of warning patients receiving insulin or oral hypoglycemic agents and those subject to spontaneous hypoglycemia that β-blockers can mask some symptoms of hypoglycemia (e.g., tachycardia).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of immediately informing clinician at the first sign or symptom (e.g., weight gain, shortness of breath) of CHF.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of patients informing their anesthesiologist or dentist about nebivolol therapy before undergoing major surgery.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinicians of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
