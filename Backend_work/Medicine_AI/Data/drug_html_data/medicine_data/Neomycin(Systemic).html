Warning

  • Neurotoxicity (manifested as both auditory and vestibular ototoxicity) can occur, even at recommended doses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Aminoglycoside-induced ototoxicity is irreversible, usually bilateral, and may be partial or total.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Neomycin (Systemic)

Neurotoxic, Ototoxic, or Nephrotoxic Drugs

Concomitant or sequential use with other drugs that have neurotoxic, ototoxic, or nephrotoxic effects may result in additive toxicity and should be avoided, if possible.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Advise patients that antibacterials (including neomycin) should only be used to treat bacterial infections and not be used to treat viral infections (e.g., the common cold).<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of completing full course of therapy, even if feeling better after a few days.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that skipping doses or not completing the full course of therapy may decrease effectiveness and increase the likelihood that bacteria will develop resistance and will not be treatable with neomycin or other antibacterials in the future.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients of the risk of toxic effects on the eighth nerve, including partial or total deafness.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> Inform patients that the possibility of acute toxicity is increased in premature infants and neonates.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>
