Introduction

Antifungal; oxaborole (boron-containing compound).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Uses for Tavaborole

Onychomycosis

Topical treatment of onychomycosis of the toenails (tinea unguium, ringworm of the nail) caused by Trichophyton rubrum or T. mentagrophytes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Tavaborole Dosage and Administration

Administration

Apply 5% solution topically to affected toenails using dropper supplied by the manufacturer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Do not administer orally or intravaginally;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> do not apply topically to eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Clean and dry toenails prior to application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Using dropper supplied by the manufacturer, place solution on each affected toenail; use dropper tip to spread solution over entire toenail surface and under toenail tip.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> More than a single drop of the solution may be necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid applying to skin that is not surrounding toenails being treated;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> wipe excess solution from surrounding skin.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Allow solution to dry on the toenails (this may take a couple of minutes).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Wash hands with soap and water after applying the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Apply 5% solution to affected toenails once daily for 48 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Use amount sufficient to cover entire toenail surface and under toenail tip.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Topical Administration under Dosage and Administration.)

Special Populations

No special population dosage recommendations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Tavaborole

Does not inhibit CYP2A6, 2D6, or 2E1 and does not induce CYP1A2, 2B6, 2C8, 2C9, 2C19, or 3A4/5 in vitro at therapeutic concentrations.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Tavaborole Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Following topical application of 5% tavaborole solution to toenails, the drug penetrates the nail plate and low concentrations are absorbed systemically.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Mean peak plasma concentration was 3.54 ng/mL (range 0.618–10.2 ng/mL) after a single dose and 5.17 ng/mL (range 1.51–12.8 ng/mL) after 14 consecutive days of once-daily topical administration of the 5% solution.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Median time to peak plasma concentration was 8.03 hours (range 0.47–24 hours) following 14 consecutive days of once-daily topical administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Steady-state concentrations achieved after 14 days of once-daily topical application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Elimination

Following systemic absorption, tavaborole undergoes extensive systemic metabolism.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Systemically absorbed drug is principally eliminated by the kidneys.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Mean terminal plasma half-life was 28.5 hours following 14 consecutive days of once-daily topical application to toenails.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Stability

Storage

20–25°C (may be exposed to 15–30°C).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

The 5% solution contains alcohol and is flammable;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> do not store or use near heat or open flame.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Keep bottle tightly closed;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> discard bottle within 3 months after insertion of dropper.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions and Spectrum

  • Boron-containing oxaborole antifungal.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

  • Integration of boron into the cyclic structure of a molecule allows for greater stability, greater physiologic availability at required site of action, and effective target binding capacity and selectivity.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Exerts antifungal activity by inhibiting an aminoacyl-transfer ribonucleic acid (tRNA) synthetase (AARS), resulting in fungal protein synthesis inhibition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Inhibits editing domain of leucyl-tRNA synthetase;<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> exhibits good relative selectivity for this fungal enzyme.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Has antifungal activity against some dermatophytes, including T. mentagrophytes and T. rubrum.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> Also has in vitro activity against Candida albicans.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

  • Antifungal activity not affected by presence of keratin.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

  • No evidence to date that T. mentagrophytes or T. rubrum develops resistance to tavaborole following long-term topical treatment of onychomycosis caused by these dermatophytes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

Importance of using tavaborole topical solution only for the condition prescribed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advise patients that tavaborole topical solution is for external use only and to avoid contact with eyes, mouth, or vagina.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advise patients to avoid applying the solution to skin other than skin immediately surrounding treated toenails, and to wipe away excess solution from surrounding skin using a tissue.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Importance of applying tavaborole topical solution with the dropper provided by the manufacturer and importance of not using the dropper for any other purpose.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advise patients to ensure toenails are clean and dry prior to application of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advise patients to wash hands with soap and water following application of the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Advise patients that tavaborole topical solution is flammable and should not be stored or used near heat or open flame.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Importance of informing clinician if area of application shows signs of persistent irritation (e.g., redness, itching, swelling).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Importance of informing clinician of existing or contemplated concomitant therapy, including prescription and OTC drugs and dietary or herbal supplements, as well as any concomitant illnesses.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Importance of women informing their clinician if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed. <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Importance of informing patients of other important precautionary information.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Cautions.)
