Introduction

Gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH) antagonist; a synthetic decapeptide analog of naturally occurring GnRH.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

Uses for Cetrorelix

Female Infertility

Used as a component of controlled ovarian stimulation regimens (e.g., recombinant FSH or human menopausal gonadotropin [hMG], and human chorionic gonadotropin [hCG]) to delay premature LH surges and consequently ovulation during assisted reproductive technologies (ART) programs.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Cetrorelix Dosage and Administration

General

  • Should be prescribed by clinicians experienced in infertility treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Prior to use of cetrorelix, initiate controlled ovarian stimulation (COS) therapy with gonadotropins (FSH or hMG) usually on day 2 or 3 of the menstrual cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Individualize dosage of gonadotropins based on the patient’s ovarian response to allow sufficient follicular development.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Initiate therapy with cetrorelix on days 5–7 of COS therapy and continue COS therapy until sufficient follicular growth is verified (e.g., ultrasound).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • When ultrasound assessement shows sufficient follicular maturation, discontinue COS therapy and cetrorelix and administer hCG to complete final follicular maturation and induce ovulation.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Perform oocyte retrieval, followed by in vitro fertilization or intracytoplasmic sperm injection, with subsequent attempts at implantation and pregnancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Do not administer hCG if the ovaries show an excessive response to treatment with gonadotropins because of an increased risk of ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administration

Administer by sub-Q injection either once as a single dose or once daily in multiple smaller doses in early- to mid-follicular phase of the menstrual cycle.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For the single-dose regimen, administer by sub-Q injection once on the day of COS when the serum estradiol concentration is indicative of an appropriate stimulation response (400 pg/mL), usually on stimulation day 7 (range: day 5–9).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

For the multiple-dose regimen, administer by sub-Q injection on day 5 (morning or evening) or on the morning of day 6 of COS and continue daily until an adequate follicular response is achieved.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Administer using 27-gauge needle (provided by manufacturer) into lower abdominal area, preferably around, but ≥2.54 cm (1 inch) from the umbilicus.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> With multiple doses, rotate injection sites to minimize local irritation.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute vial containing 0.25 or 3 mg of cetrorelix acetate sterile lyophilized powder with 1 or 3 mL, respectively, of sterile water for injection using the prefilled syringe and 20-gauge needle (provided by manufacturer).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Gently agitate vial until powder is completely dissolved;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> avoid formation of bubbles.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html> Withdraw entire contents of the vial into the syringe, then replace 20-gauge needle with 27-gauge needle (provided by manufacturer) for administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Reconstituted solutions contain no preservatives;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> prepare solutions immediately before use.<html><head></head><body><sup>b</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

May cause fetal harm; embryolethality reported in animals.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Congenital anomalies reported in women who received cetrorelix during pregnancy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Exclude pregnancy prior to initiation of therapy.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Severe anaphylaxis associated with cough, rash, and hypotension during therapy exceeding recommended dosages (10 mg daily for 7 months for an indication other than infertility) reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Monitor patients carefully after initial injection.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> Take special care in women with signs and symptoms of active allergic conditions or history of allergies.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html> Use not recommended in women with severe allergic conditions.<html><head></head><body><sup>a</sup></body></html>

Cetrorelix Pharmacokinetics

Absorption

Absolute bioavailability in healthy females is 85%.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Single 3-mg dose: Suppression of LH secretion within 1 hour.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Single 0.25-mg dose: Suppression of LH secretion within 2 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Single 3-mg dose: LH secretion suppressed for ≥4 days.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Single 0.25-mg dose: LH secretion suppressed for 24 hours.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
