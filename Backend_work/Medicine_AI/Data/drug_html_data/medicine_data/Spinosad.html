Uses for Spinosad

Pediculosis

Topical treatment of pediculosis capitis (head lice infestation) in adults and children ≥4 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

AAP usually recommends topical permethrin 1% or topical pyrethrins with piperonyl butoxide for initial treatment;<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> some clinicians suggest spinosad 0.9% suspension may be an alternative, especially for permethrin-resistant pediculosis capitis.<html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Spinosad Dosage and Administration

General

  • To avoid reinfestation or transmission of lice, most experts recommend that clothing, hats, bed linen, and towels that were worn or used by the infested individual during the 2 days prior to treatment be decontaminated (machine-washed in hot water and dried in a hot dryer).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Items that cannot be laundered can be dry-cleaned or sealed in a plastic bag for 2 weeks.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Decontaminate combs, brushes, and hair clips used by the infested individual by soaking in hot water (>54°C) for 5–10 minutes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Thoroughly vacuum car seats, upholstered furniture, and floors of rooms inhabited by infested individual.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Fumigation of living areas is not necessary.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Evaluate other family members and close contacts of the infested individual and treat if lice infestation is present.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Some clinicians suggest treating family members who share a bed with the infested individual, even if no live lice are found on this family member.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> Ideally, treat all infested household members and close contacts at the same time.<html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html>

  • Use of fine-toothed or nit comb to remove any remaining nits (eggs) or nit shells from the hair<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> is not necessary following application of spinosad.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> Some clinicians do not consider nit removal necessary since only live lice can be transmitted, but recommend it for aesthetic reasons and to decrease diagnostic confusion and unnecessary retreatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Other clinicians recommend removal of nits (especially those within 1 cm of the scalp) to decrease risk of reinfestation since no pediculicide is 100% ovicidal and potentially viable nits may remain on the hair after treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Although many schools will not allow children with nits to attend, AAP and other experts consider these no-nit policies excessive.<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html>

Administration

Apply topically to scalp and scalp hair as a 0.9% suspension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

For external use only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Do not administer orally or intravaginally; do not apply topically to eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Shake well immediately prior to use.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Apply to dry scalp, and then dry hair, in amount sufficient to completely saturate scalp and hair.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Dosage under Dosage and Administration.) Leave suspension on hair and scalp for 10 minutes, then thoroughly rinse off with warm (not hot) water.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Minimize exposing other areas of skin by performing rinse at a sink (rather than in shower or bath).<html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> Hair may be shampooed after suspension is rinsed off.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Avoid contact with eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Eyes should be closed tightly and covered with a soft towel or washcloth while the suspension is applied to or washed off of scalp hair.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If contact with the eyes occurs, immediately flush with water.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Supervise pediatric patients during application;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> an adult should apply and rinse the suspension for the child.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Wash hands thoroughly after applying suspension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Warnings/Precautions

Contains benzyl alcohol; administration of IV injections preserved with benzyl alcohol has been associated with serious adverse reactions (e.g., gasping syndrome) and death in neonates and low-birthweight infants†.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Syndrome consists of metabolic acidosis, gasping respirations, progressive hypotension, seizures, CNS depression, intracranial hemorrhage, and death in preterm, low-birthweight infants.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html>

Extent of systemic exposure to benzyl alcohol following topical application of spinosad 0.9% suspension is not known; possible increased systemic absorption in neonates and infants <6 months of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Lower limit for toxicity is unknown.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Pediatric Use under Cautions.)

Category B.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No evidence that spinosad is systemically absorbed following topical application; not expected to be present in breast milk.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Spinosad 0.9% topical suspension contains benzyl alcohol; not known if benzyl alcohol is distributed into milk following topical application of the suspension.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Use with caution in nursing women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If used in a lactating woman, she may choose to pump and discard breast milk for 8 hours (i.e., 5 half-lives of benzyl alcohol) after use to avoid infant ingestion of benzyl alcohol.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Benzyl Alcohol Toxicity under Cautions.)

Safety not established in children <4 years of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Because spinosad 0.9% topical suspension contains benzyl alcohol, do not use in infants <6 months of age.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Benzyl Alcohol Toxicity under Cautions.)

Keep out of reach of children;<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> use on children only under the direct supervision of an adult.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Insufficient experience in patients ≥65 years of age to determine whether geriatric patients respond differently than younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions and Spectrum

  • Spinosad is a 5:1 mixture of spinosyn A and spinosyn D, natural macrocyclic lactones derived from fermentation of Saccharopolyspora spinosa, a soil actinomycete.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Insecticide used as a topical pediculicide.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

  • Interferes with nicotinic acetylcholine receptors and, to a lesser extent, γ-amino butyric acid (GABA) receptors in insects, including lice.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html> Causes extended periods of neuronal hyperexcitation that result in louse paralysis, inability to feed, and death.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

  • Ovicidal at all stages of louse egg development.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> Spinosad-resistant lice not documented to date.<html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

  • Active against permethrin-resistant human head lice.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> Cross-resistance with other pediculicides not expected.<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

Advice to Patients

  • Advise patients to follow application instructions, including amount of suspension to apply, how long to leave on hair, and how to remove.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Importance of second application if live lice are present 7 days after initial application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that the suspension is for topical use on scalp and scalp hair only.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of avoiding contact with eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Advise patients to rinse thoroughly with water if drug gets in or near the eyes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Advise patients that spinosad 0.9% topical suspension should not be administered orally.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> If inadvertently ingested, patient should seek immediate medical attention.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of washing hands after application.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of keeping out of reach of children; use on children only under the direct supervision of an adult.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of informing clinician of existing dermatologic conditions or sensitivities prior to treatment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

  • Importance of women informing their clinician if they are or plan to become pregnant or plan to breast-feed.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>
