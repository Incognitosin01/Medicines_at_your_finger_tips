Introduction

Platelet-activation and -aggregation inhibitor; nonthienopyridine, reversible, P2Y12 platelet adenosine diphosphate (ADP)-receptor antagonist. <html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Uses for Cangrelor

Acute Ischemic Complications of PCI

Used as an adjunct to PCI to reduce the risk of periprocedural MI, repeat coronary revascularization, and stent thrombosis in patients who have not been treated with a P2Y12-receptor antagonist and are not being given a glycoprotein IIb/IIIa (GP IIb/IIIa) inhibitor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Dual-drug antiplatelet therapy with a P2Y12-receptor antagonist and aspirin is part of the current standard of care in patients with acute coronary syndromes (ACS).<html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>12</sup></body></html>

Potential advantages of cangrelor include rapid onset and offset of effect and less interindividual variability in antiplatelet effect (i.e., compared with clopidogrel).<html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>7</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>21</sup></body></html>

Precise role of cangrelor in the management of ACS remains to be established.<html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>15</sup></body></html>

Cangrelor Dosage and Administration

General

  • Intended to be given to patients prior to PCI in conjunction with other standard-of-care therapy (e.g., aspirin, anticoagulants).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Intended to be given to patients prior to PCI in conjunction with other standard-of-care therapy (e.g., aspirin, anticoagulants).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>5</sup></body></html>

Administration

For solution and drug compatibility information, see Compatibility under Stability.

Reconstituted drug requires further dilution prior to administration.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Administer by rapid IV (“bolus”) injection (over <1 minute) from the prepared IV infusion bag via manual IV push or infusion pump, followed by IV infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html>

Reconstitute vial containing 50 mg of lyophilized cangrelor with 5 mL of sterile water for injection (swirl gently).<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Dilute reconstituted solution in 250 mL of 5% dextrose or 0.9% sodium chloride injection to a final concentration of 200 mcg/mL for rapid IV injection and infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Rapid IV (“bolus”) injection: Administer over <1 minute.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Continuous IV infusion: Administer for at least 2 hours or for duration of PCI, whichever is longer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

Dosage

Available as cangrelor tetrasodium; dosage expressed in terms of cangrelor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

30 mcg/kg by rapid IV (“bolus”) injection prior to PCI, immediately followed by 4 mcg/kg per minute by continuous IV infusion for at least 2 hours or for the duration of the procedure, whichever is longer.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html>

After discontinuance of cangrelor infusion, administer an oral P2Y12-receptor antagonist (clopidogrel, prasugrel, or ticagrelor) to maintain platelet inhibition.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>17</sup></body></html>

If ticagrelor used, administer 180 mg any time during cangrelor infusion or immediately after discontinuance of the infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

If clopidogrel used, administer 600 mg immediately after discontinuance of cangrelor infusion; do not administer concurrently with cangrelor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

If prasugrel used, administer 60 mg immediately after discontinuance of cangrelor infusion; do not administer concurrently with cangrelor.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Specific Drugs under Interactions.)

Warnings/Precautions

Possible bleeding, especially hematoma at the site of vascular access.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>3</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>4</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>8</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>10</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications.)

In pivotal clinical trial, greater incidence of bleeding events of all severities with cangrelor compared with clopidogrel; however, rate of severe bleeding (per the Global Use of Strategies to Open Occluded Coronary Arteries [GUSTO] criteria) not substantially different between treatment groups.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html>

Antiplatelet effect negligible 1 hour after discontinuance of cangrelor infusion.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>14</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>16</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html>

Hypersensitivity reactions (e.g., anaphylaxis, bronchospasm, angioedema, stridor) reported.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Contraindications.)

Category C.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No adequate and well-controlled studies of cangrelor in pregnant women.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> In animal studies, cangrelor produced dose-related fetal growth retardation and increased incidences of abortion and/or intrauterine losses, but did not produce malformations; not considered to be a teratogen.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Not known whether cangrelor is distributed into human milk; discontinue nursing or the drug.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Safety and efficacy not established.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html>

No substantial differences in safety or efficacy relative to younger adults.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

No dosage adjustment required; however, worsening renal function reported in some patients with severe renal impairment (Clcr <30 mL/minute) receiving cangrelor in clinical studies.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> (See Renal Impairment under Dosage and Administration.)

Safety and efficacy not established in patients with hepatic impairment.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Hepatic impairment not expected to affect pharmacokinetics because metabolism not dependent on hepatic function.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>11</sup></body></html>

Interactions for Cangrelor

Drugs Metabolized by Hepatic Microsomal Enzymes

Neither cangrelor nor its major metabolites inhibit activity of the hepatic CYP isoenzymes at therapeutic concentrations in vitro.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> Cangrelor not expected to interact with drugs metabolized by these microsomal enzymes.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html>

Actions

  • Nonthienopyridine, direct-acting P2Y12 platelet ADP-receptor antagonist; unlike thienopyridines (e.g., clopidogrel, prasugrel), but like ticagrelor, cangrelor binds reversibly to P2Y12 receptor and does not require hepatic transformation to exert its pharmacologic effects.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>2</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>6</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>9</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html>

  • Prevents signal transduction of the cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP) pathway, resulting in reduced exposure of fibrinogen binding sites to the GP IIb/IIIa complex and subsequent inhibition of platelet activation and aggregation.<html><head></head><body><sup>13</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>19</sup></body></html>

  • Compared with clopidogrel, cangrelor produces more rapid and effective inhibition of platelet aggregation and has a faster onset and offset of action.<html><head></head><body><sup>1</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>18</sup></body></html> <html><head></head><body><sup>20</sup></body></html>
