Note: This document contains side effect information about gallium nitrate. Some of the dosage forms listed on this page may not apply to the brand name Ganite.

Applies to gallium nitrate: parenteral injection

Warning

    Concomitant Use with Nephrotoxic Drugs
  • Risk of severe renal insufficiency in patients with cancer-related hypercalcemia when administered concomitantly with other potentially nephrotoxic drugs (e.g., aminoglycosides, amphotericin B).<sup>1</sup>

    Concomitant Use with Nephrotoxic Drugs
  • Risk of severe renal insufficiency in patients with cancer-related hypercalcemia when administered concomitantly with other potentially nephrotoxic drugs (e.g., aminoglycosides, amphotericin B).<sup>1</sup>

  • Discontinue gallium if use of a potentially nephrotoxic drug is indicated; continue hydration for several days after administration of the nephrotoxic drug.<sup>1</sup> Closely monitor Scr and urine output during and after this period.<sup>1</sup> If Scr exceeds 2.5 mg/dL, discontinue gallium therapy.<sup>1</sup>

    • Side effects include:

    Increased BUN and Scr, hypocalcemia, transient hypophosphatemia, decreased serum bicarbonate, decreased BP.
