Note: This document contains side effect information about nifurtimox. Some of the dosage forms listed on this page may not apply to the brand name Lampit.

Applies to nifurtimox: oral tablet

General

The side effects reported most often in patients treated with this drug for 60 days were vomiting, abdominal pain, headache, decreased appetite, nausea, pyrexia, and rash.[Ref]

Gastrointestinal

Very common (10% or more): Vomiting (14.6%), abdominal pain (included abdominal pain, upper abdominal pain; 13.2%)

Common (1% to 10%): Nausea, diarrhea[Ref]

Nervous system

Very common (10% or more): Headache (12.8%)

Common (1% to 10%): Dizziness

Uncommon (0.1% to 1%): Vertigo, paresthesia, tremor, somnolence, seizure, syncope

Postmarketing reports: Vertigo, amnesia, polyneuropathy[Ref]

Metabolic

Very common (10% or more): Decreased appetite (10.5%)[Ref]

Other

Common (1% to 10%): Pyrexia, weight decreased

Uncommon (0.1% to 1%): Asthenia, fatigue[Ref]

Dermatologic

Common (1% to 10%): Rash (included rash, macular rash, maculopapular rash, morbilliform rash, papular rash), urticaria

Uncommon (0.1% to 1%): Pruritus

Postmarketing reports: Angioedema, drug reaction with eosinophilia and systemic symptoms (DRESS)[Ref]

Hematologic

Common (1% to 10%): Anemia, eosinophilia

Uncommon (0.1% to 1%): Neutropenia, leukopenia

Postmarketing reports: Thrombocytopenia[Ref]

Psychiatric

Uncommon (0.1% to 1%): Irritability, anxiety

Postmarketing reports: Apathy, agitation, psychotic behavior, sleep disorder[Ref]

Musculoskeletal

Uncommon (0.1% to 1%): Arthralgia, myalgia

Postmarketing reports: Muscle weakness[Ref]

Hypersensitivity

Postmarketing reports: Hypersensitivity reactions (including anaphylaxis)[Ref]
