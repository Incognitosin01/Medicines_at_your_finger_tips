Note: This document contains side effect information about bacitracin. Some of the dosage forms listed on this page may not apply to the brand name BACiiM.

For the Consumer

Applies to bacitracin: parenteral powder for injection

Warning

    Nephrotoxicity
  • May cause renal failure due to tubular and glomerular necrosis.<sup>104</sup> <sup>137</sup>

    Nephrotoxicity
  • May cause renal failure due to tubular and glomerular necrosis.<sup>104</sup> <sup>137</sup>

  • Restrict use to infants with pneumonia and empyema caused by susceptible staphylococci.<sup>104</sup> <sup>137</sup> Use only when adequate laboratory facilities available and constant supervision of the patient is possible.<sup>104</sup> <sup>137</sup>

  • Determine renal function prior to and daily during therapy.<sup>104</sup> <sup>137</sup> Do not exceed recommended daily dosage.<sup>104</sup> <sup>137</sup> Maintain fluid intake and urinary output at proper levels to avoid renal toxicity.<sup>104</sup> <sup>137</sup>

  • Discontinue if renal toxicity occurs.<sup>104</sup> <sup>137</sup> Avoid concomitant use with other nephrotoxic drugs, particularly aminoglycosides (kanamycin, neomycin, streptomycin), polymyxin B, colistin (commercially available in US as colistimethate), and vancomycin.<sup>104</sup> <sup>137</sup>

    • Side effects include:

    Nephrotoxicity (albuminuria, cylindruria, azotemia, rising blood concentrations of the drug); GI effects (nausea, vomiting); pain at injection site; hypersensitivity reactions (rash).

    For Healthcare Professionals

    Applies to bacitracin: compounding powder, intramuscular powder for injection

    Frequency not reported: Nephrotoxic reactions (including albuminuria, cylindruria, azotemia)[Ref]

    Gastrointestinal

    Frequency not reported: Nausea, vomiting[Ref]

    Local

    Frequency not reported: Injection site pain[Ref]

    Dermatologic

    Frequency not reported: Skin rashes[Ref]
